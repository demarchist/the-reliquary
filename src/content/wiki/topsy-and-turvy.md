Topsy and Turvy are Svirfneblin siblings — Deep Gnome twins — who were captured by Ilvara Mizzrym's Drow patrol and imprisoned at [[velkynvelve|Velkynvelve]]. Their village had been raided, and when the group arrived in the cells they found the twins quiet and watchful, with the tips of their ears docked: a mark of the prison. [[doc|Doc]] spoke with them in a dialect of Gnomish close enough to bridge the gap.

## The Underdark

The twins escaped [[velkynvelve|Velkynvelve]] alongside the group when demons gave them their opening, riding out on [[arkto|Arkto]]'s broad back with [[gundren-rockseeker|Gundren]] and [[snorbul|Snorbul]] as the winged owlbear glided down from the prison level into the dark below. They traveled west with the group toward the [[darklake|Darklake]].

During a rest stop after the escape, [[tarkathee-daru|Tark]]'s nose had been giving him trouble since they left the prison. Between him and [[doc|Doc]] they pieced together the cause: the twins were wererats, and deeply uncomfortable with what they were becoming — unsure how to manage the condition and afraid of it.

[[cirrus-al-kayan|Cirrus]] thought on it. His goddess [[selune|Selûne]] is the patron of good lycanthropes, among other things. He offered to pledge them to her — not as a cure exactly, but as a path through it, a context in which the condition could be something other than a source of shame.

Their fear of what they were becoming was enough. They agreed.

[[cirrus-al-kayan|Cirrus]] conducted a small ceremony of dedication in the dark. When it was finished, the twins decided they would leave the [[underdark|Underdark]] entirely — catch the topside teleport with [[megno-slatesunder|Megno]] and [[horatio-von-bumidere|Buumi]], go up to find Selûne's light and begin properly.

They were among [[cirrus-al-kayan|Cirrus]]'s first converts.

## The Fane of Selûne

Topsy and Turvy eventually found their way to the [[fane-of-selune|Fane of Selûne]] on the surface, where they serve as the primary administrators, overseeing its day-to-day operations on behalf of [[cirrus-al-kayan|Cirrus Al-Kayan]].

## Divergent Views

Despite their shared faith, the siblings have cultivated profoundly differing perspectives on religious dogma. While one adheres to more conventional religious ideals, the other has adopted a decidedly more radical stance, characterized by participation in self-mutilating religious rituals. This divergence creates an unusual dynamic within the Fane's administration.
