The Saurian Lance is a long-form melee weapon that the [[vampire-knight-of-frostmoore|Vampire Knight of Frostmoore]] carried into the battle above the [[aimu-estate|Aimu Estate]] — ten feet of black steel haft tipped with a metallic head whose tip splits slightly to bare twin filaments. The technology is unmistakably [[saurians|Saurian]]: the same alien sourcework that drives the [[saurian-firelance|Firelance]], adapted into a polearm rather than a hand-cannon.

## Function

In melee, the lance is a conventional cavalry weapon — long, heavy, mounted on a war-saddle for the charge. At range, the wielder tilts the lance forward; the twin filaments at the tip part wider; a ribbon of crackling electricity washes along their length; and a brilliant flash of light discharges from the head as a Lightning Bolt blazes from the weapon along its line of aim.

The bolt has the range and the lateral persistence of a true magical Lightning Bolt — when fired at [[tarkathee-daru|Tark]] in mid-air over the [[aimu-estate|Aimu Estate]], the missed shot continued through three walls of the manor and into [[lady-isabel|Lady Isabel]] at the far end of its arc.

## Significance

The lance is the second piece of confirmed [[saurians|Saurian]] sourcework technology to appear in Faerûnian hands — alongside the [[saurian-firelance|Firelances]] the party brought back from [[al-askar|Al Askar]] — and the first to be used *against* the party by a hostile force operating on this side of the [[ringed-veils|Ringed Veil]].

The implications are unsettling. Either the [[vampire-knight-of-frostmoore|Vampire Knight]] has visited Al Askar, or someone has brought him this weapon, or there exists in the upper world a supplier of Saurian technology the party has not yet identified. Each of those possibilities is worse than the last — and each ties the Damaran political situation, the [[red-wizards-of-thay|Red Wizard]] coalition, and the [[zhengyi|Witch-King's]] webwork of acquisitions to the broken world the party once thought they had left behind.

The Vampire Knight took the lance with him when he vanished. After the battle ended and the party shot his dragon mount from the sky, he was gone — invisible, saluting from the air an instant before he disappeared. The weapon's source has not yet been traced.
