Gaddock is [[tark|Tark]]'s adoptive father and the man who shaped the halfling into a monk. His fate — from enslaved prisoner to undead thrall — forms one of the most devastating emotional arcs of the party's time in [[underdark|the Underdark]].

## Who He Was

Gaddock was Tark's master and father figure, the person who first taught him to fight and who gave him a home. Through a dream spell in the Nether Mountains, Tark found him for the first time since their separation — a lone figure huddled in darkness, all skin and bone with wispy white hair and milky blind eyes. Gaddock thought the vision was a trick until Tark performed a stunning strike exactly as he had been taught. Recognizing the truth, the old man broke down and revealed his enslavement at the hands of [[zhengyi|Zhengyi]]'s followers.

The reunion sent Tark spiralling. He walked out of camp without a word and spent the night attacking trees in the forest, working through his anguish. From that moment, freeing Gaddock became one of his driving motivations.

## The False Rescue

Word eventually arrived that Gaddock had been freed. A powerful monk from Kara-Tur named [[kenshi|Kenshi]], along with his disciples, had reportedly stormed the prison complex and rescued him. In a dream meeting at the Monastery of the Yellow Rose, Tark found his master with more colour in his cheeks — bedridden but alive. Gaddock proved as wise as ever, refusing to allow Tark to rush to Damara, arguing that Zhengyi — backed by [[orcus|Orcus]] — was too powerful to face without allies.

It was all a lie. Kenshi did not exist. He was a fabrication Zhengyi had been using since the winter solstice to keep track of the group, coaxing information about their location and plans out of Tark through the manufactured relationship. The "rescue" had never happened.

## The Revelation

The truth came to light in [[gracksuluth|Gracksuluth]], when Tark was attacked by vampire Derro in the West Cleft and realized that only one person outside the party had known they were in the city: Kenshi, told during a recent dreamscape conversation. A Scrying spell on Kenshi returned nothing — the man did not exist on any plane. A Scrying on Gaddock, however, worked.

Doc B narrated what the crystal revealed: a dark, cold chamber carved from ice, a single bed covered in plush furs, and in it a pale, unmoving elderly man — strong of build, silver-haired, full-bearded. Tark entered a meditative dream to speak with him.

Gaddock confirmed everything. He had died in Zhengyi's prison. He had been raised as a vampire — kept alive, in the most wretched sense of the word, as leverage against his beloved student. He urged Tark to stay away. He could not be saved, he insisted; the only path to ending his suffering was destroying Zhengyi himself.

## Personality

Even in undeath, Gaddock retained the qualities that made him a great master: patience, clarity, and an unflinching willingness to tell Tark hard truths. He had refused to let Tark rush to his aid when he was merely a prisoner. He refused again when he was a vampire, with even higher stakes. His final counsel — *stay away; beat Zhengyi; that is the only way* — was the act of a man who still put his student's purpose above his own salvation.

## Role in the Story

Gaddock's transformation represents [[zhengyi|Zhengyi]]'s cruelty at its most calculated. The Lich-King did not merely take a hostage; he converted one of Tark's most trusted relationships into a surveillance network, turning a father figure into an unwitting informant. The revelation shattered Tark's composure — he locked himself in a bathroom and demolished it while the party waited outside — and redefined his relationship to Zhengyi from obligation to something far more personal.
