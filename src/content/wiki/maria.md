Maria is a young cleric of Helm sent from the House Invincible in Silverymoon to serve as governess to Georg von Strapp's seven children. She has an open, sun-freckled face, chestnut hair that refuses to stay pinned, and a natural warmth that won the children's affection through charm rather than discipline. She wore the simple grey robes of her order with the sleeves rolled up past her elbows.

Maria and Georg fell in love quietly — built on shared glances over the children's heads and the way Georg's rigid posture softened when she entered a room. When Baroness Elsa confronted her about the attachment, Maria fled to the House Invincible. After two weeks of prayer, she returned, telling Georg that Helm's guidance had been clear: she belonged with the family. The two married in a small chapel near the villa.

Maria fled Silverymoon with Georg and the children during the Midwinter Folk Festival, her singing voice carrying above the children's harmony during their final performance before escaping through the western gate.
