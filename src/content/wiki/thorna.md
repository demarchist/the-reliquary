Thorna is an Air Genasi Cleric and a member of the [[faerun-defense-force|Faerûn Defense Force]], serving alongside the other members of the council that governs the Barony of [[thunder-bluff|Thunder Bluff]]. She wields an icy hammer capable of channelling frost magic and calls lightning down upon the enemies of the group with devastating effect.

## Unknown Origins

Thorna's personal history is largely unknown, even to herself. When the party encountered the ancient shaman Umu at the [[obsidian-order|Obsidian Order]]'s encampment near the Sea of Silt on [[al-askar|Al Askar]], Umu conducted a spirit-walking ritual to divine Thorna's past and returned with a cryptic answer: her destiny lay in the frigid north of [[faerun|Faerûn]], and she was not alone — she was one of two.

## Al Askar

In Al Askar, Thorna's magic proved unreliable. The wild magic storms that plagued the planet caused her spells to surge unpredictably — at the Obsidian Order's camp, a simple casting triggered a wild magic eruption that a nearby elven spellcaster had to counterspell, earning her sharp admonishment and dark looks. Reckless magic was not tolerated among the survivors of a broken world.

Despite this instability, Thorna played a critical role during the ritual at the [[font-of-fawden|Font of Fawden]]. When a sabotaged Sapphire Circuit in the [[ringed-veils|Ringed Veil]] began to glow red-hot and threatened to destroy the apparatus mid-ritual, Thorna hit it with a Ray of Frost — cooling it long enough for [[gundren-rockseeker|Gundren]] to devise a fix, bypassing the sabotage entirely with a braided cord of precious metals recovered from the [[machina-factory|Machina Factory]].

## The Battle at the Font

During the defence of the Font against [[vecna|Vecna]]'s undead legions, Thorna fought at the heart of the line. She called lightning down upon the charging dead, hammered blasts into the approaching Saurian hordes alongside [[snorbul|Snorbul]]'s axe and [[tarkathee-daru|Tark]]'s fists, and held her position as the storm above Sharza intensified in answer to her magic.

When the forces arrayed against them became overwhelming — Death Knights, a Lich, Vecna himself whispering curses into their minds — Thorna threw her hand into the air, hammer held high, and called upon the gods to intervene.

Horrah, goddess of Time, answered. Time froze — all of it, everywhere — except for the group themselves. A golden light pierced the storm clouds above Vecna's pyramid. It was Thorna's prayer that bought [[cirrus-al-kayan|Cirrus]] the seconds he needed to complete the ritual and destroy Fawden's corrupted avatar, flinging the group to Yggdrasil and — eventually — home.
