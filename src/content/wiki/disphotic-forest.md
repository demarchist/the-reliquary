The Disphotic Forest is an immense subterranean ecosystem existing deep beneath the crust of Al Askar — an inner world of such scale that its far reaches are lost to fog and distance. Lit by millions of glowing Sourcestones threaded through vast pillars of stone, the cavern supports an abundance of alien life: bioluminescent coral and mosses carpet every surface, schools of floating jellyfish-like creatures drift between the columns, and gargantuan Manta Rays glide through the cloud banks that hang between the pillars and the distant ceiling.

The landscape is a shattered plane of plateaus at varying heights, connected by cliffs and chasms, with stalagmites rising hundreds of feet. Temperature varies wildly — some plateaus are sheathed in ice while others steam with volcanic vents and scalding hot springs populated by small blue crabs and Flumphs. Wild magic, which plagues the surface of Al Askar, is entirely absent in the depths of the Disphotic Forest.

Telepathic jellyfish occupy a niche similar to Myconids of Faerûn, charming creatures into joining their swirling dances. The stagnant pools are home to Sheitan — dangerous serpent creatures. The Wahid, massive reptilian humanoid giants, inhabit the deepest regions near the great pillars.

The party reached the Disphotic Forest by descending through the Obsidian Order's tunnels, a Nasnas-infested Saurian facility, and ancient slave mines. They traversed its alien landscape by fabricated mother-of-pearl glider and mount to reach the Font of Fawden at the base of a great pillar near the cavern's heart.
