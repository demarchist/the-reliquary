## Overview

Nostalgia for Infinity is an ancient Illithid collective led by a massive Elder Brain, stranded on the Prime Material Plane for approximately three thousand years. The colony inhabited a buried nautiloid — a bio-organic spelljamming vessel — fused into the roots of Ladypeak in the western Nether Mountains, beneath the ruins of the Lady's Hand Monastery.

The name reflects the collective's defining characteristic: an overwhelming, millennia-long yearning to return to the stars and the wider multiverse from which it had been severed.

## The Hive

The nautiloid was enormous, its hull merged with the surrounding stone over centuries of dormancy. Bioluminescent fluid coursed through veins in the walls, casting the tunnels in shades of pale blue and sickly green. The interior included organic tunnels with breathing membranes, assimilation chambers lined with transformation pods, a pool of tadpoles, and — at its heart — the Elder Brain's dome.

The dome was a cathedral-sized cavern where the Elder Brain floated in a web of psionic energy. Above it, an illusion or window showed the cosmos — wheeling stars, unnamed nebulae, and the suggestion of distant worlds. The psychic atmosphere within the dome was oppressive, pressing against visiting minds with almost physical weight.

## The Conspiracy

Decades before the campaign's events, the wizard Theodemir and his acolytes from the Conclave of Silverymoon entrusted the Eye of Aren — an artifact sustaining the binding spell over the lich Zhengyi — to Nostalgia for Infinity. The reasoning was sound: the Elder Brain's psionic field would amplify the Eye's power a hundredfold, and no one would think to search an Illithid hive for such a thing.

Nostalgia honoured the bargain — extracting the acolytes' memories of the conspiracy as agreed — then betrayed them. The Elder Brain attempted to keep all six mages for enthrallment, herding them with coordinated precision while separating Fëanon Sërindë from the group. The other acolytes fought free and fled. Fëanon was taken to an assimilation chamber for routine enthrallment, but his mind proved uniquely incompatible — the process collided with the fundamental architecture of his magical identity and both structures shattered. The collective discarded the drooling shell into its flesh recycler. What remained of Fëanon — a raw, primal magical aspect with no identity or fear — burned its way out of the hive alone. The psychic link forged during the botched enthrallment was never severed, leaving Fëanon plagued by the collective's stray thoughts for fifty years.

## The Escape

The collective had spent millennia slowly repairing its nautiloid for departure. When the party infiltrated the hive and reclaimed the Eye of Aren, Nostalgia — now bereft of the artifact but long prepared for this eventuality — triggered the nautiloid's launch. The massive bio-ship tore itself free from Ladypeak, devastating the mountain and leaving a crater behind. The party barely escaped the vessel as it ripped clear of the rock.

Nostalgia for Infinity escaped the Prime Material Plane — the ancient collective finally achieving its long-yearned return to the multiverse. The crater left behind became the site where the party regrouped in the aftermath.
