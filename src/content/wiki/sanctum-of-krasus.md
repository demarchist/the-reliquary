## Description

The Sanctum of Krasus is an ancient Netherese tower built during the height of the Netheril Empire, hidden within the split mountain of Shatterpeak. The tower is constructed of dark granite with harsh, angular architecture — slab-like walls covered in tiny runes and arcane script. It can only be perceived after passing through a stone archway on the mountain's north slope. Even the frost giants who live nearby can only see the tower in peripheral vision or as a mirage.

The entrance is a great black iron door etched with the same arcane symbols as the archway. Opening it requires a significant expenditure of arcane power — Fëanon Serinde channelled a sixth-level spell to make the door vanish. Once inside, visitors descend frost-coated stairs into a complex spanning multiple levels.

## Layout

The Sanctum descends from its entrance at the mountain's peak. The entry chamber houses an obsidian obelisk with four symbol-marked slots — Sun, Snowflake, Flower, and Eye — representing the ancient schools of magic and surrounded by the pentagram symbol of Netheril. This obelisk serves as both a security system and a self-destruct mechanism.

Below the entry level lies a grand hall decorated with murals representing the schools of magic, connected to quarters, laboratories, a kitchen, and chambers containing a trapped Beholder and a Gauth. The second floor houses a library, additional laboratories, and the remains of a four-armed marble golem. At the top of the spiral staircase lies the experiment chamber — where a catastrophic magical accident was frozen in a two-thousand-year time loop — and above that, a crystal-panelled octagonal scrying chamber.

## Discoveries

The party's expedition yielded critical artifacts: the incomplete Mythallar (a Netherese device for floating cities), Netherese medallions that allow spellcasting within dampening fields, a quartz tuning fork for planar travel, Prismeum in multiple forms, spell scrolls of extraordinary power, and a spellbook. The crystal scrying orb in the uppermost chamber revealed the escape of Atropos from its prison in the Far Realm and the existence of the Planet Born Dead — before a fragment of the elder evil manifested and had to be fought and banished.

## Inhabitants

By the time of the party's exploration, the Sanctum's living occupants were long dead. The complex was haunted by shadows, the Allips Promethi and Tantalisia (spectral remnants of the original inhabitants), and a Berbalang named Mervklo who had crossed from the Astral Plane to study a Netherese tome. A Beholder was trapped within a magic circle, and a Gauth roamed freely in a separate chamber.
