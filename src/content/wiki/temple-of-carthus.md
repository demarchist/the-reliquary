## Description

The Temple of Karthus is an ancient Saurian structure standing alone in a blasted wasteland on the coast — a huge domed edifice of white marble, angular pillars, and gold filigree. Its hexagonal sides are open to the elements. Inside stand primordial monoliths and an intricate central dais. The temple sits at the epicentre of devastating magical destruction, surrounded by dead terrain and swirling clouds of purple wild-magic lightning. Piles of bones and webs of corruption litter the interior — the remains of pilgrims who failed over centuries.

## Trials

The temple descends through multiple levels, each containing a trial:
- **Mirror Puzzle** — rotating mirrors must illuminate only the symbols of gods aligned to Preservation, not Ruin. Failure summons stone construct guardians.
- **Climbing Wall** — inscribed tiles shift toward spikes; the climber must touch only Preservation symbols while ascending.
- **Ethereal Visions** — each party member is drawn to a mirror matching their chosen virtue and transported to the Ethereal Plane, where one of Al Askar's gods appears in the form of their most important person to reveal truths and guidance.
- **Final Battle** — stone constructs must be defeated to prove worthiness.
- **The Font of Power** — a sacred basin beneath an alien tree-plant, filled with still water and glowing pearls visible only to the worthy. Meditating here attunes practitioners to control wild-magic surges.

## Significance

No one had successfully completed the pilgrimage in centuries before the party's arrival. The temple granted Buumi and Liara pearls of divine power, restored Cirrus's ability to manifest celestial magic (black angel wings and celestial summoning), and provided critical intelligence: Zalaam is an imposter, reagents for Feanon's ritual can be found at the Black Sea, and a portal home may exist in the Feywild.
