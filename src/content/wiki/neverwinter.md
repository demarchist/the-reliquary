Neverwinter is a major city on the Sword Coast, overlooked by a dormant volcano. The city is scarred by a massive chasm that splits its ruined section — a crevasse sealed by magical wards that descends deep underground into spider nests, undead-filled tunnels, and an ancient celestial vault. Lord Dagult [[neverember|Neverember]] rules the city and is the uncle of [[horatio-von-bumidere|Buumi]].

## The Refounding Festival

The party arrives during the Refounding Festival, a celebration of Neverwinter's independence from the Thayans and [[netherese|Netherese]]. The festival features tournament combat, an archery competition, a bards' stage, merchant pavilions, and gambling stalls around [[bluelake|Bluelake]]. [[lothli|Lothli]] won the archery tournament after a rival bard was disqualified. In the martial tournament, [[tarkathee-daru|Tark]] defeated Endomycles and Verith before advancing to the finals against Moot, while [[aliyah-faen-tlabbar|Patches]] beat Gasciogne and [[hendrell|Hendrell]], the Champion of the Moonwood — the latter with poisoned blades. [[jack-mayer-johnson-mathews|JmJm]] delivered a legendary bard performance involving a spectral steed, faerie fire, standing stones, and fireworks, charming the bard Lorde into joining him onstage.

The festival was disrupted by a coordinated cultist assault — roughly thirty enemies threw off disguises mid-tournament, supported by a powerful spellcaster in red who opened with a fireball that killed bystanders. The party and Moot slaughtered the cultists, though the enemy mage proved dangerous before Patches paralysed him with a devastating sneak attack and Tark crushed him with Moot's staff. The cultists' tracks led back to the Chasm, making the descent "doubly urgent" according to Neverember.

## The Chasm

The Chasm is a massive crevasse beneath the ruined section of Neverwinter, accessed through a wrecked tavern that had crumbled into it. The descent revealed switchbacks, stirge-infested ledges, a gargantuan spider matriarch larger than a wagon, phase spiders, an Umber Hulk, and a mist-filled labyrinth that induced madness in those exposed to it.

At the bottom, the party discovered an ancient celestial vault protected by a golden ward — bypassed using a wardstone crafted by [[gundren-rockseeker|Gundren]] and the [[harpells|Harpells]] at Longsaddle. A massive marble guardian statue greeted them and explained the vault's history. Inside, murals depicted the fall of [[orcus|Orcus]]: the Demon Lord wielding a radiant artifact and skull staff, laying waste to the world; a celestial host casting him down into the Abyss; a god stepping forward to face him, seemingly immune to the artifact; and a cataclysmic blow that destroyed both. The celestials split the artifact into five pieces and scattered them across the multiverse. The final panel showed the beast sleeping within an egg at the heart of a dark orb.

The vault's trials — a Forest of Fiends requiring negotiation with demons and devils, and a tomb guarded by ghostly paladin kings — tested the party's worthiness to claim the [[artifact-shard|artifact shard]] within.

## Other Events

The party also slew a three-headed Hydra responsible for disappearing sailors in Neverwinter's bay, discovering it in an underwater cavern beneath the docks. At the Moonstone Mask inn, the gold-toothed wizard [[kangaxx|Kangaxx]] recruited them to rescue companions transmuted into gold in the Chasm — a meeting that would have far-reaching consequences. Neverember granted the party patronage with a writ, formally founding the Spellforged Adventuring Company and commissioning them to investigate the Chasm.
