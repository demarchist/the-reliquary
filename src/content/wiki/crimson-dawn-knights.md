The Crimson Dawn Knights — formally the Order of the Crimson Dawn — are the unified military and peacekeeping order of [[damara|Damara]], formed from the merger of the Warlock Knights of [[vaasa|Vaasa]] (the [[warlocks-of-kelemvor|Warlocks of Kelemvor]]) and the Paladins of [[lathander|Lathander]]. They serve under the joint rule of King Yarin Frostmantle and [[queen-concetinna-frostmantle|Queen Concetinna]], governing under the banner *For the Greater Good*. Supreme command of the Order is jointly held by [[lord-aimu|Lord Aimu]], a Paladin of Lathander, and his partner [[lady-timea|Lady Timea]], a Warlock of Kelemvor — the partnership that holds the theological merger together in practice.

## In the City

The order's presence is immediately visible in [[helioglabulus|Helios]]. Their scarlet tabards move through the streets at steady intervals, maintaining order with the measured authority of a well-established force. They man the city gates, patrol the rings, and respond to disturbances. When [[feanon-serinde|Fëanon]] found a man whose memories had been devoured, it was two Crimson Dawn Knights — Vance and Krinn — who were trying to calm the victim. They accepted Fëanon's diagnosis and took the man to the cathedral's healers without argument.

A Paladin of the Crimson Dawn also approached [[cirrus-al-kayan|Cirrus]] on the streets to offer him a noble townhouse and concierge on behalf of [[house-aimu|House Aimu]] and the Queen's hospitality, apologising that such a distinguished guest had not been welcomed sooner.

## At the Aimu Estate

[[lord-aimu|Lord Aimu]]'s household guard at the [[aimu-estate|Aimu Estate]] in [[frostmoore-vale|Frostmoore Vale]] is a detachment of six Crimson Dawn Knights — fully armoured plate-and-mail, carrying ornate enchanted bows and blades — commanded by a plate-clad veteran named [[ishin|Ishin]]. They held the estate against the party at the front door behind a peephole grate until the dragons crested the ridge, then opened the door and joined the defence with discipline and good order under their commander's spyglass-led assessment of the field. Whatever the Order's institutional view of Lord Aimu's theft of the [[ashbringer|Ashbringer]] from the Cathedral, the men sworn to his household have followed him into hiding without apparent hesitation.
