## Background

Liara is a priestess of Zalaam — the true aspect of the death god, not the corrupted version worshipped by the Black Skull cult. She serves as a prominent member of the Usurpers of Rudjek, the revolutionary group that overthrew the vampire lord Rudjek in Iyum. She followed Rudjek's Vizier — a Black Skull cultist — out to the Saurian mine ruins in the mountains, which is where she encountered the party.

## Role in the Campaign

Liara meets the party when they emerge from the Ringed Veil into Al Askar, her group having already cleared the cultists guarding the arrival chamber. She becomes an essential guide, teaching the party to control wild sourcery to avoid dangerous magical surges, explaining why metal gear is lethal on Al Askar (it conducts source static), and proposing the Pilgrimage of the Ancients as a path to both power and access to the dead city of Sharza.

Her use of necromancy in combat — raising a fallen beast's corpse to fight — alarmed the party, particularly Tark, though she defended it as part of Zalaam's true doctrine of death as natural balance, not corruption.

## Notable Traits

- Priestess of Zalaam who opposes the Cult of the Black Skull
- Member of the Usurpers of Rudjek revolutionary government
- Capable necromancer and healer
- Teaches the party to mitigate wild sourcery surges
- Knows a lullaby about Al Askar's moon being chased by its binary star
- Revealed she was once a favoured thrall of Rudjek, giving her firsthand understanding of vampire enthrallment
