The Font of Fawden is a gleaming cavern over a hundred feet in diameter, hidden within the heart of a great stone pillar in the deepest reaches of the Disphotic Forest. At its centre, the living avatar of Fawden — an alien, inverted tree with roots spreading into the ceiling and branches reaching down in a swirling mass — hangs suspended above a wide, shallow basin of solid stone filled with eerily still water.

The chamber radiates a purple aura of otherworldly energy. Small motes of glowing light drift through the air, dissolving to water where they land. The smell of ozone and ammonia permeates the space. Rings of dead, calcified serpents circle many of the pools that dot the cavern floor, their bodies twisted as if they had tried to flee the water. The waters of the Font leak downward through fissures, feeding the bioluminescent ecosystem of the surrounding Disphotic Forest and carrying the fading power of the dying goddess.

The party reached the Font after a gruelling journey through Saurian tunnels, Nasnas territory, ancient mines, and the Disphotic Forest itself. They assembled the Ringed Veil beneath Fawden's avatar and began a ritual with The Last Word to channel the goddess's dying energy to Selune rather than allowing Vecna to consume it. During the ritual, Vecna discovered their plan and tore open a gate to Sharza, unleashing an army of undead Saurians upon the Font.
