Lathander, the Morninglord, is the god of dawn, spring, birth, renewal, creativity, and youth. His faith is one of the most widely practiced in [[faerun|Faerûn]], with temples and shrines in nearly every settlement. His clergy are optimistic and active, encouraging new beginnings and the pursuit of creative endeavours. His symbol, a stylised sunrise, is a beacon of hope across the Realms.

## Barovia

Lathander's most dramatic intervention in the campaign came at the fall of [[barovia|Barovia]]. As Castle Ravenloft materialised in a desert at dawn, Strahd forced his army of undead into the sunrise. Every Barovian suddenly cocked their head as if hearing a voice — Lathander offered each a choice: live a mortal life, or accept his embrace and be taken to rest. [[ireena|Ireena]] crumbled to dust, accepting the call. The survivors of Krezk chose the same. Strahd himself doubted any celestial would have him after all he had done, but hoped Lathander would forgive him — and walked into the light.

## The Cathedral of Helios

The Cathedral of Lathander dominates one quarter of [[helioglabulus|Helios]] — a great domed structure bristling with steeples and radiant spires that blazes like a second sun. Inside, altars and chapels sit beneath sweeping stained glass, the centre dominated by a vast golden altar and intricate solar calendars traced in polished Bloodstone across the floor. The [[ashbringer|Ashbringer]] — the celestial greatsword the group brought back from Barovia and donated to the Church of Lathander in [[neverwinter|Neverwinter]], where the clergy catalogued it as a [[sunsword|sunsword]] — had been transferred to this cathedral before [[lord-aimu|Lord Aimu]], a Paladin of the faith, broke in and stole it.

## The Crimson Dawn

The Paladins of Lathander in [[damara|Damara]] merged with the Warlock Knights of [[vaasa|Vaasa]] to form the [[crimson-dawn-knights|Order of the Crimson Dawn]], the unified military order that governs the [[bloodstone-lands|Bloodstone Lands]] under the banner *For the Greater Good*. Their scarlet tabards are a common sight in the streets of Helios.

## Celestial Servants

[[anduriel|Anduriel]], a Planetar of Lathander, lived for centuries disguised as the Abbot of the Abbey of St. Markovia in [[krezk|Krezk]], ultimately sacrificing himself to reforge the crystal greatsword into the Ashbringer. [[sergei|Sergei Von Zarovich]], Strahd's younger brother, was a devout Paladin of Lathander whose murder became the catalyst for Barovia's eternal curse.

## Other Connections

The Morninglord's influence is felt across the Realms. In [[selunia|Selunia]], local festivals honour him — one tradition involves running to the top of Red Hill and cliff-diving into Lake Selunia to demonstrate courage and vigour. The mace [[lightbringer|Lightbringer]], sacred to Lathander, was lost within [[wave-echo-cave|Wave Echo Cave]] and sought but never found by the party. In [[athkatla|Athkatla]], the ancient demilich [[kangaxx|Kangaxx]] was destroyed in the ruined plaza of Lathander — a fitting end in the Morninglord's own ground.
