The Elder Orb was an ancient Beholder who took up residence in the deepest reaches of the [[mines-of-und|Mines of Und]] in the Galena Mountains after being driven from his original home in the [[netherese|Netherese]] north. Unlike the younger aberrations the group destroyed in the village above, the Elder Orb was a creature of immense age and power — capable of warping reality around his lair, projecting psychic influence across an entire settlement, breeding copies of himself within living hosts, and feeding from sources of magic rather than flesh alone. The group slew him in his audience chamber beneath the mines.

## Discovery

After destroying the second Beholder that erupted from a cloaked dwarf in the village of [[und|Und]], [[jack-mayer-johnson-mathews|JmJm]] lingered in the [[ethereal-plane|Ethereal Plane]] atop [[steden|Steden]] and observed the mines blazing with power — lines of light pulsing through the rock like a heartbeat, visible only from the other side of the veil. The energy was far too strong for an ordinary Beholder.

[[feanon-serinde|Fëanon]] identified the source as an Elder Orb — a Beholder that had lived for centuries, its powers deepening with age. Where younger Beholders twist their immediate surroundings, an Elder Orb reshapes reality itself around its lair. The Beholders the group had killed were its children — spawned copies planted within the villagers as agents. The Elder Orb was the true [[the-dreamer|Dreamer]], projecting its will from the depths.

## Feeding

Fëanon explained that while Beholders consume flesh for sustenance, the truly ancient ones can also feed from strong sources of magic — draining enchantments and ley lines. Whatever the Elder Orb found in the mines, he had been feeding from it long enough to thread the surrounding stone with glowing [[dream-crystals|crystal]] and twist the tunnels into something too perfect to be natural.

## The Lair

The mines surrounding the Elder Orb's lair bear the marks of his influence. The stone is threaded with veins of luminous crystal, the walls unnaturally smooth and symmetrical. Eyes embedded in the rock — closed but dreaming, their orbs moving restlessly beneath the lids — served as sleeping sentinels, projecting the creature's influence outward. The tunnels were infested with aberrations drawn to his power: Ropers nesting in the upper caverns, a Spectator and a Gauth gathering crystals in the outer chambers, and a twisted spider-creature with a half-humanoid face lurking in the passages.

The deeper passages descended through a vast crystal shaft into a buried Netherese city — crystal archways, pillars, lintels, and balconies grown out of the stone in the geometric style of an enclave two thousand years dead. At the bottom of the shaft, a full crystal street stretched into the dark, terminating in an illusory portal that disguised the entrance to the Orb's audience chamber.

Most dangerously, the Elder Orb's dreams leaked into physical reality. The stone in the tunnels periodically warped and melted without warning, softening like candle wax and dragging creatures down into the rock. The group experienced this twice during their initial descent, barely pulling free before the stone resolidified around them.

## Appearance

The Elder Orb himself was a sphere of pink, fleshy folds the size of a small wagon — pendulous skin, sagging and wobbling as he hovered, with long jointed eyestalks like fingers ending in inkblack orbs. His central eye was vast and lidded in flesh that resembled the mouth of an enormous frog, and beneath it sat *human teeth* — even, white, set in pink gums, smiling. He spoke in a voice like wet rope dragged across glass and was, by his own clear estimation, the most important being in the room.

## Origin and Exile

The Elder Orb had not been born in the Mines of Und. He came from a [[netherese|Netherese]] ruin trapped in ancient ice, far to the north — an enclave that had crashed in the fall of Netheril and been preserved beneath the glaciers ever since. He had lived there for centuries, drinking the dormant magic of the ruin, becoming what he was when the group met him.

And then a *thing* had come.

A spellcaster long past his mortal death, with the patience of stone and an army at his back, drove the Orb out of his Netherese home. The ruin and its accumulated magic now belonged to the spellcaster. The Orb fled south, found the disused dwarven mines beneath Und and a smaller node of Netherese power to feed upon, and rebuilt — humbled but alive.

The Elder Orb confirmed, when [[horatio-von-bumidere|Buumi]] asked, that the spellcaster's name had been [[zhengyi|Zhengyi]]. The revelation hung in the air for the moment before the battle erupted. The Orb regarded the group's enmity with Zhengyi as common cause and invited them to set down their weapons and discuss. They did not.

## The Battle

The fight in the Orb's audience chamber was unlike any other the party had survived. The central eye's antimagic gaze sloughed off direct spells — Banish dissolved in midair, Disintegrate had nothing to bite into, even [[eclipse|Eclipse's]] divine moonfire enchantments evaporated against his hide. The eyestalks fired in rolling barrages: telekinesis ripped chunks of the floor and balcony into the air, fear and petrification rays ground down the front line, and a death ray nearly killed JmJm outright. A reality-warping beam dragged a fresh Spectator into existence from a fold of spacetime mid-fight.

[[steden|Steden]] was killed in the opening barrage — eyebeams unmade the Nightmare, dragging him back to the [[the-nine-hells|Nine Hells]] and dropping JmJm thirty feet to the chamber floor. The bard pulled on the [[arcane-hand-glove|glove]] he had carried for years and was caught a foot above the stone by the conjured Arcane Hand. He landed on his feet without a stumble.

What broke the Orb was [[cirrus-al-kayan|Cirrus]].

The Tiefling cast True Polymorph on himself and shifted into the form of a Beholder — a perfect mirror of the Elder Orb, hovering across from his counterpart with all stalks ready. The Elder Orb howled in a fury that was less pain than *insult* — the obscenity of a mortal daring to assume the perfect form. The two great fleshy spheres engaged in a magical dogfight, drifting around each other in mid-air, each desperately trying to keep its central eye on the other and out of the other's antimagic cone. Cirrus tracked the Orb relentlessly, holding his cone fixed on the central eye and denying the creature the ability to negate spells or shrug off damage. The Orb listed, sagged, and fell.

## Loot

The corpse yielded eight intact eyestalks (DC18) and a long swatch of [[elder-orb-hide|Elder Orb Hide]] cut from the underside. The central eye was unsalvageable, ruined by Cirrus's final antimagic punch. A small ornate chest beneath the balcony contained a [[potion-of-supreme-healing|Potion of Supreme Healing]], a [[scroll-of-demiplane|Scroll of Demiplane]], and an [[instrument-of-the-bards|Instrument of the Bards]] — a modest hoard for an aberration of his age. The group concluded that the bulk of his accumulated wealth remained in the original Netherese ruin in the north, either now in [[zhengyi|Zhengyi's]] possession or buried beneath the glaciers awaiting whoever could find it.
