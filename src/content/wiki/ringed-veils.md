The Ringed Veils — also known as Fargates — are a network of interconnected portals connecting multiple worlds and planes of existence. Built by the ancient Saurian civilisation, the gates consist of massive ring structures that open dimensional passages when activated using a system of addresses — coordinates that identify specific destinations across the multiverse.

Each Veil can be disassembled into radial sections for transport and reassembled at a new location. The internal components are an intricate array of metal, crystal, and arcane housings, with braided cords of precious metals carrying the arcane energy through the structure. The network includes hub locations called Crossroads where multiple gate platforms converge.

## Discovery and Recovery

The party discovered a disassembled Ringed Veil in the deepest hangar of the Machina Factory in Thueba Swamp. Feanon retrieved the addresses to Faerûn and four other Prime Material destinations from an obsidian Saurian database, and the group escaped with the gate sections via a Contingency teleport moments before the Lady in White destroyed the facility.

## The Sapphire Circuits

At the Golden Tower in Iyum, Gundren and Feanon discovered that many of the Veil's circuits had been remotely transmuted into sapphire — a subtle sabotage that continuously replaced components via a mechanism resembling Instant Summons. With Hasrala's expertise, they spent hours stripping and replacing every corrupted circuit, though at least one was missed and nearly destroyed the gate during the ritual at the Font of Fawden.

## The Ritual at the Font

The party assembled the Ringed Veil beneath the living avatar of Fawden and used it in conjunction with The Last Word to perform a ritual channelling the dying goddess's energy to Selune. The Veil's activation was suspended by the ritual spell, holding the gate in readiness until the moment of Fawden's death. During the ritual, Vecna discovered their plan and tore open a separate gate to Sharza, unleashing an army of undead upon the Font.
