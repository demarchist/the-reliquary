Themberchaud the Grand is an Ancient Red Dragon and counterpart to Protanther. Together they were tasked with planeshifting the Dragons of Shou Lung to their new lairs via the Great Tree of Bahamut in Bloodstone City.

When the Dragonflight arrived, Themberchaud was nowhere to be seen — and Protanther's attempt to scry on him failed. His Duergar attendant Gartokkar Brutt noted with alarm that the volcano to the north was likely his master's lair, and that a Red Dragon of Themberchaud's power would only allow his volcanic lair to erupt in the most dire of circumstances.

The Dragonflight flew north to investigate, finding the entrance to Themberchaud's lair guarded by two giant Oni standing over a blood-soaked sack.

## The Wyrmsmith of Gracksuluth

In [[gracksuluth|Gracksuluth]], the great Duergar city of the deep [[underdark|Underdark]], Themberchaud holds the title of Wyrmsmith — the resident dragon who fuels the city's legendary adamantine forges with dragonfire. He keeps court in a vast cavern at the end of a long brass-doored passage, sprawled across a pile of gold and treasure attended by Salamanders and the Flameguard, the Duergar warrior-servants dedicated to his service. His Flameguard liaison was Gartokkar, who was incensed by the group's rapid rise in Themberchaud's esteem.

The group met him when escorting a destroyed Mummy Lord's artifact to be purified. Themberchaud burned it out of the amber housing with a focused beam of blue dragonfire — a demonstration of power he was nudged into by [[aliyah-faen-tlabbar|Patches]] and [[cirrus-al-kayan|Cirrus]] suggesting he might not be capable of it. In exchange, he absorbed the group's debt to the city and had the Flameguard issue them golden badges marking them as his personal servants.

Alone with the group, he shifted to a shapeshifted form — an eight-foot obese Duergar with a long black beard ending in glowing embers and robes in red and gold — and revealed his real concern: he believed the Duergar intended to kill him. Every previous Wyrmsmith had helped build one of the city's Adamantine Golems and been killed by it. He wanted the group, as outsiders with no loyalties in the city, to investigate what had happened to his predecessors. He also mentioned an "Archdevil" — a "Fiery Bitch" — who had arrived at Overlake Hold weeks before and was pushing to augment the city guard with Devils.

He asked [[feanon-serinde|Fëanon]] to teach him wizardry. The elf agreed.

The arrangement soured when [[droki|Droki]] was captured by the Flameguard and, under questioning, named the group as accomplices in robbery — technically not false. Themberchaud's trust was shaken but not broken: he chose not to attack [[tarkathee-daru|Tark]] when the monk arrived in a Tiny Messenger box to warn him the city was about to collapse. He acknowledged that his death at the hands of his own creation was a distinct possibility. He still refused to leave his hoard.
