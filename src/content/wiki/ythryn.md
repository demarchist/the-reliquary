Ythryn is the name of a city of late [[netherese|Netheril]] — among the last enclaves christened by the Empire before the catastrophe of [[karsus|Karsus]]' Folly. The historical record of it is fragmentary. The ruins of it have never been found.

[[feanon-serinde|Fëanon]] recalled the name as he stood in the stilled crystal chamber of the [[elder-orb|Elder Orb]] beneath the [[mines-of-und|Mines of Und]], studying architectural details that placed the buried chamber unmistakably in the high decadence of the final Empire — fluted columns, hexagonal capitals, the scrollwork of the last architectural movement. The Elder Orb had told them where he had originally lived: a Netherese ruin preserved beneath ancient ice far to the north, from which [[zhengyi|Zhengyi]] had driven him with an army at his back.

Fëanon proposed the connection aloud. The lost city of Ythryn. A glacier-bound enclave of the late Empire, preserved in ice for two thousand years. The Witch-King now sitting in it.

The hypothesis remains unproven — but it would, if correct, account for the displacement of the Elder Orb, the persistence of late-Netherese architectural memory in the crystal chambers of Und, and Zhengyi's evident pattern of methodically acquiring the ancient magical infrastructure of the fallen Empire alongside the Mythallar of the [[sanctum-of-krasus|Sanctum of Krasus]] and the prismeum-anchored ziggurats grown from stolen life force.
