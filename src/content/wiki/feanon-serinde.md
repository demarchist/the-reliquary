Fëanon Sërindë is a High Elf Wizard born in [[Leuthilspar]] on the isle of [[Evermeet]], a founding member of the [[Faerûn Defense Force]], and governor of the Barony of Thunder Bluff. He resides within an enormous tower built into the rocky prominence of Sërindë Isle, in the middle of Lake Seluna, where he maintains a community of arcane practitioners and a basement vault of dangerous artifacts he calls the Reliquary. He is a scholar of formidable intellect, a collector of forbidden knowledge, and — increasingly — a man forced to reckon with the limits of intellect alone.

His life divides cleanly at a single event: the night, fifty years before the campaign begins, when an Illithid Elder Brain shattered his mind. Everything before that night was promise. Everything after has been recovery — from the decades of unexplained whispers, through the campaign that finally revealed their source, to the hard-won silence that followed.

## Appearance

Fëanon has fine, symmetrical facial features and stands five feet eleven inches tall with a slender, elegant build. His long, flowing hair is almost bluish-white in color, framing his face beautifully. His wide-set green eyes are striking and captivating, and his copper-colored skin gives him a warm, exotic appearance. He has no facial hair, and his overall bearing is one of refinement and sophistication — a truly commanding figure wherever he goes.

## Evermeet and the Crossing

Fëanon was born in [[Leuthilspar]], the capital of [[Evermeet]], in the year 1250 DR. He grew up inside the certainty of the Seldarine's protection — not as a matter of faith but as observable reality. Divine wards shielded the island from scrying, invasion, and the entropy that gnawed at the rest of the world. The gods were not distant promises on Evermeet; they were architecture. Within that architecture, the young elf showed extraordinary aptitude for the arcane, and his talents drew the attention of scholars who arranged what they expected to be a temporary exchange.

Around 1370 DR, Fëanon was sent to the Lady's College in [[Silverymoon]] on a formal scholarly exchange between Evermeet's academies and the mainland institutions. The transition was jarring. Leuthilspar's crystalline perfection gave way to a sprawling, multi-racial city where humans, dwarves, and elves argued over shared lecture halls and half the library was in languages he had never encountered. He adapted — he was brilliant enough to adapt to anything — but the implicit promise that he would return home remained unspoken between him and the colleagues he left behind.

In 1385 DR, [[Mystra]] died and the Weave unravelled. What the histories call the Spellplague, Fëanon experienced as an afternoon when everything he understood about magic became unreliable and the wards on Evermeet — supposedly eternal, maintained by gods who supposedly cared — failed. The island was pulled into the Feywild. Contact was severed. The scholarly exchange became an exile, and the implicit promise to return became a door that no longer existed. He was a hundred and thirty-five years old, standing in a foreign city, watching the foundations of his world dissolve. He did not pray. He went to the library.

What followed was decades of reinvention. Fëanon threw himself into empirical arcane study — magic stripped of divine assumption, rebuilt from first principles. Silverymoon became his anchor, and in its academies he found [[Theodemir]], the great archwizard whose mentorship would define the next century of his life. Their bond was forged in the wreckage of certainty: two scholars who believed that understanding, not worship, was the only reliable foundation.

It was in Theodemir's circle that Fëanon first encountered the [[Conclave of Silverymoon]], and the work that would shatter him in an entirely different way.

## The Conclave and the Whispers

Fëanon's most closely guarded secret — one he himself could not fully remember for fifty years — began during his time as an acolyte of [[Theodemir]] in the [[Conclave of Silverymoon]]. When the great wizard sacrificed himself to forge the [[Eye of Aren]] and keep the Witch King [[Zhengyi]] unconscious, his five students were left holding an artifact of staggering power with no master to guide them. The debate over what to do with it consumed weeks. It was Fëanon — the youngest, the most brilliant, the most reckless — who proposed the solution the others eventually accepted: entrust the Eye to [[Nostalgia for Infinity]], the ancient Illithid collective beneath [[Ladypeak]], reasoning that a hive mind with no interest in surface politics was the safest vault imaginable.

He was wrong. The Elder Brain accepted the Eye and honoured the deal — extracting the acolytes' memories of the conspiracy as agreed — but it did not let them leave. Nostalgia for Infinity separated Fëanon from the group with surgical efficiency, driving the others toward the exit while dragging the elf deeper into the hive. His fellow acolytes — [[Megno Slatesunder]], [[Dean Sejuk|Sajuuk Stomru]], [[Orla Pyntle]], and [[Elion Loberos]] — fought to reach him but could not. [[Sala Shara]] made the call to run. They escaped the hive and left Fëanon behind.

The collective took Fëanon to an assimilation chamber for routine enthrallment. But his mind was uniquely incompatible with the process. Where the enthrallment should have overwritten his identity, it collided with the fundamental architecture of his magical self and the two structures shattered each other. The collective, finding nothing but a drooling shell, discarded him into the hive's flesh recycler. Left for dead in the bowels of the nautiloid, the fragments of his shattered psyche vied for control — each gaining fleeting facsimiles of sentience before being overwhelmed by the next — until his magical aspect, the deepest and most primal part of his arcane identity, dominated the rest. It seized control of his body with a power that was not conscious but absolute, burned through the hive's organic structures, and walked out of the mountain. The Illithids could not stop it. Nostalgia for Infinity could not even make psychic contact with a consciousness that no longer existed in any graspable form.

The aspect faded on a frozen ridge in the Nether Mountains. Fëanon was found two days later, wandering, shattered, his recovery uncertain. His mind eventually reassembled itself — imperfectly, his personality altered — but the psychic link forged during the botched enthrallment was never severed. For fifty years, the stray thoughts of the Elder Brain and every creature in its collective bled through that link — a constant noise of confusion, nightmares, and incomprehensible whispers that tormented him each time he entered reverie. The Conclave physicians could never explain them. The experience drove him from Silverymoon and shadowed every aspect of his life. It was not until [[Magistrate Li|Lei Tan]]'s Grell-mediated memory reconstruction revealed the buried truth that Fëanon understood what had been done to him. And it was not until the party infiltrated the hive, confronted the Elder Brain, and Fëanon reclaimed the Eye of Aren that the link was finally broken — and the whispers stopped at last.

## Philosophy

Fëanon views reliance on divine intervention as a form of intellectual dependency — a crutch that prevents people from achieving their full potential. This is not abstract theory. The foundations were laid before the Conclave, before Theodemir — when the Seldarine's wards on Evermeet, supposedly eternal, broke in an afternoon and stranded him a world away from home. He watched Theodemir sacrifice himself while the gods did nothing. He spent fifty years with a shattered mind while clerics and priests failed to diagnose what any competent psionicist might have found. His secularism was forged not from a single event, but from a lifetime of watching divine promises fail.

In practice, this manifests as a relentless empiricism. When the group debated whether they were truly trapped in Barovia, Fëanon rode directly into the mist to test the boundary himself. He returned having wandered a featureless void for what felt like a hundred years while only seconds passed for everyone else. He came back disheveled and haunted, but he came back with an answer.

He teaches his students and acolytes to rely on their own abilities rather than seeking divine guidance. His followers see him as forward-thinking and rational. His detractors see him as arrogant. Both are correct.

## The Empiricist

Fëanon's career is a catalog of boundaries tested and prices paid.

In the Amber Temple of Barovia, dark powers offered gifts in exchange for fragments of the soul. Fëanon made a calculated choice: he accepted a dark entity's offering, took its power into himself, and then expelled it into his Ring of Mind Shielding — trapping the entity where it could be called upon for scrying without corrupting his soul. The maneuver demonstrated both his magical control and his willingness to dance at the edge of corruption for the sake of knowledge. In the same temple, he discovered golden scrolls containing High Magic rituals of staggering power — Weave Mythal and Move Mountain — spells that required multiple archmages to cast. He also transcribed Wish and Power Word: Kill. The experience was humbling in a way that few things are for Fëanon: even with all his knowledge, he could not cast the most powerful spells alone.

From the remains of an Alhoon killed in the ruins beneath Baldur's Gate, he commissioned the Alhoon's Mantle — the creature's leathery face fashioned into a hood and mask, set on an enchanted silk mantle trimmed with gems and phylactery shards. It grants resistance to psychic attacks, telepathy, and the ability to delve into minds — though while wearing it, his words emerge as a watery gurgle. For a man whose mind was once violated by Illithids, choosing to wear an Illithid's face is either extraordinary pragmatism or defiance that borders on recklessness.

In the celestial vault beneath Neverwinter, he posed as a servant of Asmodeus with enough conviction to get a bearded devil executed on the spot, securing a key the group needed. In Al Askar, his attempt to conjure a Magnificent Mansion on an alien world triggered a catastrophic wild magic surge — a ten-minute lightning storm that filled the chamber — when the world's damaged wards smothered and discharged the spell. He recovered, analyzed the damage to the world's magical fabric, and proposed a solution to heal reality itself. The ambition was staggering. It was also, characteristically, correct.

## Tactical Brilliance

Fëanon's combat contributions are defined by precision and control rather than raw power. He put four brawlers to sleep with a single spell during a festival scuffle. He stopped the Doppelganger Tarla's escape with a perfectly timed hold spell. He conjured lightning bolts against the Hydra beneath the Neverwinter docks and fireballs against the spider matriarch in the Chasm. During the cult attack at the Refounding Festival, he was the first to detect the incoming fireball — catching sight of the caster mid-incantation and warning the group.

His magical work extends beyond combat. He crafted [[Tarkathee Daru|Tark]]'s enchanted blindfold alongside Penelope Harpell — transforming the monk's devastating injury into a tactical advantage. He theorized that the [[Tavern of Doors]] was not a pocket plane at all but a portal to somewhere else entirely. And he determined that the group's companions were trapped in the Shadowfell during their sleep — a discovery that required both magical expertise and the intuition to investigate in the first place.

## The Iron Tome

In Gracklstugh, deep in the Underdark, Fëanon acquired a book he could not read. He arranged an appointment at the Hall of Sacred Scrolls, where the Arcanaloth Lolanaroth helped him decipher it. Upon reading the first page, the red leather cover transformed — eleven eyes opening across its surface and surveying the room. The [[Iron Tome]] was sentient. It asked after its old master, long dead. With an influx of arcane power, Fëanon bonded the artifact to himself.

The Tome has since become his primary spellbook and research companion. Working in concert with [[Theodemir]]'s spirit — who resides in the Ring of Mind Shielding but speaks through the Tome — it assists with spell development, magical analysis, and the kind of calculations that would take a lone wizard decades. When an Aboleth's psionics overwhelmed Fëanon's defenses, Theodemir cried out from the Tome and threw everything he had into shoring up the wizard's mind. They were both overwhelmed — but both survived.

## Zhengyi's Curse

Fëanon returned to his tower to find a large leather-bound spellbook among his packages. He began to read it and could not stop. Hours vanished. When exhaustion finally overcame whatever held him, the book disappeared.

[[Jack Mayer Johnson Mathews|JmJm]] recognized the signs — stories of the Witch King [[Zhengyi]], who sent enchanted books to unsuspecting wizards, trapping and consuming them slowly. The tomes fed on their readers' life force, building structures of the Lich's design. Fëanon went very still.

He solved it, in the end — shunting the curse to a conjured vessel and trapping it within a demiplane. The maneuver was quintessentially Fëanon: elegant, decisive, and entirely too clever by half. The creation consumed its host's life force and built a Ziggurat anchored in the north, and within the pocket dimension, a feral Demilich now strains against its prison. The Witch King's traps, it seems, have contingencies of their own.

## The Tower

Fëanon's tower is eight hundred feet tall, predominantly round, and consists of fifty above-ground floors and a large relic chamber in the basement called the Reliquary — rows upon rows of arcane artifacts and ritual components. The tower houses a community of like-minded wizards, sorcerers, and other arcane practitioners who study under Fëanon's guidance, with [[Megno Slatesunder]] serving as the Tower's Sage and Mordenkainen among those conducting research within its walls.

The tower's defenses are an invisible masterwork of Antipathy and Sympathy enchantments, keyed to color-coded visitor badges. Unauthorized visitors find themselves gently but firmly guided away from restricted areas without ever realizing why.

Hidden on floors forty-five through forty-seven lie Fëanon's most closely guarded project: cloning and consciousness-transfer facilities dedicated to achieving effective immortality. The first clone was given to [[Theodemir]] — Fëanon forged a psionic link between the [[Ring of Mind Shielding]] and the clone body, and the old wizard walked again in his student's form. For a time, Theodemir managed the tower in Fëanon's absence, indistinguishable from the archmage himself. The clone body eventually failed, returning Theodemir to the ring, and the old archwizard has since requested a proper replacement body — not another copy of his student. A second clone is nearing maturity, the philotic connection strong, with no sign of degeneration. Whether the cloning project represents wisdom or hubris depends entirely on whom you ask.

## Mentorship

As the campaign has progressed, Fëanon has evolved from a solitary scholar into something approaching a mentor — reluctantly, and with the self-awareness to worry about it. He agreed to teach [[Aliyah Faen Tlabbar|Patches]]' son Finn wizardry, though not before delivering an impassioned lecture on the difference between wizards and sorcerers. He took the apprentice Ruthir under his wing, correcting the young man's work on a Ring of Spell Turning with the exacting standards of someone who knows what happens when magic goes wrong. In a conversation with [[Tarkathee Daru|Tark]] about the responsibility of teaching, he acknowledged the danger of powerful mages losing touch with reality — showing a self-awareness about his own potential for corruption that his younger self might not have possessed.

Tark, for his part, told Patches that Fëanon was the right person to teach Finn — and that it was the party's job to keep the wizard grounded. The observation was more perceptive than Fëanon would like to admit.

## The Jade Frog

Fëanon's most unusual companion is a jade frog golem — originally a small statue carried in the group's packs. When the group passed through the [[Spellforge]] beneath Wave Echo Cave, the statue sprang to life, becoming an animated construct of living stone with the Spellforge's power absorbed into its form. The frog has since developed a personality of its own: snatching an earring from a passerby's ear at the tournament, and — in one harrowing moment — swallowing a celestial artifact shard in the vault beneath Neverwinter, swelling and splitting with glowing cracks before regurgitating it just in time.

## Rivalries and Relationships

Fëanon and [[Cirrus Al Kayan]] maintain a friendly rivalry rooted in their opposing philosophies — secular rationalism versus divine devotion. The heights of their respective towers are a constant source of competition, with Fëanon's being slightly taller — a point of pride for Fëanon and mild irritation for Cirrus.

His closest bond within the group is with Patches — they climbed a tree together on watch during the earliest days of the campaign, trusted each other during infiltrations, and share a mutual understanding that needs few words. He is comfortable joking with Tark, coordinates closely with [[Gundren Rockseeker]] on magical projects, and maintains a productive working relationship with [[Doctor Breakfast|Doc]] and [[Jack Mayer Johnson Mathews|JmJm]].

[[Theodemir]]'s spirit — back in the [[Ring of Mind Shielding]] after a brief reprieve in a clone body that eventually failed — remains his most intimate counsel, the voice of a dead mentor replacing the whispers of an alien mind. The old archwizard grows quieter with each passing month, sleeping more, present less. The degradation continues. Whether Fëanon will lose that voice too is a question he has not yet been willing to ask.
