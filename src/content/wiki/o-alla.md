In [[gracksuluth|Gracksuluth]], where every faction has an agenda and neutrality is a kind of power, O'Alla occupies an unusual position: she watches everything and sells what she sees. Floating through the Whorlstone Caverns in draped robes with her great eyestalks tracking all movement in her domain, the Beholder serves as the mediator and clearinghouse for the Council of Derro Savants — keeping the different factions of Warlocks from destroying one another while extracting value from each of them in turn.

Patches first spotted O'Alla by spying on the Savants' council meeting. The massive floating head presided over the assembled Warlocks, adjudicating their squabbles — including heated accusations about who had been creating too many Vampires — with the detached authority of something that could disintegrate any of them with a glance. Her watching eye, extended into the walls of the complex, surveilled every corridor.

When the party needed information on [[milgre|Milgre]], they returned to the amphitheater beneath the Hall of Sacred Scrolls and Tark demanded the Beholder appear. O'Alla found the demanding tone tiresome, but the offer intrigued her: the group would exterminate Milgre and her vampire spawn, which had been a source of complaint at the council table. In exchange, O'Alla confirmed that Milgre had been seen heading to [[bilrigger|Bilrigger]]'s private sanctum — information that proved accurate.

The party also tried to negotiate for the stolen Red Dragon egg, which the Savants had apparently been involved in acquiring. O'Alla was surprised they knew, expressed doubt she would reach a favorable decision, and left the matter open. She has no apparent loyalty to the Derro cult beyond her role as its facilitating neutral party — which means she is useful precisely because her assistance can be purchased and she won't take sides afterward.
