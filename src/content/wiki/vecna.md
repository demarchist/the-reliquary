Vecna, the Whispered One, is the god of secrets, necromancy, and undeath. Once a mortal wizard of unsurpassed ambition, Vecna achieved lichdom and eventually ascended to godhood through sheer force of will and dark magic. He is known for his missing hand and eye, which were severed by his treacherous lieutenant Kas and became powerful artifacts in their own right. His followers seek forbidden knowledge and guard terrible secrets.

In Al Askar, Vecna's threat is existential. Operating through the guise of Zalaam, he has been systematically devouring the gods of that world, consuming the deity Zalaam entirely and slowly absorbing the goddess Fawden — explaining the increasing frequency of wild-magic surges and High Storms. He commands two armies: an army of the dead in Sharza, led by Saurian Death Lords, and an army of Shadowfell monstrosities in the Black Sea. His forces have systematically hunted down every druid on Al Askar — the beings best equipped to restore the world's shattered balance.

Feanon deduced Vecna's identity by connecting the evidence: the Tome of Al Azif's visions of a human wizard from another world subjugating the non-magical, the cult's preference for necromancy and its iconography, Zalaam being an imposter who orchestrated The Calamity a thousand years ago, and the cult leader's own claim that Zalaam was brother to Zhengyi and kin to Orcus. The Ruby Dragon Jareem reached the same conclusion independently.

Vecna's power on Al Askar may be tied to a Fragment of The Last Word, a Saurian artefact within the Pyramid of Barzok in Sharza. His cult leader at the Titanic Forge warned the party that the corruption had already reached the Underdark of Toril through the alghouls — claiming the death of both worlds was inevitable.
