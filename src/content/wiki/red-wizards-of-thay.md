The Red Wizards of Thay are an ancient and powerful order of arcane practitioners from the eastern land of Thay. They supply weapons, assassins, and magical resources to various factions, making them a persistent and dangerous threat across Faerûn.

Their Thayan Drop Boxes — enchanted containers filled with weapons and resources — are discovered by the party, revealing the extent of their covert operations. The Red Wizards' involvement in regional conflicts adds another layer of danger to the party's already complex political landscape.

## The Delegation in Helios

By [[lord-aimu|Lord Aimu]]'s account, the Red Wizards have maintained a delegation at the Damaran court for some weeks — entertained openly by [[queen-concetinna-frostmantle|Queen Concetinna]] at the social functions she has continued to host while otherwise sequestered. The Wizards themselves appear only occasionally, even at their own honour-events, and have spent most of their time in the quarters provided.

A few days before Aimu fled the capital, the delegation requested an audience with the new Holy Relic of Lathander — the [[ashbringer|Sunsword]]. Aimu advised the Queen to refuse; she overrode him. He and [[lady-timea|Lady Timea]] escorted the Wizards and the Queen into the Cathedral, where the delegation spent a measured length of time examining the blade and the Queen handled it personally before returning it to its plinth. Something in the exchange put Lord Aimu on edge — the examination, the Queen's gaze on the radiance, the cool quality of her grip — and the nausea of a paladin's instinct climbed in him through the rest of the day until he took the blade that night.

The Wizards' interest in the Sunsword is openly *strong*. The reason is not yet known.

## Death over Frostmoore Vale

A Red Wizard rode at the head of a flight of eight [[ashen-white-dragon|Ashen White Dragons]] into [[frostmoore-vale|Frostmoore Vale]] at sundown — crimson robes flowing, pallid skin marked from temple to neck with dark tattooed runes, mounted on the largest of the dragons. He came to interdict the party's recovery of the [[ashbringer|Sunsword]] from [[lord-aimu|Lord Aimu]]'s chapel, working in coordination with the [[vampire-knight-of-frostmoore|Vampire Knight of Frostmoore]].

His confidence cost him. He sneered at [[tarkathee-daru|Tark]]'s attempted parlay and struck the floating monk down with a single arcane blast — and then [[aliyah-faen-tlabbar|Patches]] put a poisoned crossbow bolt into his thigh that paralysed him instantly. The Vampire Knight kicked off his saddle and flew across the gap to catch his ally, but the entire group focused fire on the rescue: half a dozen arrows and crossbow bolts hit the Vampire as he made the catch, and every one of them passed *through* the paralysed body in his arms.

The Red Wizard died in the Vampire Knight's arms. The Vampire took his body into a sphere of cold darkness and disappeared from the field. Whatever the Thayan's name and faction within the Red Wizards' hierarchy, his death over a Damaran nobleman's hidden estate signals a depth of Thayan involvement in the Vaasan-Damaran political situation that the party has not yet mapped — and that the delegation in Helios has not yet been confronted about.
