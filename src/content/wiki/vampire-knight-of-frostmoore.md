The Vampire Knight of Frostmoore is an undead lord, armoured in fluted, asymmetrical black plate, who teleported into the battle above the [[aimu-estate|Aimu Estate]] mounted on a sickly grey adult white dragon — larger than the [[ashen-white-dragon|Ashen White Dragons]] of the flight by half again — in a thunderclap of displaced air.

## Appearance

His helm has no faceplate, only a deep T-slit through which two red eyes glow softly. Below the slit, pallid lips part in a faint, deliberate smile, revealing delicate vampire fangs. He carries a long tower shield of black steel on one arm and, in the other, a ten-foot lance bearing technology the group recognised immediately: a [[saurian-firelance|Saurian]] [[saurian-lance|lance-weapon]] — the kind that had no business existing on this side of the [[ringed-veils|Ringed Veil]].

He salutes opponents with the lance before engaging. He flies without wings, leaping bodily from his saddle with the casual grace of a man who weighs nothing.

## The First Engagement

His first act on arrival was to fire a Lightning Bolt from the lance toward [[tarkathee-daru|Tark]], who had launched into the air on a borrowed spear and shadowstepped to within striking distance of the [[red-wizards-of-thay|Red Wizard]] riding the lead dragon. Tark dodged. The bolt continued past, ploughed a smouldering rent through three walls of the estate's east wing, and caught [[lady-isabel|Lady Isabel]] full across the shoulder at the end of its arc.

When [[aliyah-faen-tlabbar|Patches]] paralysed the Red Wizard with a poisoned crossbow bolt, the Vampire Knight kicked off his own saddle and *flew* across the gap of air to retrieve his ally. He caught the falling, paralysed Thayan bodily, took half a dozen arrows and crossbow bolts from the party as he did — the Wizard's body absorbing every shaft — and made it back to his dragon's saddle holding a corpse. He looked down at the riddled body in his arms, roared in cold fury, and engulfed the entire position in a sphere of cold darkness that swallowed himself, his mount, and the dead Thayan utterly.

## The Force Cage

The sphere did not, as the porch first thought, mean the Knight was gone. He had retreated into his own shroud. [[tarkathee-daru|Tark]] and [[horatio-von-bumidere|Buumi]] pursued him inside — trading blows blindly in the impossible black against an opponent who had spent centuries learning to fight in such conditions — and [[jack-mayer-johnson-mathews|JmJm]] sealed the darkness in place with a cage of force braced through it like ribs around a held breath. The Knight hit the cage twice, the second time hard enough to ring it like a bell, and could not break out.

The cage held him long enough for the rest of the battle to resolve. Outside the dark, [[lady-isabel|Izmakella]] in her copper form tore the smaller Ashen Whites apart and dropped the adults under Slow Breath. The Red Wizard was dead. The wyrmlings were dead. The Knight's support was gone.

He broke out anyway.

In a final burst of dark power the cage shattered outward in a shower of silvered shards, and the Vampire flew clear of his own shroud with the lance held low. He surveyed the field, called the retreat to the two surviving adults, and turned in mid-air to face the porch. He raised the lance in a slow, deliberate salute toward the party — and then vanished invisible, the smile and the armour and the lance dissolving in a single breath.

## The Dragon Mount

[[cassian|Cassian]] had not stopped drawing. As the great Ashen White carrying the Knight's invisible weight climbed for the ridge, the ranger loosed the last of his magical arrows in a long, beautiful arc — three shafts in close succession, followed by [[aliyah-faen-tlabbar|Patches]]' poisoned bolts and the household archers' final volley. The dragon bellowed in pain, folded its wings, and crashed amongst the distant, dark trees of the mountainside. The body would be recoverable for those who cared to search.

What became of the Vampire after his mount fell — whether he reached the [[bloodstone-pass|pass]] on foot, mist, or some third method — has not been confirmed.

## The Return by Night

The Knight was not finished with [[frostmoore-vale|Frostmoore Vale]]. Hours after his dusk retreat, when the [[red-wizards-of-thay|Red Wizards of Thay]] opened their [[the-thayans-attack|night assault]] on the [[aimu-estate|Aimu Estate]] with an earthquake and a horde of ghouls, the Armored Vampire came back to the valley out of the dark. As [[horatio-von-bumidere|Buumi]] took to the air to help [[tarkathee-daru|Tark]] — who was at that moment being seized and bitten by the vampire [[gaddock|Gaddock]] — the Knight intercepted him above the broken estate and cut his path to his friend. His reappearance confirms that he survived the fall of his dragon mount and remained in coordination with the Thayan forces working the valley, a second vampire operating alongside Gaddock in a single night's attack.

## Implications

He arrived precisely when his ally needed extraction, with a teleport-grade movement and a dragon mount available on demand. He carried a [[saurian-lance|Saurian lance]] — a piece of [[al-askar|Al'Askarian]] technology unlike anything the group has seen in Faerûnian hands. He worked in concert with a Red Wizard of [[red-wizards-of-thay|Thay]] and a flight of Ashen Whites — a coalition that ties together the dragons over the Galena Mountains, the Thayan presence in [[helioglabulus|Helios]], and an unknown source of Saurian weaponry on this side of the [[ringed-veils|Ringed Veil]]. He took the lance with him when he vanished.

His identity remains unknown. He is *not* [[gaddock|Gaddock]] — Tark's vampire master is a bald pale monk with a black crystal staff, very much not an armoured lance-knight. The Knight of Frostmoore is something else, and his sudden appearance over a Damaran noble's hidden estate, in support of a Red Wizard apparently sent to interdict the recovery of the [[ashbringer|Sunsword]], suggests a coordination that runs much deeper than the group has yet uncovered.
