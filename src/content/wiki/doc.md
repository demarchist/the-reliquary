Doctor Breakfast, known to everyone as Doc or Doc B, is a Gnome Druid and a founding member of the Faerûn Defense Force. He first appeared at the Stonehill Inn in Phandalin in the form of a humanoid cat-person — feline features, tousled and alert — and from that first session struck up an immediate friendship with [[Jack Mayer Johnson Mathews|JmJm]] that has defined much of his story. He is quiet by the group's standards, shows care through action rather than words, and commands the battlefield with elemental force that is, for his size, alarming.

## Appearance

Doc is small even by gnome standards. He favours a tall gnome hat and, since his return from the Feywild, a coat of leaf-scale armour pinned with a single holly leaf. A pipe is rarely absent from his hand or mouth; the smoke curls and drifts with a faint arcane charge, and he uses it as a natural focus when calling down storms or coaxing the earth.

His form has always been unusual. For most of the campaign he appeared as a humanoid cat-person — distinctly feline, unmistakeably him. After his training under the Leanansidhe, something shifted: JmJm, looking at him for the first time since his return, saw a gnome rather than a cat and did not initially recognise him. Tark activated his enchanted blindfold and saw the same. Both trusted their instincts and confirmed it was still Doc. Whether the cat-person form was the illusion all along, or whether the Feywild trading reshaped something fundamental, has never been resolved.

## Personality

Doc is the quietest member of the group in social situations and among the most decisive in crisis. He observes, processes, and then acts — usually with more effect than anyone expected from the small figure in the corner. His playful streak surfaces in moments of downtime: he once got entirely distracted by a massive rat in the [[Tavern of Doors]] and chased it to the miniature farm inside, terrifying the tiny horses. He spooked a Doppelganger's horse with casual practical mischief during [[Gundren Rockseeker|Gundren's]] rescue.

His druidic connection is genuine and deep — not a combat toolkit but a way of moving through the world. He communicated intent to a Treant without words and it backed away and ambled off into the forest. He once attempted druidic communion with twig blights as they attacked him. He carves prayer beads by hand and uses the slow work as meditation. Each morning, wherever the group is camped, he produces a bejewelled bowl, casts the spell, and the smell of a Heroes' Feast fills whatever space they occupy — a quiet act of care that reliably drags the entire group to the table.

## Combat

Doc's magic leans toward wind, storm, and elemental force, with earth control as a secondary register. He is an area-denial caster first: devastating gusts that reposition enemies, lightning storms that punish grouped foes, moonbeams that expose shapeshifters and scour the undead. He heals when the group needs it, but his instinct is offensive.

In Phandalin, with [[Venomfang]] launching an aerial assault on the town, Doc called down a massive lightning bolt from the building storm and knocked the dragon out of the sky. That same night, he and JmJm performed a dual synchronized Thunderwave against the zombie horde, obliterating most of them in a single combined release. Against the cult attack at the Neverwinter Refounding Festival, he summoned a full lightning storm that swept cultists across the arena.

In the Underdark, his magic adapted to close stone corridors. When the Froghemoth ambushed the group in the steam caves, he pinned it with a Moonbeam while the rest of the group tore it apart. Later, after Patches was cut down by poisoned crossbow bolts and the group was being routed at the Underdark crossroads, Doc unleashed a massive earth eruption — stone and debris pulverizing every remaining orc in the area and slamming two Drow, including the enemy wizard — and was immediately Banished mid-action by the falling wizard's last spell.

## The Partnership with JmJm

From the moment they met — *"JmJm and the cat druid hit it off immediately"* — Doctor Breakfast and [[Jack Mayer Johnson Mathews|JmJm]] have been inseparable. They retreat to the [[Tavern of Doors]] together between encounters, coordinate their magic in combat, and prepare for performances side by side. The synchronized Thunderwave in Phandalin was their first signature moment; it would not be the last. During the Neverwinter tournament, while JmJm worked his enchantments from the stands, Doc sat ringside studying the fighters with detached academic interest — then mimicked the cry of [[Hendrell]]'s eagle companion from above, momentarily distracting the Champion of the Moonwood and costing him a clean hit during his bout with [[Lothli]].

When Doc was trapped in a 9th-level Dream spell on Al Askar and could not be woken, it was Buumi and JmJm together whose combined divine entreaty finally broke through.

## Demetrius and Kazoo

In The Bog, Doc and [[Gundren Rockseeker|Gundren]] stumbled on a tea party hosted by a massive cat-like creature — a Malk called [[Demetrius]]. The friendly Grimalkin offered advice and warnings, and the encounter planted a seed: Demetrius mentioned another cat-creature in the area called [[Kazoo]], who loved clams. When Doc brought her a bucket, they discovered they might be related. Possibly gnome-sisters.

Demetrius would resurface. By Chapter 7, he had taken Doc away for Fey training, delivering him to the Leanansidhe in The Bog. What he saw in Doc, and what Lea made of him, remains partly unexplained.

## The Feywild and the Leanansidhe

Between chapters, Doc vanished. He was taken by Demetrius to The Bog for training under the Leanansidhe — a Winter Noble of the Fey. He returned to the group by stepping out of a solid stone wall in a cavern beneath Helm's Hold, wearing the leaf-scale coat, while Lea dispersed into an unseen wind behind him. As she vanished, she pinned a holly leaf to his coat and told him to prick his finger and call her name three times if he needed to speak with her. Doc wailed after her that he only remembered her nickname.

What the training gave him — beyond the coat, the changed form, and the holly leaf — he has not said. What it cost him is also unclear. The group received him enthusiastically but with suspicion, confirmed it was Doc by smell and instinct alone, and moved on. The holly leaf is still pinned to the coat.

## The Fall at Velkynvelve

With the group imprisoned at [[Velkynvelve]], Doc made the escape attempt alone. He found the largest gap in the slave pen bars and was testing it when a crossbow bolt skipped off his skull and cut him. He went anyway — leapt through the gap as it closed, landed atop a surprised Drow guard, and grabbed down the front of his armour. The Drow hit him badly and he dropped to the floor, clutching the insignia he had pulled loose.

He called to the Feywild and a giant scorpion answered. While Patches and Gundren pelted the Drow with rocks to give him an opening, Doc threw the insignia into the slave cage — proof the guard was down — and then leapt off the cliff.

Three hundred feet. He slowed his descent as best he could, lost control in the final hundred feet, and hit the water hard. He surfaced alive, hurt, and immediately chased by a Grey Ooze from the pool. He ran, found a secluded tunnel, and cast Wind Walk. By the time the rescue party was gathering, Doc materialized from the saddle behind [[Horatio Von Bumidere|Buumi]] on a phantom steed, holding up the Drow insignia and telling the group exactly where their friends were held.

## Hunted by Vecna

On Al Askar, Doc was struck by a 9th-level Dream spell. He lay unconscious for days — not dead, but unreachable, the spell holding him under. [[Jareem]], the Ruby Dragon, studied him with his psionics and confirmed what the party suspected. Then he explained why: in all his centuries on this world, Vecna's forces had systematically hunted every druid they could find. The entire world was out of balance. Druids would have been best equipped to fix it — which was precisely why Vecna killed them.

Buumi and JmJm knelt beside him and prayed together, divine entreaties combining until the spell loosened its grip. Doc stirred. He yawned dramatically, blinked at the faces gathered around him, and accepted breakfast. The group explained Al Askar, Vecna, wild magic, and everything else that had happened. Doc listened. Then he got up.
