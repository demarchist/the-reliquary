Evermeet is the legendary island homeland of the elves, hidden far to the west of Faerûn in the Trackless Sea. Its capital, [[Leuthilspar]], is a city of soaring crystal spires and ancient libraries whose collections span millennia of elven scholarship. Protected by powerful magical wards and the favor of the elven gods, Evermeet serves as a refuge and repository of elven knowledge stretching back to the earliest ages of the world. For centuries, the island maintained formal scholarly exchanges with Faerûnian academies — including the Lady's College of [[Silverymoon]] — sending its most promising students to study abroad with the understanding that they would return.

In 1385 DR, the death of [[Mystra]] and the collapse of the Weave brought catastrophe to Evermeet. The divine wards that had shielded the island for millennia failed, and Evermeet was pulled into the Feywild, severing contact with the mainland for decades. Elves abroad — including [[Fëanon Sërindë]], then studying in Silverymoon — found themselves stranded, their promised homecoming rendered impossible overnight. The island's eventual partial restoration did not undo the damage: a generation of exiles had already rebuilt their lives elsewhere, and the certainty of divine protection that had defined Evermeet's identity was permanently shaken.

The Sage Beryan maintains connections with the libraries of Evermeet, sending inquiries to her colleagues there for information the party needs. These contacts proved valuable when researching subjects such as the ancient names Abraxim, Halashnar, and Quil'Sygoth, as well as information about the goddess Auril and the magical hammer Issgard. Evermeet's vast store of knowledge has been an invaluable resource for the Spellforged, even if accessed at a distance.
