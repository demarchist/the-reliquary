## Overview

The Conclave of Silverymoon is the city's oldest and most prestigious association of practising wizards, housed within the Lady's College campus. Once a powerful institution with deep ties to the city's governance and magical defences, the Conclave served as both an academy of advanced magical study and an advisory body to Silverymoon's leadership.

## Structure

The Conclave occupies a wing of the Lady's College, with offices, seminar rooms, and a private library lining a long third-floor corridor. Portraits of former members decorate the walls. The organisation operates semi-independently from the College itself, though its members often hold teaching positions. The Dean of the Lady's College — currently Dean Sejuk — serves as the Conclave's nominal head.

## The Conspiracy of the Acolytes

The Conclave's most closely guarded secret involves Theodemir's six acolytes — Fëanon Sërindë, Sala Shara, Orla Pyntle, Megno Slatesunder, Sajuuk Stomru, and Elion Loberos — who, after Theodemir's death, conspired to steal the Eye of Aren and hide it within an Illithid hive beneath Ladypeak. The decision was made as the Conclave's political infighting threatened to diminish, endanger, or weaponise their mentor's sacrifice. The conspiracy was never exposed to the wider Conclave membership.

## Decline

By the time of the campaign's events, the Conclave's influence has waned significantly. City funding has been redirected toward trade infrastructure, the Spellguard has grown increasingly autonomous, and the younger generation of members favours applied enchantment and commercial work over theoretical research or civic defence. The old guard — those who remember what the Conclave was built to protect against — are a dwindling minority.

## Relationship with the Spellguard

The Conclave and the Spellguard were once partner organisations, working in concert to protect the city's magical infrastructure. That relationship has deteriorated over the decades, with the Spellguard assuming many of the Conclave's former responsibilities and operating with increasing independence.
