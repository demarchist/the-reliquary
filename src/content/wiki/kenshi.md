Kenshi presented himself as a powerful monk from the distant land of Kara-Tur, commanding disciples and possessing the skill — and the audacity — to assault a prison complex run by the followers of a lich. He first appeared to [[tarkathee-daru|Tark]] in dreams, visiting him at the Monastery of the Yellow Rose in a serene, familiar setting that made the contact feel safe and natural. A Sending spell reached Tark with news of [[gaddock|Gaddock]]'s rescue, and a subsequent dream meeting at the monastery confirmed the story: [[gaddock|Gaddock]] had been freed and was recovering, with Kenshi teaching him new techniques that the old monk intended to pass along to his student.

## The Dream Meetings

The Kenshi that Tark knew existed only in dreams. He appeared regularly after [[gaddock|Gaddock]]'s supposed rescue, bringing warm news and continuing Tark's martial education — introducing new techniques, maintaining the connection between master and student, providing reassurance that [[gaddock|Gaddock]] was alive and healing. Each meeting felt genuine: the familiar monastery, the cadence of instruction, the easy warmth of a trusted intermediary.

Tark told Kenshi a great deal. In an early chapter 8 dream meeting, he confided the details of the group's journey into the [[underdark|Underdark]] — their destination of [[gracksuluth|Gracksuluth]], the strange planar cracks, the demon activity in the deep. Kenshi responded with concern and a promise to tell people what he could. He offered to spar. They trained together.

## The Revelation

Deep into the group's time in [[gracksuluth|Gracksuluth]], Tark noticed something: only Kenshi knew exactly where they were. And then a Stoneguard patrol found them — someone had sold their location. The suspicion settled into certainty and when [[doc|Doc]] targeted a Scrying spell at Kenshi, the spell returned nothing. Not a warded location, not a hidden room. Nothing at all. There was no one there to scry on.

A dream contact reached [[gaddock|Gaddock]] — what remained of him. His master was in a chamber of ice, pallid and changed, unmistakably a vampire. The confirmation was brutal: Kenshi was a construct of [[zhengyi|Zhengyi]], the Witch-King of Vaasa — a deliberately designed fiction used to harvest intelligence and to keep Tark cooperative. The "rescue" was fabricated. Gaddock had been captured and turned long before the first dream meeting with Kenshi began. Everything Tark shared through those dreams, Zhengyi had heard.

Kenshi never existed. The warmth of the monastery, the lessons, the news of [[gaddock|Gaddock]]'s recovery — all of it was constructed by the Witch-King's hand and fed to the monk who trusted it.
