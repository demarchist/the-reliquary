Selûné is the goddess of the moon, stars, navigation, wanderers, questers, and good-aligned lycanthropes. Known as Our Lady of Silver and the Moonmaiden, she is one of the oldest deities in the Faerûnian pantheon, her eternal conflict with her dark twin [[shar|Shar]] shaping much of the cosmos.

## Role in the Campaign

Selûné's influence permeates the campaign through her most devoted servant, [[cirrus-al-kayan|Cirrus Al Kayan]], who abandoned a degrading Archfey pact to give himself entirely to her worship. Cirrus founded the [[cult-of-selune|Cult of Selûne]] and built the [[fane-of-selune|Fane of Selûne]] — a massive white marble spire on the shore of [[lake-seluna|Lake Seluna]], perpetually shrouded in the umbra of a total eclipse — as the centre of her faith in the [[selunia|Barony of Selunia]], which takes its very name from the goddess.

## Direct Intervention

Selûné has appeared to Cirrus directly on rare occasions. At a spa in [[neverwinter|Neverwinter]], she brushed his face, grew four new teeth into his gums, added ten pounds of muscle, and healed his blemishes — a transformation that represented her approval of the man, even as she recoiled from the trophy he had cut from a warlock's corpse. She has also guided Cirrus through coincidence: when he learned Plane Shift on the same night an Astral rift was torn open in [[sundabar|Sundabar]], he recognised her hand and attuned his [[crystal-tuning-fork|Crystal Tuning Fork]] beneath the tear.

## The Celestial Ally

Selûné's power extends through [[crisserei|Crisserei]], a Planetar in her service who can manifest as a radiant angel or as a Seraphim destroyer raining searing arrows from the sky. [[beryan|Beryan]], a Sun Elf Sage of Selûné, resides in [[feanons-tower|Fëanon's Tower]] and serves as the party's primary researcher.

## The Font of Fawden

Selûné's most dramatic role came during the climax of the Al Askar campaign. The dying goddess [[fawden|Fawden]] — being consumed by [[vecna|Vecna]] — proposed a final act of chaos: during a ritual at the [[font-of-fawden|Font of Fawden]], with [[the-last-word|The Last Word]] floating above it, a [[ringed-veils|Ringed Veil]] portal would open and Selûné would enter this world. The majority of Fawden's divine energy would flow to Selûné, denying it to Vecna. A small fragment of Fawden would survive as an aspect of the new Moon goddess. Cirrus accepted the plan, and the party executed the ritual — channelling a dying god's power across the planes to bring Selûné into a world that had never known her.
