The Saurians were an ancient and technologically advanced race that once dominated the world of [[al-askar|Al Askar]]. Murals depict them as humanoid-reptilian beings from a planet with two suns. They possessed capabilities far beyond what other civilisations could achieve — the construction of the Ringed Veils (fargates) that enabled interplanar travel, the creation of [[saurian-firelance|Firelance]] weapons, the mining of spell-infusable crystals, and the development of a mechanised civilisation fuelled by a fragment of [[the-last-word|The Last Word]]. Their ruins are scattered across Al Askar, their architecture of white marble, angular pillars, and gold filigree enduring through the centuries even as modern constructions crowd around it.

## The Phaerimm War

The Saurians were destroyed approximately a thousand years before the party's arrival, breaking their world in the process and unleashing the chaos that entangled magic and sorcery ever since. Their downfall was tied to a devastating war against the Phaerimm — ancient aberrations capable of consuming and rerouting magic. The conflict spanned multiple worlds, with the Saurians encountering their enemy even in [[faerun|Faerûn]].

In the frozen ruins of Thueba Swamp, the party witnessed ghostly echoes of the war's final days. Saurian and elven soldiers — wearing armour of gleaming metal adorned with feathers, wielding firelances and curving swords — fought desperately against the Phaerimm invaders. When conventional forces failed, an elven mage named Avitus summoned an Aldjinn, a celestial of ice and radiance, to turn the tide. But the Phaerimm adapted, and a desperate second summoning — a shadow spirit — went catastrophically wrong, imprisoning the celestial and condemning every soul in the fortress to a thousand years of undeath.

## The Broken World

When the Saurians broke Al Askar, magic and sourcery became entangled in chaos. Without proper training, any use of magic triggered a surge — unpredictable, often lethal. Worse still, casting generated source static that arced to nearby metal conductors, burning anyone wearing or carrying them. This was why no one on Al Askar wore metal. The divine severance also cut [[faerun|Faerûn]]'s gods from any influence on the world, leaving [[cirrus-al-kayan|Cirrus]] without divine connection and [[feanon-serinde|Fëanon]] and [[gundren-rockseeker|Gundren]] wielding Saurian firelances instead of their usual arcane methods.

## The Death Lords

In undeath, the Saurians became the backbone of [[vecna|Vecna]]'s army on Al Askar. Saurian Death Lords — ancient undead warriors wielding burning blades — commanded Boneclaw guards and served as [[vecna|Vecna]]'s most formidable field commanders. At the [[font-of-fawden|Font of Fawden]], a Death Lord's burning blade took [[tarkathee-daru|Tark]]'s left hand off at the elbow before the monk — empowered by [[vaketh|Vaketh]] — shattered the creature's Boneclaws and burned the ancient Saurian to dust.

## Legacy

The ancient Saurian engineer Hasrala, preserved as a soul in a phylactery crystal, provided the party with crucial information and combination keys to access Saurian technology. Their [[machina-factory|Machina Factory]] in Thueba Swamp remained partially functional, containing a database of Ringed Veil coordinates and manufacturing capabilities for machina bodies. The cities of [[al-askar|Al Askar]] — [[ras-alkan|Ras Alkan]], Sharza, and others — still bear the bones of Saurian architecture at their hearts: grand citadels of stone and brass, domed temples of ancient design, standing among the modern constructions like monuments to a civilisation that ruled and ruined a world.
