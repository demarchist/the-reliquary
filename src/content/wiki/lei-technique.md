Grell memory reconstruction is a method of psionic memory recovery developed by [[Magistrate Li|Lei Tan]], a junior investigator in the Spellguard's Arcane Crimes Division. The technique uses a domesticated Grell — an aberration with natural psychic receptors — as a biological interface to stitch together corrupted memory strands recovered from Illithid drones.

## Method

The procedure requires participants to lie in a copper basin etched with Shou script while holding corrupted memory strands against their temples. The Grell extends its tentacles through translucent tubes and establishes a psychic link between each strand and its host's mind. The memories play out as a shared, vivid hallucination experienced from the perspective of whoever originally lived them.

A living mind psychically attuned to the memories' origin must serve as a "simulation arbiter" — an anchor point that allows the Grell to stitch the fragments into a coherent sequence. Without an arbiter, the fragments remain incoherent noise.

## Limitations

The technique is imperfect. Memories are old and corrupted, resulting in gaps, distortions, and temporal jumps. Participants experience discomfort and disorientation, and the Grell — though domesticated — can become distressed if the memories contain extreme psychic trauma, causing the simulation to crash. Participants retain the memories after waking, though they fade at the edges like dreams.

## Application

Lei Tan used the technique to reconstruct decades-old memories recovered from a captured Illithid drone, revealing the conspiracy of Theodemir's acolytes and the true nature of Fëanon's enthrallment by [[Nostalgia for Infinity]]. The Grell used in the procedure — a juvenile named Mei — was recovered from a collapsed Underdark tunnel and raised by Lei Tan herself over several years.
