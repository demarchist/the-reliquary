The Dreamer is a Beholder that took up residence in the mines beneath the village of [[und|Und]] in the Galena Mountains. From below, it projected a psychic influence over the settlement that placed the villagers in a dreamlike stupor — contented, compliant, and incapable of independent thought. Four cloaked dwarves served as its agents above ground, their bodies gradually transformed by the aberration's influence.

## Discovery

When the group arrived, the village appeared idyllic to the point of wrongness — pies cooling in every window, flowers on every table, and residents who spoke in a halting, dreamy cadence. The only person unaffected was [[urkle|Urkle]], a herder from the village outskirts who had been imprisoned in enchanted stocks for asking questions.

The cloaked agents explained, with polite sincerity, that The Dreamer made everything perfect. Everything was good here.

## Confrontation

When [[horatio-von-bumidere|Buumi]] and [[feanon-serinde|Fëanon]] began dispelling the enchantments across the village, three of the four agents collapsed in agony. The fourth threw back its hood — its head distended, covered in eyes, its jaw a grotesque toothy maw. The creature screamed, ripped its own head off, and the head floated into the air as every eye sprouted into an eyestalk.

The Beholder demanded to know why the group had disturbed its dream. [[aliyah-faen-tlabbar|Patches]] shot it in the face, and the group destroyed it quickly.

As the first Beholder sank to the ground whimpering, a second erupted from another cloaked dwarf — tearing its host's head off in the same manner and floating into the air. The Dreamer, it seemed, had spawned more than one copy of itself within its agents.
