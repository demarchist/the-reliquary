## Description

Shatterpeak is a mountain in the Nether Mountains, roughly fifty miles southeast of Sundabar. It is instantly recognisable — the peak is split in half as if by a sword stroke, the edges weathered and crumbled over millennia but the cut still unmistakably unnatural. The mountain rises above the treeline into a realm of snow, wind, and cloud.

A colossal staircase, built for giants, is cut into the mountainside. Near the midpoint of the ascent sits a stone archway that the stair deliberately curves around — the only means of revealing the Sanctum of Krasus to mortal perception. Above the archway, a massive bridge of ice planks fixed by iron chains spans the gap between the mountain's halves, leading to a plateau infested with Remorhaz, including a colossal polar worm named Merrumjundr. Beyond the plateau, a human-sized staircase — immaculately cut and free of ice — leads to the Sanctum's tower at the very summit.

## The Frost Giant Settlement

On a levelled shelf partway up the mountain, a frost giant community makes its home around a great hall of interlocking ice slabs. The settlement is led by the elderly matriarch Ingrbotha, with the ancient patriarch Aegir. The giants keep herds of mammoths and giant goats on the slopes and maintain relations with an orc-barbarian village at the hot-spring-fed lake below.

## Significance

The party's expedition to Shatterpeak, funded by Megno Slatesunder and the Explorers League, yielded discoveries of immense importance — the incomplete Mythallar, Netherese medallions, a quartz tuning fork for planar travel, and a vision of the escaped elder evil Atropos. The frost giant community provided both hospitality and critical intelligence about the route to the Sanctum's entrance.
