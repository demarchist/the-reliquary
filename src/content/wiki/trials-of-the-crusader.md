The Trials of the Crusader is an ancient ceremony of Helm's Hold in which a paladin champion faces a summoned Narzugon in single combat within a warded arena deep beneath the fortress. The rite had not been performed in over two centuries — the records were so incomplete that the gold dragon Aurinax, who had witnessed the last one, was brought in to advise. The previous champion was killed in the attempt.

Horatio von Bumidere called for the Trials after receiving what he believed to be a divine commandment from Helm in a dream. He did not know he would be facing an Archdevil until Aurinax told him. The summoning was conducted by four clerics around a massive copper brazier in a volcanic arena, with a golden containment ring set into the floor.

The ceremony went catastrophically wrong. The Narzugon Prime — not merely a Narzugon, but the original Archdevil, the first paladin to lose his soul to the Nine Hells — plunged its sword into the brazier and summoned twenty bearded devil legionnaires and six lesser Narzugon cavalry through the shattered planar barriers. Faerzress crystals in the ceiling had weakened the barrier between planes, allowing the breach. The containment ring was overwhelmed and the battle spilled into the stands.

The party intervened alongside the Watchers of Helm. The Narzugon Prime was eventually crushed and banished, though two paladins fell and several of the lesser Narzugon escaped into the Underdark.
