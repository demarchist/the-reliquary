The Aldjinn is a high celestial of ice and radiance — humanoid and feminine in form, with a body composed of flowing and shifting blue crystal sand and two graceful horns curving from its brow. It was summoned during the ancient Phaerimm war by the elven mage Avitus to aid the Saurian defenders of a fortress in Thueba Swamp. Though the celestial initially turned the tide of battle, the Phaerimm adapted to its presence. When Avitus attempted a second summoning — a shadow spirit — he lost control of both beings. The shadow spirit imprisoned the Aldjinn within a block of ice and cursed the entire fortress, trapping all souls within in a state of undeath for a thousand years.

The party freed the Aldjinn by synchronising Dispel Magic and Greater Restoration to break the shadow spirit's hold. The power of Selûné overwhelmed the spirit and shunted it back to its plane.

Grateful for its freedom and horrified to learn about the state of the world — the impostor Zalaam, Vecna's machinations, and the dying goddess Fawden — the Aldjinn willingly provided Feanon with a divine scale formed from its own body. This scale was a crucial component for the Ritual of Harmony, needed to represent the Divine in the working that would repair the Weave and planar boundaries. The Aldjinn also told the party of a great metallic wyrm guarding the gateway from the Feywild to the Material Plane — a potential ally against the draconic demigod Hohonu. It then opened a portal and returned to its native plane after its millennium of captivity.
