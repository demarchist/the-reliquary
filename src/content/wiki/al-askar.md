Al Askar is an alien world with twin suns and two moons, accessed through the ancient [[ringed-veils|Ringed Veil]] portal network. The party arrived through a Veil seized from the [[cult-of-zalaam|Cult of Zalaam]] in the [[underdark|Underdark]] and discovered a world fundamentally broken — its magic entangled with chaos, its gods consumed, and its civilisations reduced to scattered survivors fighting a losing war against undeath and corruption.

## The Broken World

When the [[saurians|Saurians]] destroyed themselves a thousand years ago in their war against the Phaerimm, they fractured reality on a planetary scale. Magic and sourcery became entangled in chaos: any uncontrolled spellcasting triggers wild surges that arc to nearby metal conductors, burning anyone wearing or carrying them. This is why no one on Al Askar wears metal armour. High Storms — massive walls of sand and dust shimmering with wild sourcery — sweep the landscape regularly, spawning locust swarms and magical detonations. [[feanon-serinde|Fëanon]] recognised the damage as identical to the ritual fracture in the [[underdark|Underdark]], only on a world-wide scale, and believed he could adapt his repair ritual to heal it.

The party donned shell, hide, and carapace armour suited to the brutal climate, stowing their metal gear in [[bag-of-holding|bags of holding]]. Two suns glared from a greenish sky, temperatures climbed past a hundred degrees, and the Red Hills baked everything exposed. Alien fauna — chitinous Bulettes, garnet-encrusted dragonkin, and blood-drinking cacti — populated the hostile landscape.

## Key Locations

- **[[ras-alkan|Ras Alkan]]** — a coastal city and the party's first major settlement, near the Temple of Karthus
- **[[iyum|Iyum]]** — the largest city, ruled by Sultan Bashir and the Usurpers of Rudjek, where the party negotiated a sweeping trade agreement
- **[[thueba-swamp|Thueba Swamp]]** — frozen ruins containing the Saurian Machina Factory and ghostly echoes of the Phaerimm War
- **[[mt-saqar|Mt. Saqar]]** — volcanic mountain housing the [[titanic-forge|Titanic Forge]]
- **[[sharza|Sharza]]** — a dead city guarded by an army of corrupted dead, with the Black Pyramid of Barzok at its heart containing a fragment of [[the-last-word|The Last Word]]
- **The Font of Fawden** — temple of the goddess Fawden, site of the ritual to repair the Ringed Veil and return home

## Factions

The [[cult-of-zalaam|Cult of Zalaam]] serves [[vecna|Vecna]], who has been systematically devouring Al Askar's gods — consuming the deity Zalaam entirely and slowly absorbing the goddess Fawden, explaining the increasing frequency of wild-magic surges. The [[obsidian-order|Obsidian Order]], desert elves led by figures like [[liara|Liara]], fights against the cult. The party joined the resistance, undertaking a pilgrimage to the temples of the Ancients to gain the power and blessings needed to enter Sharza through the Pilgrim's Way.

## Severance

On Al Askar, [[cirrus-al-kayan|Cirrus]]' connection to [[selune|Selûne]] was severed completely — the goddess could not reach across worlds. The loss drove him into delusion, refusing to accept the separation. [[horatio-von-bumidere|Buumi]]'s connection to Helm, however, endured — suggesting that some divine bonds transcended planar distance while others did not.
