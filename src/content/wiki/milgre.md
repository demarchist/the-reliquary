Milgre is a Derro Savant — a wizard among the mad subterranean race — who has undergone vampiric transformation in service to [[orcus|Orcus]]. She operated from [[bilrigger|Bilrigger]]'s Sanctum in the [[whorlstone-caverns|Whorlstone Caverns]] beneath [[gracksuluth|Gracksuluth]], commanding a network of vampire spawn and two Warlock lieutenants, including the red-robed Rilka.

The name Milgre first surfaced as a whisper: Tark overheard it spoken by Derro tailing the party through Ladueger's Furrow, and [[patches|Patches]] heard it raised with concern at the Council of Derro Savants — specifically that whoever was creating all the Vampires in the West Cleft was making far too many. The Beholder [[o-alla|O'Alla]], who mediated the Savants' meetings, confirmed Milgre had last been seen heading to Bilrigger's private sanctum.

## The Hunt

[[tark|Tark]] took the hunt for Milgre personally. After discovering that the vampire Derro in Rilka's hovel were likely connected to the same network that had been feeding intelligence on the party to [[zhengyi|Zhengyi]], he walked into the Hall of Sacred Scrolls and simply shouted her name until the scribes were forced to respond. The library found sparse records: Milgre had been seen descending into the old tunnels beneath the Hall, toward the Whorlstone Caverns.

The party fought through Bilrigger's Sanctum — clearing vampire spawn from the entry chamber, facing Rilka and another Warlock lieutenant in flanking side-rooms — before Milgre herself emerged from the mist on a staircase below the Bilrigger statue. A Vampire Savant: fast, capable of charm, and able to direct the battlefield through her spawn.

## Escape

The fight went badly for her. Her charm on Fey'Anon was broken when he pushed past it. Rilka was killed by Tark. Her last lieutenant dimension-doored to safety. Milgre attempted to flee invisible — and nearly made it, until Patches thought quickly and Faerie Fired the doorway, enshrouding the invisible vampire in harmless light. [[cirrus-al-kayan|Cirrus]], already rushing in that direction, jammed a planar tuning fork into her side and plane-shifted her, shocked and outmaneuvered, to the Astral Plane.

She was not killed. The Astral Plane is a place of suspended time and minimal change, but it is not a prison. Milgre remains at large, a Warlock of Orcus displaced between planes — a potential threat that the party chose expulsion over execution.
