Steden is a Nightmare — a fiendish, fire-wreathed horse capable of traversing the [[ethereal-plane|Ethereal Plane]]. The mount is primarily ridden by [[jack-mayer-johnson-mathews|JmJm]], who uses Steden's planar-shifting ability to move between the Material and Ethereal Planes with devastating tactical effect. He has served as the party's primary means of ethereal reconnaissance, ambush insertion, and emergency extraction across multiple theatres of war.

## Tactical Role

Steden's ability to shift between planes has defined some of the party's most effective ambushes. During the Battle of the Tar Pits, JmJm led the opening assault by galloping from the Ethereal Plane astride Steden with [[cirrus-al-kayan|Cirrus]] and [[gundren-rockseeker|Gundren]], crashing into the Grand Shaman and his followers. The same tactic was used to infiltrate Aina Pulu, scout the Ziggurat of the Doomstar Legion, and breach a dragon's lair. When [[tarkathee-daru|Tark]] was trapped in the vortex of Hohonu's prison, Cirrus rode Steden into the maelstrom to haul the monk from the pit and back to the saddle.

Steden has also served as aerial scout — JmJm mounted the Nightmare to survey the ancient Saurian High Road stretching to the horizon across Al Askar, and to find his theatre in Helms Hold from above.

## Personality

Steden is not a passive mount. When the group encountered the great Wyvern and Borborygmos, JmJm dismounted on the party's insistence — and Steden, left to his own devices, began inching toward the Wyvern hoping to start something, until [[aliyah-faen-tlabbar|Patches]] looked the Nightmare dead in the eye and told him to knock it off. Steden muttered something and trotted back, head low. He has also manifested as a fiery drake when JmJm required a show of force, and he ferried the Bedouin Machmoud across the desert despite the man's initial recoiling from the devil-horse.

## Death and Return

During the assault on the Undead Legions, Steden was killed in battle — boulders crashed into the fiery steed from above and pummelled it to death, sending JmJm to the ground hard. The Nightmare was subsequently called forth again for the infiltration of Aina Pulu, resuming his role without apparent complaint.

## Uni

On the road to [[helioglabulus|Helios]], Steden met his celestial opposite in [[cassian|Cassian]]'s Pegasus Uni. The tension between the fiendish and the celestial was mutual and entirely undisguised — Uni kept as far from JmJm's devil-horse as the road would allow, and Steden shot the celestial withering looks in return.
