Fëanon's Magnificent Mansion is an extradimensional dwelling conjured by [[feanon-serinde|Fëanon Serinde]] — a pocket plane of sweeping marble, rich hardwoods, and silver filigree, built after the fashion of [[leuthilspar|Leuthilspar]], the crystal-spired capital of [[evermeet|Evermeet]] where Fëanon was born. It has served as the group's primary refuge across environments ranging from the deep [[underdark|Underdark]] to the deserts of [[anauroch|Anauroch]], the volcanic wastelands of [[al-askar|Al Askar]], and the drawing rooms of foreign nobility.

## The Door

When Fëanon casts the spell, a faintly visible shimmering silver door appears on a surface of his choosing — a rock wall, a building's interior, the side of a temple. The door is elaborate and ornate, reminiscent of the Doors of Durin: silver tracery that catches the light and seems to shift when observed from different angles. It is unmistakably magical and unmistakably Fëanon's.

## The Interior

The portal opens into a grand entrance hall with an elaborate stairway opposite, ascending to Fëanon's private quarters on the upper level. The architecture is high-elven throughout — soaring marble, carved hardwood paneling, silver filigree work on every surface — modeled on the mansions and grand structures of Evermeet that Fëanon grew up among. Unseen servants provide food, drink, and pampering without prompting, and a banquet table is perpetually laden.

The interior reality is malleable. Fëanon can reshape the space to suit the occasion — a capability he exploited spectacularly in [[blingdenstone|Blingdenstone]], where he and [[aliyah-faen-tlabbar|Patches]] spent hours transforming the interior into a perfect replica of a Chapel of [[lolth|Lolth]], complete with a hundred-foot circular chamber, rows of cushions radiating from a central spider-shaped altar with sacrificial blood grooves, and an overlooking balcony where [[gundren-rockseeker|Gundren]] set up with his rifle. The glamoured entrance resembled grand Drow cathedral doors. The Stone Priestess accepted an invitation and walked right in.

## The Guest Rooms

To each side of the entrance hall, a series of doors lead to guest rooms — each themed for one of the party members. These rooms are designed to appeal to what Fëanon imagines his friends would enjoy, which is not necessarily what his friends actually enjoy. Fëanon's understanding of other people's comforts is filtered through the sensibilities of a seven-hundred-year-old High Elf wizard who grew up in a crystal palace, and the results are frequently awkward, occasionally hilarious, and consistently more revealing of the designer than the occupant.

**Patches' Room** — A faithful recreation of noble [[menzoberranzan|Menzoberranzan]] architecture: obsidian walls, spider-silk bedding, soft violet bioluminescence, carved stone in the style of the great houses. Fëanon researched it carefully and considers it a gesture of cultural sensitivity. Patches — an apostate whose family flayed her likeness into a tapestry, whose sister tried to kill her in [[luskan|Luskan]], and who has spent her entire adult life running from the culture this room lovingly commemorates — has never once set foot in it. She sleeps in the common areas, in someone else's room, or outside the mansion entirely. Fëanon has not connected these facts.

**Buumi's Room** — A boisterous approximation of what Fëanon imagines a human warrior's off-duty life looks like: barrels of ale recessed into the walls on taps, a long oak table pre-scarred with knife marks by the unseen servants (which is somehow worse than honest wear), mounted trophy heads of beasts Fëanon selected from an encyclopaedia, and a large stone hearth that burns without fuel. By some miracle of miscalibration, it is [[horatio-von-bumidere|Buumi]]'s favourite room in the mansion. He has never told Fëanon this. Fëanon, having no idea he actually got one right, periodically threatens to *improve* it.

**Tark's Room** — What Fëanon considers monastic simplicity: a meditation space with polished marble floors, silk cushions, a contemplative garden visible through a floor-to-ceiling window that opens onto nothing (the space being extradimensional), and an elven futon of understated elegance. It is the most austere room in the mansion and still three times more luxurious than anywhere [[tarkathee-daru|Tark]] has ever voluntarily slept.

**JmJm's Room** — Originally a performance salon with excellent acoustics, a small stage, velvet seating, and a tastefully stocked bar. [[jack-mayer-johnson-mathews|JmJm]] took one look at it, declared it adequate, and has since transformed it through sheer force of personality and the enthusiastic complicity of the unseen servants into something Fëanon no longer recognises and does not inspect. It is the only room in the mansion that the unseen servants seem genuinely excited to maintain.

**Gundren's Room** — A workshop-forge hybrid featuring an anvil, a set of elven-crafted tools of breathtaking quality, and organised storage for mechanical projects. The tools are exquisite. They are also designed for elven metallurgical traditions that predate dwarven forging by millennia and are functionally incompatible with gunsmithing, artifice, or anything involving smokepowder. [[gundren-rockseeker|Gundren]] uses the room as storage and does his actual work elsewhere.

**Cirrus' Room** — Nominally a chapel of [[selune|Selûne]]: silver moonlight filtering through stained glass, a prayer alcove with a cushioned kneeler, and a basin of water that Fëanon will describe as *purified* but refuses to call *blessed*. The design reflects the intersection of Fëanon's two deepest professional disdains — sorcery (power without study, unearned and probably dangerous) and divine devotion (intellectual dependency dressed up as virtue) — both of which [[cirrus-al-kayan|Cirrus]] embodies simultaneously. The result is a room that is technically devotional and practically designed to needle its occupant. The bookshelves flanking the prayer alcove are stocked with titles on arcane theory and the history of divine failures. The reading light in the prayer nook is suspiciously good, as though its designer felt strongly that anyone kneeling there could be doing something more productive. The stained glass depicts the phases of the moon with the precision of an astronomical chart rather than the reverence of a cathedral window. Fëanon maintains that every design choice was purely aesthetic. Cirrus is not fooled.

## Widow Wednesdays

The mansion has a social life of its own. When the group is home in the Barony of [[thunder-bluff|Thunder Bluff]], Fëanon and [[jack-mayer-johnson-mathews|JmJm]] host *Widow Wednesdays* — alternating between the mansion and the [[tavern-of-doors|Tavern of Doors]] — an evening gathering whose name, guest list, and activities carry vaguely louche overtones that neither man has ever fully clarified. The unseen servants have been observed preparing for these occasions with an enthusiasm that exceeds their usual impeccable standard. JmJm's room in the mansion sees its heaviest use on these evenings. Patches, who has the misfortune of sharing a dwelling with one host and a neighbourhood with the other, has walked in on the aftermath of at least one Widow Wednesday in the mansion with an expression that suggested a firm preference for never doing so again.

## Properties

The mansion exists as a self-contained extradimensional space, which provides several practical benefits and one notable restriction: [[bag-of-holding|Bags of Holding]] and other extradimensional containers cannot be brought inside without risking a planar collision. The first time Fëanon cast the spell, he stopped the group at the threshold and insisted on a deposit area next to the door — a shaped-earth pocket for their gear. The group was not happy about leaving their equipment outside, even with Cirrus's Tiny Hut as added security.

The mansion is sealed against external conditions. The smog of [[gracksuluth|Gracksuluth]] could not enter. The abyssal energies of the Underdark were kept at bay. When Fëanon decided a Duergar townhouse was *insufficiently responsive to his requirements*, he simply cast the spell inside it, and the group ate well, slept in actual comfort, and awoke to a proper morning table. In [[helioglabulus|Helios]], after discovering listening devices throughout the Queen's hospitality, he set up his mansion within the mansion — a private plane nested inside the noble townhouse.

The entrance can be concealed. In Al Askar, fleeing the Avatar of Hohonu, Fëanon hid the mansion's entrance in the arcane shadow of an ancient glyph, rendering it invisible to magical detection. The Avatar crashed through the surrounding swamp for hours and did not find them.

## Wild Magic

The mansion is not immune to all environments. On Al Askar, where the world's damaged magical fabric caused wild-magic surges, Fëanon's attempt to conjure the mansion failed catastrophically — the spell collapsed, smothered by ancient wards, and erupted into a lightning storm that filled the chamber for ten agonising minutes. It was one of the few occasions the wizard's magic turned on him completely.

## Tark

Tark has a complicated relationship with the mansion. He routinely elects to sleep outside — in the desert, on bare rock, in the open Underdark — while the rest of the group enjoys the extradimensional luxury within. His motivations are unclear. It may be monastic discipline, a preference for the natural world, a halfling's distrust of pocket dimensions, or something else entirely. He has never explained, and no one has pressed the question hard enough to get an answer.
