Bilrigger the Savant was, by all appearances, one of the most senior Warlocks of [[warlocks-of-ceregorach|the Warlocks of Ceregorach]] — a figure of genuine intellectual weight who occupied opulent chambers in the [[whorlstone-caverns|Whorlstone Caverns]] beneath [[gracksuluth|Gracksuluth]] and commanded the loyalty of Droki, a fellow warlock who served as his messenger and guide.

## The Debate

When [[cirrus-al-kayan|Cirrus]] finally sat across from Bilrigger in his private dining chamber — red carpet underfoot, a full feast on the table, and Snorbul already eating — the encounter he expected did not happen. There was no immediate violence, no demand for obeisance to [[ceregorach|Ceregorach]]. Instead, Bilrigger wanted to argue theology.

His arguments were sharp. He contended that everything Ceregorach had put Cirrus through had been instrumental in making him stronger — that suffering, rightly understood, is cultivation. He pointed out that Cirrus's sight, a gift from Selune, could be rescinded at any moment: the goddess held the leash. A patron who gives is a patron who can take away. The Laughing God, by contrast, had forged Cirrus in fire without the pretense of benevolence.

Cirrus rejected every premise. His relationship with Selune was love, not dependency; the power he had gained through his suffering was incidental to who he had become because of her. The debate escalated until both were on their feet, voices raised — and then it became a fight.

## The Reveal

The combat was brief. Snorbul clamped a hand over Bilrigger's mouth before the warlock could speak a word of magic. [[patches|Patches]] cut his tendons. [[cirrus-al-kayan|Cirrus]] went for the green molar. As the Bilrigger simulacrum slipped into unconsciousness, Cirrus entered a dreaming to make one final appeal — finding only a creature made entirely of teeth that laughed, rasping, before it exploded.

The body disintegrated into its component material: thousands of teeth.

Fey'Anon immediately identified what had happened. The Bilrigger the party had fought, debated, and killed was a Simulacrum — a magical duplicate, not the original. The real Bilrigger had been elsewhere the entire time, in a private sanctum deeper in the [[whorlstone-caverns|Whorlstone Caverns]]. When the party eventually found that sanctum, they found Bilrigger's actual corpse: head split cleanly in two, the green molar halved. Someone had gotten there before them, hours earlier.

## Personality

The philosophical arguments Bilrigger made remained interesting even after the reveal that he was a copy. Whether a Simulacrum can hold genuinely held beliefs is unclear, but the debate felt real. His contention — that strength forged in adversity is more authentic than strength granted by love — is not obviously wrong. It is simply a philosophy that treats suffering as a value in itself, which is the theology of a god like [[ceregorach|Ceregorach]]: meaning found in madness, virtue discovered through chaos. Cirrus did not find it convincing, but he had to engage with it.
