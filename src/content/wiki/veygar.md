Veygar is a survivor who operates by a code that only he fully understands. A Duergar leader and member of the Grey Ghosts thieves' guild, he is calculating, pragmatic, and infuriatingly difficult to pin down as either enemy or friend.

## The Trap

In [[baldurs-gate|Baldur's Gate]], Veygar impersonated Lord Faendral and used [[lady-faye|Lady Faye]]'s vault job as bait to lure the group into ancient ruins beneath the estate. The entire operation was a trap. He captured [[aliyah-faen-tlabbar|Patches]], Lady Faye, and Zhink, binding them as hostages in the deepest chamber. When the group fought their way through, Veygar welcomed them with unsettling calm, attempted to bargain, and when things went sideways, unleashed mechanical spiders, a Stone Golem, and — through a portal — an Alhoon.

But Veygar was not interested in a last stand. He shot the Alhoon twice with crossbow bolts, tossed a Drow noble's signet ring to the group with the cryptic instruction "ask the Drow about it," wished them luck, and retreated through the portal. The move was deliberate. Veygar treated the entire encounter less like a battle and more like a transaction — he got what he needed and left the group alive, either out of pragmatism or because killing them was never the objective.

## The Larger Picture

[[aliyah-faen-tlabbar|Patches]] later investigated the ruins and discovered that Veygar's portal chamber was part of a much larger network. His Duergar band was not an isolated raiding party but part of a force intercepting supply convoys between [[menzoberranzan|Menzoberranzan]] and [[gracksuluth|Gracksuluth]]. Veygar's ambitions extended far beyond one heist in Baldur's Gate.

## Gracksuluth

In [[gracksuluth|Gracksuluth]], Veygar resurfaced as a member of the [[grey-ghosts|Grey Ghosts]] thieves' guild and [[lady-faye|Lady Faye]]'s contact in the deep — the botched kidnapping, it turned out, had led to a working business arrangement rather than lasting enmity. He approached the group at a dinner meeting in the Shattered Spire, sharing intelligence about a fragment of [[the-last-word|The Last Word]] he had discovered in ancient Underdark ruins. His own crew had gone silent out there and he was unwilling to go himself — not with the demon activity escalating as it was. He offered [[hemeth|Hemeth]] a place in his operation to sweeten the discussion.

As the city spiralled into demon siege, Veygar stayed in the shadows until [[aliyah-faen-tlabbar|Patches]] ran him to ground at the Barony. She found him at a table, made a direct proposal — he was coming with the group to [[gracksuluth|Gracksuluth]] to help rescue [[hemeth|Hemeth]] and Velka from Duergar custody — and when he protested, shoved him to the ground. He agreed to go.

His most useful contribution came before the battle: he used the Dreaming to contact Velka directly, establishing communication with his imprisoned colleague and extracting the intelligence the group needed. The prisoner convoy carrying [[hemeth|Hemeth]] and Velka would cross Ladueger's Furrow at roughly ten-thirty in the morning in an armored wagon under minimal guard. This information was the foundation of the ambush plan. He led the group through the alley routes after they teleported into the demon-besieged city.
