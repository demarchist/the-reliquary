Atropos is the elder evil at the heart of the campaign — *The Rise of Atropos*. Known as the Planet Born Dead, it is a cosmic force of annihilation whose fragmentary dark powers were once imprisoned within amber sarcophagi in the [[amber-temple|Amber Temple]] of [[barovia|Barovia]], and whose crystal prison in the Far Realm has since been found empty and broken.

## Nature

Atropos defies easy categorisation. The Balgura Prophecy calls it "the Planet Born Dead" — an entity of planetary scale. The celestial vault murals beneath [[neverwinter|Neverwinter]] depict [[orcus|Orcus]] sleeping "within an egg at the heart of a dark orb," suggesting Atropos is the vessel or womb from which the Demon Lord draws power. Baba Lysaga, high priestess of Atropos, worshipped it as a god of endings. The dwarven vampire cleric Kenga chanted to Atropos as his dark deity. Whether planet, entity, or something beyond mortal taxonomy, Atropos represents the fundamental force of annihilation — the promise that all things end.

## The Escaped Prison

When [[feanon-serinde|Fëanon]] used a crystal scrying orb in the incomplete [[mythallar|Mythallar]] chamber, the vision descended through the Far Realm's layers into a vast crystal prison — massive chains broken and scattered. The group's collective consciousness recognised it: the prison of Atropos. Now empty. The crystal ball exploded in orange sparks and a mass of flesh and bone flowed from the pedestal — a fragment of the Planet Born Dead, wailing and blasting necrotic energy. The party fought it with everything they had before [[megno-slatesunder|Megno]] banished the weakened fragment back to the Far Realm.

## The Atropal

Atropos projects fragments of itself into the material world as Atropals — malformed masses of necrotic flesh that drain life force and regenerate the undead they serve. An Atropal Knight led undead forces above [[baldurs-gate|Baldur's Gate]] and detonated alongside [[kangaxx|Kangaxx]] when destroyed. [[rahadin|Rahadin]]'s dragon form harboured a small Atropal within his body, its shadow draining life from [[horatio-von-bumidere|Buumi]] and [[tarkathee-daru|Tark]] to regenerate the dragon's protective aura during the final battle at [[castle-ravenloft|Castle Ravenloft]]. A fragment corrupted [[feanon-serinde|Fëanon]]'s ring of mind shielding until master and student forced it out and destroyed it.

## The Cosmic Threat

[[feanon-serinde|Fëanon]] concluded that the necromantic mists, the Thayan attacks, and the spontaneous reanimation plaguing [[faerun|Faerûn]] all stemmed from Atropos drawing nearer to the Prime Material Plane. [[lemerlais|Lemerlais]] laid it out plainly: Atropos and Orcus would not stop with one planet — they would hunt anyone powerful enough to oppose them. Fleeing was not an escape. Only [[the-last-word|The Last Word]] could end it.

Wizards and warlords alike sought accommodation with the coming darkness. Hugh believed that [[shaahir|Shaahir]] and other wizards of [[amn|Amn]] and Thay were backing Orcus in exchange for promises of Undeath — a way to survive the coming calamity.
