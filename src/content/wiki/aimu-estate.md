The Aimu Estate sits at the back of [[frostmoore-vale|Frostmoore Vale]], a wooded mountain valley in the Galena foothills a day's ride north of [[ironfang-keep|Ironfang Keep]]. It is the late wife's old estate of [[lord-aimu|Lord Aimu]] — a modest noble manor by the standards of [[helioglabulus|Helios]]'s upper rings, comprising a two-storey house of fine stone and slate, terraced gardens of vines and vegetables, a small orchard near the lake, and a glittering lake fed by a cascade of small waterfalls running down from the high peaks.

The most prominent structure on the grounds is not the house itself but a separate, ornate chapel of [[lathander|Lathander]] — set apart from the manor, with a slate roof bearing a Morninglord sunburst and tall narrow windows. The chapel houses the altar on which Lord Aimu placed the stolen [[ashbringer|Sunsword]] when he arrived at the estate in flight from the Cathedral.

## Defences

The estate is held by Knights of the [[crimson-dawn-knights|Order of the Crimson Dawn]] sworn to Lord Aimu's household, led by a plate-clad veteran named [[ishin|Ishin]]. When the party arrived at sundown — bluffing their way past a peephole-guarded front door under [[jack-mayer-johnson-mathews|JmJm]]'s pretence of being a troupe of travelling performers — six fully armoured knights opened the door in formation, ornate bows already strung and enchanted blades drawn at the sight of an incoming dragon.

## The Battle

On the evening of the party's arrival, a flight of eight [[ashen-white-dragon|Ashen White Dragons]] crested the valley ridge, carrying a [[red-wizards-of-thay|Red Wizard of Thay]] on the largest of them and answered shortly after by a Vampire Knight on a sickly adult white dragon mount wielding an adapted [[saurian-lance|Saurian lance-weapon]]. The estate's east wing was torn open by a lightning bolt fired from the lance; trees and vineyards were scorched; the lake bay below was filled with the wing-beats of a copper dragon in adult form. The battle was joined as the sun dipped below the rim of the mountains.

The chapel itself was untouched.
