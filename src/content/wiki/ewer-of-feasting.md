The Ewer of Feasting is a magical silver vessel carried by [[gundren-rockseeker|Gundren Rockseeker]] that conjures elaborate meals, campfires, and spreads of food when poured. It has been a constant companion of the group since its acquisition and has seen use in haunted castles, elven inns, freed slave camps, taverns, volcanic wastelands, and moonlit farmsteads.

## Acquisition

Gundren found the ewer in the [[amber-temple|Amber Temple]] in [[barovia|Barovia]], where it sat at the centre of a ghostly dinner table, magically summoning food for spectral diners. When he grabbed it, the ghosts' calm features twisted into masks of murderous rage and they launched themselves at the group — the psychic assault aged [[feanon-serinde|Fëanon]] twenty years and [[tarkathee-daru|Tark]] forty. Gundren slammed the ewer back on the table to calm them, then immediately grabbed it again once [[horatio-von-bumidere|Buumi]] had laid the spirits to rest with the Holy Symbol of Ravenkind.

## Notable Uses

The ewer has appeared throughout the campaign in moments both mundane and significant. On the eve of the assault on Castle Ravenloft, Gundren poured a feast on the oak table while [[mordenkainen|Mordenkainen]] prepared his scrying. At an elven inn on the road to Silverymoon, the group produced the ewer to conjure a proper meal when the cuisine proved disappointingly light on meat. In Neverwinter, the ewer fed a camp of freed slaves — Gundren supplemented the conjured banquet with stores from the ship's hold while an older half-orc woman named Keerna introduced herself. In a tavern, Gundren poured its contents over an empty table and a bountiful feast erupted across the wood, the flowing red wine becoming a tablecloth beneath plates of food, to the cheers of every patron in the establishment.

On a Damaran farmstead in the [[bloodstone-lands|Bloodstone Lands]], Gundren poured out a campfire and a full spread for the group's first evening with [[cassian|Cassian]], welcoming the planar traveler over a midnight feast.

## Wild Magic

The ewer is not immune to environmental hazards. On the slopes of Mt. Saqar in [[al-askar|Al Askar]], where wild-magic surges were a constant threat, Gundren poured out the ewer and was thrown twenty feet down the mountainside by the resulting surge. He trudged back up with a new bruise. In the sanctified Temple of Vaketh — safe from wild-magic effects — the group feasted properly for the first time since arriving on Al Askar.
