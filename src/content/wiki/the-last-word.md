The Last Word is an ancient artefact of Creation, broken into fragments and scattered across the multiverse. Its name and nature suggest it represents the fundamental power of creation itself — a word spoken at the dawn of existence whose fragmentation threatens the stability of all reality. It is the central macguffin of the campaign: whoever reassembles the fragments will wield power beyond comprehension, and every cosmic antagonist — [[orcus|Orcus]], [[zhengyi|Zhengyi]], [[vecna|Vecna]], and ultimately [[atropos|Atropos]] — is hunting for them.

## Discovery

The group first learned of the artefact when Archmage Amaram revealed that the golden relic [[kangaxx|Kangaxx]] had sought from the celestial vault beneath [[neverwinter|Neverwinter]] was a fragment of something called The Last Word. Amaram could not say what it did or how to use it. He knew only that Orcus was responsible for its sundering, and that reuniting the shards would produce power beyond comprehension.

## The Fragments

The fragments have been located across multiple worlds and planes:

- The **celestial vault shard** was recovered from beneath the Chasm in Neverwinter, where murals depicted the artefact at the heart of the ancient [[saurians|Saurian]] empire's power — the relic that fuelled their mechanised civilisation and conquest across the planes.
- A shard was built into the **[[eye-of-aren|Eye of Aren]]** by [[theodemir|Theodemir]], who used it as the keystone of the binding spell imprisoning Zhengyi. Theodemir poured his life force into the artefact, his spirit preserved within it.
- A fragment was held by **Strahd Von Zarovich** in [[barovia|Barovia]], who channelled it in [[castle-ravenloft|Castle Ravenloft]]'s desecrated chapel to tear the castle free of the demiplane. Baba Lysaga confirmed it was their only exit from Barovia.
- A fragment lies within the **Black Pyramid of Barzok** in the dead city of Sharza on [[al-askar|Al Askar]], where [[vecna|Vecna]]'s undead armies guard it.
- [[zhengyi|Zhengyi]] controls fragments through his **Orbs of Dragonkind** and operations in the Bloodstone Lands.

## Cosmic Significance

[[lemerlais|Lemerlais]] laid it out plainly to the party: Atropos and Orcus would not stop with one planet — they would hunt anyone powerful enough to oppose them. Fleeing was not an escape. Only The Last Word could end it.

[[demogorgon|Demogorgon]] himself, when he assembled at the bridges of Ladueger's Furrow in [[gracksuluth|Gracksuluth]], oriented immediately to [[tarkathee-daru|Tark]] — who normally carried the fragment. One of the Demon Prince's heads spoke in Abyssal: it knew him, it was looking for him, and its presence in the [[underdark|Underdark]] was deliberate. It had come for the artefact.

## The Font of Fawden

The artefact's most dramatic use came at the [[font-of-fawden|Font of Fawden]] on [[al-askar|Al Askar]], where the group assembled a Ringed Veil beneath the dying avatar of the goddess Fawden and began a ritual with The Last Word to channel Fawden's divine energy to [[selune|Selûne]] — denying it to Vecna. The ritual succeeded, but at tremendous cost: Vecna discovered the plan, tore open a gate to Sharza, and unleashed an army of undead upon the Font in the campaign's most desperate battle.

## Volatility

The fragments are dangerously volatile. When [[tarkathee-daru|Tark]] opened his [[bag-of-holding|Bag of Holding]] to inspect a fragment, a charge of energy built around him and twenty feet behind, [[horatio-von-bumidere|Buumi]] lurched forward as if shoved by an invisible force. Tark closed the bag quickly. In [[neverwinter|Neverwinter]], [[feanon-serinde|Fëanon]]'s jade frog golem swallowed a shard and swelled with glowing cracks before regurgitating it just in time.
