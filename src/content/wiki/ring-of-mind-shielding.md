The Ring of Mind Shielding is a plain metal band worn by [[Fëanon Sërindë|Fëanon]] since the early days of the campaign. Its enchantment renders the wearer's thoughts unreadable and their mind resistant to psionic intrusion — but the ring's true significance lies in what it carries inside. Over the course of Fëanon's journey, the ring has housed a succession of souls and fragments, each chapter of its history reflecting the wizard's evolving relationship with power, knowledge, and responsibility.

## History

### The Tournament

Fëanon won the ring at a firebending tournament. When he first attuned to it, he discovered it was not empty — a trapped soul already resided within, left by a previous owner who had died wearing the band. The spirit's identity and fate remain unrecorded.

### The Atropal Fragment

In the Amber Temple of Barovia, Fëanon accepted a dark entity's offering and expelled it into the ring, intending to trap the power where it could be studied without corrupting his soul. The entity — an Atropal fragment — proved less containable than expected. Dark veins spread across Fëanon's hand from the ring, a visible mark of the corruption housed within. The fragment remained imprisoned in the ring through the party's time in Barovia, a constant reminder of the cost of Fëanon's empiricism.

### Theodemir's Spirit

After the party recovered the [[Eye of Aren]] and freed [[Theodemir]]'s spirit from the artifact, the Atropal fragment was expelled and destroyed to make room for something far more valuable. Theodemir's spirit was transferred into the ring, and from within it, the old archwizard continued to teach his student — imparting knowledge of time and space magic, assisting with spell development through the [[Iron Tome]], and shoring up Fëanon's mental defenses when they came under psionic assault.

### The Clone Transfer

When Fëanon's cloning project produced a viable body, he forged a psionic link between the ring and the clone. Theodemir's soul flowed through the connection and into the waiting body, and for the first time in decades, the ring stood empty. The vacancy did not last.

### The World Tree

During the party's journey beyond their own world, a dying World Tree's soul cried out in its final moments. Fëanon offered the ring as a liferaft — a vessel to preserve whatever fragment of the ancient consciousness could be saved. A piece of the World Tree's essence took refuge within the band, carried back to Fëanon's world in a ring that had already housed an unknown spirit, a dark god's fragment, and an archwizard's soul.

### The Return

The clone body Theodemir inhabited eventually failed, and his spirit returned to the ring. The World Tree fragment's current status within the ring — whether it coexists with Theodemir or has since faded — remains unclear.

## Current State

The ring currently houses Theodemir's slowly degrading spirit. The old archwizard slumbers more with each passing month, present less, his voice growing quieter. Theodemir has requested a proper replacement body — not another clone of his student — but whether that is possible remains an open question. For now, the ring endures as it always has: a small, plain band carrying far more than its size would suggest.
