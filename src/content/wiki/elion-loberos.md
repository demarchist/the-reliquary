Elion Loberos was a young elf of striking beauty with dark hair and pale features, one of Theodemir's five acolytes who participated in the conspiracy to hide the Eye of Aren. He was the quietest of the group — watchful and reserved, rarely offering opinions during debates but observing everything with an intensity that unsettled his peers.

During the debate over the Eye's fate, Elion said nothing, simply inclining his head when the decision was made. When the Elder Brain betrayed the acolytes inside the hive, Elion moved with a speed and martial grace that did not match his mild scholarly manner, cutting a path toward Fëanon with blades that materialised from nowhere. He almost reached him — but a telekinetic wall slammed him into the cavern floor. By the time Megno dragged him upright, the Illithids had sealed the passage. Fëanon was gone.

He was experienced through the memory simulation by Tark, who noted that something about the memory felt concealed — as though it was deliberately holding back.
