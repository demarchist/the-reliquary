The Arcane Hand Glove is a single leather glove that [[jack-mayer-johnson-mathews|JmJm]] has carried at his belt for as long as any of his current companions have known him. He has never worn it casually. He has never spoken about its origin. It appears at his side only in the worst moments of the campaign — and when it does, it works.

## Function

When JmJm pulls the glove onto his right hand, an enormous translucent disembodied hand the size of a horse coalesces in the air beside him. The hand has ten fingers, all wider than a man's leg, and moves with the deliberate intelligence of an extension of his will. It does not speak. It does what he wants it to do.

It catches him when he falls.

It strikes when he commands.

It crushes what he points at.

When the conjured hand expends itself or its purpose is fulfilled, it dissolves into the air and the glove is once again merely a glove — though always slightly warmer than it should be, as though the energy it stored briefly remembers the shape of having been used.

## Notable Uses

At the [[font-of-fawden|Font of Fawden]], during the climactic battle against [[vecna|Vecna]]'s undead legions, JmJm pulled on the glove for the first time anyone could remember when a second Death Knight broke the line. The Arcane Hand smashed the creature into the ground and held it there until the bard could finish it.

In the lair of the [[elder-orb|Elder Orb]] beneath the [[mines-of-und|Mines of Und]], the Elder Orb destroyed [[steden|Steden]] mid-battle with a barrage of eyebeams. JmJm fell thirty feet from the Nightmare's saddle and pulled the glove on as he plummeted. The Arcane Hand caught him a foot above the chamber floor, cradled him upright, and set him down without so much as a stumble. He turned back to the fight without acknowledgement.

## Origins

JmJm has never explained the glove. The most that has been gleaned is that it predates his current life — older than his time with the [[faerun-defense-force|Faerûn Defense Force]], older than the period when the persona of the Dark Whistler dominated him. It may be a relic of [[sigil|Sigil]], the City of Doors where he was born; it may have been a tool of his earlier profession; it may have been gifted by someone whose name has not yet appeared in the campaign. He carries it always, on his person, never in a pack — at his belt, in reach, the way a man keeps a key to a door he might one day need to open.

He reaches for it without hesitation when the moment requires it. He has always known it was there. He has always trusted it would work. Whatever else it is, it is part of him in the same way the [[inkblade|Inkblade]] is part of him — a piece of his actual self, unconnected to the costume he performs in.
