Thunder Bluff is a thousand-foot cliff overlooking the River Rauvin along the Trade Way between [[neverwinter|Neverwinter]] and [[silverymoon|Silverymoon]], the thunder of rapids echoing off its sides. To the north rises the magical forest of the [[moonwood|Moonwood]]; to the south, the grey expanse of the [[evermoors|Evermoors]] stretches to the horizon. The village of [[kossenberg|Kossenberg]] nestles at the base of the Moonwood.

## Geography

The Thunder Bluff region is a place of dramatic vertical contrasts, where ancient glacial forces have sculpted a landscape of soaring plateaus, thundering waterfalls, and mist-shrouded lowlands. The terrain descends stepwise from the frigid heights of the north toward the endless southern swamps — each tier distinct in character and elevation.

### The High Moonwood Plateau

Dominating the northern and western reaches, the [[the-moonwood|Moonwood]] occupies a vast elevated plateau — the remnant bed of a long-receded glacier. Its surface lies significantly higher than the lands to the south, a broad highland cloaked in dense ancient forest that lends the region an ethereal, moonlit quality even by day. A mighty river, born from the glacial meltwaters of the Spine of the World far to the north, carves a powerful valley through the heart of the plateau and flows southward before plunging off its southern edge.

### The Red Hills Cliffs and Lake Seluna

The southern margin of the Moonwood terminates in the Red Hills — abrupt, near-vertical cliffs that drop hundreds of feet in a breathtaking escarpment. The great river from the plateau cascades off these cliffs in roaring waterfalls, sending curtains of mist down into [[lake-seluna|Lake Seluna]] far below. The lake sits at the base of the cliffs, a deep, dark body of water constantly replenished by the high-plateau river and glacial outflow, its surface perpetually shrouded in shifting mist.

### The Thunder Bluff Shelf

South of [[lake-seluna|Lake Seluna]] lies the Thunder Bluff shelf — a distinct mid-elevation plateau, noticeably lower than the Moonwood but still elevated above the southern lowlands. This shelf supports the city of Thunder Bluff, built amid a dynamic braided river system draining the lake. Multiple interlaced channels weave across the shelf, varying with seasonal melt and rainfall to create islands, gravel bars, and ever-shifting waterways. The city is integrated into this hydrological network, with bridges, stilted structures, and riverside districts harnessing the constant motion of the water.

At the southern edge of the shelf, these braided rivers converge and plunge over the Thunder Rapids — a series of powerful cascades where the shelf drops sharply into the canyon below. The roar is said to be audible for miles, and gives the entire region its name.

### The River Rauvin Canyon and the Evermoors

Below the rapids lies the deep River Rauvin canyon, carved over eons by erosive force, channeling the combined outflow westward toward the broader continent. South of the Rauvin, the land drops into the vast, low-lying [[the-evermoors|Evermoors]] — a seemingly endless expanse of dark swamps, marshes, and wetlands stretching indefinitely southward, characterized by slow-moving black waters, dense vegetation, and a brooding, primordial atmosphere.

## The Barony

The Barony of Thunder Bluff was awarded to the party by High Marshal Arasume in the aftermath of the failed Amnian coup at [[silverymoon|Silverymoon]]. The entire region for miles around lies within the barony's political boundaries, governed by a council of adventurers who call themselves the [[faerun-defense-force|Faerûn Defense Force]] — though their detractors know them as the Heralds of Woe.

When the party first arrived, the ranger Moogie gave them a survey: the ruined castle of High Hold (Moorwatch), destroyed two years earlier by the Orcish Horde; [[kossenberg|Kossenberg]]; Old Kossenberg, the ruins of the original settlement at the base of the bluff; Lake [[selune|Selûna]] and its ranger lodge; the Red Hill with its iron deposits and the Glittering Caves beneath; and Oldstone, a sacred menhir older than local memory.

## Settlement

A tribe of orc deserters who had fled the Horde and been driven from caves beneath the bluff by the spirit troll Shadowclaw were the barony's first citizens. The party offered them Old Kossenberg in exchange for proof of strength — destroying Shadowclaw from the caverns. Freed slaves from across the North later made their way to [[selunia|Selunia]], drawn by the promise of a fresh start.
