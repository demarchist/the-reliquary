The Oathbow is a longbow of dark heartwood, its limbs inlaid the full of their length with a single unbroken line of silver script too old to read. [[jack-mayer-johnson-mathews|JmJm]] acquired it in the gear-and-craft layer of [[bytopia|Bytopia]] during the planar errand that delivered [[azoo-the-archer|Azoo of Shou Lung]] to her lair — recognising the weapon, as he recognised a good audience, by the weight of the silence around it.

## The Price

The gnomish artificer who kept the stall had no use for gold. The craftsfolk of the Twin Paradises trade in things that are *hard*, and what the gnome wanted from JmJm was the one currency the bard had spent a lifetime accumulating and never been asked to spend: his legend. His bardic knowledge, his songs, his stories — the catalogue of a performer who had made an enemy fall in love with him on the stage of the Refounding Festival in front of a roaring crowd.

JmJm gave it over — not the whole of himself, but enough. He sang into the wood, told the bow three stories it had never heard, and the silver script along its limbs took a little light as he did, like ink drying.

## The Oath

The bow names its own price beyond the gnome's, and JmJm paid it. He swore an Oath: to add to the weapon's legend — to carry it into stories worth telling, to make of it a thing that future bards would haggle away their own songs to hold — and, when he was through with the artifact, to give it to a wielder worthy of it, or else to return it to the stall, the layer, and the gnomes of Bytopia who had kept it. The words settled over the bow and over the bard both. He strung it, and it fit his hand as though it had been waiting for him.

The oath binds the keeping of the weapon as much as its use. JmJm holds the Oathbow as a borrowed legend, not an owned one — a thing he is obliged to *grow* and then, eventually, to pass on. It joins the [[inkblade|Inkblade]] and the [[mysterious-glove|mysterious glove]] among the instruments he carries that are less possessions than extensions of who he is.
