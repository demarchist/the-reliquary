The Ethereal Plane is a misty, fog-filled dimension that exists as a border between the Material Plane and the Inner Planes. Creatures on the Ethereal can see into the Material Plane as ghostly, translucent images, and vice versa under certain magical conditions. The plane serves as a conduit for travel between dimensions and is home to ghosts, phase spiders, and other creatures that exist between states of reality.

## Tactical Use

[[jack-mayer-johnson-mathews|JmJm]] made the Ethereal Plane a regular part of the party's tactical repertoire through his Nightmare mount [[steden|Steden]], whose planar-shifting ability allowed the group to move between the Material and Ethereal Planes at will. During the [[battle-of-the-tar-pits|Battle of the Tar Pits]], JmJm led the ambush by galloping from the Ethereal astride Steden with [[cirrus-al-kayan|Cirrus]] and [[gundren-rockseeker|Gundren]], catching the halfling cultists completely off guard.

The group has also used the Ethereal Plane for reconnaissance. In [[barovia|Barovia]], the plane's overlap with the cursed demiplane allowed them to scout locations and observe enemies who could not perceive them. The Ethereal's ghostly translucence made it simultaneously useful and unsettling — they could see the Material world laid out before them, but everything was drained of colour and substance, as though viewed through frosted glass.

## The Battle Against Hohonu

The Ethereal Plane played its most dramatic role during the battle against Hohonu on [[al-askar|Al Askar]]. JmJm and [[tarkathee-daru|Tark]] witnessed the ancient dragon's prison erupting in the Ethereal as the Great Wyrm attempted to pull itself free. Tark bravely entered the Ethereal to retrieve the soul of the fallen [[camea|Camea]], storing it in a waterskin while swimming through a cloud of spectres near the Death Wyrm. The plane's overlap with reality allowed Hohonu to project his power through the weakened veil, striking at the party even from within his prison. Cirrus rode Steden into the vortex of the prison to pull Tark from the Death Wyrm's clutches.

## The Mines of Und

In the Galena Mountains, after the group destroyed two Beholders in the village of [[und|Und]], [[jack-mayer-johnson-mathews|JmJm]] lingered in the Ethereal Plane atop [[steden|Steden]] and observed the [[mines-of-und|mines]] beneath the village blazing with power — lines of light pulsing through the rock like a heartbeat, invisible from the Material Plane but unmistakable from this side of the veil. The energy was far too strong for the Beholders they had killed, leading [[feanon-serinde|Fëanon]] to identify the true threat as an [[elder-orb|Elder Orb]] — an ancient aberration feeding from a magical source deep below. JmJm also consulted a ghost at a ranger cabin further down the mountain, asking whether evacuated villagers could shelter there — the spirit was confused but agreeable.

## The Shadowfell Connection

The party discovered that the Ethereal Plane sometimes bleeds into — or is bled into by — the [[shadowfell|Shadowfell]]. In the [[underdark|Underdark]], passages leaked Shadowfell energy through tears in the Ethereal boundary. During their companions' sleep, the group found that their allies were being drawn into the Shadowfell through the Ethereal — a discovery that required both [[feanon-serinde|Fëanon]]'s magical expertise and the intuition to investigate in the first place.
