## Selgaunt

The [[Faerûn Defense Force]] arrived in [[Selgaunt]] on the shores of the Sea of Fallen Stars — a sun-baked Mediterranean port city that hummed with the commerce of nations at war. They had come pursuing intelligence on [[Zhengyi]], following the thread of the Witch King's operations east. The city bustled with trade and military activity: a key supply-line hub for the Alliance, linking the northeastern nations of Vaasa and Damara to their Amnian allies to the southwest. As such, it had become a frequent target — though no one in the party yet understood what that meant.

They found lodgings and settled in. The harbour was crowded with merchant vessels and naval escorts. Soldiers from half a dozen nations moved through the streets. The war felt close here, even in the warmth of the afternoon.

## The Raid

It came after dark.

An ominous humming cut through the night — a sound with no precedent in the lexicon of Faerûnian warfare. Then the sky exploded. Shapes screamed overhead, impossibly fast, raining devastation on the port with surgical precision. The city's defenders were obliterated in minutes. Port facilities shattered into rubble. Merchant ships along the quay erupted into floating infernos, their burning hulks casting hellish light across the harbour.

The attackers vanished as quickly as they had come, leaving behind a city in flames and a population in shock. No one had seen anything like it. No dragon, no siege engine, no war wizard had ever struck with such speed and impunity. The nature of warfare in Faerûn had changed in a single night, and the people of Selgaunt were only beginning to understand the implications.

## The Storm

As dawn broke over the burning city, the party sprang into action to help fight the fires. [[Jack Mayer Johnson Mathews|JmJm]], impatient and impulsive as ever, took matters into his own hands. His summoned rainstorm was supposed to help.

It evolved into a tempest of biblical proportions. Rain lashed the flood-prone coastal city in sheets. Mudslides cascaded down the steep hillsides. Selgaunt's streets became raging rivers as the deluge transformed the fire response into a new disaster entirely.

The fires were extinguished. So was any goodwill from the citizens. The locals made it abundantly clear that the [[Faerûn Defense Force]] had overstayed their welcome — cold shoulders, wary glances, and pointed suggestions to leave while they still could.

## The Departure

As the group contemplated their next move, [[Fëanon Sërindë|Fëanon]] and [[Gundren Rockseeker|Gundren]] exchanged a look — the kind of look that said they had been keeping a secret for a very long time — and teleported away without explanation.

The rest stood in stunned silence. Through a surprising burst of arcane insight, they determined the destination: [[Silverymoon]], half a continent away.

Whatever Fëanon and Gundren had been hiding, it had something to do with what had just fallen from the sky.
