## Crisis of Confidence

In the days following the crash, [[MCAS Mythalmar]] felt different. The Silver Flagon was quieter. The hangar crews worked with a tension that hadn't been there before. [[Jack Mayer Johnson Mathews|JmJm]] lay in the infirmary, battered and bruised, staring at the ceiling with the expression of a man replaying a flat spin behind his eyes.

The programme teetered. Word came down from Alliance Command that [[Project STARFALL]] was under review — the crash had given ammunition to those who argued the [[r-14-thunderchaser|R-14]] was too unstable for operational deployment. Engineers debated stability margins. Brass debated funding. The pilots debated whether the machines they had been asked to fly were trying to kill them.

[[Gundren Rockseeker|Gundren]] threw himself into the technical problem with the single-minded ferocity of a dwarf who had built something and refused to see it fail. He tore apart the two-seater's wreckage, identified the jetwash vulnerability, and redesigned the engine intake baffles. [[Fëanon Sërindë|Fëanon]], watching from the control tower as always, convened the University researchers and guided them through the arcane integration issues — the Thunder Drive's interaction with turbulent magical fields needed refinement, not abandonment.

[[Tarkathee Daru|Tark]] visited JmJm in the infirmary. The monk said little. He sat with his friend in the silence, and the silence said what needed saying.

[[Gustavo]] barely left JmJm's side. He carried the weight of having ejected clean while his friend hit the canopy — survivor's guilt wrapped in a bard's easy smile that fooled no one who looked closely.

## The Arrival

It came late one night. The control tower picked up an unknown contact approaching from the south — low, slow, trailing smoke. Alarms sounded. Pilots scrambled to the briefing room. Fëanon's voice crackled over the Rocky-talkies: hold positions, do not engage.

A damaged [[vk-31-frostbite|VK-31 Frostbite]] limped into Mythalmar's airspace, one engine sputtering, arcane fire licking from a cracked fuselage panel. It waggled its wings — the universal signal of surrender — and set down hard on the main runway in a shower of sparks and shattered crystal. Emergency crews swarmed the craft as it skidded to a halt.

The canopy opened. A single Amnian pilot climbed out, hands raised, and asked — in accented Common — to speak with whoever was in charge.

## The Debriefing

They brought him to the briefing room. Fëanon and Gundren led the interrogation while the rest of the team watched from behind a one-way scrying mirror. The defector was gaunt, exhausted, and visibly terrified — but coherent. He had flown his damaged Frostbite across four hundred miles of contested airspace to reach the one facility he'd been briefed to avoid at all costs.

He told them everything. The [[amn|Amnian]] aircraft were being manufactured at a concealed facility — a fortified arcane foundry hidden in the mountains. He provided its location, its defences, its patrol schedules, and the number of completed Frostbites sitting on its flightline. The factory was accelerating production. Within weeks, the Amnians would have enough aircraft to launch a sustained aerial campaign that no Alliance ground force could withstand.

The window to strike was narrow and closing fast.

## The Choice

Fëanon called the team together. The holocrystals projected the defector's intelligence over the briefing table — the factory's location, approach vectors, defensive emplacements, anti-air wards. It was deep behind enemy lines. The R-14s would be operating at the edge of their range with no margin for error.

[[Cirrus Al Kayan|Cirrus]] spoke first. He had studied the R-14's systems more thoroughly than anyone during his all-night sessions in this very room. The machines were sound, he said. The crash was a known hazard, not a design flaw. Gundren's modifications had addressed the vulnerability.

[[Aliyah Faen Tlabbar|Patches]] laid out the tactical approach — ingress routes, timing, the sequence of targets. Her cockpit might still be wallpapered with torn manual pages, but the information had clearly gone in.

[[Horatio Von Bumidere|Buumi]] said what they were all thinking: they hadn't come this far to sit on the ground while the enemy won the war from the air.

From the infirmary doorway, JmJm appeared — bruised, bandaged, leaning on [[Gustavo]]'s shoulder. He looked at the projected map, then at his friends.

"When do we fly?"
