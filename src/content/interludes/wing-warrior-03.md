## First Briefing

The following morning, the group assembled in the briefing room — finding [[Cirrus Al Kayan|Cirrus]] already there, dark circles under his eyes from an all-night study session. [[Jack Mayer Johnson Mathews|JmJm]] was conspicuously absent, passed out and hungover from his "Great Balls of Fire" performance the night before.

[[Fëanon Sërindë|Fëanon]], who would serve as the grounded tactics instructor throughout the programme, introduced them to their flight instructor: "Viper" — a stern, grizzled figure who would guide them through a series of training sorties. Cirrus tested Viper's patience with subtle challenges to his authority, but ultimately played along. The first exercise was simple: get airborne, familiarise yourselves with the R-14's flying characteristics.

## First Flight

They were ushered to a ready room and issued flight suits, jackets, and helmets equipped with magical communication stones embedded in the lining — "Rocky-talkies," the crews called them. Then they shuffled out to the flightline, climbed into their planes, and Viper walked them through the startup procedures over the comms.

[[Aliyah Faen Tlabbar|Patches]] had plastered loose pages from her shredded manual all over the cockpit — every available surface covered in torn diagrams and half-legible notes.

The [[R-14 Thunderchaser|R-14s]] taxied through a wide, low slot-opening in the mountainside and out to the runway. One by one, they throttled up and roared into the sky — some more aptly than others.

## The Dogfight

The party formed up at altitude and practised basic flight manoeuvres. Once they had demonstrated a basic proficiency, Viper turned them loose on each other with non-lethal loadouts. Last one standing was the winner.

What followed was clunky, chaotic, and glorious. [[Tarkathee Daru|Tark]] went down first. The others weaved and burned through the training airspace over the Moonwood until only [[Aliyah Faen Tlabbar|Patches]] and [[Gundren Rockseeker|Gundren]] remained — the two of them circling each other with the patient precision of seasoned predators, neither able to score the winning shot. Their planes ran low on fuel before either could claim victory.

On the way home, Tark buzzed the tower. Despite the pattern being full. The base commander wanted blood, but Viper — who valued the warrior spirit over regulation — let it slide with a grudging smirk.

## The Two-Seater

Debriefed and released, the group regrouped in the hangar where they caught up with JmJm, who was loudly defending his absence while pacing between the aircraft. His eye caught a large object hidden under a massive tarp. With characteristic defiance, he grabbed the canvas and pulled.

Beneath it sat a massive two-seater plane — an earlier model developed before the R-14, slower but more heavily armed. Gundren explained it was a prototype from an earlier phase of the programme. JmJm's eyes lit up. He insisted this would be the plane he flew.

## Volleyball

Outside the hangar doors, a volleyball court baked in the late-afternoon sun. As golden hour settled over [[MCAS Mythalmar]], the group got sweaty.

Teams formed: [[Fëanon Sërindë|Fëanon]], JmJm, and Patches versus Tark, [[Cirrus Al Kayan|Cirrus]], and [[Horatio Von Bumidere|Buumi]]. What began as friendly competition escalated into magical chaos within minutes.

Cirrus temporarily banished Fëanon to a pocket dimension to prevent him from defending a spike. Fëanon retaliated by magically manipulating the weight of the ball to score on an unwitting defender. Tark channelled his dragon chi to dominate a return. JmJm summoned a Bigby's Hand to defend the net. All rules were off. The match became a magical arms race disguised as sport.

The score was tied. Tark, Cirrus, and Buumi were about to score the winning point when a shirtless stranger dove into the court at the last second, defended the shot, and set JmJm up for a commanding spike that won the game.

## Gustavo

The stranger was [[Gustavo]] — an old friend of JmJm's from his days as the feared mercenary known as The Dark Whistler. Gustavo worked at the base, and the reunion was immediate and warm. JmJm insisted on the spot that Gustavo would be his back-seater in the two-man plane.

That night in the canteen, Gustavo insisted that a woman at the bar had "lost that loving feeling." The two bards — old comrades, natural showmen — led the entire establishment in a raucous rendition of the song. The canteen had never been louder.
