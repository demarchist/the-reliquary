The Dragons of Shou Lung find themselves at the edge of the jungles and swamps of the western Empire — for the first time in thousands of years, their people have left the hidden lands of the Dragons. They take flight and survey a landscape below dotted with Lizardmen ruins: monolithic pyramids and temples long fallen into disrepair, though spots of firelight flicker around some, and the scent of civilization drifts up from others.

They catch the scent of a Green Dragon lair and descend to investigate one of their kin, long separated by the centuries. While the foreign dragon treats with the half-dozen metallics that have descended on her territory, **Asha**, **Azoo**, and **The Black Arrow** slip inside. They make off with treasure and eggs while the others make small talk — the local dragon little more than a wide-eyed country bumpkin by their estimation. They leave her be and follow the compulsion west.

Over the jungles they descend on a Tabaxi village. **Vakuun** cows the locals into worship and anoints one as his chosen before departing with his ego appropriately stoked. **Aurelian** swings back around and incinerates the anointed before catching up with the group.

After hundreds of miles of jungle the dragons find a great ruined tower to rest for the night. By morning the land has changed around them — great monoliths of stone have burst from the earth in their wake. They exude magic as they teeter on the edge of ascension to ancienthood. The compulsion calls them northwest.

Jungle gives way to foothills and then to arid mountains bordering a vast desert. In the foothills where the mountains meet the steppe they find their first signs of true civilization outside Shou Lung: a walled city of hundreds of stone towers, home to Rock Gnome Magi ruling a vital trade hub in the desolate landscape. The area teeters on hard times, near to bursting with refugees fleeing a warlord on the nearby steppe.

**Kalphena** and **Asha** are immediately seized by nesting instincts, selecting and mending a tower to make a proper lair while engaging the locals in excited conversation about architecture. The Gnomes are terrified. Their archmage, bedecked in plates of precious metals and gemstones, loses his composure and deploys the city's greatest artifact — a Ring of Wishes — and whisks the dragons from his city. **Aurelian** resists the Wish and remains behind to help those in need.

Most of the dragons lament their expulsion but submit to the geas pulling them west. **Fellasin** battles the compulsion and returns — then sees **Aurelian** already tending to the refugees and stays his wrath against the city. Instead he finds the archmage and demands the Ring as recompense. The Gnome extends his hand. **Fellasin** bites it off, takes the Ring, and gives into the compulsion with **Aurelian** in tow.

The desert stretches a thousand miles. Even the mightiest begin to tire. They find an abandoned Blue Dragon lair — its former occupant long dead, only a mummified corpse remaining. **Fellasin** does what he can to ruin and dominate the old den while the others find decent quarters. **Xuefang** refuses to be contained in a desert hovel and goes hunting in the canyons, battling and feasting upon a monstrous spider before claiming its lair for the night.

The slumbering dragons reshape the land once more. **The Black Arrow**'s dreams summon a fetid oasis from the sands; **Asha** supercharges it with plant growth.

The desert gives way to a glittering coast. They fly out over the inland sea for hours past the fishing boats of lesser creatures before spotting a pack of massive sea creatures below. A hunt is called — each dragon takes one, kills it, and hauls it into the sky. **Xuefang** picks the largest and needs **The Black Arrow**'s assistance to carry it off.

Humanoids scatter from fishing villages on the northern coast as the Dragonflight presses northwest toward the barrier mountains of the Bloodstone Lands. They find secluded vales and an extinct volcano to nest for the night. **Xuefang** hunts on a nearby glacier, slays a Remorhaz, and settles into a crevasse. That night a mysterious figure descends to the caldera and meets with the sleeping **Vakuun**. Roars of rage echo in the dark. By morning the extinct volcano is active, lava spilling down its sides. **Asha** shields a vale on the mountainside from the encroaching flow before the group takes flight once more.

They fly into Damara and follow the call to the Galena Mountains — to **Bloodstone City** in the pass between Vaasa and Damara. They have been called to the Great Tree of Bahamut.

Atop the highest terrace of the fortress city, in a plaza of gleaming marble gardens surrounding a massive white tree, two dozen yellow-robed monks encircle the vast shining form of **Protanther**, an Ancient Gold Wyrm and abdicated king of his kind. The Dragonflight lands — all save **Xuefang**, who flies north to scope the tundra.

Protanther explains that he and his counterpart — **Themberchaud the Grand**, an Ancient Red Dragon — are to help planeshift each of the dragons to their new lairs, where they will ascend. The Great Tree links to Yggdrasil itself — the pathway to the planes.

Each dragon is assigned a powerful attendant to serve as their majordomo. The metallic attendants: Gilruthad of Evermeet (High Elf, Gold), Opheria of Dokkar (Drow, Silver), Humperdink of Bytopia (Rock Gnome, Copper), Jaryk of Ysgard (Wood Elf, Brass), and Lilliana of The Great Sea (Triton, Bronze). The chromatic attendants: Gartokkar Brutt of Gracksluulth (Duergar, Red), Yolen of Nidavellir (Svirfneblin, Blue), Holek of Acheron (Orc, Black), and Julianna of The Great Dale (Human, Green) — though they have no chromatic dragons to watch over them yet, as their emissary Themberchaud is nowhere to be seen.

The metallics eagerly attend Protanther. The chromatics disperse into the city, showing little desire to converse. The dragons note that Protanther's left forearm is missing from the elbow — an old wound that festers with the dark energies of the chromatic goddess, preventing healing. The chromatics note this with some satisfaction.

Protanther's attempt to scry on Themberchaud fails.

**Xuefang**, ranging north, finds his attendant camped outside the city in the tundra — and something else. Ominous clouds build on the horizon. The ground rumbles. A plume of volcanic ash rises from the north.

Gartokkar Brutt remarks that it is likely his master's lair. A Red Dragon of Themberchaud's power has full control over the volcanic nature of his lair — he would only allow it to erupt in the most dire of circumstances.

Protanther asks the chromatic dragons to fly and investigate their kin. Several metallics join in the spirit of cooperation. They streak north into ash-choked skies, visibility dropping even for the advanced senses of dragons.

Through the smoke they circle the volcano, noting great elementals of fire and lava moving through the flows below. They find the entrance — a great rent in the mountainside — and two giant Oni standing guard outside, a huge sack between them, stained through with wet blood.

The dragons dive to attack.
