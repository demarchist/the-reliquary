## The Summons

After a tense wait at a Selgaunt inn, a silver-robed elf appeared before the group with a cryptic summons from [[Fëanon Sërindë|Fëanon]]. [[Cirrus Al Kayan|Cirrus]], pride still smouldering from being left out of whatever secret Fëanon and [[Gundren Rockseeker|Gundren]] shared, rebuffed the messenger — sending him away with instructions to return later. A power play that failed when Fëanon, knowing the stakes, swallowed his pride and accommodated Cirrus's schedule.

When the messenger returned, the group relented. They found themselves teleported to a nondescript lobby in an unknown location — spartan, cold, and smelling faintly of secrets.

## The Mountain

Magical non-disclosure agreements were presented. The group, following Cirrus's lead, refused to sign. The security elf's frustration was palpable. A manager swept in, tore up the signed forms, and made an exception for these "august VIPs." The rules, it seemed, did not apply to the [[Faerûn Defense Force]].

They boarded a funicular and ascended through a hidden tunnel in the mountainside, passing through multiple security wards that triggered different magical screening effects. A twenty-minute journey through the rock built anticipation until they emerged into a bustling underground facility — lab-coated elves from the University of [[Silverymoon]] and grease-stained dwarves from Rockseeker Corporation moving with urgent purpose through a complex that thrummed with energy and the periodic rumble of engine tests.

They were led to a dimly lit briefing room. Told to wait. Again.

## Project STARFALL

Some minutes later, Fëanon and Gundren emerged on the briefing room's stage. The party erupted in questions — all except Cirrus, who attempted to appear disinterested. Fëanon waved them to silence and delivered the briefing.

Magical holocrystals projected images above the conference table. What the party had witnessed at [[Selgaunt]] represented a fundamental shift in warfare. The enemy craft were designated [[VK-31 Frostbite|VK-31 "Frostbite"]] — Amn's newest weapon, capable of manoeuvres that defied conventional engineering. At least seven confirmed strikes on Alliance infrastructure, each with devastating precision.

Gundren took the stage next. He spoke of the partnership between the University and Rockseeker Corporation — formed with a single objective: achieve aerial superiority by any means necessary. Modified Spelljammer technology, discovered in wreckage found in the Moonwood, had provided the breakthrough. His innovations in crystalline matrix manipulation, paired with the University's advances in thaumaturgical field dynamics, had produced something entirely new.

The [[R-14 Thunderchaser]]. A thoroughbred — beautiful, powerful, and occasionally wild.

"That's why you're here," Gundren said.

Fëanon returned to the lectern with a faint, knowing smirk. He pressed a button. A low rumble filled the room as massive blast shutters slid open behind him, revealing a sprawling underground hangar buzzing with activity. Lab-coated elves and oil-stained dwarves swarmed around a flightline of sleek, gleaming R-14 Thunderchasers, their crystalline matrices pulsing faintly as mechanics prepared them for flight.

"Ladies and Gentlemen. Welcome to [[Project STARFALL]]."

## The Tour

The group insisted on getting out onto the flightline immediately. Cirrus, entirely out of his depth, feigned familiarity and expertise while the rest climbed over the jets to the chagrin of the crew chiefs. They peered through windows into sealed rooms where technicians static-tested the engines and test-fired the crafts' gatling-wand cannons — the whole facility shaking with each burst.

Gundren distributed thick flight manuals — reminiscent of a real-world NATOPS manual — and instructed them to study. They were to assemble at 0600 for their first exercise.

## Great Balls of Fire

That evening, the group found the base canteen. [[Jack Mayer Johnson Mathews|JmJm]] needed no invitation. He enraptured the room with a rousing performance of "Great Balls of Fire" — an exaggerated ballad recounting some of his more harrowing battles, delivered with the full force of a bard who had once led entire taverns into riots of applause.

As the night grew late, JmJm stayed in the canteen. Cirrus headed directly to the briefing room and remained there all night, studying. [[Aliyah Faen Tlabbar|Patches]] took the flight manual to heart. In an anxiety-fuelled cram session that spiralled into something closer to a breakdown, she tore every page from the binder and spread them across her barracks room — a blizzard of arcane schematics and crystalline matrix diagrams. By the time sleep took her amid the chaos, she had little hope of reassembling the book in anything resembling its original order.
