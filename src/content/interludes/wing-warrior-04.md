## Morning Briefing

Viper ran them through the day's plan. Every pilot flying an [[R-14 Thunderchaser|R-14]] would visit the techs to be paired with a Modron back-seater — small clockwork creatures of clicking gears and monotone chatter, the fantasy equivalent of an astromech droid. They would then visit their crew chiefs, choose weapon loadouts, and get airborne for a group engagement against Viper, his wingman Jester, and several other instructors flying smaller, more manoeuvrable aircraft designed to simulate the Amnian threat.

[[Jack Mayer Johnson Mathews|JmJm]] would fly with [[Gustavo]] in their two-seater instead.

## The Sortie

[[Fëanon Sërindë|Fëanon]] watched from the control tower as the rest of the squadron went through the routine — starting engines, taxiing through the mountain slot, taking off with mixed proficiency, and forming up at altitude. Once they had established formation, Viper's voice crackled over the Rocky-talkies with a crisp "Good morning, aviators" before swooping in with his OPFOR formation.

The scene devolved into a complete furball. Bogeys everywhere.

JmJm and Gustavo proved the worth of their heavier firepower immediately, downing one of the instructor planes within seconds with blistering, highly accurate fire. The rest turned and burned through the sky in a brutal aerial dogfight, systematically picking off the instructors one by one.

By the time the last OPFOR craft trailed illusory smoke and peeled away, the score was absolute: the entire opposing force eliminated without a single loss. The R-14 Thunderchaser was a true force to be reckoned with.

## The Flat Spin

Victory seemed assured as the squadron formed up for the return to [[MCAS Mythalmar]].

Then JmJm crossed [[Cirrus Al Kayan|Cirrus]]'s jetwash.

One engine stalled. The two-seater pitched violently, caught in the wake turbulence, and entered an uncontrollable flat spin — the craft careening toward the sea in a howling spiral. Both JmJm and Gustavo attempted to eject. In a cruel reversal of the expected script, Gustavo made it out clean. JmJm struck the canopy.

The aircraft hit the water.

## The Rescue

In the sea, Gustavo found his friend. He cradled the severely wounded bard, blood clouding the water around them, and poured what bardic magic he had into stabilising JmJm — just enough to keep him breathing, just enough to hold death at arm's length.

Above them, [[Tarkathee Daru|Tark]] roared overhead on full afterburner in a low pass — a salute, a promise, a monk's way of saying he was watching. The Thunder Drive's shockwave rattled the waves.

Rescue craft converged. JmJm and Gustavo were pulled from the water and returned to the base — the bard battered but alive, his friend shaken but solid. The rest of the squadron circled overhead, their triumph tempered by the knowledge of how quickly these magical marvels could turn on the pilots who flew them.

The R-14 had proven itself in mock combat. It had also proven that the sky does not forgive carelessness.
