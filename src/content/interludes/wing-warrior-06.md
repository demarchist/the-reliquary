## Scramble

Pre-dawn. [[MCAS Mythalmar]] hummed with a different energy — not the experimental buzz of a test facility but the taut, purposeful silence of a base preparing for war. Crew chiefs swarmed the [[r-14-thunderchaser|R-14 Thunderchasers]] under the blue-white glow of arcane work lights, loading live ordnance for the first time. Seeker Spheres racked under wings. Lightning Lances charged to full capacity. The crystalline matrices pulsed with a deeper resonance, as though the machines themselves understood what was coming.

[[Fëanon Sërindë|Fëanon]] stood at the lectern one final time, running the squadron through the mission plan. Ingress low over the Moonwood, then south at altitude across contested territory. The defector's intelligence gave them a corridor between patrol rotations — tight, but workable. [[Aliyah Faen Tlabbar|Patches]]'s tactical overlay identified the factory's key structures: the main foundry, the crystal-growth chambers, the completed [[vk-31-frostbite|Frostbites]] on the flightline. Hit them all, hit them fast, and get out before the enemy could marshal a response.

Fëanon would coordinate from the control tower via long-range Rocky-talkie relay. He could not fly with them. But his voice would be in their ears.

[[Gundren Rockseeker|Gundren]] — Viper — would fly lead.

The squadron walked to the flightline in the grey half-light. [[Jack Mayer Johnson Mathews|JmJm]], still carrying the bruises from the crash, climbed into the two-seater's cockpit with [[Gustavo]] behind him. Neither spoke. Neither needed to.

Engines lit. The Thunderdome's great doors rolled open. One by one, the R-14s taxied through the mountain slot and roared into the pre-dawn sky.

## Contact

They crossed into enemy airspace as the sun broke the horizon. For twenty minutes, nothing — just the hum of Thunder Drives and the vast silence of altitude. Then [[Cirrus Al Kayan|Cirrus]]'s Modron back-seater clicked a warning. Contacts. Multiple. Closing fast from the southeast.

VK-31 Frostbites — a patrol flight, two craft, vectoring to intercept.

Gundren's voice came over the comms, calm and hard. "Weapons free."

The first real engagement between R-14 and VK-31 lasted ninety seconds. [[Tarkathee Daru|Tark]] and Cirrus peeled off to engage while the rest held formation. The Frostbites were fast and manoeuvrable — everything the training simulations had promised — but the R-14 pilots had drilled for this. Tark feinted high, drew his target into a turning fight, and Cirrus swept in from below with a Lightning Lance burst that shattered the enemy craft's crystal matrix. The second Frostbite broke off and ran. Tark let it go. They couldn't afford to chase — the factory was the mission.

But the patrol had transmitted their position. The clock was running.

## The Gauntlet

More contacts appeared as they pressed deeper into enemy territory. A flight of four Frostbites rose from a forward airstrip to cut them off. [[Horatio Von Bumidere|Buumi]] and Patches broke to engage, weaving through a vicious dogfight while the rest of the squadron punched through. Buumi absorbed a glancing hit that sent sparks cascading from his port wing but held his line, his R-14 shuddering but intact. Patches — cockpit still decorated with torn manual pages — fought with the cold precision she brought to everything, putting a Seeker Sphere into an enemy engine intake at impossible range.

They cleared the intercept and reformed. Two more Frostbites were down. But the element of surprise was gone. The factory would know they were coming.

Fëanon's voice crackled through the relay: "Accelerate the timeline. You're two minutes ahead of the scramble response. Use them."

## The Factory

The facility appeared through the mountain haze — a fortified arcane foundry built into a cliff face, its chimneys trailing the acrid smoke of crystal-forging. Defensive emplacements studded the ridgeline. On the factory's flightline, a row of completed Frostbites sat gleaming in the morning sun, ready for delivery to frontline squadrons.

Gundren rolled in first, his R-14 screaming down the valley at full throttle. Lightning Lance fire raked the anti-air positions on the ridge, silencing them in a cascade of shattered stone and sparking ward-crystals. Tark and Cirrus followed, strafing the flightline — completed Frostbites erupted one after another, their crystal matrices detonating in bursts of blue-white fire that sent shockwaves rippling across the tarmac.

Patches put two Seeker Spheres through the crystal-growth chamber's ventilation shafts. The building imploded, its contents — months of irreplaceable work — consumed in a conflagration of raw arcane energy that mushroomed above the ridgeline.

Buumi made a high pass over the main foundry, dropping ordnance through the roof. Secondary explosions rippled through the structure as forging crucibles and stockpiled materials cooked off.

And then JmJm and Gustavo came in for the killing blow.

The two-seater's heavier armament had been designed for exactly this. They roared through the smoke at the heart of the facility and unleashed everything they had into the main production line — the assembly hall where half-built Frostbites hung from arcane gantries and raw Spelljammer crystal was being cut and shaped. The devastation was total. The hall collapsed inward, burying the Amnian aerial programme under a thousand tons of burning rubble.

As they pulled up through the smoke, Gustavo whooped over the comms. JmJm was quieter. He banked the two-seater over the burning wreckage, looked down at what they had done, and keyed the Rocky-talkie.

"Splash one factory."

## Exfiltration

The ride home was not clean. A scramble flight of Frostbites caught them over the border — six aircraft, angry and hunting. The engagement was short, brutal, and decisive. The R-14 pilots fought as a unit for the first time — not trainees competing for points, but a squadron covering each other's wings. Tark and Cirrus flew as a pair, their earlier rivalry dissolved into seamless coordination. Patches and Buumi bracketed a Frostbite between them and brought it down in a crossfire. Gundren flew with the steady hand of an instructor who had been waiting to see his students become pilots.

JmJm crossed behind Cirrus's wing — deliberately this time, close enough to rattle both cockpits. The Tiefling's voice came over the comms, flat and dry:

"You can be my wingman anytime."

JmJm grinned. "Bullshit. You can be mine."

The remaining Frostbites broke off. The R-14s formed up — battered, low on fuel, some trailing smoke — and turned for home.

## Return

[[MCAS Mythalmar]]'s control tower picked them up thirty miles out. By the time they touched down, the entire base had turned out — engineers, researchers, crew chiefs, and support staff lining the taxiway. [[Fëanon Sërindë|Fëanon]] stood at the edge of the flightline, arms crossed, wearing the expression of a man whose students had just exceeded every expectation he had never admitted to having.

Gundren was the last to shut down his engine. He sat in the cockpit for a long moment, looking at the row of R-14s — scarred, scorched, and magnificent — and allowed himself a rare, broad smile.

[[Project STARFALL]] was no longer an experiment. The R-14 Thunderchaser was no longer a prototype. And the [[faerun-defense-force|Faerûn Defense Force]] had just struck a blow that would reshape the course of the war.

That night, the Silver Flagon had never been louder.
