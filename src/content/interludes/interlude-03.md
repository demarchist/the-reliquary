Ares blazes north toward the great storm, his retinue in tow. The Dragonflight gives chase.

The rearguard — the young White Dragons — are set upon first. Ares glances back and bellows at the Adult White Dragon: *"Die for me!"* The beast obeys without hesitation, wheeling and breathing on the foreign dragons in a frenzy, its younglings following suit. The battle is short and brutal. The warriors of Shou Lung tear the smaller dragons apart. The rest of the Dragonflight presses past the melee to pursue Ares.

Ares plunges into the storm. His Efreeti bodyguard is waiting just inside, blazing blade drawn, and attacks in a fury. Numbers tell again — the Efreeti Firelord is overwhelmed, though not before his blade finds **Xuefang**. The Efreeti dies in a great combustion and returns to his home plane.

The dragons that bypassed the Efreeti finally catch Ares.

Despite the slaughter of his entire entourage, the Ancient Red is unperturbed. He laughs and taunts as claws rake his form — but nothing draws blood. The strikes tear into something else entirely: an ethereal armor, a field of force projected outward from the metal exoskeleton, focused through the gemstone in his chestplate. The Ancient is functionally untouchable.

He batters **Vakuun** in return, still laughing.

Within the storm, a great Tornado becomes visible — amber lightning flickering through its walls, the scent of death hanging thick in the air. It turns with a sluggish, deliberate menace.

All eight dragons harry Ares at once. Gouts of flame, acid, poison, lightning, and raw magic rake the ethereal armor while claws and teeth work at its edges. The arrogance in Ares's voice begins to thin. He has miscalculated. Eight adult dragons of Shou Lung are more than he bargained for.

A wave of green poison washes over the armor. The shield shatters.

Ares produces a scroll and works the magic in an instant, just as the first real strikes begin to land on his ancient scales.

He casts Dimension Door. Five hundred feet in any direction — and he is gone.

The Tornado has not moved far. Its amber walls flicker close despite the speed of their flight and the scent of death is stronger now.

Then a roar from the north — unmistakably Ares — and his taunting voice carries back to them across the wind. He wants them to follow.

The Great Bell of Death rings once more.
