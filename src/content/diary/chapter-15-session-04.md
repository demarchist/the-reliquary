## The Death of Lord Brusso

[[tarkathee-daru|Tark]] and [[feanon-serinde|Fëanon]] set out from the [[magnificent-mansion|Magnificent Mansion]] to rejoin the group at [[house-muskov|House Muskov]]. Fëanon still wore his nobleman's guise, and Tark walked half a step behind as his retainer — a presentation the house guards recognised without question. [[dimitri-muskov|Lord Dimitri]] had been expecting visitors.

They were crossing the entrance hall when a scream came from above. Tark was moving before it faded, taking the stairs at speed, Fëanon close behind. They reached the second-floor landing to find the household frozen around the open bedroom doors.

[[brusso-muskov|Lord Brusso Muskov]] was dead.

The old man lay in his bed, his body at rest in a way it had not been the previous day. The rictus was gone — the contorted agony that had locked his limbs had unwound in death, leaving him simply still. The servants who had endured his raving stood motionless, uncertain what to do with the sudden quiet.

Tark and Fëanon found the rest of the group and got a quick account of the morning's events. One of the guards departed immediately to find the Lord's son while the remaining guard and Dimitri attempted — without success — to usher the group from the house. [[jack-mayer-johnson-mathews|JmJm]] had other ideas.

He announced that he could bring the Lord back.

The declaration landed like a stone in still water. JmJm began preparing a Reincarnation ritual — and whatever the household had been expecting, it was not this.

He undressed. He produced oils. He began to work the oils into the corpse with slow, methodical, deeply uncomfortable thoroughness — kneading the dead lord's limbs, massaging the unguents into cooling flesh, murmuring incantations with the unhurried focus of a man who was going to do this properly regardless of who was watching. The servants stared. The guards stared. Dimitri stared — and then stopped staring, and then stared again, unable to fully look away from what was happening to his uncle's body. JmJm, naked and glistening, made eye contact with him over one oiled shoulder and did not break it.

Dimitri took his leave to find his cousin, who would surely know best.

Several servants rushed after him, hoping to locate the Lord's son before the strange bard did anything worse. Those who remained pressed themselves against the walls of the bedchamber, caught between duty and the overwhelming desire to be somewhere — anywhere — else. The ritual showed no signs of approaching its conclusion. JmJm had, if anything, settled into a rhythm.

The group, for their part, searched the room and examined the corpse between JmJm's ministrations, looking for signs of foul play and vampirism. They discovered a number of wards — inactive — and the slight foulness of necromancy lingering on the body, but nothing that screamed *vampire*. JmJm continued, undeterred, through every discovery and every appalled glance.

The ordeal stretched on for the better part of an hour.

## The Young Lord

A commotion rose from the floor below — boots on stone, voices giving orders — and a sizable retinue ascended to the bedchamber.

Dimitri walked just behind a vibrant nobleman roughly his own age. The man sported a shaved head with a combover mohawk and a long black handlebar moustache that would have been absurd on a lesser personality. His longcoat was silk, delicately embroidered and lined with white ermine, and a silver bejewelled sabre hung at his hip. He moved with the easy authority of someone accustomed to being the most important person in any room he entered.

Behind him came two house guards, four [[crimson-dawn-knights|Knights of the Crimson Dawn]], and four city guard. Flanking the nobleman were two figures in dark robes whom [[cirrus-al-kayan|Cirrus]] noted immediately — Warlocks of [[kelemvor|Kelemvor]], the God of the Dead and judge of the afterlife. Not servants of a demon lord or twisted power. Kelemvor embodied the natural process of life and death. Balance.

The young lord surveyed the scene — the dead patriarch, the naked bard straddling the corpse with both hands deep in aromatic oils, the armed foreigners standing around this tableau as though it were a reasonable thing to be happening — and nearly had JmJm seized and thrown from the house on the spot. One of the Warlocks stopped him. The Warlock studied JmJm's work with the detached interest of a professional recognising another professional's craft and confirmed, to the visible disbelief of everyone in the room, that the bard was casting a legitimate Reincarnation spell.

The young lord relented with the expression of a man who had lost all confidence in the universe.

He introduced himself as [[boris-muskov|Boris Muskov]], son of the deceased.

Cirrus and Boris attempted to take measure of one another — lord to lord — while JmJm continued his labours in the background, the wet sounds of the ritual providing a deeply unfortunate soundtrack to what would otherwise have been a dignified exchange. They began sharing information with the careful precision of two men who both suspected they were holding half of a larger picture, and who were both doing their absolute best to pretend the naked man behind them did not exist.

Lord Brusso had been acting strangely for the last two weeks, distant and sickly, doing little beyond running the house. The last major event he had attended was a party the Queen had thrown three weeks ago to mark the start of summer. He had last been seen in public four days ago at the arena, and since then had been bedridden. [[aliyah-faen-tlabbar|Patches]] quietly collected some gambling receipts from the bedside and tucked them away.

The Thayans, Boris confirmed, had been staying at [[house-huron|House Huron]] for weeks at the Queen's invitation. A great floating golden orb had visited House Huron roughly a week ago — Amnian foreigners had descended from it, conducted their business, and departed. The peasants had taken the orb as a great sign, like a second sun.

The Warlocks worked with Tark and Fëanon to examine the corpse in the intervals JmJm permitted, confirming what the group had already found — a vague presence of necromancy, but nothing more specific.

Boris insisted they use a resurrection scroll on his father. Cirrus persuaded him to allow JmJm to finish first — the spirit could always decline to return. Boris agreed, though the look he cast at the still-naked bard suggested he was beginning to question every decision he had made since entering the room.

At last, JmJm completed the ritual.

The spirit of Lord Brusso Muskov was unable to return.

A silence settled over the room, heavier than the one that had preceded it. The Paladins and Warlocks packed the Lord's body into an ornate palanquin to be carried to the Cathedral — holy ground, in case the corpse transformed or the dead man returned in a less welcome form. They took their leave with the body, and the weight of what had happened settled onto Boris Muskov's shoulders like a mantle.

## Gaddock's Shadow

Boris and Dimitri remained with their guards and servants. The group asked the question that had been building since they first set foot in the house: did Boris know anything about a pale monk?

The answer came swiftly. The monk was [[gaddock|Gaddock]]. He had been hired by Lord Brusso on the recommendation of the Thayans a few weeks ago and had been living in the house ever since as a personal bodyguard. Boris noted, with the offhand observation of someone who had grown accustomed to oddity, that the man bore a strange wound — a shard of crystal embedded in his head, horn-like — but the monk seemed to pay it no mind. He was reserved. Strange. But he had lived in the house without incident.

He was away at present on personal business. He would return either that night or the following day.

The group exchanged glances. They explained the situation to Boris — carefully, but completely. Gaddock had almost certainly killed Lord Brusso, or played a role in his transformation and death. The Thayans had placed a vampire in his house, and his father had been dead before anyone knew it.

Boris's expression hardened.

Lord to lord, Cirrus offered an alliance of friendship. Boris accepted without hesitation. His lawyers would reach out to draft an official arrangement.

While the others continued to talk, Tark slipped away upstairs. He climbed to the fourth floor — the corridor still bearing the marks of the previous incursion — and found Gaddock's quarters.

The room was spartan. Every piece of furniture and decoration had been pushed to the walls, leaving the centre of the floor open save for a rug. Tark pulled the rug back. The wooden floor beneath was heavily scratched and clawed — deep gouges radiating from a central point, as though Gaddock had sat here night after night, meditating, and something inside him had fought to get out.

Tark stood over the marks for a long moment. Then he moved into the hallway and began reconstructing the fight that had taken place there — the one between Gaddock and [[mister-luko|Mister Luko]]'s ill-fated heist team. He read the scene step by step.

It had been ridiculously one-sided. One man had been disintegrated. Another had been smashed clean through the floor. Both feats that the human Gaddock — the monk Tark had trained under, the man he had loved like a father — had never been capable of performing. Whatever [[zhengyi|Zhengyi]] had made of him, it was something far beyond what Gaddock had once been.

Patches found him there in the last moments of his analysis, sent up by the others. Tark rejoined the group in silence, mulling over what he had found.

## Leads

The group departed House Muskov and made their way across the city to the [[rising-sun|Rising Sun]]. As they walked, Tark spotted several figures eyeballing him from the crowd — the same flavour of attention as the Kryshchek who had approached before — but no one closed the distance.

The Rising Sun was sleepy in the mid-afternoon, the gambling floor barely occupied. They were ushered past the main room, up to the dining level, and through to the kitchens, where [[mister-luko|Mister Luko]] was conducting business while overseeing preparations for the evening.

Luko handed over their payment without ceremony. But the real currency was information.

The Crown believed [[lord-aimu|Lord Aimu]] had fled east to his estate on the border of the steppe. Luko had it on good authority that the nobleman had doubled back, swapping horses and fleeing in secret into the foothills of the Galena Mountains — specifically, to his wife's old estate. He pulled out a map and marked it for the group: turn off the [[bloodstone-pass|Bloodstone Pass]] at [[ironfang-keep|Ironfang Keep]], head north for a day's travel into [[frostmoore-vale|Frostmoore Vale]].

The group thanked him. Luko offered more work if they wanted it.

Patches had one more lead to follow before they committed to a course of action, and they headed out of the city toward the arena. On the way, a street urchin sidled up to [[feanon-serinde|Fëanon]] and passed along a message from his contacts among the city's rogues: the Amnians were due to return within the next day or so. [[house-huron|House Huron]] was preparing for guests, and the rumour was Amnian.

Fëanon tipped the boy, who vanished into the crowd.

Patches noted the hungry eyes of local thieves and beggars tracking the fabulously well-equipped group through the streets — but anyone who started toward them was quickly discouraged by guild members moving in the periphery, ostensibly on Luko's orders.

## The Arena

They made their way through the bustling market outside the main walls to the arena. Patches greased some palms and made enquiries with the bookies.

She found what she was looking for. [[brusso-muskov|Lord Brusso Muskov]] had been in the private box of [[house-huron|House Huron]] just days ago — seated with [[natasha-huron|Lady Natasha Huron]], the noblewoman [[cirrus-al-kayan|Cirrus]] had charmed at the Rising Sun — shortly before his illness left him bedridden.

Patches reported to the group. The picture was becoming difficult to ignore. House Huron sat at the heart of some manner of conspiracy — the Thayans staying under their roof at the Queen's invitation, the Amnian visitors arriving in a golden orb, Lord Brusso attending the arena in Natasha's company only to fall deathly ill within days, and [[gaddock|Gaddock]] placed in the Muskov household on Thayan recommendation.

As they left the arena district, a town crier's voice cut through the market noise — among the usual announcements, a going-out-of-business sale at a magic shop called [[tazmikella|Tazmikella]]'s Odds and Sundries.

## Tazmikella's Odds and Sundries

Never ones to pass up a magic shop, the group followed the crier's directions to a sleepy corner of the merchant ring. They found a converted house on a sequestered cul-de-sac, its modest frontage sitting across from a much more impressive establishment called Izzy's Stuff.

They pushed through the door into a hodgepodge of random and exquisite antiques — shelves crowded with curios, display cases of contradictory quality, the sort of inventory that suggested either an extraordinary eye or an absolute indifference to curation. No one greeted them. They spread out and browsed.

After a few minutes, an old woman appeared from the back. She was wizened and bowed by time, leaning heavily on an amber walking stick, layered in fine old robes with a colourful silk shawl draped over her bent shoulders. Her hair was a thick mess of grey with a few strands of coppery red still visible. She smiled up at them with a slender, creased face and introduced herself as Taz.

She looked over the group — and their gear — and stood a little straighter, a spark of interest replacing her shopkeeper's pleasantry.

They asked why she was closing. Taz remarked that her competition across the street, run by someone called Izzy, was forcing her out. She seemed mildly annoyed but waved it off — she was old, she said, and ready to retire.

The group asked about a couple of sundries that had caught their eye. The old lady shuffled over to inspect them, and the prices she quoted defied comprehension. For a powerful holy amulet, she asked for two hundred years of time — producing a great hourglass as the apparent receptacle for payment. For another piece, a flat million gold.

The group balked. The old lady appeared to be having the time of her life.

Patches, suspecting something, touched Taz with a holy medallion. Nothing happened. Taz burst out laughing — and winked at Patches, her eyes shifting in that instant to slitted, catlike pupils.

Patches winked back, letting her own eyes shift from the grey of human Nora to the orange of her true drow self. Taz's eyebrows rose, and a low chuckle escaped her.

The group exchanged knowing looks. Whatever Taz was, she was decidedly not human — and the prices in this shop were decidedly not negotiable. Time to check the competition.

They left Taz smiling at their backs as they crossed the cul-de-sac to Izzy's Stuff.
