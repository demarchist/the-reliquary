## The Bone Hunter

[[Jack Mayer-Johnson Mathews|JmJm]] charged in atop [[Steden]] and made it about ten feet before the entire group shouted at him to stop. He reluctantly dismounted. Steden, left to his own devices, began inching toward the Wyvern — hoping to start something — until [[Aliyah Faen Tlabbar|Patches]] looked the Nightmare dead in the eye and told him to knock it off. Steden muttered something and trotted back, head low.

Patches moved in to scout. With dappled light from the vine canopy and her [[Piwafwi]] blending her into the long shadows, she slipped past the resting Wyvern — which shifted and sniffed the air — and circled the titanic Cyclops. The thing's armour and weapons were masterfully crafted from tanned hide and carved bone, each piece the work of its own enormous hands. She crept closer. The Cyclops tilted its head, closed its single eye, and pushed its fingers into the rock.

Tremorsense. Patches froze, then backed away with renewed stealth.

[[Cirrus Al-Kayan|Cirrus]] flew in to attempt diplomacy. He tried several languages — getting no response until Draconic, which produced a frown of disgust. He switched to Netherese, and the behemoth's eyebrow rose in surprise. Borborygmos had lived in this desert since time immemorial, and he spoke the tongue of a dead empire.

Cirrus persuaded the Cyclops to let them have the Wyvern. Borborygmos had been waiting for the creature's death anyway. But once it was dead, he would contest the spoils. Only the strong would leave with anything.

## The Kill

[[Feanon Serinde|Feanon]] prepared a teleportation circle. Patches rigged the vine canopy above with a satchel charge, then crept to the Wyvern and stuck a second bomb — wrapped in a dismantled tar bomb — to the creature's leg. She sprinted away as the group attacked to distract it.

The ancient Wyvern went into a frenzy — claws, jaws, and the massive poison stinger lashing out in every direction. The group took brutal hits but returned them in kind. Patches's first bomb detonated, shaking the cavern. The Wyvern screamed and leapt skyward, just as the second charge blew the vine canopy apart. Cirrus polymorphed a boulder into a young Silver Dragon that lunged at the Wyvern as flaming debris crashed down on its wings.

The creature was pounded to the ground and slain moments later.

Patches rushed in and severed the remaining tendons of its leg. The group rallied around the corpse.

Borborygmos clapped his hands. He stood.

The group sprinted for the teleportation circle while [[Horatio Von Bumidere|Buumi]] and Patches dragged the severed Wyvern leg. The polymorphed dragon seized the carcass as a distraction. Borborygmos roared in dismay, raised his hands and one foot, and smashed them into the ground.

A tidal wave of stone and sand rushed toward the group. They vanished into the circle just in time.

## The Barony

They materialised atop [[Feanon's Tower]], safe. Machmoud — the Bedouin they had rescued from the canyon — fell to his knees sobbing. He received the standard speech about new citizenship, was informed of a monthly Anauroch trader he could wait for, and was gently directed to the Paladins of Helm.

Cirrus sent a messenger back to Borborygmos with a gift and a letter.

[[Gundren Rockseeker|Gundren]] headed directly to the [[Rockseeker Manufactory]] with the Wyvern material. The more he considered the work ahead, the more he wanted to get it perfect. He would need knowledge — possibly from another plane.

Patches meditated in her sanctum and prayed to [[Eilistraee]]. She sought an edge against House [[House Faen Tlabbar|Faen Tlabbar]] — knowledge of their ancestry and the Jun Sahara of Al Askar, the uncursed Drow. The goddess told her to go to [[Svartalfheim]], and gave her a name: Yggward.

[[Tarkathee Daru|Tark]] walked to Shadowmoon village to visit the orcs. His instincts were gnawing at him — something dark was approaching. The orcs had dealt with a goblin bat-rider raid the night before, slaughtering the attackers expertly. [[Bolg]], the Mountain Giant, sat by a bonfire sharpening a gargantuan gladius — a custom order from the Rockseekers.

Tark asked about the raid. Bolg described two dozen bat-riders from the south, shooting bows and dropping alchemist's fire. The orcs had shot them from the sky. Bolg had cleaned up the survivors.

But Bolg had something else. He had been sleeping badly. The night before the raid, he had dreamed of a storm giant being flayed and twisted — tortured. Something bad was coming.

Tark agreed. He headed to The Establishment to tell his friends.

## Dokkar

The group gathered beneath [[The Establishment]]. Patches made her case for a quick trip — less than twenty-four hours — to the city of [[Dokkar]] in [[Svartalfheim]]. She needed to see [[Finn]], her brother, whom she had hidden there. She needed to know he was safe.

Cirrus wished to go as well — his body was deteriorating, and he had been told to seek the Towers of Twilight. Gundren needed information on working Wyvern bone. Only Tark was reluctant, reiterating his sense that something bad was imminent.

They asked Feanon to maintain contact with the Material Plane in case of emergency. The wizard assured them he could.

From a private room in The Establishment, Cirrus planeshifted them to the Dark Elf city of Dokkar. They appeared in the main plaza before the city gates — marble floors threaded with silver, a platinum fountain of dancing elves at the centre. Great obsidian walls gleamed in the pale moonlight that filtered through the titanic cavern's ceiling. Motes of light drifted down like soft snow.

The city was far busier than on previous visits. Traders, craftsmen, and caravaners were organising for some massive undertaking. But the group was tired from the long day in the desert. They found an inn, and slept.
