## The Steam Caves

The massive corpse rose to its feet, visible through the thick steam.

The corpse shuddered and ripped apart, the arms splitting from mid hand all the way up to the joint as the possessing creature violated the massive body.

JmJm rushed forward to engage the vile thing, followed quickly by Patches and Tark.

The massive thing took brutal hits from the group, seeming not to care as its body was damaged. In response, it swept JmJm and Patches up in its tentacle-like arms, crushing them.

Doc B summoned a Moonbeam on the thing while Fey hurled firebolt after bolt into the corpse and [[cirrus-al-kayan|Cirrus]] summoned the glittering armor of the Intellect Fortress.

The creature was quickly ripped apart, but not before swallowing JmJm.

As the massive creature had its head blown off by Eldritch Blasts, another great form rose and lurched out of the steam.

Another of the creatures attacked.

JmJm crawled out of the first monster, now destroyed, just in time to see Tark swallowed by the second massive undead thing.

[[gundren-rockseeker|Gundren]] surveyed the battlefield and noted a small glowing Jellyfish rise from the first corpse. He fired on it but failed to kill it.

Like the first creature, the second proved dangerous and brutal, but the group destroyed it relatively quickly. Tark even burst from its rotten stomach in a deluge of foul liquid.

As the second creature died, another Jellyfish rose out of the corpse. JmJm hacked away at it and it disintegrated to ash.

The other Jelly fled the area and the group did not pursue.

Looking around the battlefield and inspecting the bodies, the group discovered a few things of note.

Patches harvested some good cooking salt from one of the vents.

Doc B found a massive 10lb salt crystal down a side area and returned to the group with it.

Fey, JmJm and [[cirrus-al-kayan|Cirrus]] all looked over the bodies, recognizing them as a subspecies of Froghemoth and the Jellies as a type of Demon.

Deciding to get some rest, the group headed down a side tunnel to find a good spot to set up Cirrus' Tiny Hut.

They picked an alcove, set up the Hut and then Doc B molded and shifted the earth to cover the dome, leaving only a long crack for ventilation and a very thin section to act as a way to escape.

The group settled in for a secure night's rest.

## The Rock Covered Hut

Inside the walled off hut, Patches began to discuss entering [[menzoberranzan|Menzoberranzan]], recounting the rules of Lolth.

Worship of any entity other than Lolth was banned on pain of death. Refusing an order from a priestess resulted in punishment up to and including death. Slaves had no legal protection. Pretending to be a Drow when you were not, or a specific Drow when you were not, would result in death. If there were no witnesses to a crime, no crime was committed.

As Patches told an attentive Tark, some of the others were discussing their Drow disguises for entering the city. They stopped pretty quickly when it was pointed out that if that was discovered it meant death.

During the discussion, the group realized that they didn't even know if Finn was in [[menzoberranzan|Menzoberranzan]] and decided to scry on him.

Not having it prepared, Fey turned to Doc to cast the spell.

Doc could cast it but had no focus to do so; he said he needed a crystal worth at least 1000g.

Patches sighed and took out the large chunk of Amethyst she picked up in the elemental Geode and handed it over to Doc.

Doc proceeded to scry on Finn, getting a vision of a Drow fortress: carved spires of rock glowing with arcane fires and a nimbus of energy. The vision cut off as it tried to enter the compound, hitting the wards.

Instead of heading directly to the Drow City, Patches mentioned heading to [[gracksuluth|Gracksuluth]] first to try and secure travel and status documents from her underground contacts there.

[[cirrus-al-kayan|Cirrus]] agreed, as he too had business in [[gracksuluth|Gracksuluth]] with another Warlock of [[ceregorach|Ceregorach]].

The group decided to head across the [[darklake|Darklake]] to the Duergar city.

[[gundren-rockseeker|Gundren]] finished up the jeweled bowl for Doc B and handed it to the little Gnome.

The group enjoyed a meal of the Bluecap bread and rations and settled in to sleep.

[[cirrus-al-kayan|Cirrus]], feeling rejected and far from his goddess, began to indulge far too heavily in his smoking and eating Mayonnaise. He vomited and passed out.

Seeing his companion unconscious, [[gundren-rockseeker|Gundren]] moved in to keep working on his project. Propping the Warlock up, he took the final measurements of the Tiefling's gums and mouth.

[[gundren-rockseeker|Gundren]] fitted [[cirrus-al-kayan|Cirrus]] with a set of silver dentures, nailing them into place into the bone. Stepping back and admiring the handiwork, [[gundren-rockseeker|Gundren]] nodded in satisfaction and headed to bed.

Fey stayed up, staring at the Tiny Hut and thinking he could do better. He finished the last few equations of his new spell and then settled in for his Reverie.

The night was uneventful other than another round of nightmares of dark things chasing and consuming the group in the depths.

Waking up in the morning, [[cirrus-al-kayan|Cirrus]] yelled at the group excitedly.

He thought that the silver teeth were a blessing from his goddess and the rest of the group went along with it, not wanting to burst his bubble.

[[cirrus-al-kayan|Cirrus]] tried to eat some solid food for the first time in a while and bit his tongue pretty badly, not used to having teeth.

With the silver eyes and teeth, the warlock was looking quite freaky.

JmJm woke and rose from his silken bed roll, completely naked.

Patches rolled her eyes and cast darkness on the Bard, obscuring him completely and filling the tiny hut.

She relented and removed the Darkness once JmJm sighed and covered himself.

The group packed up and went to leave, needing to crack through the stone crust enacted the night before. Doc B pointed out the spot to try and crack through and Tark leapt at it, smashing through the wall.

It hurt. Tark landed on the other side, holding his bruised shoulder and looking back at the cracked rock wall, and the thin section just next to where he pushed through.

The group headed out, returning to the Steam Caverns and heading on, down the tunnel Patches recognized the day before that led to a Drow made bridge across a chasm.

## The Severed Bridge & the Sphere

Travelling to the bridge and chasm was relatively quick and easy and they made it there within three uneventful hours.

The tunnel widened and turned, the wall simply disappearing into darkness as the tunnel opened to the chasm.

Walking along the ledge, they arrived at the location of the bridge and noted that it had been destroyed.

Great braids of spider silk were attached to the wall there, but they ended after a few feet, burned away.

[[cirrus-al-kayan|Cirrus]] sent his owl out to see how far it was to the other side. The little bird quickly discovered that it was about 200ft across the abyss.

Not wanting to mess around, the group petitioned Doc B to Wind Walk them for the day and the Cat-Gnome agreed, puffing on his pipe and exhaling the swirling cloud that scooped them up in its wake.

The group rushed across the chasm, past a waiting Roper — a stalactite-looking monstrosity — and through the next tunnel.

Travel was much faster and safer, but nowhere near as quick as above ground as the group still needed to stop and discern their path.

Many hours and many miles later, the group was still travelling when they were all knocked from the spell, a surge of wild magic disrupting the Druidic casting.

Looking around for the source of the disturbance, the group continued on with Patches leading.

They found the source not long after: a massive black sphere taking up the majority of a cavern with the bones of hundreds of creatures beneath it.

Seeing the bones as a clear warning, no one touched the thing but Fey, [[cirrus-al-kayan|Cirrus]] and JmJm all moved in to inspect it while [[gundren-rockseeker|Gundren]] inspected the bones.

They quickly discerned the sphere to be a chunk of the Negative Energy plane; pure death.

Hearing about planes again, Tark enacted the magic of his blindfold and immediately noted the planar cracks around the sphere, the gold glowing fissures seemingly focused on this spot.

[[gundren-rockseeker|Gundren]] meanwhile saw something shiny among the bones and recovered it, making sure not to touch the sphere. He stood up, holding an Ivory figurine depicting a Winged Owlbear.

[[gundren-rockseeker|Gundren]] could feel the magic in the thing and thought he knew exactly what it was: a figurine of Wondrous power. He stashed it for later.

Inspecting the cavern, the group realized they wanted to be on the other side of the sphere and began to look for a way around. They found a section that provided a small 2ft gap between the sphere of death and the wall of the cavern.

The group asked Doc nicely to move the earth away and he did so, creating a much larger opening for the group to easily move through.

They continued on.

## The Fantastic Fortress and the Mind Flayer

Despite having Wind Walked for most of the day, the group decided to begin looking for a place to rest.

The group was beginning to see more flora and fauna: glowing worms, mosses and mushrooms.

As they discussed it, the talking got a little loud and everyone was shocked into silence when they heard something up ahead.

Fey'Anon turned to the rock wall and cast his new spell: Fey'Anon's Magnificent Mansion, an elegant silver door appearing in the rock.

Before anyone could go inside, Fey stopped them and talked about the Bags of Holding and the issues bringing them inside may cause; as such the door had a deposit area next to it for extra planar bags.

The group was not too happy about having to leave their stuff outside, even with a shaped earth pocket to leave their things in.

[[cirrus-al-kayan|Cirrus]] enacted a Tiny Hut around the entrance as an extra precaution.

Tark and Patches both decided to stay outside with [[cirrus-al-kayan|Cirrus]] to keep guard.

Everyone else headed into the Magnificent Mansion and were astounded by its majesty. Much like the mansions and grand structures of [[silverymoon|Silverymoon]], the interior was all sweeping marble, rich hardwoods and silver filigree, the high-elven architecture evident. As they looked in awe, Fey mentioned that he built it after the fashion of Evermeet, his childhood home.

The group inside began to get pampered by unseen servants and enjoyed the massive banquet prepared for them inside.

Meanwhile, those outside had no idea what they were missing.

Patches, Tark and [[cirrus-al-kayan|Cirrus]] ate a little and then bedded down in silence, watching and keeping guard.

An hour went by with Tark and Patches taking the first rest while [[cirrus-al-kayan|Cirrus]] kept watch.

Just as Tark was about to go to sleep, he saw a strange spot on the stone outside the dome. Peering at it, it suddenly moved, shifting into the stone. He got a good enough look at it though: a Mindflayer.

Tark bolted up and let Patches and [[cirrus-al-kayan|Cirrus]] know and then rushed into the Mansion to let the others know.

Bursting into the mansion, Tark took a moment to be shocked at how opulent it was, and then yelled out that a Mindflayer had been watching them in the Dome and he sensed more of them waiting out there.

The group discussed what to do but ended up deciding that between the Tiny Hut and the Magnificent Mansion, they were fine and the Mind Flayers would go away.

Patches came in at that point as well and spotted a naked JmJm waltzing around with a crystal glass of wine. She cast darkness on him and smiled as she heard him trip and drop the glass.

Tark and Patches headed back out to the dome and [[cirrus-al-kayan|Cirrus]] told them nothing else had happened.

They settled in to rest.

Later that night, [[cirrus-al-kayan|Cirrus]] was thinking about his goddess and blinked. As his eyes re-opened, the dome was gone, dispelled, and a wave of psionic energy washed over him. He shrugged it off and turned to see a Mindflayer not 5ft from him.

[[cirrus-al-kayan|Cirrus]] yelled.

Patches sprang forward, weapons materializing in her hands, and went to strike the tentacled intruder.

As her weapons reached it, the Mindflayer called out into their minds "Wait!" and then enacted a Planeshift. It exploded into giblets, showering the area in purple blood and gore.

Patches stood there shocked, and covered in filth.

Tark went invisible, standing next to the Bags of Holding while the other two headed into the Mansion.

[[cirrus-al-kayan|Cirrus]] exclaimed his wonder at the opulent mansion as he and Patches saw Fey float down from a balcony.

Fey looked at the dirty Drow unappreciatively but Doc rushed over with a towel and began to help her clean up.

[[cirrus-al-kayan|Cirrus]] and Patches told the others what happened and Fey was shocked: at the Mindflayer exploding and seemingly wanting to talk?

Concerned but still feeling quite secure in the mansion, the group decided to worry about it all in the morning.

[[cirrus-al-kayan|Cirrus]] and Patches headed back outside and re-established another Tiny Hut.

They remained on edge the rest of the night, looking out into the darkness for more Mind Flayers.
