## The Tower

[[Feanon Serinde|Feanon]] had things to do that could not wait. He ascended to the higher levels of [[Feanon's Tower|his tower]] and found Sinya Maya doing menial tasks in the lab. He took the time to interview her properly. Her academic resume was impressive, but she had never spent more than five years in any position — a red flag for even a young elf. Still, he needed students and tuition. He accepted her provisionally.

As he left the lab, a message caught his eye on the crystal data slate taken from the [[Machina Factory]] in Al Askar. In perfect Faerûnian Common, it read: *This planet is wonderful...*

The last person Feanon had spoken with through that tablet was [[Lady in White|the Lady in White]] — one of [[Vecna]]'s most powerful mages. As far as he knew, she was dead.

A mystery for later. He found [[Theodemir]] waiting in the conservatory. The old archwizard — still inhabiting the clone body, wearing Fëanon's face — spoke of wanting his own form restored, not another of his student's clones. Now that immediate death was no longer a pressing concern, he had begun to think seriously about the matter. Feanon scoffed at the idea that anyone would willingly give up a body for the purpose, but admitted that getting Theodemir back to full strength would be excellent for the tower.

He looked in on [[Mordenkainen]], [[Megno Slatesunder|Megno]], [[Beryan]], and the apprentices engaged in research, decided to leave them be, and scried on the rest of the group in [[Luskan]].

He saw them engaged in furious battle in a dark alley, beset by Drow.

The wizard leapt to his feet and rushed into town to find [[Tarkathee Daru|Tark]].

## The Quiet Before

Hours earlier, Tark had gone into the forest to quiet his mind. He was heading east to vanquish the great villain in his life. He focused on a stump and smashed it apart in a flurry of blows too fast to follow. Then he turned and leapt at a boulder, his prosthesis glowing red, and launched the great stone twenty feet through the forest.

With the sun long set, Tark headed to the town centre to meet his friends. He found [[Horatio Von Bumidere|Buumi]] waiting there, packed and ready — but no one else. Everyone had taken a day trip to Luskan to find out who had attacked Patches, but the paladin had stayed behind to begin the journey east with Tark.

Feanon arrived at speed on his spectral steed and told them the others were under attack. He teleported the three of them to Luskan.

## The Rogue

In the alley, [[Aliyah Faen Tlabbar|Patches]] hogtied the surviving Drow and stuffed him into her [[Bag of Holding]]. [[Gundren Rockseeker|Gundren]] ransacked the corpses — excellent gear, all bearing the insignia of [[House Faen Tlabbar]].

As they turned to re-enter One Eyed Jacks, a familiar human man barred their way: Barrabus the Grey, an associate of [[Jarlaxle]] they had last seen in [[Menzoberranzan]] before the showdown with the Demon Lords.

He remarked that the fight was a good one, but the other Ships would take an interest. He knew they wanted Morik the Rogue and could lead them through the streets to his ship, *The Moniker*.

Gundren, watching from the sky astride [[Arkto]], rockied down that he thought they should move. Barrabus made a hand gesture to Patches in Thieves' Cant, scaled a nearby building almost as fast as she would have, and vanished.

Patches led the group through the dark alleys, following Thieves' Cant sigils left by Barrabus. Gundren spotted several armed groups shadowing them and conducted fly-overs, dropping Grease into the alleyways. The pursuers panicked and fell flat. The group picked up the pace.

They ducked under a wharf at low tide and found a rowboat manned by Drow, Barrabus waiting at the prow. The group knew this was likely a trap — but questioned the captive Drow from Patches' bag first. He refused all interrogation, willing to die rather than speak, and taunted Patches about her mother flaying her skin into an apostate tapestry.

Patches summoned [[Eclipse]] and proclaimed the name of her new house, calling on the power of [[Eilistraee]]. The warrior cowered and fell silent.

They took the boat. It glided across the dark harbour to a large three-masted vessel with an oversized rear quarters — beautiful stained glass windows glittering on the balcony of the captain's cabin.

## The Ambush

Inside, a lavish feast was laid on a table with a cloth hanging to the floor. An ancient human man lounged on a throne at the far end — wispy white hair, pale thin skin, liver spots contrasting with ornate garb. He leaned on a cane and welcomed the [[Faerûn Defense Force]], gesturing to the food.

Feanon felt uneasy. The dimensions of the room were wrong — smaller than they should be.

The group did not sit. Patches demanded to know who had put the price on her head.

A flowery laugh erupted. A familiar voice called: *Why, I did, my dear!*

The walls vanished, revealing five feet of hidden space on three sides, harbouring over a dozen figures. Morik slumped back in his chair — a man whose part in this drama was done.

[[Jarlaxle]] stood in the centre of a dozen Drow warriors, crossbows trained on the group, wand in hand. He was flanked by two Drow that immediately stood out.

One was clearly a wizard — adamantine staff tipped with a crystal spider, black robes so dark they hurt to look at, body shining like warded metal, outline blurring with mirror images.

The other was a short female in full adamantine plate armour covered in web filigree and spider motifs. Her silver hair cascaded in long braids. Her head was backlit by a dark halo and her eyes blazed with purple fire as she strode forward, pointing at Patches with a writhing snake-headed whip.

*I, Ishta of House Faen Tlabbar, will see you dead, Apostate! Die, Sister!*

A Silence spell rendered the room eerily quiet.

Patches dived under the table — and found not legs but six large barrels of smokepowder, one with a glowing ruby embedded in its side. She scrambled out and sprinted for the back window, signalling her friends to retreat.

She dived through the stained glass just in time to see Morik pull out a small ruby and mouth: *I fucking hate Drow*. Then he locked eyes with Jarlaxle.

Jarlaxle winked at her.

A flurry of counterspells neutralised the first volley of magic. [[Jack Mayer-Johnson Mathews|JmJm]] dived backward through the stained glass, discharged his rings in a lightning bolt that tore through the front ranks of Drow, and landed on [[Arkto]]. [[Cirrus Al-Kayan|Cirrus]] leapt from the balcony and hurled a fireball back into the cabin.

The smokepowder detonated. The entire rear half of the ship was obliterated, chaining off the main stores. The vessel was blown to splinters. The group was hurled into the harbour.

They pulled themselves from the water and agreed they had outstayed their welcome. Cirrus opened a demiplane door in the harbour wall. Everyone stepped through.

The group was furious at Jarlaxle. Patches, far calmer than the rest, explained. Jarlaxle was a businessman operating from [[Menzoberranzan]]. He likely had to take the job from Faen Tlabbar. The explosion, Morik holding the detonator, and the wink — it was all part of a larger scheme. Her enemies would think her dead, or at least have a very hard time following.

They rested in the demiplane overnight.

## The Bloodstone Lands

In the morning, Cirrus Planeshifted the group to the Material Plane — directly to the outlying farms of Helioglabulus, the capital of [[Damara]].

Tark looked upon his homeland for the first time in almost seven years.

The capital sprawled some ten miles across flat grasslands from atop the gently sloping hills of the Damaran Steppe. Concentric city walls of dark stone were threaded through with gleaming scarlet bloodstone. At the centre, a classic Eastern Orthodox castle of glittering ore rose with turrets capped by teardrop swirls of colourful architecture. A great domed structure bristling with steeples and spires dominated another quarter — the Cathedral of [[Lathander]].

Farmland stretched in every direction. Carts and wagons bustled along the roads. To the north, then wrapping west and south out of sight, a vast snow-capped mountain range dominated the horizon.

The group worked alongside farm hands for a few hours to get the lay of the land. Much had changed. The Warlock Knights of [[Vaasa]] had made peace with Damara. They had merged with the Paladins of Lathander to found the Order of the Crimson Dawn. King Yarin Frostmantle and Queen Concetinna ruled jointly, commanding the new order under the banner *For the Greater Good*. The [[Red Wizards of Thay]] had an embassy in Helioglabulus and were trading openly.

The group settled in for lunch and began to discuss their next move.

Tark felt cold. A subtle heartbeat scratched at the back of his mind. [[Li Xian]]'s dagger — the knife that had killed his family — thrummed in the [[Bag of Holding]], pulling him north.
