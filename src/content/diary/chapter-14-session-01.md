## High Sun

The group found themselves in the middle of the town square on a hot summer's afternoon. Festival decorations for High Sun — the summer festival of [[Lathander]] — hung from every eave and post, giving them their first indication of how long they had been gone.

Wounded, bloody, and exhausted, most of them shuffled into [[The Establishment]]. Calls went up from the townsfolk — *They've returned!* — and word began to spread through the streets that the [[Faerûn Defense Force]] was back.

[[Jack Mayer-Johnson Mathews|JmJm]] split from the group without a word, trotting [[Steden]] across the square to his theatre and throwing open the ornate front doors with a boom and a cawing of ravens. Inside, he found local actors mid-rehearsal. He screamed at them to get out. All scrambled for the exits — save one, who was unceremoniously launched into the seats by the Arcane Hand. JmJm slumped into his throne at centre stage and began to think. Then to write.

## The Gambling Den

[[Cirrus Al-Kayan|Cirrus]] remained in the square to address the gathering crowds. Everyone else headed for a drink, but when they noticed [[Snorbul]] was barely breathing, they relocated from the bar to the dark gambling den beneath The Establishment.

The smoke-filled room was packed with Svirfneblin traders from [[Blingdenstone]], enjoying a raucous game. [[Aliyah Faen Tlabbar|Patches]] told them to leave. Recognising the war-weary drow, they scooped up their winnings and scattered — leaving a generous ruby as a gift for their gracious host.

[[Mr. Pickles|Pickles]] was dispatched to alert [[Magistrate Li]] of the group's return. [[Tarkathee Daru|Tark]] gave the goblin a bone knife from Al Askar as thanks, which Pickles accepted while poking curiously at the monk's missing hand. [[Horatio Von Bumidere|Buumi]] dug through his pack and produced the prosthetic [[Gundren Rockseeker|Gundren]] had crafted — oversized for a halfling, but functional.

[[Claudine]] arrived and worked with [[Doc|Doctor Breakfast]] to stabilise Snorbul. The battered gnome woke, still missing limbs, but alive. Food and drink arrived courtesy of a new bartender — a human named Jackie, who mentioned the Barony was experiencing a culinary boom from the influx of refugees and exotic meats brought in from the Evermoors by [[Bolg]] and the orcs. Giant Eel pie, courtesy of Ivan the baker, proved surprisingly good.

Outside, JmJm stormed from his throne to the theatre's front doors and flung them wide. Ravens scattered. He nailed a huge roll of parchment to the door and stalked back inside. The crowd surged forward to read it — a cast list for his new masterpiece.

## The Briefing

[[Magistrate Li]] descended into the gambling den — a place she had never set foot — and delivered her report with a polite cough at the permanent haze of smoke.

Four months. That was how long the group had been gone.

In that time, the Barony had doubled in size from war refugees, including a contingent of High Elves from Myth Drannor fleeing the Netherese. The war itself had shifted — Scornubel had fallen to some unrelated cataclysm, and the new front lay in the Heartlands around Elturel as northern forces were pushed toward the coast. The Amnians and Thayans were doing well.

The Barony had received 150 parcels of new land from [[Silverymoon]]. Gundren's dwarves at the [[Rockseeker Manufactory]] had been filling orders for extradimensional latrines — less disease, less digging — and producing two firearms a month. Several intricate metallic boxes of unknown origin had appeared around the Barony. No one could figure out what they were.

The group thanked Li, and she returned to the [[Keep of Thunder Bluff|Keep]].

## Separate Ways

Night fell and the group scattered to their homes.

Gundren took his brothers and Buumi out drinking in the auto-carriage. Snorbul was carried home to his hole by Doc, who then retired to his grove. Tark headed for his treehouse, grateful for familiar stars. [[Feanon Serinde|Feanon]]'s carriage arrived with his acolytes — his nephew and the mage [[Ruthir]] — and he departed for [[Feanon's Tower|his tower]].

Patches, now alone, descended deeper into the maze of tunnels beneath The Establishment, heading for her sanctum. Three secret doors in, a familiar face waited for her — [[Lo'Dan]], the [[Bregan D'Aerthe]] agent.

The drow greeted her with outstretched empty hands and a cocky grin. He was here on a job from [[Jarlaxle]] himself: spy on Patches, and find [[Finn]] for her mother, [[Vladalma]].

Patches resisted the urge to gut him. Lo'Dan clearly felt the tension, and explained — Jarlaxle could not refuse a commission from a Matron Mother, but he was offering the information up front as a professional courtesy.

Patches told him to get out. He smirked, and left.

She retired to her sanctum to pray, to rest, and to begin plotting her mother's death.

## The Cursed Tome

Feanon arrived at his tower as the sun was setting. Giuseppe was at the front desk, buried in unopened mail and packages, and casually mentioned that he and Uira would be leaving soon — the teleportation circle work was finished.

In his chambers, Feanon found that his cloning vat orders from the Manufactory had been fulfilled. Among the packages lay a large leather-bound spellbook. He began to read it — elementary, already known — but found himself unable to stop. Hours vanished without his noticing.

When he finally closed the book, a wave of crushing exhaustion washed over him. He turned back. The tome had vanished.

JmJm arrived at the tower that evening, having convinced Doc to conjure a summer thunderstorm for dramatic effect. The bard had bypassed the wards by riding Steden through the [[Ethereal Plane]] — and noted, in passing, a baleful singularity of energy emanating from Feanon's laboratory.

They retired to the reading room to discuss the Netherese, but JmJm's mention of cursed tomes jogged an old memory — stories of the Witch King [[Zhengyi]], who had once sent enchanted books to unsuspecting wizards, trapping and consuming them. The tomes killed their readers slowly, growing structures from their stolen life-force.

Feanon went very still.

## The Monks

The next morning, Patches arrived at Buumi's door with a proposition. She needed him to take care of Finn if anything happened to her — and she would do the same for [[Cercei]]. They had made this promise before, but Patches wanted it binding. In the eyes of their noble houses and their gods. She proposed marriage.

Buumi was deeply hungover and profoundly startled. He thanked her and asked if he could think about it when he was in a proper state of mind.

Meanwhile, Tark walked through the forest toward his dojo. [[Magistrate Li|Li]] had mentioned that someone was teaching *Tark Kwon Do* there, which meant they were either very good or very foolish.

A flash of light blinded him. A small blonde woman appeared beside him and punched him in the face. As he hit the ground, a blue dragonborn dropped from the trees above. Tark threw Darkness over all of them and fought blind — stunning the dragonborn and trading blows with the woman until both attackers called a halt.

They were Minnow and Goro, disciples of Renfir — an old friend of [[Gaddock]]'s — sent from the Bloodstone Lands after hearing tales of the Shadow Monk. They had feared Gaddock's student had turned to darkness, as his master now had.

The stories they brought were grim. Nobles and wizards across the Bloodstone Lands — anyone standing against Zhengyi — were being slaughtered in the night. Witnesses described Tark's old master: emaciated, red-eyed, pale, vanishing into the mists. King Frostmantle of [[Vaasa]] had signed non-aggression treaties with the Lich and his Thayan allies.

Tark listened in silence, and then went to tell his friends.
