## The Rift Above the Sea

As the ship neared the island fortress and its moored prize, [[Cirrus Al-Kayan|Cirrus]] stepped off the railing and summoned his wings. A flash of lightning — a wild-magic surge — and reality tore open above the ship, a portal to the [[Shadowfell]] gaping in the sky.

Four things fell through before the rift sealed itself.

Cirrus lost focus and hit the water as one of the creatures splashed down nearby. The other three landed on deck — demons of shadow and corruption that surged forward immediately. One tore an unfortunate crewman in half.

The group sprang into action. [[Feanon Serinde|Feanon]] hit one with a disintegrate that failed to kill it outright — these fiends were powerful. Cirrus erupted from the water on black wings and summoned a celestial to fight the shadow creatures. [[Tarkathee Daru|Tark]], [[Snorbul]], and [[Horatio Von Bumidere|Buumi]] threw themselves into melee, keeping the crew from harm as pirates fled below decks. [[Drizzly Dawg]] guarded the stairs, hurling knives with lethal precision.

One demon erupted in a fountain of oozing corruption. Another slashed Tark badly — but Cirrus, hovering above, cast one of the most powerful healing spells anyone aboard had ever witnessed, curing Tark and Snorbul of everything ailing them.

The last fiend dived overboard, trying to escape into the wider world. Cirrus sent his celestial after it, and flashes from the depths confirmed the kill. He spun the encounter as a random event, and the crew mostly bought the story. The group burned and cleansed the corruption before it could spread.

## The Lord and Lady

In the aftermath, no one had noticed the rowboat approaching. Two human women in red robes called out, asking if everything was all right. After a brief, diplomatic exchange, the group found themselves — and any crew who wished — invited to the island the next morning for breakfast with the Lord and Lady of the isle: Nehi and Takhat.

They rowed ashore the next morning to find a welcome procession on the beach: a dozen robed acolytes holding torches, platters, and instruments. A palanquin descended the stone stairs, and the party got their first look at their hosts.

Feanon identified them as vampires instantly.

They were pale, but lounging in indirect sunlight beneath overcast skies. Their acolytes had a drugged, enthralled quality — happy enough on their idyllic island, but clearly not themselves. The fortress above was ancient, its proportions built for beings much larger than humans. [[Liara]] wondered if it was Titanic in origin.

The group accepted the invitation to dine, reasoning they could learn more over food. In the grand hall — stone walls, an open oculus to the grey sky — they ate well on seafood while subtly warning their hosts that they were not prey and not to be trifled with.

The vampires, in their arrogance, simply laughed. They did not realize the group saw through them. Both Nehi and Takhat began to touch their guests, trying to charm them with conversation and actual charm spells. Small static shocks accompanied each contact, drawing the victims closer, making them want to stay and eat and talk.

Buumi and Tark shared a look. If these were undead monstrosities with a cult of enthralled victims, those people needed saving.

But for now, they were guests. And tensions, while high, had not yet broken into violence.
