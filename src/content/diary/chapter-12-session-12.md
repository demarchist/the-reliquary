## The Drinking Contest

The group challenged [[Drizzly Dawg]] for free accommodations. The captain returned with a confident grin and a jug of milky white liquor. [[Tarkathee Daru|Tark]] was pushed forward as their champion.

[[Horatio Von Bumidere|Buumi]] riled the crew into a cheering crowd as the two settled on a barrel amidships. Shot after shot went down. Tark made faces — at the flavour, not the alcohol. As the captain grew increasingly affected, he squinted at the monk: *"Are you resistant?"*

Buumi grew immediately uncomfortable. Tark was indeed immune to the effects of alcohol. The paladin clapped Drizzly on the shoulder, hiding the small flash of divine light as he cleansed the liquor from the captain's system. Thinking he'd caught a second wind, Drizzly returned with vigour — only to face an opponent who was physically incapable of intoxication. He eventually toppled from his stool. Tark caught him and eased him into the arms of [[Naima]], the Water Genasi second mate — short-cropped green hair, pale blue skin, a thin scar across her jawline.

## Dark Studies and Darker Beds

Below decks, [[Feanon Serinde|Feanon]] found a secluded spot and unfolded the [[Tome of Al Azif]]. As he read, writing appeared in the book — threatening him, enticing him with undeath. The wizard resisted the entity's pull and extracted a new spell from its pages. [[Snorbul]], watching from his hammock, shivered but didn't interfere.

[[Cirrus Al-Kayan|Cirrus]] badgered his way into the captain's quarters, fastepping into the bed whenever [[Naima]] or the groggy Drizzly tried to remove him. In exasperation, Drizzly pulled something from his ear and tossed it beneath the bed — a portable hole that dumped both bed and warlock out the stern and into the ocean. Cirrus fastepped back to the deck, prestidigitated himself dry, and the crew stared wide-eyed, muttering about Ifrits.

Cirrus spent the rest of the night preaching about [[Selune]] and the moon to the more receptive crew members.

Drizzly, impressed by Feanon's power, asked the wizard to fabricate a new bed. In exchange, he offered a bottle of Elven Wine from the Lost Isle. Feanon shaped the bed seamlessly from the deck itself — intricate ceramic filigree formed from raw materials. The captain gasped. This kind of magic was unheard of outside legends. He asked who they really were. Feanon, realizing he had revealed too much, took his leave without answering.

## Sea Battles and Storms

The voyage was brutally hot. Both suns turned the ship into an oven. The group helped where they could — Snorbul hauling lines, [[Gundren Rockseeker|Gundren]] tying knots, Tark coiling ropes, Cirrus offering enthusiastic encouragement about the moon.

Six massive turtles struck the hull, splintering wood. Cirrus banished two. Buumi hauled one aboard in a fishing net, then agonized over killing it as it mewled on its back — it took far too long. Tark manned a deck cannon, a larger version of the [[Saurian Firelance]], though underwater targets proved difficult. Feanon lightened the ship to outpace the rest.

A High Storm followed. Feanon tried to manipulate the ship's weight but overstressed the turtle-damaged hull. Gundren scrambled to make repairs. The crew noted the spellcasting and muttered about witchcraft. They survived, barely.

## The Vote

As the storm cleared and the crew took stock, Thirsty Fingers saw his chance. He called out the captain for being absent during every crisis and demanded a public vote.

Both men made their case. Thirsty accused Drizzly of drunken negligence. Drizzly countered that the drinking contest had been rigged — and more dangerously, that Thirsty had arrived with a group that included a probable Ifrit. The crew grumbled. One pointed out all the bad luck since the outlanders came aboard.

But Drizzly played his strongest card: many of his crew had been serfs or downtrodden before he gave them better lives. The loyalty held. Only a quarter of the crew — mostly new hands — voted for Thirsty.

In gracious victory, Drizzly offered a deal. He knew of a ship moored at an island fortress along the coast — pristine, never moved, practically begging to be stolen. It was Thirsty's, along with any crew who wished to join him. He held out his hand and asked: when Thirsty had his own ship, might they be the start of a fleet?

Thirsty grinned, shook on it, and looked back at the group — a mix of shock, anxiety, and a few smiles. Buumi, who had been pale and wrestling with the morality of the situation, visibly relaxed.
