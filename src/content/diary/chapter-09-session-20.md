The group collected the wounded wererat and placed him on the Tenser's Disc [[gundren-rockseeker|Gundren]] had summoned. Gundren harvested parts from the fast-decaying demons — a Barlgura tusk and a good piece of Chasme carapace — before [[boulda|Boulda]] took the lead, escorting them through the warren toward the town centre.

The smell was immediate. The warren was filthy, rats scurrying everywhere, and dozens of beady eyes watched from the shadows — the rat-like eyes of the Deep Gnomes here reflecting torchlight.

## Chipgrin

The central chamber was massive, showing typical Svirfneblin architecture buried under a century of neglect. The plaza was full of some seventy Deep Gnomes, many loading wagons and supplies as if preparing to flee.

At the far end, a very fat wererat Deep Gnome stepped onto a balcony — a great moustache waxed into a magnificent set of whiskers. [[chipgrin|Chipgrin]] greeted the party and thanked them for saving his daughter and his kin.

The response to the group's proposal of alliance was frosty. The wererats had been considering it, but they had suffered too many injuries from the constant ooze attacks. Looking around, the group saw it — most of the Svirfneblin wererats bore some form of burn or wound. Their lycanthropic nature was regenerating the worst of the damage, but it was calorie-intensive and they were running out of food. Their gnomish connection to the earth had weakened considerably — no divine or druidic magic remained to them.

[[snorbul|Snorbul]] swore on his honour that the wererats would not be mistreated and convinced Chipgrin to meet with Chief [[dorbo|Diggermattock]].

## The Alliance

The group escorted the wererat chief through [[blingdenstone|Blingdenstone]] to the war table. Dorbo offered a curt greeting, wrinkling his nose at his counterpart. Negotiations began tense — but with the group's mediation, the two chiefs quickly found more common ground than difference. They agreed to put aside hostilities for at least as long as it took to clear the oozes and their master.

The ooze-impostors were raised. Chipgrin remarked that his people could sniff them out — they had nipped that problem early.

The chiefs shook hands. [[gurnik|Gurnik]] agreed wholeheartedly to provide the wererats with proper healing, and his cadre of priests and druids spent the rest of the day attending to the flow of injured wererats arriving from the northern warren.

The group took the wererats to the local tavern to mingle with the settled Deep Gnomes. Snorbul noted a few of his kin giving the newcomers hostile looks. He gave them a stare intense enough to wilt stone. When one Deep Gnome spat on the ground and tried to shoulder past, Snorbul caught him and hurled him ten feet across the room into a table.

He made it clear: the wererats were kin, cursed from *helping* a traveller after surviving the Drow destruction a century ago. Any Deep Gnome who showed them disrespect would answer to the new Wardens of Blingdenstone.

The mood dipped — then recovered over twenty minutes of drinking. By the end, the Deep Gnomes and their wererat kin were getting along fine.

## Prismeum

That night, [[feanon-serinde|Feanon]] walked the city searching for signs of arcana. Finding nothing, he ended up at Gurnik's shrine. He asked if any great arcanists had once lived in Blingdenstone.

Gurnik summoned an ancestral spirit. The ancient Gnome confirmed an archmage of some renown: [[carrowill|Archmage Carrowill]]. They summoned Carrowill's spirit directly. Feanon discovered that a powerful ritual was hidden somewhere in Blingdenstone — the ritual to create Prismeum, a crystal powder used in enchanting and to replicate spell components.

## The Assault Plan

The next morning, the combined forces of Blingdenstone finalized their assault plan. [[tarkathee-daru|Tark]], [[snorbul|Snorbul]], and [[aliyah-faen-tlabbar|Patches]] would attack from the north with the cave badgers and Trisk's warriors. [[gundren-rockseeker|Gundren]], [[cirrus-al-kayan|Cirrus]], [[gwen|Gwen]], Feanon, and Gurnik's priests would attack from the south. The main Gnomish forces — the bulk of their army — would hit from the centre, flanking the enemy as they moved south to engage the ranged combatants.

Up north, Tark enacted Pass Without Trace and they moved in, spotting two oozing Barlgura demons on patrol.

[[aliyah-faen-tlabbar|Patches]] felt a pull from [[eclipse|her celestial weapon]] and cast Etherealness, stepping into the [[ethereal-plane|Ethereal Plane]].

## The Southern Offensive

Down south, the attack began. [[gundren-rockseeker|Gundren]] opened fire. [[feanon-serinde|Feanon]] unleashed a massive lightning blast. [[cirrus-al-kayan|Cirrus]] hurled Eldritch blasts. The Gnome priests summoned a field of jagged, cutting crystals among the demons.

The area exploded into chaos. Hundreds of oozes and dozens of demons turned south and charged. They smashed into the crystal field as the southern group cast spell after spell — Gundren ringing two [[chardalyn|Chardalyn]] bells to bless his comrades before grabbing his rifle. Gwen and Feanon both loosed roaring blasts of lightning, burning away great swathes of oozes.

In the north, Tark and Snorbul launched their ambush. Thorna rushed past in a blur of hammers and hurled an icy spear at nearby oozes.

Patches was gone. Alone in the Ethereal Plane, she could see the energy of the battle — the glowing souls of her friends, the burning presence of demons — and decided to scout ahead. She floated through the misty echo of the material world, past dozens of monsters, and reached the great stone Dome: the Pudding Court, as the wererats called it.

It was aptly named. The place was literally oozing with caustic jellies, puddings, and oozes. Demons stood guard at the doors. Chasme demons nested on the ceiling inside.

Patches pushed forward into the Dome. Inside, a powerful demon — its soul burning brightly — stood before three of the ooze-impostors. The lesser oozes turned toward the southern commotion, but the great demon sensed Patches across the Ethereal divide, dozens of faces rippling across its massive ooze-body.

She was already moving, slipping past the Greater Demon and down a ramp into a natural tunnel. As she bypassed the [[hisesophax|Balor of Juiblex]], it called out in a warbling voice: *"noooOOOOoooo, the Master is not reaaaaaaaddyyyyyyyyy!"*

The cry echoed as far as Tark and Snorbul. Patches thought that meant now was the perfect time, and ran.

## The Demon Lord

The tunnel opened ahead of her — carved faces of a Svirfneblin man and woman flanking the entrance, their eyes glittering with gemstones. She entered a circular tiered cavern of natural stone with an unnatural order to its layout. This was Entemoch's Boon, the holiest site of the Deep Gnomes and a gate to the Plane of Earth.

If there was a portal, she could not see it. The entire recessed floor was filled like a great pool of tar.

As she watched, the massive pool rippled and rose in a column. Orbs of light moved up its body into a semblance of a head with many eyes. The great abyssal lord [[juiblex|Juiblex]] shifted and undulated, faces and limbs growing and forming and merging back into the main mass.

Patches stared up in horror — facing a Demon Prince entirely alone, protected only by the thin veil of the [[ethereal-plane|Ethereal Plane]].
