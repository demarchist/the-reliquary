## The Aftermath

The two surviving raiders rushed for the nearby horses. One kicked his mount east and rode hard without a backward glance. The other spun in the saddle and shouted — "Keegan will hear of this!"

[[gundren-rockseeker|Gundren]] put an arrow in the man's arm as he fled. The raider galloped past [[jack-mayer-johnson-mathews|JmJm]], who leaned from [[steden|Steden's]] saddle and drove a dagger through the man's temple. He slid from the horse, dead before he hit the ground.

JmJm grinned. Steden grinned. [[tarkathee-daru|Tark]] and [[horatio-von-bumidere|Buumi]] exchanged a look of weary disapproval.

[[cirrus-al-kayan|Cirrus]] arrived at last, having gotten thoroughly lost in the previous night's [[magnificent-mansion|Magnificent Mansion]].

The group's attention turned to the razed hamlet. They spread out and searched the smoking ruins for survivors. Three teenagers were found tied up in the only standing structure. The group cut them free and together they gathered the dead — the corpses of villagers scattered among spilled sacks of rice. The raid had been for food.

They carried the bodies to a graveyard overlooking the hamlet. Cirrus performed a lengthy ceremony for the dead while his familiar Patches threw clods of dirt on him from one of the open graves. The surviving teenagers thanked him for laying their kin to rest. Gundren burned the bodies of the raiders — but not before stripping them of valuables.

The group left the survivors a considerable sum of gold along with the salvaged equipment and horses of the slain raiders. Though it had been a horrible day for the villagers, they would at least be wealthy.

## Into the Mountains

The party continued along the [[bloodstone-pass|Bloodstone Pass]] to the strumming of JmJm's lute, the bard riding at the head of the column and playing as though they had not just walked through a graveyard.

The road climbed higher. The broadleaf trees thinned and gave way to sparse conifers, and the grassy hills yielded to rocky foothills with the true peaks of the Galena Mountains rising steeply in the distance. [[aliyah-faen-tlabbar|Patches]] spotted a cluster of flying creatures miles away, circling above one of the far mountains. She pulled out her spyglass — dragons. Eight of them, some kind of dull grey she had never seen before.

No one had heard of grey dragons, but JmJm perked up, remembering an old story about a Red Dragon that kept sickly, ashen dragons as pet hounds. Could these be Ashen White Dragons? The questions lingered as the eight creatures dove in unison and disappeared behind the closer peaks. The group rode on with greater urgency.

As the sun sank low, they came to a crossroads of paths and wagon trails with a well-used campsite. They made camp — some inside the Mansion, others beneath the stars. The view at sunset was spectacular: rolling hills giving way to the distant Steppe, and on the horizon the spires of Helios catching the last amber light.

## The Evening Watch

JmJm leaned against a rock near the fire, sulking. Tark had traded the Saurian Deathlord's greatsword for a rat-hat and a bottle of jelly, as JmJm saw it, and the loss of the ancient blade gnawed at him. When Tark noticed and offered one of his own daggers as a replacement, JmJm's mood only soured further. Patches pointed out that he had never bothered to examine the sword when he had it — he was only sulking now because it was gone. This made JmJm pout more.

Patches unrolled a large leather swatch and began assembling her bombs. Tark took first watch.

He was enjoying the view across the slopes when movement in the treeline caught his eye. He relaxed as a group of small Golden Tamarin monkeys peered out from the deepening twilight and tentatively approached, swinging and leaping through the branches. They were familiar — half-remembered from a childhood he rarely spoke of. He broke off a piece of hardtack, which the monkeys snatched greedily, and then he was among them, swinging through the trees, chasing flashes of gold-red fur.

Patches glanced up in annoyance. One monkey darted onto her work surface and snatched a detonator from between the delicate bomb components. She snatched it back with a sharp "Hey!" and the creature fled, chattering. JmJm did not move a muscle when one of the larger monkeys picked up a small rapier by the hilt and dragged it off into the woods.

JmJm watched it go. Then he cast his daily Wish, and a ring appeared on the ground — an intricate golden band in the shape of a monkey with gemstone eyes.

The night passed uneventfully until the small hours. Around three in the morning, a roar echoed across the landscape — strange and distorted by the mountain vales. Patches, on watch, could not identify it, but considered dragons likely based on what they had seen earlier.

## Fëanon's Midnight

Meanwhile, inside the Mansion, Cirrus prayed to [[selune|Selûne]] over a particularly fine-looking rock he had plucked from the mountainside. He cast True Polymorph on the stone, sculpting it into a High-Elf woman with a silver eye and silver fingertips. Her name was Roquelle — a new priest of Selûne, born from rock and devotion.

[[feanon-serinde|Fëanon]] studied his new spells in his quarters. While returning his inks to his bag of holding, his hand brushed the Al'Askarian Tablet — and he felt a waiting message pulse against his fingertips.

*"You have a wonderful home."*

Fëanon snatched up the tablet. In a panic, he teleported back to [[feanons-tower|his Tower]], appearing in the lobby in a puff of displaced air.

[[ruthir|Apprentice Ruthir]] was meditating behind the lobby desk and jerked awake as the archmage materialized before him. Fëanon peppered the young elf with questions. Nothing was out of the ordinary — no intruders, no disturbances, no breach. Still, Fëanon called all residents to an emergency meeting in the library.

Within minutes, Fëanon, Ruthir, [[megno-slatesunder|Megno]], [[beryan|Beryan]], [[theodemir|Theodemir]], Maya, and Grymedire had gathered. Fëanon struggled with the tablet for a moment before Grymedire helped him duplicate the message onto a larger display, and the group began to brainstorm.

While the students discussed, Megno headed back to bed — he was too old for this. But Theodemir approached, looking much like his old self thanks to some illusions and an Alter Self spell. He had a request: he wanted his true body back. Not another of his student's clones — his own form. He asked whether a resurrection, or growing a new clone to his original specifications, might be possible. Fëanon promised to look into it.

Grymedire then remembered he had received mail for Fëanon while working the front desk a few days prior. He produced a beautiful purple velvet envelope sealed with black wax flecked with gold. The moment Fëanon's fingers touched the material, overlapping whispers filled his mind: *Mordenkainen.*

He broke the seal. The letter let out a small, satisfied whimper, and the whispering stopped.

Fëanon appreciated the showmanship, even if he found the human Archmage distasteful.

The letter was an invitation to the Astral Plane — to a place called The Rock, to meet The Circle. It was a great many proper nouns, which piqued Fëanon's interest. The letter noted that time was malleable and that Fëanon should contact [[mordenkainen|Mordenkainen]] when he had some time to spare. Fëanon pocketed the invitation and thought about his archwizard's staff, still in the planning stages.

His thoughts were interrupted by Maya proclaiming that the message on the Tablet was probably just a friendly "Tome and Chill?"

Fëanon dismissed the group, deciding to keep an eye on the message, the tablet, and the tower. He responded to the mystery sender with *"Perhaps we could collab?"* — double-checked the labs and library for the night, and retired to his rooms to rest and teleport back to the group in the morning.

## The Vale of Razorhogs

The group woke to mountain birdsong and the chill of altitude that burned away quickly in the rising summer sun. They left the Mansion. Buumi attempted to cook eggs, only to burn them repeatedly in his skillet. In frustration he tossed them and announced he would make muffins in the Mansion that evening instead.

Fëanon returned from the Tower and the group set off. The morning passed without incident as they descended into a vale and began climbing the far side toward the saddle of the next mountain. They saw and heard signs of wildlife — and just as they were about to push on, two sharp bangs caught their attention.

Looking back across the valley, they spotted two smoke plumes and bursts, like fireworks. A huge commotion erupted in the woods below — a herd of massive boars rushed through the trees, squealing in fright. Two rangers emerged in pursuit.

Fëanon and a few others flew down to investigate, startling the woodsmen with their grandeur. The rangers were friendly — they had used fireworks to herd the great beasts, which they called Razorhogs. They were hunting them for the village.

Further up the pass, the rangers said, lay the small village of Und. A few hours beyond that stood [[ironfang-keep|Ironfang Keep]] and the branching road to the Sunlit Vale of Lord Aimu.

The group thanked them and returned to the road. Fëanon lingered just long enough to harvest one of the Razorback piglets.

They trekked on for several uneventful hours, enjoying the weather, until they came across a well-stocked ranger's cabin with a sign inviting travellers to help themselves. They found a donation box and dropped a hefty amount of gold into it before continuing toward Und.

## The Village of Und

The idyllic village consisted of a dozen houses nestled in the foothills of the Galena Mountains. Dwarves and Humans milled about what appeared to be a quiet mining and logging settlement. The group approached.

They spotted a Dwarf in a pair of ankle stocks on a small platform, dozing.

After speaking to the residents for a few minutes, it became clear that something was deeply wrong. Their halting, dreamy speech patterns — slow and distant, as though each word had to swim up from a great depth — made the group wonder whether the villagers were quite right in the head.

They turned to the man in the stocks, hoping for better answers, and hopped up onto the platform to speak with him.

The man's eyes went wide the instant they stepped up. He mouthed silently at them, gesturing frantically — and the group found that they too had been silenced. The platform was enchanted.

They stepped off and turned on the townsfolk, demanding an explanation. The prisoner's name was Urkle, they were told, and he had been sowing discord. The stocks would help him think about how his behaviour affected the community.

Patches slipped away from the gathering crowd and began checking the houses, expecting to find something cultish. She found nothing of the sort. In fact, the place was bizarrely perfect — a pie in every window, flowers on every table, bees humming and songbirds trilling. It was idyllic in a way that made her skin crawl.

## The Dreamer

The group freed Urkle and pulled him off the silenced platform just as four cloaked dwarves entered the village from the direction of the mine.

Patches took up a sniping position. The group questioned Urkle quickly. The dwarven man said he lived on the edge of the village, herding livestock. A few weeks back, folk in the village had started acting strangely. He had come in and asked questions, and the villagers had turned on him and put him in the stocks. He had no idea what was happening — only that the people were in some kind of dreamlike stupor.

The four cloaked figures approached and, in strange distorted voices, politely asked the group their business.

The group responded with threats, demanding to know what was happening to the town.

The cloaked figures were taken aback. They served The Dreamer, they said. It lived in the mines. It made the village and the villagers perfect. Everything was good here.

The group decided to free the villagers. Buumi began to cleanse as many townsfolk as he could while Fëanon cast a powerful Dispel Magic across the settlement.

The effect was immediate. People collapsed unconscious throughout the village. Others stood rigid and pointed at the group, screaming a high-pitched wail — but otherwise not moving, as if their bodies had forgotten how to act without the dream guiding them. Three of the hooded dwarves fell to the ground screaming in pain.

The fourth threw back its hood.

The dwarf's head was distended and twisted, covered in eyes, its jaw swollen into a huge toothy maw, and one of its original eyes grotesquely expanded to fill half the skull. The creature screamed — and ripped its own head off.

The head floated into the air. Every eye sprouted into an eyestalk.

The Beholder screamed at the group: *"YOU DISTURB THE DREAM! WHY!?"*

Patches shot it in the face.

The whole group attacked the aberration as it swept them with eyebeams and its antimagic gaze. The powerful creature was killed quickly — overwhelmed by the combined fury of the party before it could leverage its full arsenal.

Just as it sank to the ground, whimpering and asking *"Whyyyyyyyyy...?"*, one of the other cloaked dwarves jerked to its feet with a shriek. It braced its hands against the underside of its own skull and jaw — and tore its head off.

A second Beholder floated into the air.
