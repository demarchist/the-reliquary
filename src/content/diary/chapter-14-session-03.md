## The High Plateau

The group trekked through the wasteland toward the ever-looming High Plateau of the northern [[Anauroch]]. Their time in Al Askar had made this harsh terrain seem almost tame by comparison, and without the second sun, the temperatures remained bearable. They leapt chasms, traversed dangerous sands, and reached the base of the great cliffs.

They took to the skies. [[Tarkathee Daru|Tark]] called on the power of his new arm and simply leapt the five-hundred-foot cliff in two or three bounds, flying upward. The rest ferried each other aloft. [[Gundren Rockseeker|Gundren]] shot a great vulture out of the air when three swooped in to investigate, and the others retreated.

Atop the plateau, the wind was chill despite the blazing sun and the air was thinning. They continued north, in the direction they had seen the Wyvern fly many hours prior. [[Jack Mayer-Johnson Mathews|JmJm]] jaunted to the [[Ethereal Plane]] and found pools of dark shadow and weakened planar barriers — the [[Shadowfell]] was close here, and denizens had begun to bleed through. He did not remain long.

## The Canyon

Hours passed before a mass of plant life became visible ahead — a tangle of massive twisting thorny vines, trunks as thick as trees, with foot-long thorns. The scouts found an alternative route: a cleft in the stone leading down into a canyon with deep sandy dunes and strangely porous, holed walls.

[[Horatio Von Bumidere|Buumi]] complained of shoulder pain and [[Aliyah Faen Tlabbar|Patches]] attempted an awkward massage through plate armour — a sight so unprecedented that the rest of the group stopped to stare and tease her. She pulled back immediately, just as a voice echoed off the canyon walls in warning and enormous worms exploded from the sand around the party.

The Bobbit worms — ambush predators that had ground the canyon's stone to sand over centuries of burrowing — lunged for the group. Warned by the shouted alert, they defended themselves and drove the creatures back long enough to scramble onto a rocky outcropping.

There they found a lone Bedouin adventurer sheltering on the rock: Machmoud, a follower of the Fell God — the harsh desert aspect of [[Lathander]]. He was the sole survivor of a party of treasure hunters who had come seeking the Wyvern Graveyard.

The group split up to bypass the canyon. JmJm ferried Machmoud on [[Steden]] — the Bedouin initially recoiled from the devil-horse but was persuaded. Patches scaled the porous canyon walls and decapitated a worm that lunged from its burrow. [[Cirrus Al-Kayan|Cirrus]] drove off vultures with Eldritch Blasts on the plateau above, while Gundren burned through the vine canopy with his [[Thundercannon]].

## The Graveyard

The canyon group squeezed through a narrow section and emerged beneath the great vine canopy — a ceiling of twisted vegetation spanning a wide valley, with a trickling stream eroding the stone below. Scattered bones of long-dead great beasts littered the area — ancient, crumbling, and gnawed to uselessness. Hardy plants grew where the water allowed, and soft sand dunes filled the sheltered canyon floor.

They had found the Wyvern Graveyard.

The surface group descended to join them, and they explored carefully, noting signs of large scavenger beasts near the water. Down a particularly large box canyon, they spotted their quarry.

A massive Wyvern — easily the size of an adult dragon — sat near the water's edge, resting. It was watching something even larger than itself, seated against the back wall of the box canyon by a large cave opening.

A gargantuan Cyclops with fiery red hair, wearing armour of tanned Wyvern hide and carved bone, sat on a rocky outcropping. Its workmanship was exquisite — not the crude labour of small hands, but the meticulous craft of a being with culture and tools. In one fist it gripped a massive flail of lashed Wyvern-tail stingers.

The ancient Wyvern watched the giant. The giant watched the Wyvern. A standoff between two apex predators, each waiting for the other to die.

Machmoud fell to his knees, trembling. He named the titan as Borborygmos — the Bone Hunter, a mythical figure to his people.

JmJm looked on in wonder, leapt atop Steden, and charged toward these two great creatures of legend.
