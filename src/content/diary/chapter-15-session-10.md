## The Darkened Ring

The sphere of cold darkness still hung over the slope where the [[vampire-knight-of-frostmoore|Vampire Knight]] had vanished. The colour and the sound of the world ended at its border.

[[tarkathee-daru|Tark]] and [[horatio-von-bumidere|Buumi]] were inside it.

The two had pursued the Knight into his own shroud — Tark on foot through the impossible black, Buumi on the [[cloak-of-wings|Cloak of Wings]] above him — and were trading blows blindly against an opponent who had spent centuries learning to fight in conditions exactly like these. The lance came out of the dark in flashes. Tark answered with the staff of [[vaketh|Vaketh]], the white-wood prosthetic flaring in dim threads of red as he caught and turned blows he could not see. Buumi was a measured presence above — landing arrows where breath disturbed the air, then peeling away when a return strike scored the leather of his harness.

Neither of them was going to win. Neither of them was going to lose, either, so long as one of the party kept the Knight contained.

[[jack-mayer-johnson-mathews|JmJm]] did exactly that.

The bard reined Steden up at the edge of the darkness, lowered the singing tip of the [[inkblade|Inkblade]], and *thought*. A cage of force snapped into being around the shroud — a faceted prism of silvered air braced through the dark like ribs around a held breath. The Vampire reached the edge of his own darkness and *hit* the cage. It did not flex. He hit it again, harder, with the lance braced flat across his shoulder, and the cage rang once with the chime of a struck bell and did not break.

The Knight was sealed inside with his two pursuers. The dragons outside had to fight alone.

## The Wyrmlings and the Copper

They did not last long.

The smaller [[ashen-white-dragon|Ashen Whites]] — the wyrmlings that had screamed past Buumi at the start of the engagement — broke from formation and stooped on the slope where the great Copper had reared up, copper hide bright against the dimming valley. Three of them landed claws-first. The fourth came in low for the throat.

[[lady-isabel|Izmakella]] caught the first one in her jaws and tore it apart against the cascade waterfall. She caught the second across the chest with a single sweep of her foreclaw and broke its back along the line of the lake's stone retaining wall. The third was already wheeling away when her tail took it sideways out of the air and dropped it, broken, into the shallows. The fourth — the throat-diver — was met head-on by a column of slow breath that washed the wyrmling's wings out from under it and left it tumbling down the slope at half its diving speed.

From the porch, the archers finished what she started.

[[cassian|Cassian]]'s bow had not slowed. [[aliyah-faen-tlabbar|Patches]] had switched to her heavy crossbow now that the engagement had spread, and the [[crimson-dawn-knights|Crimson Dawn]] archers behind her were firing in disciplined volleys, plunging arrows into wyrmling backs as the small dragons fell. The thundercannons of the household guard fired in two great coughs that took the last airborne wyrmling cleanly out of the sky.

The wyrmlings died in the space of a minute. The valley floor was scattered with their pale, sickly bodies.

Izmakella turned her serpentine head toward the two surviving adults — the ones that had not dared engage her in melee — and *breathed* again. The golden haze of her Slow Breath rolled up the slope a second time. The adults lurched in mid-air. One of them began to drop.

## The Vampire's Salute

Inside the cage, the Knight stopped fighting.

He had been calculating since the cage went up. Now, with the wyrmlings dead and his support in pieces and the Copper turning on his adults, the calculation had resolved. He gathered himself in the dark, summoned the cold from the marrow out, and *broke* — a burst of dark power blasted the force cage outward in a shower of silver shards, and the Knight flew clear of the shroud with the lance held low at his side.

He surveyed the field. The valley below. The Copper. The Paladin on the porch with a blade of living sunlight in his hand. The two adult dragons spiralling in slow circles, one of them already bleeding heavily from Cassian's arrows. The wyrmlings on the ground.

He called the retreat.

The two adults peeled away from the Copper at his command, breathed a last desperate jet of ice at the slope below them, and turned hard for the ridge. Their wing-beats were sluggish under the Slow Breath, but they were still faster than anything that could pursue from the ground. They climbed the darkening sky toward the high notch of the pass.

The Vampire Knight turned in mid-air, faced the porch, and raised the lance in a slow, deliberate salute.

Then he vanished — invisible, the lance and the armour and the smile all dissolved in a single breath of unmade light.

The valley held its breath.

On the slope above the estate, Cassian had not stopped drawing. He set the next arrow against the string, took the deep breath of a man calculating a shot he should not have been able to make, and loosed.

The arrow climbed in a long, beautiful arc — through the deepening twilight, past the cresting ridge, into the throat of the great Ashen White that had carried the Vampire's silent shape into the sky. A second arrow followed. A third. The last of the magical shafts in his quiver. Then [[aliyah-faen-tlabbar|Patches]]' poisoned bolts in a high, falling cluster, and the household archers' final volley as the dragon laboured past their effective range.

The white dragon bellowed in pain. Its wings folded. It crashed amongst the distant, dark trees of the mountainside, and the sound rolled back across the vale as a single muffled crack.

The battle was over.

## The Sister's Game

Most of the [[crimson-dawn-knights|paladins]] stayed at their posts.

[[lord-aimu|Lord Aimu]] did not. He sheathed the [[ashbringer|Sunsword]] — the radiance banking back to the hairline of light it had been on the altar — and walked out onto the trampled lawn of his own estate with the slow deliberation of a man who has spent a long evening pretending not to be a paladin and was now permitting himself to be exactly that. He stopped a respectful distance from the Copper Dragon on his lake's edge.

The Copper Dragon turned her head, considered him, and *shifted*.

The change ran down her body in the same warm copper ripple that had taken her in the first place — scales unmaking themselves into bruised skin, the long serpentine neck shortening, the wings folding away into the space behind a tattered evening gown that re-knit itself out of the shift. The woman who had been the dragon stood barefoot in the leaf litter, hair caked dark with blood on one side, and smiled at the Lord as if they were meeting at a salon.

"Lady Isabel," Lord Aimu said. His voice was not steady.

"My lord," she answered.

He had known the woman for years. He had bought from her shop. He had stood across rooms from her at his queen's gatherings. He had not known. The set of his face moved through several expressions in the space of a breath, none of them landing.

"A game," she said, lifting one shoulder in a small, careless shrug. "My sister and I have been playing it for some time. You will forgive us. I should have been more careful with the dragons in the sky."

Her sister. The party's eyes met across the lawn. None of them said the name aloud, but each of them was thinking it: [[tazmikella|Tazmikella]] — the old woman with cat's eyes who ran the going-out-of-business sale in the merchant ring, who had once let a signet ring fall at Patches' boot. Two adult Coppers. One game. Helios under their watch the entire time.

Lord Aimu accepted the dragon at her word, because there was nothing else to do, and gestured toward the chapel. The unconscious [[gnome-of-the-vale|Gnome]] was still inside. The bite was still working. The conversation that had to happen had to happen in front of holy stone.

They walked together to the chapel doors.

## The Curse and the Cure

The Gnome had not stirred.

She lay where JmJm had placed her, on the cold flags of the chapel floor at the altar's foot, her small body breathing shallowly under the lingering brightness of Lord Aimu's healing. The bruises and cuts were gone. The puncture marks on her neck were not. They oozed a slow, viscous black under the chapel's lamplight, and the skin around them had begun to take on the grey, waxen pallor of a body that no longer understood it was alive.

The curse was in its final hours. They all saw it.

"How long?" [[cirrus-al-kayan|Cirrus]] asked.

"Long enough to bury her," Lord Aimu said. "Not long enough to cure her, by any method I know."

He laid the [[ashbringer|Sunsword]] across the altar with the same care a man uses to set down a sleeping child. The chapel filled, briefly, with the held-breath quiet of an institution. Lady Isabel knelt at the Gnome's side without explaining why and rested one bruised hand on the small chest.

The truth they all knew but were each waiting for someone else to speak: vampirism was easier to cure *before* it took hold. Once the curse turned, you could not pry it loose with channelled grace or counter-magic or any application of holy will. You had to start over. You had to let the body die — cleanly, in hallowed circumstances — and then *bring it back*, into a body that had never been bitten.

JmJm said it.

"We have to kill her."

Isabel's head whipped around. The shock on her face was a real shock — not the elegant practised dismay of a socialite but the genuine wound of someone who had carried a child down a mountain at sprint speed and was now being told the last thing she had to do was put a knife in her. She rose halfway, refused, refused again, refused a third time as the argument circled the chapel. The Sunsword on the altar caught the light and threw it warmly across her bruised face.

It was [[cirrus-al-kayan|Cirrus]] who turned her, in the end. He spoke gently. He explained the chain — the kill on hallowed stone, the diamond, the prayer to [[selune|Selûne]], the body remade unbitten. He told her about the [[anduriel|Planetar]] he had once watched lay down their life so that a sword might rise. He told her that this was not killing. This was unmaking the bite by remaking the woman.

Isabel looked down at the small face.

"All right," she said.

## A Diamond and a Knife

Lord Aimu gave his blessing in two sentences. He did not speak the prayer of his own god; he simply nodded once toward the altar and said, "*Lathander* will hold the stone."

JmJm knelt by the Gnome's chest.

He drew the slim knife from his sleeve — the one that had once been a working tool of the Dark Whistler and that he had carried ever since out of an instinct he had never explained — and balanced its point above her heart. His other hand cupped the side of her small face, the way he had once cupped the face of a child whose mother had stood between them and a sword.

Cirrus took position above her head. From his coat he drew a diamond the size of a hen's egg — one of the great gems he carried for moments like this — and held it above her in both hands. He began to chant. The chant was not yodelling, for once: it was low, careful, and clean. The diamond began to gather a silver light at its heart.

The chapel filled with the cold steady light of [[selune|Selûne]] — moonlight in a building dedicated to the morning. The two faiths held each other for a long moment in the air above the Gnome's body. Lord Aimu did not interrupt.

JmJm pushed.

The knife slid into the small chest with the practised, unhesitating motion of a man who knew exactly how much pressure was required and refused, on principle, to use any more. The Gnome's breath caught once. Then it stopped. Then her body began to *change*.

She *grew*.

Her face lengthened. Her limbs stretched. The cream of her foreign-cut traveller's robes split and fell away as her shoulders broadened and her neck unfolded into the long copper sweep of a dragon's throat. Wings spread along the chapel's vaulted floor. By the time her hindclaws had come to rest, the body filling the aisle was the brilliant, freshly-struck copper of an adult dragon — *larger* than [[lady-isabel|Izmakella]] in this form, by half a length.

The diamond above Cirrus's hands disintegrated in a single soft flash. The silver light poured down into the dragon's chest.

She breathed.

Life flowed back along the long copper neck. The wound at her heart closed. Her eyes — slitted, intelligent, the colour of polished bronze — fluttered open and tracked across the chapel ceiling, past Cirrus, past JmJm, and settled on Isabel.

Isabel laid one bruised hand on her foreleg and breathed out the breath of someone who had been holding it for days.

## Azoo of Shou Lung

The dragon spoke first in a language none of them recognised, then corrected herself, then introduced herself in heavily-accented Common.

*Azoo*, she said. [[azoo-the-archer|Azoo of Shou Lung]]. An Adult Copper Dragon, member of the [[dragons-of-shou-lung|Dragonflight]] — the nine ascending ancients who had flown west from the hidden Empire to take new lairs in the world. She was the Archer of her flight. Her gnome-form was a careful disguise. She had been the unconscious foreign-looking child on the floor for two reasons: because the bite had nearly killed her, and because the place she had been running from was a place where dragons were being hunted.

She told the tale carefully. The group listened.

Ten of them had crossed west, not nine. They had flown to the [[great-tree-of-bahamut|Great Tree of Bahamut]] in the [[bloodstone-pass|Bloodstone Pass]] to ascend through the planes to their assigned lairs. Before they could leave, however, several of the flight had broken off to the north, searching for a missing Red Dragon emissary the Pass had warned them was overdue.

They had not found their emissary. They had found something else.

A great Red Dragon — older and crueller than any Red they had ever scented — working in concert with an Archlich. The Red Dragon's name was [[vulkan|Vulkan]]. The Archlich's name was [[zhengyi|Zhengyi]], and Azoo spoke it as if she had heard it pronounced too recently. The two of them had taken station on the back of a great [[floating-castle|Floating Castle]] hovering above an army of Oni who were performing a slow, deliberate dark ritual over the ruined husk of a half-orc city to the north.

Lord Aimu went still.

"[[palischuk|Palischuk]]," he said.

He did not need confirmation. He had it from Azoo's face. The half-orc city was ruined. The army of Oni was now standing in its bones, raising arms beneath a floating mountain, calling something into the world the dragons had not been able to identify in the moment of fleeing. The Dragonflight had peeled off from the ritual and tried to escape, but Vulkan and a hunting pack of Oni had given chase — and beside them, drifting along on the air like a moon, a great floating *eye*. A [[golden-orb|Golden Orb]] the colour of fresh metal.

One of the dragons had fallen. They had watched the Orb's gaze pass across her and her flight had simply broken in mid-air.

Isabel spoke for the first time. She had been called to the Dragonflight's defence by [[bahamut|Bahamut]] in the same hours, summoned across the vale to join with [[protanther|Protanther]], the ancient Gold who tended the Tree. She had reached Protanther in time to fight beside him.

She had watched Protanther fall before [[vulkan|Vulkan]].

The chapel was very quiet for a long moment.

She and Azoo had been the only two she knew of who had escaped from the immediate engagement, though she believed most of the surviving Shou Lung Dragons had scattered to safer parts of the continent and were not, yet, dead. The Vampire Knight's flight had cut Azoo off from her route to the Tree, harried her south through the foothills for days, and finally driven her to the [[aimu-estate|Aimu Estate]] in [[frostmoore-vale|Frostmoore Vale]] — the only hallowed ground Isabel had known of in the region — with a bitten ward in her hand.

Azoo, she said now, still had to *ascend*. Bahamut's compulsion had not faded with the bite or the flight. She was commanded by her god to go to her assigned lair on [[bytopia|Bytopia]] and become an ancient.

Lord Aimu took the news with the stoicism of a man who had spent his life absorbing larger blows than this one. He said, plainly, that he could not help her with the planar travel — interplanar movement was beyond his order's discipline, and he knew of no wizard in the kingdom whom he would trust with the keeping of a Copper Dragon's destination.

[[cirrus-al-kayan|Cirrus]] stepped forward.

So did [[jack-mayer-johnson-mathews|JmJm]].

They could take her.

Azoo studied the two of them — the tiefling priest with the moon-cold eyes and the half-elf bard with the lacquered door tucked into his coat — and inclined her great copper head. She accepted.

## The Sunsword on the Table

While Cirrus and JmJm and Azoo withdrew to prepare the planar step, the rest of the group walked with Lord Aimu and Lady Isabel up out of the chapel and into the manor proper. The east wing was still smouldering where the lightning bolt had ploughed through it; the household staff were already at it with buckets and damp cloth. The lord led them past the damage to a comfortable, undamaged room at the front of the house and gestured them to chairs.

He laid the [[ashbringer|Sunsword]] across the low table between them.

The radiance of the blade settled to a soft, banked light against the polished wood — a hilt and a hairline of brightness, no more than the working glow of any dormant sunsword. Lord Aimu sat down opposite the group, smoothed the lapels of his hakama, and asked them to tell him their story.

[[tarkathee-daru|Tark]] did the telling.

He spoke quietly, in the cadence of a monk who had learned to compress his life into the shapes a stranger could carry. He covered [[orcus|Orcus]] and [[zhengyi|Zhengyi]] in two sentences. He covered the [[al-askar|Al'Askarian]] campaign and the [[font-of-fawden|Font]] in one. He covered the Battle of [[krezk|Krezk]] and the reforging of the [[ashbringer|Ashbringer]] from the [[anduriel|Planetar]]'s sacrifice without raising his voice. He came at last to [[helioglabulus|Helios]] — to the [[muskov-family|Muskovs]], to the unsettling pattern of nobles' deaths, to [[gaddock|Gaddock]] and the suspicion that a vampiric hand was reaching into the city, and to the reason the group had come for the Sunsword in the first place.

Lord Aimu listened with the still attention of a man who was being told things he had already half-suspected.

When Tark stopped, the lord nodded once and began.

He and his partner [[lady-timea|Lady Timea]] were the supreme commanders of the [[crimson-dawn-knights|Order of the Crimson Dawn]] — Paladins of [[lathander|Lathander]] partnered with the [[warlocks-of-kelemvor|Warlocks of Kelemvor]], the two orders bound under the banner *For the Greater Good*. Their compatriots found the [[red-wizards-of-thay|Thayans]] abhorrent and would have run them from the kingdom on principle, but the political reality was that Thay was a neighbour and the realm was not currently at war. The Thayan delegation was tolerated. The nobles wheeled and dealt with the Wizards openly. The order disliked it. The order did not act on it.

The Queen — [[queen-concetinna-frostmantle|Concetinna Frostmantle]] — had been entertaining the delegation for some weeks now. The King had been at her side. The Red Wizards themselves were reclusive even by their order's standards, appearing only occasionally in public or at the gatherings the Queen hosted in their honour.

A few days ago, the delegation had asked something specific. They had requested an audience with the newly-acquired Holy Relic of Lathander.

The Wizards, Lord Aimu said, had a *strong* interest in the blade.

He had advised the Queen — formally and quietly — to deny the request. She had overridden him. The audience had been granted.

He and Lady Timea had escorted the Queen and the delegation into the cathedral. They had spent a measured length of time examining the blade. The Queen had taken the weapon in hand, had turned it once, had set it back on the plinth. The delegation had withdrawn to their quarters.

The exchange had been correct in every visible detail.

It had also been *wrong*.

Lord Aimu paused on the word. He did not have a more precise term for it. The Queen had been sequestered, for some weeks, in a way that did not entirely fit her usual disposition — appearing only at functions, otherwise unseen. The paladins had probed, gently, with the discreet methods their order used to check the wellness of monarchs they were sworn to defend, and had detected no fault. But their methods were unobtrusive and, he admitted, by no means infallible. Something in the way the Queen had held the Sunsword, something in the cool quality of her gaze on it, had put the Lord on the slow, climbing edge of nausea that paladins learned to listen to.

The hours after the audience had not eased the nausea. They had made it worse. The relic was in danger.

He had taken it.

He and his closest paladins had stolen the [[ashbringer|Sunsword]] from its plinth in the cathedral that night and ridden out of Helios under the cover of a borrowed escort, doubling back into the mountains where his late wife's family estate could give him a defensible position to *think*. What he had done was not technically treason. The blade was a Lathanderian relic, and as a Lord of the Order he had a defensible claim to its custody. The act was, however, politically catastrophic. He could not return to the city with the sword still in his possession. He could not return without it.

He had been kneeling at the altar in the chapel, deciding, when JmJm had burst in with a bitten Gnome under one arm.

The lord finished. He did not ask the group's view. He simply rose, said he would retire to meditate, and asked that they take their evening meal at the house while he decided whether to hand them the blade.

The Sunsword stayed on the table when he left.

## The Twin Paradises

Cirrus, JmJm, and Azoo stepped through a long doorway of moonlight and were elsewhere.

The light that washed across them as they arrived was warm, low-angled, gold — the unhurried light of a midsummer afternoon in a country that had never known winter. They stood on a soft grassland of close-cropped meadow grass, rolling away in every direction in shoulders of green hill toward a horizon that, if you let your eye climb too high, looped slowly back over their heads. The inverted under-sky of the upper land hung above them, a second continent of pasture and orchard and slow-moving cloud, mirrored in the bright sky between.

[[bytopia|Bytopia]]. The Twin Paradises. The afterlife of the Gnomes.

Azoo lowered her head and breathed in the air of the place as a long-lost relative breathes in the smell of a kitchen they have not visited in years. Her tension — the carriage of a dragon who had been hunted for days — began, by degrees, to leave her.

A warmth approached.

The figure that came up the slope toward them was not, strictly, walking. He drifted forward at the gait of a man who had once walked but no longer needed to — a tall, gold-skinned Deva in robes the colour of a freshly-broken geode, his face composed in the steady, untiring welcome of a being who had been awaiting them. His hands were folded across the front of his robe. He carried no weapon. The grass behind him bent in the wake of his passing and slowly came back up.

He greeted them in flawless Draconic.

He was, he said, a Deva of [[callarduran-stonehands|Callarduran Stonehands]], the gnomish god of the Underdark and the deep places, into whose stewardship Azoo had been assigned for her ascension. The lair prepared for her lay in the nearby hills — a great worked cave-system in the side of an extinct volcano, fitted for an Ancient Copper Wyrm and waiting for her to arrive.

Azoo turned her great copper head to the bard and the priest who had brought her this far.

The thanks she gave them were brief and dragon-formal. They would do.

She bowed her head once to the Deva, and then she and the Deva went together up the slope, the dragon's claws scarcely bending the grass, and disappeared between the rolling shoulders of the hills.

Cirrus and JmJm turned to one another.

A gnomish settlement lay below them in the fold of the valley — a small cluster of round-roofed cottages, blue smoke curling from low chimneys, a bustling open-air market full of strange and wonderful things on small tables shaded by patchwork canopies. A small crowd of gnomes had gathered already at the bottom of the slope, drawn by the sight of two unfamiliar figures stepping out of nowhere onto their afternoon, their faces upturned in the candid, friendly curiosity of a people who had not, in living memory, been afraid of anyone.

The market hummed.

Cirrus straightened his cassock. JmJm adjusted the brim of his hat. They walked down together toward the gnomes of Bytopia and whatever the market had on offer.
