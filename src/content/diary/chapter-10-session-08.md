The battle did not go well for [[yeenoghu|Yeenoghu]].

The adventurers pressured the Gnoll Lord until the great beast bit its own wrist, flinging tar-like blood in an arc before it. Hyenas formed from the droplets where they landed. [[yeenoghu|Yeenoghu]] swatted the paralysed [[tarkathee-daru|Tark]] from its back, hurling the monk twenty feet, then turned to flee as shots and spells hammered into the creature. A blast of ice from [[feanon-serinde|Feanon]] ended the conjured Hyenas in moments.

The Goristro, meanwhile, had been roused from its stupor by three enraged Hyenas. It made quick work of the beasts before turning its gaze on the party. As [[yeenoghu|Yeenoghu]] fled past [[aliyah-faen-tlabbar|Patches]], the [[drow-faction|Drow]] darted from the shadows, striking at the Demon Lord's knees. [[yeenoghu|Yeenoghu]] crashed through a ruined wall, stumbled, and snarled back that he would not forget their scents — then went invisible. [[cirrus-al-kayan|Cirrus]] encapsulated the area in a Wall of Force, trapping the Demon Lord. Momentarily caught, [[yeenoghu|Yeenoghu]] began casting a teleportation spell. [[feanon-serinde|Feanon]] moved to counterspell, but without being able to see the invisible caster, he could not target it. The Demon Lord escaped.

The Goristro fared worse. Surrounded by the party, it howled in fear and tried to run. They cut it down, collecting its heart along with the Demon Lord blood and a pair of demonic bracers. Not wanting the bloodshed to draw unwanted attention, they pushed deeper into [[the-labyrinth|the Labyrinth]].

## The Instant Fortress

They hiked until [[gundren-rockseeker|Gundren]] found a suitable campsite — but [[aliyah-faen-tlabbar|Patches]] had spotted something: boot prints. Surface Dwarves, partially obscured by newer minotaur tracks. Unwilling to risk trouble while exhausted, they set up the Tiny Hut and rested. During the night, [[aliyah-faen-tlabbar|Patches]] heard a rhythmic metal pounding, like a blacksmith's hammer on an anvil.

They picked up the Dwarven trail the next morning and followed it toward the sound. A few hours later, they found the source.

In the centre of a smallish cavern stood a large metal fortification — twenty feet on a side, fifty feet to the tip of its crenellated roof, complete with arrow slits. A [[delzoun-instant-fortress|Delzoun Instant Fortress]]. Besieging it were half a dozen bloodied minotaurs. Around the base lay dozens of corpses — goblins, gnolls, and kobolds, fairly rotten and pierced with crossbow bolts. The defenders had held for some time.

The group moved in without hesitation. [[aliyah-faen-tlabbar|Patches]] hurled a card from her [[deck-of-illusions|Deck of Illusions]], conjuring a Succubus to distract the beasts, and they attacked from the darkness and the tunnel mouth. The minotaurs were slaughtered quickly, though the leader put up a better fight than most. [[tarkathee-daru|Tark]] groaned at the wounds he suffered, and [[cirrus-al-kayan|Cirrus]] and [[horatio-von-bumidere|Buumi]] blessed the injured.

## The Nabassu

The tower appeared to belong to Surface Dwarves, and a male voice called from the second-storey arrow slit, thanking them for the rescue and asking them to find a way to open the door — the captain with the password had died two weeks prior. [[aliyah-faen-tlabbar|Patches]] reported no movement at the arrow slit. She doubted anyone was alive in there.

[[tarkathee-daru|Tark]] went invisible and shadowstepped through an arrow slit. [[aliyah-faen-tlabbar|Patches]] scaled to the roof. [[feanon-serinde|Feanon]] began working on the password.

Inside, [[tarkathee-daru|Tark]] found the first floor untouched — supplies and equipment for four Dwarven miners, covered in weeks of dust. A Dwarven rune on the back wall bore the symbol of [[gauntlgrym|Gauntlgrym]] — the Delzoun Kingdom. He climbed to the second floor and found a barracks with two bunkbeds. Three were occupied by corpses — Dwarves dead for weeks, seemingly peacefully.

A shadow on the other side of the room shifted. [[tarkathee-daru|Tark]] stared into the darkness and made out the shape of a fourth Dwarf, standing in silhouette by the arrow slit. It was staring back at him.

[[tarkathee-daru|Tark]] dropped his invisibility. "Hi! I'm Tark!"

"I'm Nabassuu," the creature hissed before lunging. The Dwarven body elongated, stretching into a lithe ink-black figure with bat-like wings. The shadow demon scuttled forward, slashing with fang and claw. [[tarkathee-daru|Tark]] felt invisible eyes lock onto his soul — the Nabassu was trying to consume it.

Outside, the group heard combat. [[feanon-serinde|Feanon]] worked out the password but not the pronunciation. [[gundren-rockseeker|Gundren]], fluent in the old tongue, rushed to the front door and spoke it. The metal doors swung inward. Above, the roof hatch lifted and [[aliyah-faen-tlabbar|Patches]] looked down to see [[tarkathee-daru|Tark]] fighting for his life — just as a lesser demon leapt from the shadows at her.

[[cirrus-al-kayan|Cirrus]] charged through the ground-floor door and was instantly set upon by shadow demons that stole his life force. With [[horatio-von-bumidere|Buumi]] on his heels and covering fire from [[gundren-rockseeker|Gundren]] and [[feanon-serinde|Feanon]], the furious warlock blasted them to oblivion. [[gundren-rockseeker|Gundren]] threw two gemstones from his helmet, bathing the metal tower in sunlight. The lesser demons burned away in the light.

[[tarkathee-daru|Tark]], badly wounded, drank the fanciest potion he could find and rushed upstairs to rejoin [[aliyah-faen-tlabbar|Patches]] against the Greater Demon. She had been fighting defensively, holding her own until reinforcements arrived. [[tarkathee-daru|Tark]] came in with stunning strikes. Surrounded, the Nabassu was backed into a corner — burned, smashed, and finally obliterated when [[feanon-serinde|Feanon]] pointed a finger and disintegrated its legs.

The tower fell quiet save for heavy breathing. The dark corpse melted away to nothing.

[[gundren-rockseeker|Gundren]] looked at the group and grinned. "Hey — let's go lock the door on *our* new tower!"
