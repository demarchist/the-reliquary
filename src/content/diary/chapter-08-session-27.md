Uira, a small plump moon elf acolyte, welcomed the group as they entered the teleportation chamber and then went to fetch Li Tan.

The group ascended from the temporary teleportation chamber and arrived in the tower proper.

Depictions of Fey'Anon's life, in grand engravings, ringed the tower and followed the flow of the stairways up.

The group headed through a set of beautiful wooden doors, colored light shining on the marble floors from stained glass windows, and into a large dining hall.

[[gundren-rockseeker|Gundren]] poured out the ewer of feasting and everyone partook — except [[claudine|Claudine]], who was too nervous.

The group finished and returned to the main hall to exit [[feanons-tower|Fey's Tower]] into the evening, a full moon rising.

It was a clear chill night with the Tears of Selune clearly visible, strong moonlight bathing the valley.

The group looked across the small island holding Fey's grand tower, its sides sweeping and twisting into the sky and still under construction.

Across from the tower entrance, not 300 meters away, stood a set of Dwarven docks built from sturdy wood.

Past the docks, the group looked over the lake and could clearly see the Fane of Selune.

The Fane was grand, with an hourglass-like shape and a similar twist to Fey's tower, except with thorn-like protrusions up its length.

While Fey's tower rose a good 150 feet into the air, the Fane was shorter and squatter at around 100 feet.

[[claudine|Claudine]] fell to her knees and began to sob, overwhelmed by finally returning to the surface after a life of slavery.

Marzeline was in shock but dropped to her mother's side and comforted her, holding her in her arms.

As the group scanned the area, the Red Hill on the edge of the Moonwood was clearly visible and the Dwarven mine was lit by torchlight.

They looked south and saw the sheer, seamless walls of the Keep of Thunder Bluff constructed seamlessly of red-hued stone reflecting the moonlight.

The Barony was bathed in a surreal glow from the full moon and the large, smooth red-stoned structures.

Li came down from Fey's tower behind the group and greeted them warmly.

She introduced herself to [[snorbul|Snorbul]], [[claudine|Claudine]], and Marzeline.

[[snorbul|Snorbul]], though, was having a hard time dealing with the sky, feeling as though he would simply fall into the great void above.

After pleasantries, Li took the group across the lake from Fey's tower to the Fane and its garden via boat.

The Fane of Selune was nestled in the outer forest of the Moonwood, its hourglass-like shape ascending above the treetops from a clearing.

The group headed into the Fane and instantly noted a Wood Elf shrine to Selune set up on the altar beneath a massive statue of [[cirrus-al-kayan|Cirrus]].

Honored, [[cirrus-al-kayan|Cirrus]] investigated with [[patches|Patches]] and noted the center of the shrine was made from a fallen meteorite — a "tear" of Selune.

As the group gazed upon the shrine, the Deep Gnome Topsy came up to greet them, meeting [[patches|Patches]] first and then giving [[snorbul|Snorbul]] a parasol to help with the sky.

They then headed down the cobblestone road from the Fane to New Daggerford.

As the forest thinned out and opened into the plains of the valley, the Tradeway was clearly visible and a new large watchtower and gatehouse stood on the western road.

New Daggerford was still only a handful of smaller houses, though more were under construction.

The group headed into the center of town, to the two buildings that dominated the area: a massive tavern connected via sky-bridge to a theater.

Nearby villagers noted the group and greeted them with cheer — many were the freed slaves from [[neverwinter|Neverwinter]] and remembered their saviors.

Doc B took his leave, called away by a need to explore.

The rest of the group headed into Over the Moon, a large three-story manor house made of brick and timber functioning as the tavern, inn, and town hall of New Daggerford, as well as the center of [[patches|Patches]]'s operation, the Obsidian Order.

Inside, the group was met by a scene of good cheer and, although the main floor was massive and sparsely populated, those that were there were clearly happy.

[[patches|Patches]] headed over to the bar and the girl working behind it that she did not recognize, approaching as much for drinks as for information.

Lilly Blackthumb introduced herself — daughter of the local blacksmith.

She explained that she had figured out that Kitty was unusually clever.

She was young but wanted to learn, and after an earnest appeal, [[patches|Patches]] hired her properly.

Everyone else started to drink and chat.

They quickly learned from the townsfolk that a child was missing and that [[buumi|Buumi]] was up the road at a town meeting in Kossenberg about it.

JmJm heard of his own theater construction and his reaction was as expected: he stormed out, declaring it was all wrong.

Finishing their drinks, the group headed out into the town center to discuss where to go next, and Li took her leave.

They decided to send a note to [[veygar|Veygar]] to have him leave [[gracksuluth|Gracksuluth]] via messenger box.

[[tark|Tark]] took a miniaturizing potion and was mailed via instant courier back to the [[underdark|Underdark]].

The box arrived at its destination and [[tark|Tark]] instantly heard the sounds of conflict.

[[tark|Tark]] opened the box to see a pitched interior battle between [[veygar|Veygar]]'s crew and assaulting Stoneguard in some kind of basement.

[[tark|Tark]] dropped darkness over the whole chamber and activated his blindfold while warding off attacks.

He called out to [[veygar|Veygar]] and the surprised Duergar called back.

[[tark|Tark]] threw him the potion and [[veygar|Veygar]] drank it just as an enlarged Duergar tried to grapple him, and missed.

As [[tark|Tark]] was trying to extricate [[veygar|Veygar]], [[veygar|Veygar]]'s crony realized an escape was happening and tried to hitch a ride — leaping for the box and failing.

[[tark|Tark]] saw Velka and [[hemeth|Hemeth]] get captured by Stoneguard with large hooked cleavers and dragged into the press of bodies.

[[tark|Tark]] picked up the tiny [[veygar|Veygar]], closed the box lid, and with it being dismissed, returned to the caster: [[cirrus-al-kayan|Cirrus]].

The box appeared at the crossroads in New Daggerford and the top opened, [[tark|Tark]] exiting with [[veygar|Veygar]].

[[veygar|Veygar]] resumed normal size and told the group about the Stoneguard attack and that they had been doing a job around the Palace.

He guessed that his new teammate, Brassifordt, had betrayed him.

As the group was chatting with [[veygar|Veygar]] about his information on the artifact piece, they saw [[buumi|Buumi]] coming down the street from the north.

[[buumi|Buumi]] was not alone — he was with three others, paladin trainees including his niece Brigitta, daughter of Georg and Seska.

[[buumi|Buumi]] greeted the group and then sent the paladin trainees back to the keep for the night.

[[buumi|Buumi]] said his hellos properly and said he was ready to head with the group down to the [[underdark|Underdark]], his family being as settled as they would ever be.

Returning to grilling [[veygar|Veygar]], the group pressed him about whether he needed Velka to take them to the ruins in the Labyrinth.

He said she would help tremendously, but it was not strictly necessary — though Velka had far more information on the interior of the ruins than he did.

He told the group that his cousin Velka and their friend [[hemeth|Hemeth]] would most likely be executed, as they had been caught during heist planning.

The group said they were sorry, but they did not have time to rescue Velka, [[hemeth|Hemeth]], and Bassifordt.

[[veygar|Veygar]] looked a little shocked, then shrugged and said that it was what it was.

[[buumi|Buumi]] was dismayed but said nothing.

[[veygar|Veygar]] headed off to drink away his sorrows and asked [[patches|Patches]] one last time if she was serious that they would not help his kin and [[hemeth|Hemeth]].

[[buumi|Buumi]] told the group about what he had been dealing with: a farm boy was missing.

The group decided to find the child, or at least check in with the family, and headed north to the outskirts of the other small village.

On the largest farm, a sizeable crowd of villagers was showing support for the family of the missing boy and search parties could be seen returning and reporting in.

As the group approached, the crowd went quiet in respectful awe.

The party discussed whether they should personally handle this but decided against it, settling on delegating.

[[buumi|Buumi]] was shocked.

[[buumi|Buumi]] admonished the group, pointing out that they had also refused to save [[veygar|Veygar]]'s crew, and asked why they were acting this way.

Several of the group felt guilty, and when [[buumi|Buumi]] stated that he would look for the child himself, the group relented and decided to assist.

They entered the farm to check it out and see if anything could lead them to the child.

Thinking about scrying on him, several headed upstairs to search the child's room, a shared space for all the family save the parents.

Searching the area, they found a handful of copper and a blunt iron knife under a floorboard beneath the child's bed.

In the rafters, a small window was open for ventilation. [[patches|Patches]] hopped up and noted something: claw marks.

Throwing down a rope, [[gundren-rockseeker|Gundren]] was hauled into the rafters to look at the claw marks himself, and he confirmed what they were all suspecting: Oni.

[[tark|Tark]] grabbed the child's scent and the group headed out east, toward the river.

They found footprints after about 500 meters — the Oni touching down from its flight — which continued all the way to the shallow river heading out of the Moonwood.

Here the group figured it had used the river to hide its tracks, so [[patches|Patches]] headed upstream while the others went downstream.

[[patches|Patches]] quickly decided north was the wrong way and followed her gut, heading south and rejoining the group.

They continued south for some time following the river.

[[tark|Tark]] used his staff to fly and get a good lay of the land, noting a basin — a forested dale nestled within some rolling hills.

At the center was a clearing with flowing water and, at the center of that, a massive standing stone.

The group recognized it as a hallowed area, a holy site that fiends, undead, and similar creatures could not enter.

[[fey|Fey]] pointed out that any creature possessed that was brought here would be freed of possession.

[[patches|Patches]] started — a solution to a problem resolving in her head: with her wish spell contained in her sword, she could wish [[cercei|Cercei]] here and free her from [[corpsetaker|Corpsetaker]].

Looking across the sunken grove, [[tark|Tark]] noted a harsh line between the lush stand of trees and wildflowers and the rocky scree fallen from the sheer drop into the cul-de-sac.

Nestled among the rocky overhang, [[tark|Tark]] spotted movement from a deep shadow, a crack in the base of the stone cliff.

From a thin cave crawled a multi-armed Oni, covered in blood and skittering across the stone.

Held on its back by a set of arms was a weeping human child.

[[tark|Tark]] immediately returned to the group, warned them, and they rushed to save the child under the cover of Pass without Trace.

As they went, [[patches|Patches]] once again accidentally stepped into the Border Ethereal Plane.

While there, she saw the spirit of Theodemir, who was surprised to see her.

They discussed and decided it was a turn of events to be explored at a later date, and [[patches|Patches]] returned to the prime material.

Just in time to watch the group absolutely demolish the Oni.

[[cirrus-al-kayan|Cirrus]] paralyzed it, [[gundren-rockseeker|Gundren]] point-blank shot it three times, [[tark|Tark]] rescued the child, and [[fey|Fey]]'s ghostly hand ripped out its soul.

The thing fell dead, a blasted mummified mess.
