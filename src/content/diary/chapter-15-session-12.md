## The Duel

The ghoul horde surged forward and split — part of it crashing toward JmJm, the rest throwing itself at the Copper Dragon on the slope. [[gundren-rockseeker|Gundren]] took careful aim at one of the spellcasting [[red-wizards-of-thay|Red Wizards]] and fired. The shot detonated across a Dome of Force, leaving the Thayan summoner entirely unharmed. Gundren called out the protection to the group and shifted his aim to [[gaddock|Gaddock]], where the vampire monk was trading blows with [[tarkathee-daru|Tark]] in the dark below the tree line.

[[tarkathee-daru|Tark]] had the [[sunsword|Sunsword]] in hand. He struck at his master, and Gaddock drew his own weapon in answer — the staff of black crystal, the same instrument the group had first heard described by Mister Luko's injured agent in House Muskov. It glowed green as Gaddock attacked. The blow missed — Tark was too quick, too disciplined, every lesson his master had ever taught him now turned against the man who had taught them. The staff's tip touched the earth instead and disintegrated a ten-foot hole in the valley floor.

Tark counterattacked. He struck not at the body but at the crystal jutting from Gaddock's skull — the horn, the leash — and tried to wrench it free.

The moment his fingers touched the crystal, the valley disappeared.

## The Vision

He was somewhere cold. Impossibly cold, the kind of cold that lived in stone for centuries before it moved into the air. A vast chamber of black crystal, formations rising from the floor like a frozen court, and at its centre a throne carved from the same dark material — and on the throne, unmoving, radiating power from every line of its iron frame, a skeleton.

Not a skeleton as death leaves them. An iron skeleton, preserved and deliberate, entombed in ancient ice that had grown up around the throne over long years. Every joint and surface glowed with the cold light of active magic, and from somewhere in the dark beyond the throne came the sound of spellcasting — a measured, inhuman voice speaking words of power across what felt like a vast distance.

Tark understood it in the same instant. The crystal in Gaddock's skull was not merely a leash. It was a channel. [[zhengyi|Zhengyi]] — the lich, the ancient spellcaster who had driven the Elder Orb from Ythryn, who commanded the Floating Castle above ruined Palishuk, who had turned this good man into a vampire and pointed him at his own student — was casting through Gaddock from his throne of black crystal many miles to the north. His master was not merely controlled. He had been made into a familiar.

The vision released him. The valley came back.

## The Wound

[[tarkathee-daru|Tark]]'s [[sunsword|Sunsword]] found Gaddock's undead flesh, and the holy blade cleaved. The vampire howled — and then shadowstepped, vanishing from the open slope and reappearing within the protective dome of the nearest Thayan's Force bubble. The dome sealed around him. Tark looked at the barrier and at the other vampire still in the field and made his decision without hesitation: he went through the dome after his master.

The battle outside continued without him. A second [[red-wizards-of-thay|Red Wizard]] revealed herself, floating in the forest above the tree line, and immediately drew fire. [[cassian|Cassian]]'s bow sang. [[aliyah-faen-tlabbar|Patches]]' crossbow answered beside it. The shots found their mark, breaking the woman's concentration and collapsing the Dome of Force that had been shielding the Thayan summoner. She screamed in rage and blasted at Tark before [[gundren-rockseeker|Gundren]]'s careful aim ended her.

[[lord-aimu|Lord Aimu]] summoned a griffon mount from thin air and charged with his remaining paladins, blessing [[feanon-serinde|Fëanon]] as he passed and hurling a Banishment at two Oni that had materialised in the trees, firing Nightmare Rays from the darkness. The Crimson Dawn paladins flanked the wizard, two of them pressing in close as an escort and loosing their longbows across the field.

## Master and Student

Inside the dome, [[tarkathee-daru|Tark]] fought [[gaddock|Gaddock]] alone.

They had trained together for years. Tark knew every combination his master had ever drilled into him, every tell, every preferred angle of attack. Gaddock knew the same things about his student. What neither of them had trained for was this — the grief of it, the terrible intimacy of knowing the person you were trying to kill and having them know you in return. The black crystal in Gaddock's skull moved against the light as they traded blows. Whatever lived behind the lich's magic watched the duel from a great distance with the patient attention of something that had been waiting a long time.

They dealt terrible wounds to one another. The crystal staff and the sunblade crossed again and again in the dark of the dome until [[tarkathee-daru|Tark]] stumbled — his body finally giving up the ground his will had refused to yield. He went down. His master closed over him and bent to the wound at his throat, feeding again, pulling the blood from him with the slow efficiency of a man who had stopped feeling what he was doing.

[[jack-mayer-johnson-mathews|JmJm]] rushed forward. The Arcane Hand — the great translucent force that had been at the bard's side since he pulled the [[mysterious-glove|Mysterious Glove]] onto his hand — slammed into Gaddock with everything JmJm could give it.

The vampire exploded into ash.

## The Knife

The ash had not settled before the [[vampire-knight-of-frostmoore|Vampire Knight]] appeared above the prone form of [[tarkathee-daru|Tark]].

He came from wherever the night kept him — invisible, then not, the black armour materialising out of the dark an instant before the blade came down. He drove it into the halfling's heart with the unhurried precision of someone completing a task.

[[tarkathee-daru|Tark]] coughed blood. His eyes went wide. He died.

He saw his body fall from somewhere slightly above it. His friends scrambled toward him across the ruined estate, their souls blazing in the Ethereal Plane — each of them a different quality of light, warm and urgent and moving fast. The [[vampire-knight-of-frostmoore|Vampire Knight]] was something else entirely here: a dark abyss of power, a shape that drank the light around it rather than reflecting it.

But Tark's gaze was pulled south-east. Toward the city of [[helioglabulus|Helioglabulus]], distant on the dark horizon. He could sense [[gaddock|Gaddock]]'s spirit — his master's soul, freed at last from the body that had imprisoned it — fleeing in that direction, drawn toward something vast and dark that dwelled in the city. The thing whispered. The lingering taint of vampirism in Tark's blood let him hear it clearly: a voice, cold and sovereign, calling the newly-dead home.

He watched his master's soul disappear into the ether, returning to his Queen.

## The Smoke

[[horatio-von-bumidere|Buumi]] screamed.

The rage that came out of the paladin was not the measured fury of combat. It was grief, weaponised. He charged the [[vampire-knight-of-frostmoore|Vampire Knight]] and a smite blazed white across the vampire's vision, blinding him — and in that same instant [[aliyah-faen-tlabbar|Patches]] was already moving.

She dropped a smoke bomb. The valley filled with grey. She found [[tarkathee-daru|Tark]]'s body in the smoke by feel and by the shape of him, and she put him in the [[bag-of-holding|Bag of Holding]] with the careful speed of someone who had decided what mattered and was not going to let anyone stop her.

[[jack-mayer-johnson-mathews|JmJm]] turned the Arcane Hand and drove it through the smoke like a battering ram. Patches leapt onto it and the great force-hand carried her clear of the smoke and across the trampled lawn toward [[lord-aimu|Lord Aimu]], who had already seen what was happening. He reared his griffon mount and threw himself from the saddle with a large diamond in his fist, landing in front of the rogue just as she produced the body.

The gem pulsed. The lord knelt over the fallen halfling and poured the Revivify into him with the steady hands of a man who had done this before and intended to do it correctly. [[tarkathee-daru|Tark]] drew a sputtering breath. [[horatio-von-bumidere|Buumi]] was already at his side with healing before the breath had finished, closing the worst of the damage with a paladin's disciplined grace.

The monk was alive. Barely, and unhappily, but alive.

## The Gate

[[lady-isabel|Izmakella]] tore through the ghouls that had surrounded her with swift contempt and turned her attention to the spellcasting Thayans. The battle was turning.

Then a voice rolled across the valley — deep, resonant, thick with power — and the [[red-wizards-of-thay|Thayan]] leader finished his incantation.

A Gate opened in the air above the estate. Through it stepped a Balor: a towering black shape of burning flesh, its lower body a ring of jaws and teeth where a stomach would have been, every mouth laughing independently in a cacophony of razored sound. It carried a great hammer that crackled with electricity from head to haft, and its passage through the Gate left a trail of ash and embers and scorched ground wherever its footsteps fell.

The Gate stayed open. Other shapes began to take form within it.

The Thayan leader laughed. He addressed [[cirrus-al-kayan|Cirrus]] directly — mocking the warlock, promising to slay him and offer his teeth to their lord. He would live again, the wizard said. And then he said the other thing, the thing that recontextualised the whole of his presence in the Bloodstone Lands: he was one of the Warlocks of [[ceregorach|Ceregorach]]. One of two remaining.

[[feanon-serinde|Fëanon]] caught the shape of the monologue and moved on it before the man had finished speaking. A Hypnotic Pattern bloomed across the Thayan's position just as [[cirrus-al-kayan|Cirrus]] released a Synaptic Static into the same space. The Thayan leader cried out — his concentration shattered — and the Gate blinked out of existence, the half-formed shapes within it collapsing back into wherever Gates went when the spell that held them was broken.

The Balor remained.

Its greater invisibility, however, was not the leader's spell. Someone else in the field was holding that. The group turned their attention across the burning estate, seeking the second concentration, while the Dragon and the paladins and [[horatio-von-bumidere|Buumi]] collapsed onto the greater demon. [[lord-aimu|Lord Aimu]] levelled a glowing mace from his griffon's back and rode at the fiend, a Banishment already forming on his lips.

The Balor's belly-mouths laughed at all of it. Its flesh had begun to crack and glow where [[lady-isabel|Izmakella]]'s slow breath had worked into it — the demon burning from within, furious and delighted, swinging its hammer in great arcs that shook the ground each time they fell.

The second half of the battle was joined.
