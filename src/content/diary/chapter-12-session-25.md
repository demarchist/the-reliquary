## The Druid Awakens

They woke on the northern shores of the Throat of Al Hawia. [[Jareem]] studied the unconscious [[Doctor Breakfast|Doc]] with his psionics and confirmed what [[Cirrus Al-Kayan|Cirrus]] and [[Feanon Serinde|Feanon]] suspected — a ninth-level Dream spell was keeping the druid asleep.

[[Horatio Von Bumidere|Buumi]] knelt and prayed to Helm and [[Karthus]]. [[Jack Mayer-Johnson Mathews|JmJm]] joined him, and together their combined divine entreaty disrupted the spell enough for Doc to stir. The gnome yawned dramatically, blinked at the gathered faces, and accepted breakfast while the group explained Al Askar, [[Vecna]], wild magic, and everything else that had happened since the [[Amnians|Amnian]] spelljammer.

Jareem had a theory about the Dream spell. In all his centuries on this world, he had watched Vecna's forces systematically assassinate every druid they could find. The entire world was out of balance, and druids would have been best equipped to fix it — which was precisely why Vecna hunted them down. The Dream spell on Doc was likely a targeted attempt to remove or kill the druid.

## The Obsidian Order

Jareem revealed more. He was not alone in his fight. He had been working with the elves of this land and their secret society: the [[Obsidian Order]]. He was not its leader but sat on its council, and the Order could assist the group if they sought it out.

When pressed on what the Order had accomplished in centuries of resistance, Jareem looked uncomfortable and spoke in hushed tones. He and the elves had managed to cleanse the Temple of Horrah some time ago — but it had been kept secret. The goddess was feigning her continued malady so as not to alert Vecna that she was free to act. Horrah alone could not stop Vecna, but with the other gods freed from corruption, they might stand a chance.

Feanon nodded. It made sense that the elves would have been working on something.

## The Flight to Iyum

They broke camp and took flight, Jareem bearing them across the final stretch toward [[Iyum]]. The altitude made the wind cold, but the twin suns created punishing dehydration. They spotted carrion birds circling a wrecked ship dashed against the rocky coast — except the birds were gargantuan, eighty-foot wingspans, with smaller eagle-cat creatures darting in for scraps.

Doc shapeshifted into an eagle and negotiated passage with the great vultures. The group searched for survivors but found none — only chests of money, art, noble clothing, and silks. JmJm found a particularly magnificent turban. They took the treasure and resumed the journey.

Before long, the fires of humanoid settlement appeared on the horizon, and the city of Iyum resolved itself in the distance. Titanic stone edifices now inhabited by common folk, ringed by a massive wall and divided into districts — an impressive harbour, a great palace built into a small mesa, and a black stone tower jutting nearby. Ancient beyond measure, the city's roads were worn with twin ruts from a thousand years of carts far smaller than the Titans who had built them.

Jareem circled wide, landed behind a dune, and shifted down. The group walked the last miles along the ancient road.

## The Speaker in the Plaza

They entered through the Titans' Gate and onto Caravan Way. At the gate, Buumi mentioned [[Liara]] and [[Djoser]] and was waved through — the guards assumed the group was part of "the excavation." Liara was in the slums dealing with a blight outbreak; Djoser was in the Brickyard, the industrial district, hiring workers.

Moving through the Merchants' District and into the edge of the Noble Quarter, [[Aliyah Faen Tlabbar|Patches]] attempted to steal gems from a shop and found, alarmingly, that she could not filch the lustre of cut stones — a troubling development.

They reached a plaza filled with people watching a speaker on a wooden stage. A man in black robes — a Speaker of Zalaam — preached to the crowd, flanked by cultists and a young woman. The world was ruined, he declared, and must be ended and reborn by Zalaam. The crowd seemed half-convinced. A cultist raised a knife above the girl.

Cirrus summoned his wings and leapt into the air, calling out against the speaker. Guards moved on the outskirts of the plaza, led by a Tabaxi. Tensions rose as the warlock and the cultist argued — and the crowd hung in the balance.
