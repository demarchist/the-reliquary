## The Game

The group found a promising inn named *Her Gift* and marvelled at the exquisite otherworldly grandeur of the extraplanar city. As they approached, [[Tarkathee Daru|Tark]] felt someone brush against him in the crowd. Three Drow guards in gleaming scale armour barrelled toward him, clearly intent on tackling the halfling.

Tark smiled and waved.

The lead guard snarled — *I have you now, Igni!* — and leapt. Where Tark had been standing, a Svirfneblin none of them recognised had appeared, an illusion shifting Tark's appearance. His displacement cloak shifted his position by inches, and the guard missed the tackle. So did the next two.

[[Aliyah Faen Tlabbar|Patches]] spotted a duplicate of Tark shifting off into the crowd and gave chase. [[Gundren Rockseeker|Gundren]] followed. They found the ingenious gnome climbing a building facade, his skin camouflaged to match the stone. Patches threw a dagger at his fingers pommel-first. The gnome fell but landed on his feet. Gundren sprayed him with Grease. The guards hurled a magical bola and tangled his feet — but the gnome flopped onto his belly and slid like a penguin through the slippery mess, leaving the guards flailing.

He came to rest at [[Feanon Serinde|Feanon]]'s feet and asked for a hand. They cut him free. He pressed a slate card into Feanon's palm — engraved with the name *Igni* — and darted into the crowd.

An onlooker explained: *The Game*. The whole confrontation had been non-violent, almost theatrical. Nearby diners raised their glasses. The guards seemed more amused than angry.

## The Dragon's Hoard

The inn was nearly full. A raucous group of Kenku filled the common area — their mimicked speech a cacophony of words plucked from different sources and stitched together, their laughter a patchwork of stolen *ha's*. A robed Hobgoblin wrote quietly in a corner. An eight-foot brass-and-glass cylinder stood near the bar, attended by a cloaked figure polishing the glass. Something moved within the opaque liquid.

The innkeeper explained that due to current festivities, payment required something more *interesting* than coin. A Silver Dragon was coming to Dokkar to ascend, and the entire city was competing to furnish her lair with the finest hoard. Every citizen was donating treasures. Igni was likely a spy for a second dragon — a Blue — coming to lair in nearby [[Nidavellir]], tended by the dwarves and gnomes. The real competition was between the two hoards.

The group paid for an exquisite suite of rooms with a coalesced memory orb of Patches finding the Jun Sahara of Al Askar — uncursed Drow from the ancient Illythiri line. This proved extraordinarily valuable. They retired to marble rooms overlooking the tiered city.

## The Endless Stair

The next morning, they ascended to the higher tiers to find [[Finn|Finn Faen Tlabbar]]. Feanon scried on the young wizard and felt a divine agent read his intent — determining it to be non-hostile before allowing the spell to proceed.

Finn was in the gardens by the Endless Stair.

The interior of the great stalagmite that formed Dokkar's core was hollowed into a vast cavern of gardens — stalactites hanging from the ceiling, stained glass windows glowing within white marble gothic architecture. Lawns and groves were surrounded by couches and lounge chairs. Poets composed in whispers, paintbrushes moved on canvas, dancers swirled through the parks. Everyone was creating art for the Silver Dragon's hoard.

At the centre of the gardens, a hole several hundred feet across opened into the earth. An ancient set of marble stairs spiralled down into the depths — the Endless Stair. Waterways from the gardens disgorged a steady mist into the descent. A crowd had gathered at the top, where a Drow woman in flowing silver robes stood motionless, staring down the stairs.

Finn was at the edge of the crowd. He spotted the group, leapt up, and rushed over.

Dokkar was wonderful, he said, but odd for the living. Time passed strangely. There were almost no children. The older elves had a slow regard of all things — some barely registering the world around them. The woman at the stair was one such dreamer, over ten thousand years old, taking her last journey down to Yggward to slumber among the ancient souls.

At the bottom of the Stair lay [[Nidavellir]], where the planes met — Pandemonium, the [[Shadowfell]], the [[Astral Plane]], Ysgard, and others. On the border of Nidavellir and [[Svartalfheim]] sat the great Twilight Cathedral at the gates of Yggward, where petitioners could speak to the slumbering Drow of the necropolis.

Finn shivered and looked away. He would very much like to return to Faerûn.

## The Night Market

Patches thought it over. Her mother was still an extreme threat — [[Finn|Finn's]] safety required careful planning. For now, the group split up to shop in Dokkar's Night Market.

[[Horatio Von Bumidere|Buumi]] and Patches went on a date — street food and obsidian crystals. Buumi purchased a massive raw lump of obsidian for delivery to the Barony, and Patches picked up a platinum and onyx card case for her [[Deck of Illusions]], intending to gift it to Finn for his protection.

Tark found a bookshop and bought a book called *Tres Liches* to research [[Zhengyi]]. He could not read very well, but he was determined.

Feanon and Finn browsed bookshops together — a scroll of Slow for Finn, a book on glowing architecture for Feanon.

Gundren sought something ineffable — a material to bring the Bloodrinker crossbow together. He examined exotic materials including angel feathers before finding a bar of **Aurumn** — enchanted gold vaporised by a Gold Dragon and collected with a Wish spell. He traded the power core of the Amnian Spelljammer and a large Sourcestone Fabrial for it. Then he visited the Hall of Hunters, where a grizzled ranger named Korin showed him a bone bow encrusted with emeralds — Lok'Delar, from the last Crown War, crafted from Sandpiper Wyvern bone by a bowcrafter named Opheli Lu, who now slumbered in Yggward.

Feanon cast Legend Lore and confirmed: Opheli Lu slept far below, in the necropolis. If the group was willing to descend the Endless Stair — through Nidavellir, to where shadow, thought, and chaos met — they could ask the ancient crafter how she had made her masterwork.

## The Githzerai

As Tark left the market, he spotted the Illithid he had seen earlier in the gardens — a Remembrancer, seemingly tolerated by the citizens.

Then he saw a green-faced figure in azure robes dart into an alleyway.

Curious, Tark followed. A Gith slipped from shadow to shadow ahead. Thinking he might have a lead on the Githyanki Psion Heart he needed for his Windrunner armour, Tark pursued — and rounded a corner into an ambush. The unarmed man struck at his pressure points, trying to stun him.

Tark deflected the blows easily. He told his attacker he was looking for a Githyanki Psion.

The figure corrected him. He was not Githyanki. He was Githzerai. He watched the *Gaichk* — then threw a flash bomb and vanished.

Tark returned to the inn with his book, no rare materials, but two potential leads.

The group reconvened over wine and food, surrounded by curious Drow, noisy Kenku, and extraplanar visitors. Patches and Gundren both had reasons to descend the Endless Stair. The necropolis of Yggward called.
