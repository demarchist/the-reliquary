## The Grand Hall and the Murals

The grand hall of the [[Sanctum of Krasus]] lay before them, frost-covered and littered with debris. The group split to investigate.

[[Tarkathee Daru]] and [[Horatio von Bumidere]] checked three smaller doors at the top of the chamber — each so thoroughly encrusted in ice that getting through would require smashing either door or ice. [[Fëanon Serinde]] and [[Megno Slatesunder]] went to one of the murals while [[Aliyah Faen Tlabbar]] headed for another. [[Cirrus Al Kayan]] spotted a smaller open door leading into darkness and sent his spider familiar skittering through to investigate. [[Gundren Rockseeker]] busied himself with field repairs on [[Gwen]].

Over the next several minutes, they studied the murals and determined that each represented a school of magic. At the centre of every mural, a precious symbol corresponded with one of the slots on the obelisk at the Sanctum's entrance. Patches eyed the symbols with professional interest, but a stern look from Megno discouraged her — only if the Sanctum was actually going to explode would he condone defacing the ruin. Patches sighed and moved on.

Tark and Cirrus peered into the darkened room the spider had scouted — a long-ruined kitchen with a pantry, ancient butcher's bones lying in a frost-covered pile. A horrible scraping sound split the air behind them. The group spun to see Gwen tearing through the leftmost iced-over door at Gundren's command. The ice and door shredded in moments, but a burst of frost cascaded over the automaton — a glyph trap that seized her gears. Gundren, Tark, and Megno hauled her back.

## The Platinum Chest and the Berbalang

Patches moved through the double doors at the far end of the hall, leaping the threshold she expected to be trapped. Sure enough — a glyph on the floor, which she called Fëanon over to disarm. Beyond lay a T-junction hallway with two large white marble statues at the junction, conspicuous against the black granite. Patches inspected them thoroughly and concluded they were golems, probably tied to the now-destroyed glyph. Their feet were not connected to their bases.

The rest of the group filtered in. Two large sets of golden double doors led deeper. Patches picked the mundane lock on the first — its enchantment had corroded over millennia — but missed the tripwire. A dart skimmed her hand, barely breaking the skin. It burned.

They opened the door wider and found a treasure room. Intact bookshelves flanked a ruined four-poster bed, with a glittering, perfect platinum trunk at its foot. A frosted door with glass windows let in feeble light at the far end. Tark shadowstepped onto a bookcase — and a shadow lunged from beneath it. Two more emerged from under the bed and the corner. The claws raked into Tark, but between Fëanon's bolts, Patches' blades, and the rest of the group piling in, the shadows were dispatched quickly.

Tark opened the windowed door and struggled through the encrusting ice to find a small balcony in a crevasse on the mountainside, the rock ending twenty feet away where swirling clouds took over. Fëanon and Megno found fifty intact tomes on the bookshelves — the shelves themselves enchanted for preservation. While inspecting the platinum chest, Fëanon slipped on ice. Lying on his back, he noticed a small lockbox under the ruined bed and reached for it. Patches dashed over and stepped on his hand. She found the box sitting on a pressure plate, located a piece of ancient iron, and swapped the two — thwarting the trap.

Inside the lockbox they found truly exotic materials: a pound of Prismeum, a marble of refined Prismeum, and two chits of Glacium. Gundren scraped gold filigree from the ruined furniture.

They returned to the platinum chest. The enchanted lock had faded with time and Fëanon broke it easily. The lid rose to reveal nine spell scrolls — three made of solid gold and capped in shimmering metal — a red silk sash, and three books, the last of which was a spellbook. Gundren had Gwen shred the magnificent chest for raw materials once they had emptied it.

Patches had already moved to the second set of doors. She picked the lock and opened without incident. The room beyond was in far worse shape — no surviving furniture except a lectern. At that lectern, a creature hunched over a book, flipping through pages. It looked like an oversized imp — gangly arms, folded wings, large eyes peering from an elongated head.

Patches threw a flash bomb at its feet. The creature shrieked as it was blinded and she rushed in with tripwires, hogtying it before the rest of the group arrived. The creature — furious, swearing, and thoroughly restrained — introduced itself as [[Mervklo]]. During its ramblings it mentioned a "Dream Orb" (which the group took to mean a Beholder) and "the others" (likely the ghosts haunting this place). The Beholder was responsible for dreaming the smaller beholder-kin into existence. Mervklo had come from the [[Astral Plane]] to study the book for two weeks without success.

The group examined the book. Though written in Netherese, they could see text — unlike the creature, which found this deeply annoying and stopped talking entirely. Not wanting to kill Mervklo but unwilling to leave a potential enemy behind them, they kept him hogtied on a Tenser's floating disk.

## The Mold and the Beholder

Heading back through the grand hall with Mervklo in tow, the group approached the iced-over doors. Intense cold radiated from the one Gwen had shredded. Tark spotted the cause — a massive patch of brown mold. Remembering their training, they knew intense cold would kill it. Megno cleared a path with ray of frost, then Cirrus stepped into position, thumped his staff on the floor, and unleashed a cone of cold down the entire hallway. The brown mold was annihilated, the corridor glittering with an even thicker layer of ice.

Patches moved to the nearest large door, picked the lock, cracked it open, and immediately shut it. Her eyes were wide. A floating head with ten tendrils, each ending in an eye, a great central orb above a toothy maw — a Beholder.

The group weighed their options. Minutes ticked by with no attack. Patches mentioned a silver light inside. Cirrus decided to try diplomacy, with Buumi as bodyguard. They entered. The creature simply watched. Cirrus realised it was trapped within a beautifully constructed magic circle and would need help to escape.

On a whim, they brought Mervklo in. The moment the Beholder's gaze fell on the Berbalang and the floating disk, both flashed out of existence — the antimagic cone dispelling both constructs simultaneously. The group found this peculiar but kept moving. They looked around, found some small baubles, and left — closing the door on the utterly infuriated Beholder as it swore to consume and flay them.

Three smaller doors remained opposite the ice-encrusted ones, and another set of golden doubles. The first small room was an acolyte's quarters, the furniture destroyed. They found a small brass pot with a faint magical emanation — a chamber pot that once removed waste via magic. They took it, thinking reverse-engineering could prove useful. The second door's frame was cracked, the ceiling held up only by the door itself. Gundren confirmed that opening it would collapse the room. Gwen clawed a small hole through which they spotted a magnificent greataxe gleaming among the refuse. Tark shadowstepped through, grabbed the axe, and dodged falling blocks as the ceiling caved in, shadowstepping back into the hall just as the room filled with rubble. The axe had a diamond-shaped head, a carved ivory haft, and a blue silk-wrapped handle with a flowing cord.

The third small door — Patches opened it, saw another floating head with writhing eye-covered tentacles, and shut the door instantly. Another beholder. Cirrus went back to the first one and told it the second was far superior. The trapped beholder became incensed, screaming that the other was merely a Gauth, not a true beholder. Armed with this information, the group locked the Gauth's door with an arcane ward and an alarm spell, deciding not to bother.

Behind the final golden doors, Patches found red cloth among the debris. Shadows flickered at the room's edges. She carefully extracted a magnificent set of robes and withdrew.

## The Ghost of Thesia

Back at the marble statues in the T-junction, the group found a small button in the wall between them. It opened to reveal a dark stone staircase ascending to a barely visible landing.

They missed the glyph trap. Patches and Gundren stepped onto the landing and a marble statue swung a stone fist at the rogue. The group piled in — Tark shadowstepped above it, landed a blow, darted away. Focused fire dismantled the golem in moments, though it landed several powerful hits. On the next landing they found and destroyed the glyph before triggering the second golem.

The second floor was oddly shaped — a hallway with two doors opposite each other, a third further in, and beyond that, the room opened up with debris and detritus visible from the stairs. The first room had caved in. The second was unlocked. Fëanon looked inside and saw the spectral form of a woman crying over destroyed laboratory equipment. He moved in to speak. The ghost spun, cried out in Netherese — and vanished into him.

Fëanon bolted from the room, running deeper into the complex. Tark saw the exchange and assumed the worst. He tackled the elf. The ghost flashed from Fëanon into Tark. The possessed monk stood and sprinted toward a large spiral staircase. Patches tripped and tackled him, pinning him from behind.

Tark babbled in Netherese as Buumi charged after them, ploughing through an explosion of fire as he accidentally triggered a glyph. The paladin stumbled out the other side reddened and singed but intact, reaching his friend and channelling the power of Helm to drive the spirit from Tark's body.

## Promethi Arrives

Just as the group gathered around Tark, a ghostly form emerged from the darkness in the room behind them. Twisting shadow and tattered robes, hatred radiating from its undead form. The spectre shimmered and split into four images of itself.

"Excellent," it whispered, the word slithering through the cold air. "Guests."
