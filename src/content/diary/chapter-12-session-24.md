## The Death Lord's Last Stand

The battle's tide turned, but the [[Saurians|Saurian]] Death Lord had tricks remaining. He raised his gauntleted hand — the sourcestone embedded in it blazing — and hurled a fiery orb of purple and dark swirling energy at the group. [[Feanon Serinde|Feanon]] reached out to counterspell but found the magic could not be countered. The detonation burned the group badly and broke [[Thorna]]'s concentration on her banishment spell. A Retriever blinked back into existence.

The Death Lord dispelled [[Gundren Rockseeker|Gundren]]'s Daylight gemstone, plunging the temple back toward shadow, then savaged [[Horatio Von Bumidere|Buumi]] and collapsed his Aura of Purity.

[[Cirrus Al-Kayan|Cirrus]] saw his companions faltering. He released his own banishment — the second Retriever returned — then leapt into the air on dark wings and erupted with a Holy Aura, reinvigorating his allies and searing the undead. The group rallied. Healing spells flew. The damage dealers surrounded the Death Lord.

The nearest Fae-touched Tyrannosaurus Rex blocked the Retrievers, but the metal spiders paralysed it with beams of magic. [[Tarkathee Daru|Tark]] destroyed the Death Lord's last Mirror Image, but the Haste spell persisted, keeping the ancient lord faster than any of them.

As the last of his Boneclaws fell, the Death Lord attempted to teleport. Feanon counterspelled it. The group poured everything they had into the Saurian lord — then he stood, hissed, and teleported again. This time, Feanon could not stop it. The Death Lord vanished into the aether.

The remaining undead disintegrated under the group's holy auras. Then, in rapid succession, the Retrievers blinked out of existence and the dinosaurs vanished in a burst of wild magic. The temple fell silent.

## Trust and Friction

Tark marched to [[Jareem]] and punched the dragon in the face. He demanded to know if this had been a setup — and why Jareem had not shifted into his draconic form for the battle. Jareem barely registered the blow but acknowledged Tark's frustration. He explained that the temple's architecture made his dragon form impractical, that shapeshifting was no trivial effort, and that jumping between forms mid-battle was exhausting. Tark was not satisfied and stormed off to the healers.

The group split. Buumi, [[Aliyah Faen Tlabbar|Patches]], Cirrus, and Feanon rested in a magnificent mansion conjured against the temple's outer wall, while the others descended with Jareem to begin the cleansing.

## The Trials Below

The first trial was a maze of fire walls. Gundren and Jareem, both immune to fire, navigated it with methodical precision and guided the others through.

The second level presented a chamber with lava for a floor, save for a central dais. Switches around the perimeter had to be depressed simultaneously — trivial with two fire-immune members walking across molten stone.

Before they descended to the third level, Jareem asked the group to step back. The next chamber was the trial of strength, and the group was wounded. It was time for him to change shape.

A flash of ruby light. The genasi's form twisted and expanded. Crackling stone and glittering crystal growths erupted outward, and a massive Ruby Crystal Dragon stood where a man had been.
