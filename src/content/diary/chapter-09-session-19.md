## The Apprentice

[[feanon-serinde|Feanon]] was woken from his Reverie by a knock. [[beryan|Beryan]] the Sage was already walking off with a bundle of books, calling back that his apprentice was waiting in the lobby.

It took Feanon a moment to process the words. He rushed to dress and took the elevator down. A young High Elf stood near Guiseppe's front desk, wearing school robes in disrepair, a class ring, and a noble pendant.

Feanon gave the lad a look and accurately predicted his backstory: newly graduated from the [[silverymoon|Silverymoon]] mages' academy, a few years of adventuring behind him, now seeking a master. [[ruthir|Ruthir]] K'Ree wished for that master to be Feanon.

Feanon was thrilled — and didn't show it, performing the part of a wise and measured mentor. He accepted the youth despite the lack of a letter of recommendation and took the offered tuition: a broken Ring of Spell Turning from Myth Drannor. He showed Ruthir to chambers one level below the great Library hall. The young elf got to work immediately.

## Tark's Dreams

[[tarkathee-daru|Tark]] had been enjoying himself in the woods, the bear cub trailing behind him as he trained and meditated. He crossed paths with [[doc|Doc B]] on one of the little druid's walks. Doc mentioned casually that he knew the location of the treehouse. Concerned, Tark extracted a promise of secrecy.

Doc invited him to come by the Crystal Cave to see [[cercei|Cercei]] the next day. Tark agreed. He visited [[horatio-von-bumidere|Buumi]] at the [[keep-of-thunder-bluff|keep]] that evening — they shared a drink and food, Buumi showing off new cooking skills. Tark asked if the paladin would come to [[blingdenstone|Blingdenstone]]; Buumi said he would if wanted. Tark thanked him but said it wouldn't be necessary. Buumi mentioned he would be leaving for a few days to visit his brother and take Patches' book to a printer — Tark correctly guessed she wanted a copy for each member of the group.

That night, Tark dreamed the familiar dream — his parents, his childhood room. This time he took control. He turned his head and finally saw what cast the brilliant reflections across the ceiling: in the hearth, at the heart of a roaring fire, a large crystalline egg. He reached in and touched it. A friendly, curious presence brushed his mind and warmth flooded his hand.

He woke. He thought on it. The next morning he visited Doc and had a breakfast of Goodberries, saw that Cercei was slowly healing, and then returned to prepare for departure.

## Bytopia

[[cirrus-al-kayan|Cirrus]] tried the [[well-of-many-worlds|Well of Many Worlds]] once more. This time the portal cast golden light.

He stepped up and beheld an alpine meadow on a mountainside falling away to rolling hills, plains, and meandering rivers of idyllic beauty. Up the slope: a grand mountain of ice-sheets and glaciers. But instead of open sky above the peaks — another landmass, inverted. Gravity reversed above the mountains, some connected by great columns of ice. The lower land was warm, amber and red as if in perpetual autumn. The upper was verdant, wet, and stormy.

Cirrus stepped through and closed the portal. He stood on a well-travelled cobblestone trail carved into the mountainside, grass growing between the bricks. Great elk, marmots, and golden eagles populated the slopes. His owl familiar spotted a massive tower carved into a distant peak, and farther along the path — barely distinguishable at that distance — a small caravan.

Two hours of walking brought him to a covered wagon manned by Rock Gnomes. When they learned he had no idea where he was, they grew concerned — ensuring he intended to be on this Plane of Existence. This was [[bytopia|Bytopia]], the Twin Paradises, afterlife of the Gnomes. They pointed up the road to a stone fortress: Stronghaven, domain of the Gnomish god Gaedral Ironhand.

The caravan leader, [[bildaboi|Bildaboi]], inspected the warlock and, satisfied, agreed to a trade: a planar tuning fork for a fork. Cirrus offered his fork for the Plane of Water. The leader was mildly disappointed — elemental forks were less valuable — but accepted in exchange for a future favour worth ten thousand gold. Cirrus took the solid gold fork and bid them farewell as they headed to their town of Rumblecuppse in the valley below.

## Mordenkainen

The final day before departure, Patches got a visitor.

Stepping into her private rooms beneath [[the-establishment|Over the Moon]], she sensed something amiss and slipped into the deeper shadows. A figure sat slumped in a chair by the fireplace. She recognized the robes: [[mordenkainen|Mordenkainen]]. His sentient staff Stick stood upright beside the chair, far more upbeat than its master but noting that if Patches got within two feet of Mordy, he'd have to *frag* her.

Patches stopped. The Archmage looked terrible. A minion of [[vecna|Vecna]] on [[oerth|Oerth]] had gotten a good shot — disintegrating half his body. He pointed out that she should see the other guy. He waved away the idea of a healer, tapping a ring of regeneration and the amniotic sack securing his insides. Three days, he said. He needed a place to lay low.

Patches showed him the rubbings from the Elven shrine beneath the [[chardalyn|Chardalyn]] mines. Together with Stick, Mordenkainen translated the ancient reliefs: Elven celestial history from before or during the Crown Wars. They depicted Abraxim, the arrogant Archmage, traversing the outer edge of existence and sneaking past the Watchers. He was consumed — or infected, the distinction unclear — by Halashnar, the Pallid Prince, and returned to the world as a vessel for a great evil. He changed all those he encountered until the Elves, led by Sehanine Moonbow — an aspect of [[selune|Selûne]] — defeated Halashnar and drove Abraxim away past great icy mountains.

Patches wondered if that meant the Spine of the World. Mordenkainen agreed it was a sound theory.

She mentioned the Barony's upcoming auction house. Mordy smiled — he was always interested in magical items.

## Return to Blingdenstone

The group teleported back to [[blingdenstone|Blingdenstone]] with Jin the painter and [[topsy-and-turvy|Topsy]] in tow, arriving in a room cleared of wild magic. Word spread quickly. Armed and armoured Gnomes marched through the city — much of the gear new and mythril. A dozen Earth Elementals patrolled the caverns.

Chief [[dorbo|Diggermattock]] and [[gurnik|Gurnik]] briefed them: a massive force of demons and oozes surrounded a building called the Dome, and the entire northern caverns were infested.

Patches nudged Cirrus. The warlock stepped forward and proclaimed he would visit the wererats and bring them to the cause, proffering Topsy as his emissary. Dorbo agreed.

## The Warden Tattoos

[[snorbul|Snorbul]] headed to [[gurnik|Gurnik]]'s shrine with Jin to meet Trisk. The next four hours were excruciating — Trisk, Gurnik, and Jin worked spell-gem ink into the root of a Warden's tattoos around the base of his skull and shoulders. The ink glittered under the skin, tiny pieces of gemstone catching the light. Snorbul grinned as new powers infused his body, his connection with the stone and earth growing stronger.

## Into the Warrens

The group headed north to visit the wererats. [[boulda|Boulda]], the girl they had saved from petrification, met them and admitted she was unsure she could have survived the trip alone. Topsy came along, shy but waving a greeting; Boulda returned a toothy grin.

At the edge of settled Blingdenstone, they watched a metal gate open and an Earth Elemental battle a lone Barlgura demon. Deep Gnomes rushed in with mythril spears, skewered the demon as the elemental grappled it, and returned to their posts. Beyond the tunnel, it was inhospitable.

On the other side: three oozes, dealt with quickly. A gelatinous cube devouring a Deep Gnome corpse, destroyed. Then the walls shifted — a dozen large oozes converging on new prey. Among the refuse, two wererat creatures battled a Barlgura and were losing.

The group leapt in. They saved the wererats, destroyed the oozes, and killed a Chasme demon that rushed around a corner — [[feanon-serinde|Feanon]] ripped the moisture from it with a necromantic spell before [[tarkathee-daru|Tark]] finished it off.

Boulda approached the wererat corpse as it began to regenerate, sputtering back to ragged breathing. [[cirrus-al-kayan|Cirrus]] healed it with the power of [[selune|Selûne]], the group crowding around as the suffering creature stabilised.
