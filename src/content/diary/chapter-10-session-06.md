[[feanon-serinde|Feanon]] had been so distracted by his personal project that he had not yet gone downstairs to meet [[kleve|Kleve]]. The personal project in question was finishing his clone.

In the laboratory, he went through the final checklist, examining the body floating in amniotic fluid. There was a minor divergence — a slight cleft palate, easily corrected — but physically the clone was sound. More important were its philotic connections to the Weave and to Psionics. Both were strong. The clone had come out in excellent condition.

[[feanon-serinde|Feanon]] contacted [[theodemir|Theodemir]] in the Ring of Mind Shielding and woke the spirit. The time had come — the body was ready. Using his burgeoning psionics, [[feanon-serinde|Feanon]] forged a link between the ring and the clone, creating a conduit through which [[theodemir|Theodemir's]] soul could flow. The clone's eyes twitched. [[feanon-serinde|Feanon]] pulled the release lever, draining the tank and jettisoning the body onto a padded mat. He threw a blanket over it and had an Unseen Servant carry the clone to a chair.

Over the next several minutes, [[theodemir|Theodemir]] began to exert control — slowly, shakily, but with improving confidence. [[feanon-serinde|Feanon]] assigned a servant to watch over him and headed to the lobby.

## Dinner with a Spider

The group had assembled in the lobby of [[feanons-tower|Feanon's tower]], ready to be taken to dine with [[vizerion-devir|Vizerion DeVir]]. Before they could leave, [[aliyah-faen-tlabbar|Patches]] pulled [[feanon-serinde|Feanon]] aside. Clearly stressed about the entire situation in the [[underdark|Underdark]], she warned him that whatever he thought he knew about the [[drow-faction|Drow]] wizard was only the tip of a very dangerous iceberg. [[feanon-serinde|Feanon]] chuckled and said he was prepared. [[aliyah-faen-tlabbar|Patches]] responded that if he thought he was prepared, he was not as wise as he believed. The wizard accepted a small modicum of humility and nodded.

They approached [[kleve|Kleve]], who had been patiently waiting. The Death Slaad became visible at their request and extended its hands — the fingers beginning to elongate unnervingly, regaining some of their natural proportions as [[kleve|Kleve]] explained they needed physical contact for the Planeshift. The party reluctantly complied.

A flash of light and a thunderclap of displaced air.

They found themselves not on the [[astral-plane|Astral Plane]] as warned, but in a dark stone reception chamber. Green light filtered through stained glass windows in a sharp, Gothic style — unmistakably [[drow-faction|Drow]] architecture, though stripped of the typical spider-themed embellishments. [[kleve|Kleve]], now in its true form, loped forward on elongated arms and pushed open a set of double doors, revealing a dining hall. [[vizerion-devir|Vizerion]] sat at the head of a long dark table in emerald green robes. He stood graciously and invited them to sit.

He addressed [[aliyah-faen-tlabbar|Patches]] directly, asking about her views on [[lolth|Lolth]]. She was not a fan. [[vizerion-devir|Vizerion]] smiled approvingly, then inquired about her [[piwafwi|Piwafwi]] — asking if she was of House Xorlarrin, the fourth house of [[menzoberranzan|Menzoberranzan]], known for their wizards. She was not. A clap brought half a dozen Unseen Servants bearing drinks and food. Another clap populated the table with exotic dishes — [[illithid-faction|Illithid]] tentacle calamari and Giant Spider legs among them.

After pleasantries, [[vizerion-devir|Vizerion]] got to business. The Demon Lords running rampant through the [[underdark|Underdark]] was a disaster that must end. He explained that [[gromph-baenre|Gromph Baenre]], Archwizard of [[menzoberranzan|Menzoberranzan]], had been responsible for summoning the Demon Lords from the [[abyss|Abyss]] — the first being [[demogorgon|Demogorgon]], which appeared in the middle of the [[drow-faction|Drow]] capital. The Demon Lord had been driven away but dealt devastating damage. Gromph was now nowhere to be found, and very few knew of the Baenre's involvement.

[[vizerion-devir|Vizerion]] offered a solution. He had been in [[gravenhollow|Gravenhollow]] finishing a ritual spell designed to draw all the Demon Lords together, whip them into a frenzy, and have them battle to the death. [[tarkathee-daru|Tark]] liked the idea but pointed out they would keep regenerating. [[vizerion-devir|Vizerion]] nodded — the first few deaths, yes, they would consume ambient magic and reform. But the magic would quickly deplete, resulting in true banishment. The group could then kill whichever Demon Lord remained standing.

He led them from the Magnificent Mansion into the real [[underdark|Underdark]] and up through his tower — [[feanon-serinde|Feanon]] noting with grudging admiration that it held not one but three pocket planes. On the fifth level, [[vizerion-devir|Vizerion]] showed them his laboratory: a work table buried in notes and apparatus, a bubbling empty tank, and three shrouded bodies on mortuary slabs. He laid out what he still needed for the ritual: six angel feathers from six different angels, the heart of a Goristro Demon, the blood or ichor of a Demon Lord, and [[gromph-baenre|Gromph's]] Grimoire. All save the Grimoire could be found in [[the-labyrinth|the Labyrinth]], a vast section of the [[underdark|Underdark]] southwest of the [[darklake|Darklake]]. The Grimoire was not strictly required but would help significantly. And the spell itself needed to be cast near the site of the original summoning — the Clawrift in [[menzoberranzan|Menzoberranzan]].

## Plotting in the Barony

Back atop [[feanons-tower|Feanon's tower]] — [[vizerion-devir|Vizerion]] having gifted them a chest of [[drow-faction|Drow]] [[piwafwi|Piwafwi]] cloaks as a show of good faith — the group began to dissect what they had learned. No one trusted [[vizerion-devir|Vizerion]]. [[feanon-serinde|Feanon]] confirmed there was more to the spell than met the eye, and after consulting [[theodemir|Theodemir]] confirmed that the ritual included a necromantic drain whose target he could not yet pinpoint — he suspected it might be the group themselves. More damningly, [[vizerion-devir|Vizerion]] had clearly lied: the spell did not need to be cast in [[menzoberranzan|Menzoberranzan]].

[[aliyah-faen-tlabbar|Patches]], however, had an idea. If they summoned the Demon Lords to the Qu'Ellarz'Orl — the noble plateau of [[menzoberranzan|Menzoberranzan]], specifically the Kyorbblivvin mushroom forest — it would serve as a magnificent distraction while keeping most of the city's population out of the line of fire. And during the chaos, they could mount a rescue of [[finn|Finn]] from [[house-faen-tlabbar|House Faen Tlabbar]].

The group committed: they would save [[finn|Finn]] by using the Demon Lords as a distraction.

They debated whether to assault Thanatos first but agreed that [[orcus|Orcus']] throne city was too well defended for just the group. They would focus on the fragment of [[the-last-word|the Last Word]] connected to [[the-labyrinth|the Labyrinth]] and the ancient ruins there, while working to rid the [[underdark|Underdark]] of the Demon Lords.

A melodic elven voice called from the darkness of the night sky — [[lodan|Lo'Dan]]. The [[bregan-daerthe|Bregan D'Aerthe]] agent floated in on a flight enchantment, covered in mean burns and limping badly. Over drinks at the tavern, he explained that he had been in Mantol Derith and battled [[corpsetaker|Corpsetaker]] and [[li-xian|Li Xian]]. A fireball had thrown him from the outpost high above the [[darklake|Darklake]], and the fall — miraculously — saved his life. The rest of his team had died along with every inhabitant of Mantol Derith.

[[lodan|Lo'Dan]] was pleased to hear the group had avenged his men. When [[aliyah-faen-tlabbar|Patches]] mentioned rescuing [[finn|Finn]], he perked up — but such an operation within the [[drow-faction|Drow]] capital required the approval of [[jarlaxle|Jarlaxle]] himself. He would arrange a meeting at the ruins of the old Do'Urden compound on [[menzoberranzan|Menzoberranzan's]] northern wall.

## The Well of Many Worlds

That night, [[cirrus-al-kayan|Cirrus]] retreated to his chambers in the [[fane-of-selune|Fane]] and cast the [[well-of-many-worlds|Well of Many Worlds]] against the wall. The portal opened to an underground lake fed by a roaring waterfall, the place teeming with plant and fungal growth, the stones glittering with mineral deposits — all suffused with a welcoming silvery light. He stepped through and closed the portal behind him.

Hours passed as he wandered. Finally, a voice called from the shadows — a great beast, curious rather than hostile. It introduced itself as [[kirangelos|Kirangelos]], an ancient Behir. The many-legged creature coaxed a pearl necklace from the warlock and in exchange revealed his location: the plane of Ysgard, specifically [[svartalfheim|Svartalfheim]].

[[cirrus-al-kayan|Cirrus]] continued until he heard laughter echoing down a tunnel. He rounded a corner to find two [[drow-faction|Drow]] lounging by a subterranean river, drinking silvery wine — just as a sword came to rest on his shoulder. But these Drow were nothing like the [[lolth|Lolth]]-sworn he knew. They smiled with warmth, wore white and silver, and were covered in elegant mithril jewellery. They were followers of [[eilistraee|Eilistraee]], residents of [[svartalfheim|Svartalfheim]], hailing from the petitioner city of [[dokkar|Dokkar]] — three days north. They offered to escort the obvious follower of [[selune|Selune]] to their city, where he could acquire a planar fork.

[[cirrus-al-kayan|Cirrus]] accepted, and they rode summoned steeds toward the extraplanar [[drow-faction|Drow]] city devoted to [[eilistraee|Eilistraee]].

Back in the material world the next morning, the group gathered to depart for [[the-labyrinth|the Labyrinth]]. [[cirrus-al-kayan|Cirrus]] did not show. After an hour, they found his rooms in the [[fane-of-selune|Fane]] empty. [[feanon-serinde|Feanon]] suspected the warlock had opened a portal to the Higher Planes. Confused and worried, they could only wait.
