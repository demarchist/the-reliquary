## The Second Beholder

The second Beholder rose screaming from its host and the group met it with steel and spell before it could gather itself. The aberration shot skyward, rising above the rooftops of [[und|Und]], and ripped a nearby footbridge from its moorings with a telekinetic ray — the wooden planks spiralling upward to form a crude shield between itself and the archers below.

A second beam lanced down and struck [[horatio-von-bumidere|Buumi]]. The paladin went rigid, every muscle locked, and toppled sideways like a felled tree.

A third eyebeam caught [[jack-mayer-johnson-mathews|JmJm]] square in the chest. Grey stone crept across his skin, racing up his arms and neck, locking his joints — petrification. For a terrible moment, the bard stood frozen mid-stride, his face a rictus of surprise.

Then the stone cracked. JmJm shattered the encasing rock with a snarl, shaking fragments from his shoulders, and vaulted onto [[steden|Steden's]] back. The Nightmare's hooves burst into flame and the pair plunged sideways out of reality — into the grey mist of the [[ethereal-plane|Ethereal Plane]] — reappearing behind the Beholder an instant later, flanking the creature between themselves and the shooters below.

[[gundren-rockseeker|Gundren]] and [[aliyah-faen-tlabbar|Patches]] opened fire from the ground, shots cracking through the morning air. But [[feanon-serinde|Fëanon]] had heard too many tales of these aberrations to take chances. He sprinted for the nearest house, dove through the doorway, smashed the window out with his staff, and levelled a finger at the hovering Beholder.

The Disintegrate hit it centre-mass. The creature screamed — a sound like tearing metal — as its body unravelled from the inside out, flesh and chitin dissolving into a fine grey ash that drifted down over the village like dirty snow.

Silence settled. The battle had lasted seconds.

## The Mines Glow

JmJm sat astride Steden in the Ethereal Plane for a moment longer, surveying the landscape from the other side of the veil. The village of Und was a ghostly sketch of itself — translucent walls, faded colour, everything drained to the palette of frosted glass.

But the mines were different.

The entrance to the tunnels beneath the village blazed with power. Lines of light threaded through the rock, pulsing with a slow rhythm like a heartbeat, visible only from this side. Whatever lay in those depths was radiating energy into the Ethereal — a beacon impossible to miss.

JmJm shifted back to the Material Plane and found Fëanon. He described what he had seen.

The wizard's expression darkened. An ordinary Beholder — even a powerful one — could not warp reality this thoroughly. It could not twist a village into a waking dream, breed copies of itself within living hosts, and cause the mines to glow with power visible from another plane. The aberrations they had killed were children. Servants. What lived below was something far older.

An Elder Orb.

Fëanon explained what he knew. Elder Orbs were ancient Beholders — creatures that had lived for centuries, their powers deepening with age until they could reshape the fabric of reality around their lairs. They consumed flesh like their younger kin, but the truly old ones could also feed from strong sources of magic, draining enchantments and ley lines the way a leech draws blood. Whatever this one had found in the mines, it was feeding from it — and it had been doing so long enough to twist the stone itself into something unnatural.

## The Evacuation

The group decided the villagers could not stay. The enchantment had been broken, but the people of Und were in no condition to defend themselves — many still unconscious, others confused and disoriented, blinking in the sunlight as though waking from a long illness.

They organised the evacuation quickly. The villagers were gathered and led down the mountain trail to the ranger cabin they had passed the previous day — the well-stocked shelter with its open-door policy and donation box. It was far enough from the mines to be safe and sturdy enough to house the survivors while the group dealt with what lay beneath.

JmJm rode ahead on Steden, slipping into the Ethereal Plane to check the cabin. A ghost drifted through the walls — one of the mountain's restless dead, tied to the pass. JmJm asked whether it would be all right if the villagers rested there for a time.

The ghost seemed confused by the question. It considered, translucent brow furrowed, and then allowed that it should be fine.

## Into the Mines

With the villagers settled, Patches moved ahead alone into the mines to scout.

The tunnels opened from the hillside into worked stone — old dwarven mining passages with pick-scarred walls and timber shoring. But the deeper she went, the more the stonework changed. The rough-hewn walls gave way to something smoother, threaded with veins of glowing crystal that pulsed faintly in the darkness. The rock was too perfect — too symmetrical, too clean. Whatever force had twisted the village above had been reshaping the mines from within, and the deeper tunnels had the same uncanny, dreamlike quality as the village streets.

Patches noted the airflow. Tunnels to the left drew air downward — deeper passages leading to lower chambers. The Elder Orb's lair would be further in, further down.

She moved carefully forward, her Drow eyes adjusting to the bioluminescent glow of the crystal-veined walls, and came to a large cavern pocked with deep pits and jagged crevasses in the floor. The place was silent. Still.

If not for her heritage — decades in the [[underdark|Underdark]], a childhood spent learning to read the shape of stone and the particular stillness that meant a predator was waiting — she would have walked straight into a Roper nest. The stalactite-like creatures clung to the cavern ceiling, their tendrils hanging motionless, indistinguishable from natural rock formations to anyone who had not grown up beneath the earth. Patches marked their positions and gave them a wide berth.

Pressing deeper, she spotted something worse. Eyes in the walls — closed, embedded in the crystal-threaded stone, but the orbs beneath the lids were moving. Back and forth, back and forth. Dreaming.

The mine was alive with them. Sleeping sentinels, watching nothing, projecting the Elder Orb's influence outward even in their dormancy. Patches did not want to find out what happened when they woke.

She turned back and returned to the group.

## The Magnificent Mansion

Fëanon conjured the [[magnificent-mansion|Magnificent Mansion]] as the afternoon shadows lengthened across the mountain. The shimmering silver door materialised against the rock face near the mine entrance, and the group stepped through into warmth, light, and the smell of roasting meat from the perpetual banquet table.

They rested. They planned. Patches described what she had found — the crystal-threaded tunnels, the Roper nest, the dreaming eyes. Fëanon shared what he knew of Elder Orbs and their feeding habits. The creature would be deep in the mine, near whatever source of magic it had claimed, and the tunnels between here and there would be shaped by its influence — reality bending around the aberration's dreams like heat haze over stone.

They would go in prepared. They would find what it was feeding from. And they would kill it.

## The Descent

The group buffed up and headed out — back to the [[mines-of-und|mines]] with Patches leading the way.

They had barely crossed the threshold when an eye opened on the far wall — one of the dreaming sentinels, now very much awake. A beam of necrotic energy lanced out and struck Fëanon. The wizard staggered but kept his feet. The group retaliated, firing at the exposed eye, but the thing ripped a section of the mine floor upward with telekinetic force, blocking the shots behind a slab of crystal-veined stone.

They blasted the rock apart and then the eye behind it, which burst in a spray of dark ichor.

They moved deeper, giving the Roper nest a wide berth and taking the path Patches had not scouted on her first pass. The tunnels glittered — crystal veins catching and refracting the party's light sources into prismatic scatter across the walls and ceiling.

Without warning, the ground beneath their feet began to twist. The stone softened and warped, melting like candle wax, and they found themselves sinking — boots, then ankles, then shins disappearing into what had been solid rock moments before. They wrenched themselves free and scrambled clear of the warping zone, hearts pounding.

Fëanon studied the effect as the stone resolidified behind them. The reality-warping influence of the [[elder-orb|Elder Orb]], he theorised — the creature's dreams leaking into the physical world, reshaping matter on a whim. The closer they got to its lair, the worse it would become.

They pressed on. A group of ghostly miners drifted through the tunnel ahead — translucent figures going about their work with pickaxes and carts, oblivious to the living. The group gave them space and moved past without incident.

They found a small breakroom carved into the tunnel wall — a table, a chair, and a weeks-old corpse slumped forward with its head on its arms. When they examined the body, they found that every bone had been pulverised. The flesh was intact, the skin unbroken, but the skeleton beneath had been reduced to powder. No weapon they knew of could do that. The mystery went unanswered.

The ground warped again as they investigated — the floor and walls twisting and melting around them, stone flowing like water, and the group sinking into the rock for a second time. They pulled free quickly and left the area, Fëanon's jaw set.

## The Crystal Chamber

Further in, they passed an alcove containing a twisted creature — something that had once been a spider but was now something else entirely. Half its face was humanoid, grotesquely fused with the arachnid body, its multiple eyes blinking independently. The thing clicked and chittered but did not attack.

Patches scouted ahead and returned with a report: two Beholder creatures in the next chamber, gathering crystals from the walls.

The group returned to the spider-thing and charmed it — a meatshield, sent skittering into the crystal chamber ahead of them.

The larger aberration — a Gauth, its central eye surrounded by a crown of smaller stalks — turned at the sound of the spider's approach. Three eyebeams fired in rapid succession: the first stunned the spider mid-stride, the second put it to sleep, and the third caught Gundren just as the group rushed in behind it.

They unleashed everything on the smaller creature — a Spectator, hovering near the far wall with crystals clutched in its tentacles. It died instantly, obliterated before it could bring a single eye to bear.

The Gauth screamed and answered with a barrage of its own — eyebeams raking across the chamber in wild arcs. The group responded in kind. Shots, spells, and blades hammered the fleshy orb until it shuddered, listed, and crashed to the floor.

It lay still. Crystals tumbled from its tentacles and scattered across the stone, tinkling like glass.

The chamber fell quiet. Bioluminescent fungi gleamed from the walls, their soft light catching the iridescent crystals that littered the floor and casting the mine interior in a slow-shifting kaleidoscope of colour. The air smelled of ozone and ichor and old stone.

The outer chambers were clear. The Elder Orb waited somewhere below — deeper in the dark, feeding from whatever ancient power had drawn it here. The group caught their breath in the glittering silence and prepared to go further.
