## The Plateau

The group found themselves high on a pillar overlooking the [[Disphotic Forest]] — an inner world existing beneath the crust of the planet, lit by millions of glowing Sourcestones. [[Feanon Serinde|Feanon]] checked the edge of the plateau and was nearly blown off by a thermal updraft. He peered over cautiously. The drop was so vast that distance itself became difficult to gauge.

A school of squid-like creatures swirled around the plateau's edge and darted away into the luminous haze.

The group began to prepare for rest. [[Cirrus Al-Kayan|Cirrus]], anxious as he was, confirmed they had time for one — barely.

Feanon summoned his Magnificent Mansion and walked inside. [[Snorbul]] was there, standing in the main hall.

The wizard stared. He had *just* summoned the Mansion. How could Snorbul possibly be inside it?

The small gnome was in visible distress. He explained, haltingly, that he had been transported to an unknown location while looking for soup in [[Iyum]]. He had encountered elves who looked like [[Ahmahd]] — the same Al Askarian features — and they had helped him through a portal. He had stepped through, and found himself inside the Mansion the instant Feanon opened the door.

The implications were deeply uncomfortable. The group decided to investigate at a later time.

## The Storm

Every living thing in the cavern retracted at once — a great wave of motion as creatures, plants, and bioluminescent organisms dimmed and hid. Moments later, a massive stormfront of glowing energy swept across the plateau.

Everyone dove into the Mansion except Cirrus, [[Tarkathee Daru|Tark]], and [[Aliyah Faen Tlabbar|Patches]], who remained outside holding the [[Bag of Holding|bags of holding]]. The psychic assault hit them like a wall. They heard and felt the death-throes of [[Fawden]] rip through their minds.

As the wave of energy passed, Tark and Patches emerged from cover to find Cirrus standing exactly where he had been. He had simply weathered it. A trickle of blood ran from his nose. He turned without a word, muttered something about running out of time, and retired to the Mansion.

Ahmahd entered the Mansion, came back out moments later shaking her head and muttering about *abuse of power*, and settled in with the camping group. After being talked at too enthusiastically, she separated herself for some solitude.

## The Glider

The next morning, Ahmahd took her leave. She was unwilling to go deeper — being trapped below the [[Disphotic Forest]] with no way out was not a risk she intended to take. The group said their goodbyes, and the ranger began the long trek back to the surface alone.

They turned their attention to the problem of traversal. Cirrus pointed into the far distance and down. The Font was *that* way — many miles across a landscape of shattered plateaus, fog banks, and bottomless chasms.

Feanon proposed a glider. While he worked out the mechanics, Snorbul scoured the plateau for shellfish that might secrete a natural adhesive. [[Horatio Von Bumidere|Buumi]] and Tark went looking for kindling at the water's edge, where Tark grabbed a red kelp-like frond — and was instantly wrapped in it and dragged into the lake.

Patches saw it happen, dropped everything, and dove in after him. Tark surfaced, declared something had grabbed him, and was yanked back under. Moments later, both he and Patches surfaced having dealt with the threat below.

Feanon Fabricated the glider from shells supplied by Snorbul. The result was beautiful — a graceful craft of mother-of-pearl, all sweeping curves and iridescent sheen. The controls needed refinement; the spell had not perfectly captured the full mechanical design. But it could carry two, and with the thermals of the cavern, Feanon was confident they could cover many miles.

The group assembled their various means of transport. Feanon pushed the glider from the plateau's edge, dropped into a shallow dive, and pulled up into a steady glide.

## The Manta Ray

The thermals carried them swiftly over the swirling clouds. The descent was inevitable, and eventually they dipped below the first fog bank — dodging indistinct shapes in the mist — and emerged damper but intact, the vast expanse of the Disphotic Forest spreading below them.

Shattered plateaus of varying heights stretched in every direction, stalagmites hundreds of feet tall threaded with Sourcestone and coated in living coral. The great pillars ascended like towers into the clouds above, each one an ecosystem unto itself.

Feanon spotted a thermal rising from a volcanic vent. A school of squid circled it. He adjusted course toward the updraft.

A massive form resolved from the clouds beside them.

One of the gargantuan Manta Rays — vast beyond reckoning — congealed from mist and rose to engulf the school of squid in its colossal mouth. Feanon tried to evade, but a glider does not evade. The beast clipped the craft, snapping the delicate tail, and the glider went into a spin.

The group scrambled. Snorbul grabbed the spinning tail section and moved to extract [[Gundren Rockseeker|Gundren]] from the disintegrating craft. Tark, strapped to a Tenser's Disc being towed behind, spun helplessly. Snorbul matched the rotation and steadied the tail while Gundren seized both pieces — but he needed a free hand to Mend the break, and no one was within reach.

Buumi flew over with Patches and dropped her onto the tumbling glider. She held the tail in place while Gundren cast the repair. The moment it set, Patches released — caught by Buumi — and Snorbul pulled Gundren clear on his broom.

Feanon righted the craft a few hundred feet above the ground, cleared one final plateau, and ditched the glider amongst the coral flora. It had served its purpose.

## The Deep Forest

They continued on mounts, led by Cirrus's unerring sense of Fawden's location. Hours of traversal through the alien landscape — climbing over chasms and cliffs, the humidity oppressive, the temperature swinging wildly from one plateau to the next. Some gulleys were sheathed in ice. Others steamed with thermal vents and boiling pools.

They came upon hundreds of large glowing jellyfish swirling above the stone spires of a plateau — perhaps thousands. One approached and puffed out a cloud of gas, charming Snorbul into a trance. He began to wander toward the swarm before the group broke him free. They edged through carefully, taking a few more puffs but resisting the telepathic urge to join the creatures' dance. Feanon thought they occupied a similar niche to the Myconids of Faerûn.

Beyond the jellyfish fields, they found a cliff face covered in glowing kelp. Tark shadow-stepped to the top and discovered scalding water pouring from above — thermal pools teeming with small blue crabs and tiny glowing jellyfish floating near a steam geyser.

The group climbed up. Buumi's foot broke through the mineral crust into the boiling water beneath. He screamed, yanked off his boot — skin came with it — and howled until his regeneration sealed the wound. He cleaned out his boot in grim silence.

The tiny jellyfish were not jellyfish. Snorbul recognised them first: Flumphs. Feanon communed with the small telepathic creatures, who warned them cheerfully to avoid the stagnant pools — that was where the Sheitan lived. Feanon received a vague impression of dangerous serpents, and brains.

They noted the warning and moved on, Tark leading them safely through the hot springs.

## The Great Pillar

The day was wearing on. They asked Cirrus how much farther. The warlock pointed into the distance, where a pillar of vast proportions loomed from the mists — still many miles, but visible at last.

They trekked on. Patches spotted an anomaly ahead — a plateau sheathed entirely in ice and snow, the temperature plummeting at its boundary. They went around.

More hours passed. The terrain was punishing even with mounts. They hauled themselves up yet another cliff and found themselves level with their destination.

The massive spire of stone ascended into the clouds before them, blue coral blooming across the landscape in a dense jungle. Motes of energy drifted on the warm air. Schools of creatures darted through gulleys.

Everything went to ground.

The pillar blazed with purple light, misty energies billowing from its surface before detonating outward — another psychic stormfront from the dying Fawden. Everyone dove for cover. Snorbul did not make it.

When he came to, he was bleeding from his ears and twenty feet off the ground, [[Horatio Von Bumidere|Buumi]] holding him by the ankle. The psychic wave had driven him mad, and he had tried to flee off a cliff. Buumi had cleansed his mind.

They pressed on.

Near the base of the pillar, Tark spotted a massive reptilian humanoid — a [[Wahid]], easily twenty feet tall, wearing primitive leather, plodding toward a rock formation. It smashed the stone apart with a great bone and sat down to eat the small blue crustaceans that swarmed out to defend their nest.

Tark wrapped the group in shadow and cast Pass without Trace. They covered the last few miles in silence, unwilling to test the giant's disposition.

Cirrus led them to a hidden vale at the pillar's base, steam rising in a constant column, the narrow canyon teeming with life. At the back of the vale, a small cleft led into the stone itself, climbing gently into the heart of the pillar.

The tunnel opened into a gleaming cavern several hundred feet across. The ground was covered in steaming pools of multicoloured mineral deposits. Purple and blue energies radiated from the flora. Small motes of light drifted through the too-still air, dissolving to water where they landed. Rings of dead, calcified serpents circled many of the pools, their bodies twisted as if trying to flee the water.

Cirrus turned to the group.

This was it. This was the place from his vision — the fissure where the [[Font of Fawden|Font]] waters leaked from above. Somewhere in this gleaming, reef-like cave was their goal.
