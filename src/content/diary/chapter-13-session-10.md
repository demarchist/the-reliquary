## The Horde

The group had barely cleared the mists when a series of shrill horn blasts sounded from behind them and to the north.

They turned to take to the skies — but a horde of halflings surged from the jungle before they could gain altitude. At their head rode [[Ruru]], the remaining champion of [[Hohonu]], astride a massive Sacrosu beast.

[[Cirrus Al-Kayan|Cirrus]] unleashed a Sickening Radiance into the oncoming mass. A pulse of energy from the [[Ethereal Plane]] — Hohonu himself — tried to counterspell it, but failed. A halfling shaman dispelled it moments later, though not before the radiance had burned through a swathe of warriors. The halflings responded with a volley of skull-bombs flung from slings. They fell short, detonating into a mass of pulsating corruption — and within the writhing mass, a draconic face formed, twisting and snarling.

The group did not fight. They fled skyward.

The halfling shamans attempted to give chase, but [[Aliyah Faen Tlabbar|Patches]] picked them from the air with precise shots, sending them crashing against the stones below.

## The Avatar

The draconic face in the corruption reared back and breathed forth a devastating blast of energy.

[[Tarkathee Daru|Tark]], being carried aloft by [[Horatio Von Bumidere|Buumi]], spotted the incoming beam and shouted a warning at the last possible second. Buumi interposed his shield. The blast struck them with staggering force, hurling the two apart in mid-air. Tark caught his friend as Buumi pulled out of his spiralling dive, and they took flight again — shaken, but alive.

Below, the horror continued. Ruru chanted in agony as the Sacrosu beneath him twisted and grew, its flesh tearing apart as it consumed nearby wounded halfling warriors. The abomination began to devour Ruru himself. What rose from the carnage was a grotesque amalgam of creatures in the vague form of a dragon — an Avatar of Hohonu, assembled from the flesh of the faithful. It spread its ragged wings and took flight in pursuit.

The group poured on every ounce of speed they had. Slowly, painfully, they outpaced the pursuing Avatar.

## The Arcane Shadow

[[Gundren Rockseeker|Gundren]] warned that [[Arkto]] was nearing his limits. They would have to set down soon. Behind them, the dark speck of the Avatar still hung on the horizon.

They found a set of old ruins beneath an overhang — enough shelter to hide a camp. [[Feanon Serinde|Feanon]] concealed the entrance to his Magnificent Mansion in the arcane shadow of an ancient glyph, rendering it invisible to magical detection. Tark and Patches stayed outside in the Material Plane, guarding the [[Bag of Holding|bags of holding]]. They pulled their cloaks tight and lay motionless, wrapped in concealing magic.

Hours passed.

Something crashed into the swamp a few hundred metres away. It smashed through the undergrowth, uprooting trees, demolishing everything in its path. The Avatar was searching for them.

It did not find them.

After hours of circling and crashing through the jungle, the thing's search took it out of sight and out of earshot. It did not return.

## The Temple of Fawden

The next morning, the group set off with every precaution, moving southwest toward the [[Machina Factory]]. The swamp slowed their progress and insects plagued their every step. It was a long, miserable day.

Toward the end of the hot, muggy afternoon, Feanon spotted something through the canopy — a ziggurat, surrounded by crumbling ruins.

They investigated. It was an ancient temple of [[Fawden]], long desecrated, now guarded by two Gorgons — great iron-scaled bulls that breathed petrifying gas. Cirrus convinced the constructs that he was an agent of their goddess, which was not entirely untrue. Feanon returned a piece of the temple that had been stolen — a carved eye he had found on the petrified remains of halflings outside the ruins. They reset the missing eye in the idol, and the Gorgons vanished.

Cirrus received a vision. Fawden spoke to him directly, warning that [[Vecna|Zalaam]] had set a trap in the Machina Factory. This confirmed what the Obsidian Order had suspected. The goddess confirmed that a Ringed Veil — the portal they needed — did exist within the Factory. She would do what she could to help them.

The vision ended.

## Into the Dark

The group pushed on, unwilling to remain anywhere near the Avatar's hunting ground. They found a secluded spot on the edge of an ancient battlefield, scattered with derelict Machina — the rusted husks of [[Thueba Swamp|the swamp]]'s forgotten war. Feanon used the same arcane shadow technique to hide the Mansion's entrance.

That evening, they discussed their plans: how to deal with the Factory, and how to use the Ringed Veil against Vecna in the Font of Power. Feanon suspected the Factory itself would be deserted and that the gate would be the trap.

Patches and Tark took the night watch outside, pulling their cloaks tight against the endless clouds of biting insects as the rest of the party retired to the Mansion's comfort.
