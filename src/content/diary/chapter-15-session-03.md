## Evening at Rising Sun

[[mister-luko|Mister Luko]] rose from his grand dining table, thanked the group for their precious time, and offered them free food, refreshment, and lodging for the night. The [[rising-sun|Rising Sun]]'s hospitality was theirs. In the morning, they could meet his contact inside [[house-muskov|House Muskov]] by simply knocking on the front door. Alternatively — he added with the careful neutrality of a man presenting options he expected professionals to evaluate — they could go tonight, via the sewers to the House's basement.

They opted to rest. [[jack-mayer-johnson-mathews|JmJm]], somewhat placated by the prospect of free food and drink, decided that a night of partying was exactly what he needed and descended from the dining room to the grand gambling hall. [[cassian|Cassian]] — who had been maintaining an illusion spell for the entire evening — headed upstairs to rest with undisguised relief, [[tarkathee-daru|Tark]] and [[feanon-serinde|Fëanon]] following him up to meditate. [[horatio-von-bumidere|Buumi]] and [[aliyah-faen-tlabbar|Patches]] slipped out for dinner at a quieter restaurant Luko had recommended.

[[cirrus-al-kayan|Cirrus]], still on the street from when he had elected to remain outside, asked around for directions and quickly discovered that the Rising Sun was the place to be. He disarmed himself at the coat-check, walked onto the gambling floor — metal teeth gleaming in a grin — and scanned the room until his attention settled on a particular knot of noblewomen and their admirers gathered around a card table.

## Lady Natasha

The entourage appeared to radiate around a single figure. She was young, strikingly pale — a natural translucence to her skin that showed blue veins beneath the surface — with black shimmering hair tied and pierced through with clasps of precious metals, red painted lips, and eyes of an almond-shaped stark blue. She was wrapped in shimmering, colorful layers of silk that moved when she moved. She introduced herself as [[natasha-huron|Natasha Huron]], scion of [[house-huron|House Huron]].

JmJm and Cirrus parted the nobles with practised ease. JmJm performed — the sort of effortless, magnetic display that cleared social space as effectively as a battering ram — while Cirrus filled it with the kind of small talk that made people feel they were being taken seriously by someone interesting. Natasha was fascinated. She invited Cirrus to join her table.

They played a few rounds of cards. Cirrus, over the course of the game, persuaded the Lady to extend an invitation to the soirée House Huron was hosting in a few days' time. She agreed with apparent delight. As the night wore on, they took their leave.

## The Bone-Splitter

On the street of the high-class merchant ring, a Paladin of the [[crimson-dawn-knights|Crimson Dawn]] and a smartly dressed servant approached Cirrus. The Paladin apologised — they had not been made aware that such a distinguished guest was visiting, and the news had only just reached the proper people. Cirrus was welcomed to the city and offered a noble townhouse and concierge for his stay, courtesy of [[house-aimu|House Aimu]] and on invitation of the Queen's hospitality. The concierge informed him that [[gundren-rockseeker|Gundren]] was already at the residence — a scant few hundred metres away in the next district, opposite the House Aimu Manor.

Cirrus sent for the others with a Tiny Cirrus — an instant messenger shaped in his own diminutive image — and headed for the new abode.

Meanwhile, Patches and Buumi were enjoying a pasta dinner at a small secluded restaurant in a stone-walled garden. Heading back after being told of the change in residence, they noted a commotion down one of the side streets. Patches moved in to investigate, slipping easily past the city guard who were keeping a handful of civilians — and a small, pampered dog — at a distance.

What she found was a scene of absolute horror. The victim was humanoid but no longer identifiable — every part of the body had been torn and ripped apart, all bones splintered, the marrow sucked out. This was not a murder committed by any tool or weapon Patches recognised. Something had fed on this person with a thoroughness and a violence that went beyond killing.

She returned to Buumi. They walked quickly to the new townhome.

## The Noble Townhouse

Patches told the group about the killing. Fëanon mentioned the man he had found the previous evening — the servant whose mind had been hollowed out by something that pressed ten fingerprints into his skull. Two different kinds of victim. Two different kinds of predator, perhaps — or two different appetites belonging to the same one.

The group went over the townhome thoroughly, finding a number of listening devices and basic wards planted throughout. Fëanon set up his Magnificent Mansion within the mansion — a private plane nested inside the Queen's hospitality — and the group rested without incident.

## House Muskov

The next morning, Cirrus, Cassian, JmJm, Patches, and Buumi headed to the nearby [[house-muskov|House Muskov]]. The approach led along a cobblestone road lined with granite decoration and the occasional bloodstone statue or fountain. High granite walls topped with flowering wisteria were punctuated by a rich wooden gate that stood open, flanked by two guards in house colours bearing shields, spears, and heavy splint armour.

The rogues made a cursory inspection of the wall and found it heavily trapped and warded. They opted for invisibility and the rest of the group as a distraction instead. Cirrus, JmJm, and Buumi — all resplendent in their gear — approached the guards. The group was expected. A [[dimitri-muskov|Lord Dimitri Muskov]] greeted and welcomed them into the house.

They entered through the front door. The rogues moved in through the kitchen, following a worker from the herb garden, and began to explore the house while Dimitri led the other three through the corridors, past the kitchens, and toward a locked parlour — with some key-juggling from Patches, who had pickpocketed the herb girl Anabelle on the way in.

Inside the locked room lay the sole survivor of the previous heist team — brutally wounded and comatose on a makeshift bed.

Dimitri explained. He had hired [[mister-luko|Mister Luko]] to steal the family Seal for his own political ends. He didn't particularly care if his family learned it had been stolen after the fact, but he needed it out of the house. The previous team had come a few days prior. There had been a commotion on the fourth floor. When Dimitri went to investigate, he found a hole smashed through the third-floor ceiling and a blood trail leading into a nearby closet. He had found this agent, brought him down to the kitchen with Anabelle's help, and kept him alive and secret ever since. Healing potions and poultices had stabilised him, but he would not wake.

Cirrus stepped forward and healed the man with a touch.

## The Pale Monk

The man opened his tired eyes and answered the group's questions with the hollow precision of someone replaying a nightmare.

His team had breached the wards and unlocked the windows on the fourth floor. The small crew approached the Study where the Seal was held and came across a human man standing in the hallway, blocking their path.

He was bald. Tattered, raggedy monk's robes hung from a powerful frame. His skin was pale, his black bushy beard threaded with grey, his eyes bloodshot and red. He wielded a staff of black crystal.

He came at them without a word. He touched the wizard with the staff and in a flash of green light turned him to dust. The survivor tried to fight — missed every strike — and was smashed through the floor for his trouble. The pale monk had seemed, throughout the whole encounter, sad and borderline expressionless. Not angry. Not threatened. Just going through the motions of violence with a kind of distant sorrow.

The survivor had crawled into a closet to hide and passed out.

The group looked at one another. A monk — pale, powerful, expressionless, wielding a staff of dark crystal. They knew who that was. [[gaddock|Gaddock]]. [[tarkathee-daru|Tark]]'s master, turned vampire by [[zhengyi|Zhengyi]], now apparently guarding the upper floors of a noble house in [[helioglabulus|Helios]]. News for Tark — and none of it good.

Their target was on the fourth floor.

Patches was already gone.

## The Fourth Floor

She had heard the location during the survivor's testimony and had not waited for the discussion that would inevitably follow. Up through the house, up to the fourth floor. She picked the lock. The main hallway showed signs of the earlier incursion — disarmed traps, minor damage, a hastily patched hole in the floor with faint old blood around it.

She bypassed the remaining traps without difficulty, picked the lock on the Study door, found the Muskov family Seal in a box inside the desk, took it, and made her way back down to the kitchen — easily avoiding the staff and guards — arriving just as the rest of the group was turning to leave to go steal the Seal.

She held it up.

Dimitri did not take it. The Seal was to go to Mister Luko, who would sequester it on Dimitri's behalf. Patches shrugged and dropped it into her [[bag-of-holding|Bag of Holding]].

## Lord Brusso

With the job done and the promised lead on [[lord-aimu|Lord Aimu]] and the [[sunsword|Sunsword]] waiting for them with Mister Luko, the group moved to leave [[house-muskov|House Muskov]].

Cassian arrived with a report. He had been casing the rest of the house and had found something on the second floor: a large set of rooms belonging to the Lord of the House, thick with nervous activity. Servants waited in the adjoining living room, each carrying trays of food, towels, and drink with the stiff posture of people who had been shouted at recently. From behind the bedroom doors, raving could be heard.

Cassian — invisible — had moved in. [[brusso-muskov|Lord Brusso Muskov]] was hunched over a meal tray, wrapped in fur blankets before a roaring fire in a room with no windows. The man was sixty years old, thin and wiry and obviously tall, with wispy silver hair. He had hurled his food tray across the room — the raw meat atop it scattering — and screamed for it to be rarer. A servant had rushed in, scooped up the discarded food, and hurried to the kitchen to oblige. The Lord continued to rave, rocking back and forth, complaining about the cold and his pounding headache.

Cassian thought Lord Brusso might be one of the creatures they were hunting.

Cirrus noted that the injured man in the parlour had likely fought Gaddock — Tark's vampire master. With Gaddock apparently guarding the fourth floor and the Lord of the house displaying symptoms that bore close scrutiny, the group decided to go up and confront the man — or the beast.

Dimitri was hesitant. He opted to hang back.

The guards on the second floor clocked them instantly and informed the group that Lord Muskov was currently unavailable for guests — even with Lord Dimitri present. JmJm and Cirrus convinced the guards that what a sickly lord truly needed was their healing. The guards relented.

They were escorted into the living room just in time to see one of the serving girls drop a tray of raw meat, clasp her hands to her face, and scream.

The doors to the bedroom stood open. Lord Brusso Muskov lay sprawled on his arched back, arms and hands locked into an agonised rictus. His face was frozen in a pained scream and his body was utterly, terribly still.
