## The Rising Sun

[[Cirrus Al Kayan]] asked around on the street for no more than a few minutes before someone pointed him in the direction of the [[Rising Sun]] — the unambiguous happening place in the high-class merchant ring of [[Helioglabulus]]. The gaming hall occupied a wide three-storey building of polished granite and pale timber, its front windows lit amber against the evening street. He walked in and found the rest of the group already at dinner.

[[Mister Luko]] had made his hospitality plain. From his seat at the head of a long dining table, he rose and thanked them for their precious time, then gestured to the staff waiting at the edges of the room. Free food, free drink, and lodging for the evening — on the house. He then outlined two routes to the family seal inside [[House Muskov]]. His contact inside the house, the junior lord [[Dimitri Muskov]], would receive them formally the following morning — a knock on the front gate, civilised and clean. Alternatively, the sewer system beneath the merchant ring communicated directly with the Muskov basement. They could be inside before midnight.

They looked at one another across the candelabra and the platters and came to the obvious conclusion. The beds were already made.

## Evening's Diversions

The group dissolved into the city in their various directions.

[[Cassian]] — who had maintained an illusion spell throughout the evening, wearing a borrowed face — was quietly relieved at the thought of a proper bed, and excused himself to the rooms upstairs. [[Tarkathee Daru|Tark]] and [[Fëanon Serinde|Fëanon]] went with him, both inclined toward meditation and preparation.

[[Horatio Von Bumidere|Buumi]] and [[Aliyah Faen Tlabbar|Patches]] were directed by the concierge staff toward a quieter dining establishment — a small restaurant [[Mister Luko]] had recommended, tucked into a stone-walled garden on a side street off the main ring.

[[Jack Mayer-Johnson Mathews|JmJm]] loosened his belt, handed his weapons to the cloakroom attendant without being asked, and headed downstairs to the grand gambling hall.

Cirrus followed him, metal teeth gleaming.

## The Lady at the Card Table

The gambling floor of the Rising Sun was warm and low-ceilinged with a constant negotiated murmur of card play beneath the percussion of chips and wheel mechanisms. The clientele dressed well and their voices carried the easy clipped accent of inherited comfort.

Cirrus scanned the room and noted a gravitational anomaly at one of the tables — a knot of young noblemen and noblewomen all oriented around a single figure at its centre. He began making his way across the floor. JmJm was already several steps ahead of him.

The young lady at the table had black shimmering hair pulled up and threaded through with clasps of precious metal. Her silk was layered in shifting colour. Her skin was pale — genuinely pale, the kind that showed the blue of the veins beneath, tracing faint lines under her wrists and temples. Her eyes were almond-shaped and a stark, cold blue. Her lips were painted deep red. Her expression was the studied composure of someone accustomed to being watched.

She glanced up as Cirrus parted her orbit of admirers, and her composure shifted by just enough to indicate she had been presented with something genuinely novel.

JmJm produced his lute and the table's attention fractured agreeably. Cirrus sat down.

She introduced herself as [[Natasha Huron]], scion of [[House Huron]].

They played several rounds of cards — Cirrus neither winning nor losing in any way that drew attention — while conversation moved at his pace. By the end of the second hand he had her genuine interest. By the end of the third he had an invitation to the soirée House Huron was hosting in several days' time. She offered it as a kind of afterthought, with the air of someone who had not quite decided to extend it until the moment she did.

They took their leave as the night wore on. The evening had produced exactly what Cirrus had come for.

## An Unexpected Welcome

On the cobblestones outside the Rising Sun, the night air carried the cool edge of a northern summer. Cirrus was still filing away the details of the evening when a Paladin in formal armour stepped into his path, flanked by a smartly dressed man with the bearing of a senior house servant.

The Paladin apologised. They had only recently received word that such a distinguished guest was in the city, and he expressed his sincere regret that a proper welcome had not been arranged sooner. On behalf of [[House Aimu]] and by invitation of the Queen's hospitality, Lord Cirrus was welcomed to Helioglabulus, and a noble townhouse had been arranged for his stay — a concierge was already installed. The residence stood in the next district, opposite the Aimu manor itself.

The servant mentioned, with careful neutrality, that [[Gundren Rockseeker|Gundren]] was already at the residence.

Cirrus summoned a tiny version of himself — a miniature officious herald, dispatched across the city to inform the others — and turned east.

## What Patches Found

On the quieter side of the merchant ring, Buumi and Patches had settled into exactly the kind of restaurant [[Mister Luko]] had described: a stone-walled garden enclosure, oil lamps strung between the walls, a short menu of excellent pasta, a carafe of red wine, and nobody trying to impress anyone. They ate slowly and without urgency.

Heading back after receiving word of the change of residence, they took a slightly different route through the side streets. A commotion a block ahead drew Patches off course.

City guard held a loose cordon across the mouth of an alley. A few civilians pressed against it, including a woman clutching a small, pampered dog to her chest. The dog had gone rigid and would not stop shaking. The guards were not engaging with questions.

Patches was past the cordon before anyone registered her moving.

The alley ended at a scene she wished she had not needed to see. A body lay against the far wall — humanoid, though barely recognisable as such. The killing had been thorough in the worst possible sense. Every surface was torn and ripped. Every bone had been splintered and then emptied, each long bone cracked open and sucked clean of marrow, with the clinical precision of something that had not been disturbed and had not needed to hurry. There was nothing left to identify. The city guard who had noticed her had started moving in her direction.

Patches returned to the street. She walked quickly back to Buumi and neither of them said much for the rest of the route.

## Swept

The townhouse was precisely what a senior House concierge produces on short notice for a visiting noble — furnished, warm, staffed, and impeccable. Cirrus accepted it with the quiet satisfaction of someone whose own assessment of their importance had just been externally confirmed.

Then they went through it again more carefully.

The sweep turned up a series of listening devices — small, mechanically discrete, fitted behind wall panels and beneath window sills with a craftsman's patience — and basic wards at the entry points. Nothing exotic, but thorough. Whoever had prepared this welcome had not left the interior to good faith alone.

Patches relayed what she had found in the alley. [[Fëanon Serinde|Fëanon]] listened and then mentioned, quietly, that someone in the city appeared to be missing their memories — he had encountered something in that vein earlier and lacked the context to make sense of it at the time. The two pieces of information sat alongside one another without quite connecting.

Fëanon dismissed the listening devices as an inconvenience, stepped to the centre of the main reception room, and opened his Magnificent Mansion. The group filed in. The night passed without incident.

## House Muskov

In the morning, Tark and Fëanon remained behind while the rest — Cirrus, Cassian, JmJm, Patches, and Buumi — made their way to [[House Muskov]].

The house sat along a cobblestone road in the merchant nobility district: granite walls dressed with bloodstone inlay, garden statuary and a quiet fountain cast in the same deep red stone, catching the morning light. Tall walls topped with flowering wisteria enclosed the property, broken by a rich wooden gate that stood open. Two guards flanked the entrance in house colours, spears at rest, heavy splint armour, shields propped at their sides.

The rogues performed a quiet assessment of the perimeter wall and found it trapped comprehensively enough to make any thought of climbing it short-lived.

The plan arranged itself. Cirrus, JmJm, and Buumi presented themselves at the gate in appropriate finery and were received by Lord [[Dimitri Muskov]], who welcomed them in with the warm efficiency of a man who had been expecting them and very much needed what they were bringing. They entered through the front door.

Patches and Cassian went invisible and slipped in behind a worker carrying fresh herbs from the kitchen garden.

## The Locked Parlour

Dimitri led his guests through the house at a pace that suggested a destination. The kitchen was a temporary stop — long enough for Patches, already ahead of the group and invisible, to lift a key from a young woman named Anabelle who had been carrying a basket of herbs and minding her own business. The girl continued through the kitchen without breaking stride.

The key unlocked a parlour off the service corridor. Inside, on a low bed, lay a man in a condition that made three members of the group look away from the door.

He was alive. The shallow, irregular breath confirmed it. Beyond that, the damage was extensive — deep lacerations, poorly-healed bone injuries, the kind of systemic punishment that reduced most people to ruin. He had been kept alive on healing potions and poultices for several days. The potions had done their work imperfectly but persistently.

Dimitri spoke in low, measured tones. He had hired [[Mister Luko]] to steal the family seal for political purposes he was willing to own — his family would work out who had taken it after the fact, and he did not particularly mind. He needed it outside the house first. The previous team had come a few nights ago and entered successfully. He had been woken by a commotion on the fourth floor. By the time he reached the third floor landing he found the ceiling breached and a blood trail that led to a closet. He had found this man inside it. Anabelle had helped him carry the agent to the kitchen. Everything else, he said, was upstairs.

Cirrus stepped forward and placed his hand on the man's chest.

The breathing deepened. The man's eyes opened.

## The Pale Monk

He looked at the faces around him and took a moment to confirm that what he was seeing was real. Then he spoke, slowly and with effort.

He and his team had taken the fourth floor windows — a clean entry, nothing complicated. Their target was the Study and the route was clear. They turned a corner in the hallway and found a man standing in it.

Bald. Tall. Tattered monk robes, grey and thin, hanging from a narrow frame. Skin with the particular translucency of something that had not seen sunlight for a long time. A black bushy beard shot through with grey threads. Eyes so bloodshot they appeared uniformly red. In his hands, a staff of black crystal.

The man did not speak. His expression did not resolve into anything that could be called emotion — it sat somewhere south of sadness and remained there throughout. He moved toward the group with the calm indifference of someone dealing with a minor administrative task.

He touched the wizard with the staff. Green light flared and the wizard ceased to exist.

The survivor attacked. Every strike missed — not deflected, simply absent from where they had been aimed, in a way that felt impossible. The pale monk swung the crystal staff once and the survivor went through the floor. He crawled into a closet. That was where Dimitri had found him three days later.

The group exchanged looks. The description settled into the room and accumulated weight.

Those who knew Tark understood what the description meant: a pale monk in tattered robes with a staff of black crystal, expressionless, devastating, and apparently very difficult to kill. News for later. Tark was not here.

The seal was on the fourth floor. The group turned toward the door.

## Already Gone

[[Aliyah Faen Tlabbar|Patches]] was not among them. She had slipped out while the survivor was still describing the layout of the upper hallway.

The fourth floor corridor showed the signs of the prior visit — disarmed traps left in place, the hastily-patched breach in the floor, a faint wash of old blood at the edge of the repair. Patches moved through the remaining traps the way one walks a known path in the dark. The Study lock yielded on the second pick. Inside, the desk held a lacquered box. Inside the box, a seal.

She was back in the kitchen and pulling the drawstring of her [[bag of holding]] closed by the time the conversation in the parlour concluded.

The group turned from the parlour doorway, ready to head upstairs, and found Patches already beside them.

Dimitri confirmed the seal was not to come to him directly — it was to go to [[Mister Luko]], who would sequester it on his behalf. Patches nodded and tucked the bag away.

## The Second Floor

While the parlour meeting had been taking place, Cassian had been moving through the rest of the house — quiet, invisible, methodical. The second floor held his attention.

The lord's private quarters occupied a broad stretch of the level, and outside the bedroom doors a cluster of servants waited with the frozen anxiety of people who had been holding laden trays for considerably too long. Food. Towels. A warming drink. None of them had been admitted.

From inside the bedroom, a voice was raving.

Cassian moved in. [[Lord Brusso Muskov]] sat hunched before a roaring fire in a room with no windows, wrapped in fur blankets that had failed to keep the cold from his bones. He was tall in the way of someone who had once been very tall — wiry now, sixty years old and reduced, wispy silver hair, the kind of thin that suggests hard decades rather than easy ones. He hurled his food tray across the room. The raw meat scattered across the floor. He screamed for it to be rarer and one of the servants rushed in to scoop it up and hurry it back to the kitchen. Brusso rocked and complained about the cold and his pounding headache and then complained about the cold again. He showed no interest in the meat once it had left his hands.

Cassian noted the sealed-up room, the craving, the discarded food, the raving, the aversion to light, and formed an assessment. He returned to the group downstairs as the last of the morning's business was concluding.

He reported what he had found, and offered his considered opinion that Lord Brusso Muskov might be one of the creatures they were in this city to hunt.

Cirrus noted that the man lying in the parlour behind them had likely survived a direct encounter with [[Gaddock]] — or with whatever Gaddock had since become. He said it briefly and did not elaborate. The others heard the significance.

With the seal in hand and [[Mister Luko]]'s promised intelligence on Lord Aimu and the [[Sunsword]] waiting for their return, the group moved to leave. Then stopped.

## The Lord's Chamber

Dimitri opted to remain on the ground floor, with no particular explanation.

The guards outside the lord's quarters were unmoved even by Dimitri's nominal presence. Lord Muskov was unavailable for guests, they said, even distinguished ones. JmJm and Cirrus introduced the prospect of healing — framing it with the sincerity of people who meant exactly as much as was useful. The guards considered the obvious state of their employer, assessed the bearing of the group in front of them, and permitted them into the living room.

One of the serving girls crossed the room carrying a fresh tray of raw meat. She stopped walking. Her hands opened. The tray hit the floor.

The bedroom doors stood open.

[[Lord Brusso Muskov]] lay sprawled on his back across the floor, both arms rigid, his hands locked into a clenched rictus aimed at the ceiling. His face had set around the shape of a scream that had produced no sound. His body was entirely still. The fire had burned low.

Whatever Lord Brusso had been in the process of becoming, the process had completed itself sometime in the night. The cold of the room settled over the group.

The serving girl pressed her hands to her face.
