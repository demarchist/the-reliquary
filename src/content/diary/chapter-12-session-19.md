## The Treasure Hunter

[[Feanon Serinde|Feanon]] was still shaking when dawn came. The waking nightmare continued — he flinched at things the others could not see, and launched a firebolt at a particularly terrible hallucination that splashed harmlessly against a nearby boulder. [[Horatio Von Bumidere|Buumi]] cast a healing spell that helped but did not cure.

As the group dealt with Feanon's deteriorating state, a lone wood elf appeared on a nearby ridge astride a Tujalri mount. [[Gundren Rockseeker|Gundren]] summoned [[Arkto]], [[Jack Mayer-Johnson Mathews|JmJm]] called forth his Nightmare steed — manifesting as a fiery drake — and [[Snorbul]] rose on his flying broom. The elf was instantly cowed.

Buumi and [[Tarkathee Daru|Tark]] went to welcome the stranger, who introduced himself as [[Ventari]] — a treasure hunter looking for something valuable to bring back to his clan. They invited him for breakfast, which he accepted, and Ventari joined them around the campfire after [[Aliyah Faen Tlabbar|Patches]] burned the eggs.

## The Vote

The group debated their next objective. The volcano, the Temple of [[Vaketh]], [[Sharza]], and the portal to the Fae Wild all circulated. Ventari — bemused by the casual ambition of these outsiders — said the Fiery Gorge on [[Mt. Saqar]] was the most promising, as legend placed the [[Titanic Forge]] there. He was adamant, however, that the Temples of the Gods and especially Sharza were death traps.

Then Patches received a communiqué on the Sending Tablet from [[Hasrala]], the ancient [[Saurians|Saurian]] scholar. Hasrala believed she had located a database of addresses for the [[Ringed Veils]] — a way home to [[Faerun|Toril]]. The database lay in an ancient manufactory hundreds of miles to the west, in the [[Thueba Swamp]].

This changed everything — their first major lead on a way home that did not come from the gods of this world. They put it to a vote: they were already close to Mt. Saqar, so they would enter the volcano first — find the Titanic Forge and the dragon [[Horatio Von Bumidere|Buumi]] had been told to save. After that, they would make for Thueba Swamp, and if they passed Sharza on the way, they would scout it.

Ventari shook his head at the mention of Sharza but was more than willing to enter the volcano. They packed up and began the trek.

## The Caldera

Within an hour, the terrain transformed from sandy badlands to black volcanic grit punctuated by smoking sulphur pits. The heat, already brutal, became oppressive. A massive chasm scarred the mountainside — the Fiery Gorge, their way in.

Buumi stepped on a thin crust and almost plunged headfirst into a hidden lava pool. Ventari caught him and hauled him back. They began checking their footing and noticed charred humanoid remains — a great many of them. Hands moved to weapons.

Inside the gorge, the sky vanished behind ash and volcanic gas. The only light was the red glow of magma. The heat burned exposed skin. The group fought for breath as they pushed through a half-mile of choking fumes before the gorge walls expanded into the caldera itself.

The sight stopped them cold. A vast lake of lava dominated the crater, fed by a thousand-foot lava-fall. The ruins of a truly ancient city rose from the molten stone. A great figure of ash, smoke, and fire — some elemental being — strode to the edge of the magma river, pulled something from beneath the flowing stone, and disappeared into the smog.

They pressed forward. Scattered pools of lava spread outward from the main lake. Something enormous sat on the caldera rim — the source of the lava-falls — and other shapes moved through the haze. A blast of air and a rending sound preceded a rain of molten stone that sent the group scrambling for cover. When hulking, bony fiends stalked past through the gases, the party hid under concealing magic and held their breath until the pack moved on.

[[Gundren Rockseeker|Gundren]]'s eyes were locked on the partially submerged ruins. He touched a ruined arch and declared it Titanic — not crafted by mortal hands.

## Beneath the Falls

They followed a widening lava-flow for another hour through fire, ash, and smog until they reached the shores of the vast magma lake. A massive arched bridge of Titanic design spanned the lake — and ended directly beneath the lava-falls.

The source of the falls became clear: a gargantuan worm, hundreds of feet above, its twenty-foot maw disgorging an endless stream of molten stone. The group stared in silence.

Gundren theorised that whatever lay beyond the falls — accessed by this architecturally significant bridge — was the Forge. They wrapped themselves in concealing magic, crept past prowling fiend-packs, and began to cross.

At the far end, [[Cirrus Al-Kayan|Cirrus]] created a wall of force to cut through the cascading lava and form a narrow, protected walkway. The heat was almost unbearable. They rushed through one by one, singed and burned. Buumi nearly refused to walk through it until Patches took his hand and led him.

Cirrus dropped the wall. Lava closed over the passage behind them.

They stood in a great tunnel of Titanic design, leading deep into the volcano. Gundren was certain now. Ventari backed him up. This was the way to the Forge.

As they advanced, Feanon was visited by another hallucination — Zalaam in human form, whispering: *"Why do you deal with these pathetic mortals? They should be your thralls."* Not realising it was a vision, Feanon rushed forward to negotiate, and the group followed — straight into a trap. A bucket of lava and stone was dumped on them from a great archway above. Everyone was badly burned.

They pushed forward with more caution, and the tunnel opened into a vast, magma-lit cavern. Chains, pulleys, and cages hung from the ceiling. Before them, chained to the floor in front of an ancient Titanic forge, was a dragon — red and crystalline, tortured by black-robed cultists in skull masks who prodded it with firelances. The creature let out a piercing cry and slumped. It was too weak to free itself.

The [[Cult of Zalaam|cult]] leader addressed the Ruby Crystal Dragon as the group slinked forward, and JmJm and Buumi shared a look. As Patches began to climb the cavern walls to flank, the cult leader's words became discernible — he was trying to convince the dragon to join their cause. A cultist approached and warned him of intruders. The lava trap had announced them.

Buumi and JmJm stepped out of stealth together, buying time for their friends — and for Patches.

The cult leader spoke, his words dripping with enchantment. He said they were expected, for Zalaam had foretold their arrival. He urged them to join or flee. He declared that Zalaam would engulf this world into glorious darkness, and that [[Toril]] was next — that the corruption was already in the [[Underdark]], spreading through the alghouls. *"You have heard the words of doom,"* he said. *"The death of your world is inevitable, as is this one."*
