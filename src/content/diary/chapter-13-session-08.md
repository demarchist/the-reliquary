## The Approach

The "prisoners" approached the dark ritual and were acknowledged by the presiding shaman. He was surprised to see [[Camea]] but appreciated the sacrificial offerings and sent [[Aliyah Faen Tlabbar|Patches]] and [[Tarkathee Daru|Tark]] under guard around the rim of the tar pit to be presented to Camea's father — the Grand Shaman himself.

They spotted a hovel across the bubbling expanse and a number of halflings engaged in ceremony. As they moved along the lip of the crater, they noticed another prisoner: a dwarf of [[Al Askar]], bound and being prepared for sacrifice.

In the [[Ethereal Plane]], the rest of the party watched their companions being led away and followed. [[Cirrus Al-Kayan|Cirrus]] looked down into the pit — and saw it for what it truly was. A prison of epic proportions, primed with human sacrifice to detonate and release the titanic entity within. The seals were on the verge of shattering. Three more sacrifices and the prison would break open.

Cirrus told the others. They had to stop this — now.

## The Ambush

The dwarf prisoner watched the newcomers with sharp eyes, quietly working his bonds loose. If these strangers gave him an opening, he would be ready.

Tark and Patches neared the hovel. Neither was a spellcaster, and neither knew exactly how much longer the Gaseous Form spell would hold their companions in their bottles. They knew only that the moment was close.

A large halfling warrior wearing a bone helmet confronted Camea before the hovel. This was one of her brothers, and he doubted her newfound devotion. She pushed past him to her father — the Grand Shaman — who, though also sceptical, welcomed his daughter back with genuine affection. The Grand Shaman and his cabal were horribly twisted by corruption, their bodies warped and changed. There was no doubt they were fully enthralled by the Death Wyrm imprisoned beneath them.

Camea presented the prisoners. Tark and Patches played their roles well. Convinced that an attack was imminent — because the prisoners had told him so, as part of the bluff — the Grand Shaman dispatched a contingent of warriors to guard the perimeter.

With the tribal forces thinned and the Gaseous Form about to expire, [[Jack Mayer Johnson Mathews|JmJm]] sprang the attack.

## The Battle of the Tar Pits

Galloping from the Ethereal Plane astride [[Steden]] the Nightmare, Cirrus, JmJm, and [[Gundren Rockseeker|Gundren]] crashed into the Grand Shaman and his followers. All hell broke loose.

Tark and Patches shattered their fake bonds as the glass bottles at their hips burst outward, releasing the quickly reconstituting forms of [[Feanon Serinde|Feanon]] and [[Horatio Von Bumidere|Buumi]].

Caught completely off guard by the assault from the Ethereal Plane, the Grand Shaman was hammered by blows and spells. His closest guards were cut down by Patches and Tark before they could rally. Cirrus called forth a powerful celestial that bore down on the Shaman with singular focus, striking him again and again.

The tar pits bubbled. The Shaman called upon his dark god, and undead conglomerates of bone and tar — Necrichors — began to pull themselves from the boiling lake, surging up the walls of the crater.

Across the pit, the dwarf prisoner made his move. He snapped his bonds, pitched his captor into the boiling tar, and sprinted to a nearby pile of confiscated belongings. He drew a greatsword — and as it came free, it dislodged a section of the pile, revealing the handle of an ancient [[Saurian Firelance]].

The dwarf grabbed it, levelled it at the Grand Shaman, and opened fire.

## Hohonu Stirs

The Grand Shaman absorbed brutal hit after brutal hit. He recognised that he was facing opponents of terrifying power — but he could not win through brute force. He could, however, still complete his objective.

He rushed at his traitorous daughter and hurled her from the lip of the crater. Camea struck the rocks below with a sickening crack, crying out in pain, but did not fall into the tar. JmJm and Tark rushed toward the pit to pull her to safety, fighting through halfling warriors, lesser shamans, and the oozing Necrichors.

Then the Grand Shaman called upon [[Hohonu]] and cast a Circle of Death into the pit. Necrotic energy washed outward. Everyone screamed. Camea crumpled to the ground — dead.

The Necrichors struck at Tark, JmJm, and Steden. Their paralytic touch locked Tark in place. JmJm, seeing they were about to be overwhelmed, grabbed his frozen friend and Camea's body and fled into the Ethereal Plane.

High above the battlefield, Cirrus surveyed the carnage — and a stabbing pain lanced through his mind. A dark voice, the same one that had warned him before, spoke from within the prison. Hohonu cast Feeblemind on the warlock. The only thing that saved him was his unyielding faith in [[Selûné]]. He held the image of a full moon in his mind and rode the dark enchantment through to the other side. He resisted — barely.

## The Shaman Rises

Below, the rest of the party had been cutting through the halfling defenders. The Grand Shaman finally fell, his skull crushed by the celestial's assault. The group turned toward the remaining tribal warriors.

Then the Grand Shaman stirred. He sat up, cast a spell at the group, and rose to his feet — regenerating before their eyes.

The tar boiled. A surging tidal wave of scalding pitch erupted from the pit and engulfed Buumi, who screamed as it seared his flesh. More Necrichors formed and surged outward. Cirrus began to hear laughter in his mind as the Death Wyrm projected his power through the weakened veil, revelling in the spectacle.

In the Ethereal Plane, Tark shook off the paralysis and looked across the chaos. He saw the regenerated Shaman — and something else. A distinct cord of power, visible as a luminous thread, connecting the Grand Shaman to the Altar of Hohonu.

The Altar was the key. Destroy it, and the ritual — and the Shaman — would fall.
