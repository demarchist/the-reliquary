## The Battle Turns

[[Fëanon Serinde]] saw the house disintegrating and flew out the back, launching a firebolt at the enemy archer. The ballroom assassin disengaged and fled into the gardens. In the foyer, [[Li Xian]] grabbed [[Cirrus Al Kayan]] by the front of his robes, dragged the warlock outside, attempted a curse that failed, then hurled him twenty feet to land hard on the gravel.

Patches was cornered — an assassin in front, another bursting through a doorway behind. She swung the door shut, barged past the attacker, and sprinted through Mr. Shon's office into the servant corridors. She burst into the ballroom, startling her allies as she leaned against the wall, bleeding and catching her breath.

Outside, [[Corpsetaker]] addressed [[Doc]] from above. "I too can call upon the storm!" A massive chain of lightning cascaded into [[Jack Mayer Johnson Mathews]], [[Tarkathee Daru]], and the arcane hand, tearing up the ground and filling the air with ozone.

The Amnian wizard Farrim called up from the crushing hand: "Corpsetaker, help me!" The red-robed figure answered: "Help yourself, fool." Farrim dimension-doored free, took flight on a potion, and the arcane hand chased him across the gardens until the halfling archer Dellig destroyed it with magical arrows.

JmJm and Tark surrounded the cleric Salama. The half-orc, barely alive after their onslaught, called to the skies. A column of radiant fire descended, incinerating her body and pushing both fighters back with the blast. Li Xian cursed Tark with blindness as the monk charged, screaming that the Oni was a family killer.

Across the city, fires burned. The massive red dragon — larger than [[Rahadin]] — continued strafing the harbour. Flashes lit the sky over the keep where another battle raged.

## The Retreat

Crouched in the ruins of a fountain half-disintegrated by Farrim's disintegration spell, Cirrus looked to the sky. Enough was enough. Calling on [[Selune]], he banished both Corpsetaker and Li Xian. Shafts of moonlight descended. Corpsetaker vanished. Li Xian darkened for a moment, then physically shrugged the spell away. The Oni's power held.

Farrim struck. A beam of ruby energy — disintegration — lanced through the space where Cirrus had been an instant before, burning away a ten-foot cube of fountain and garden. Cirrus dove under a hedge. A flash in the sky and Corpsetaker returned, having broken the banishment. It laughed and directed Li Xian to deal with the warlock.

The Oni loomed over him again. Cirrus closed his eyes as the flint blade struck — and a tomb of diamond-hard ice formed around him, stopping the blow. Li Xian's weapon chipped the ice but left the warlock unharmed within.

Two more flashes lit the sky. The cries of adult dragons rent the air. [[Aurinax]] and [[Anaxtrasa]], gold and blue, flew over the city — lit from below by the fires of [[Neverwinter]]. They made straight for the harbour and the ancient red.

Corpsetaker paused, calculating. Then its voice boomed across the battlefield: "We are leaving. Retreat!"

The blind Tark turned toward the sound and called upon the shadows, sucking all sound from around the creature — Silence. But the assassins had already heard. They scattered, misty-stepping into the gardens and fleeing into the night. The Amnian agents Xor and Oana downed potions of flight and rose into the storm.

Ernst, overzealous, outpaced his allies and caught up to a fleeing assassin. The man spun and his dagger found the nobleman's throat. Ernst fell dead. [[Horatio von Bumidere]] screamed, stopped his pursuit, and pulled a diamond from his belt. He prayed to Helm. The gem was absorbed and Ernst's wounds closed. The young man breathed again.

Patches caught another assassin, stabbed him in the back of the leg, garroted him with a tripwire until he lost consciousness, and bound him for interrogation.

[[Gundren Rockseeker]] and [[Gwen]] piled out through a window as the smoke became unbearable. Fëanon flew to the front of the crumbling manor and saw Farrim preparing a teleport. He counterspelled — but Farrim countered back. Li Xian, Corpsetaker, Farrim, and Dellig vanished in a flash of arcane light, the Oni unleashing one final cone of cold across the front of the house as they departed.

## The Cost

As it left, Corpsetaker imparted a gift. A feeblemind enchantment seized Cirrus within his ice tomb, making him a prisoner in his own mind — a mental vegetable with almost no control of his body. When the ice melted, the warlock turned to see familiar faces and shambled toward them, drooling.

The assassins fled into the night, leaving the Bumidere estate in ruins. The front gates were thrown wide and twenty city guard rushed down the gravel drive, led by a hulking Dragonborn. All of the Heralds were battered, bloody, and reeling. Above the harbour, dragons battled. Flashes still lit the sky over the keep. The storm rumbled on.

No word had been heard from [[Sundabar]] since the attack began.
