## Sloodublop

Sloodublop lay in ruins and the streets were drenched in blood, but the demonic ritual had been stopped.

The group ambled over to the somewhat decrepit Duergar steamship, JmJm already leaning on the railing and waving for everyone to come aboard.

Back in the main plaza, by the ruined shrine to the Deep Father, [[shuushar|Shuushar]] and Ploop began to talk, the last three cultists tied up and unconscious at their feet.

Shuushar left the gathering of his people and followed the group over to the Duergar ship.

[[tark|Tark]] asked the Kuo-Toa if he intended to stay. He did.

Still wondering about Shuushar's meditative techniques, the older Kuo-Toa said that out in the darkness, with the absolute silence of the [[darklake|Darklake]], he had found enlightenment and freedom from madness.

[[tark|Tark]] resolved to find a quiet spot on the [[darklake|Darklake]] to meditate.

Meanwhile, on the ship, [[hemeth|Hemeth]] had come up from below deck and begun to converse with the group, albeit with some difficulty.

[[patches|Patches]] and [[tark|Tark]] understood the Duergar fine in Undercommon, but his Deep Dwarvish took some getting used to for [[gundren-rockseeker|Gundren]].

[[hemeth|Hemeth]] called [[gundren-rockseeker|Gundren]] a blood traitor and showed some veiled hostility toward the surface Dwarf.

Eventually a working system of translation was sorted out and they began to converse.

As the discussion got underway, a large furry form galloped up from the hold below, but stopped short seeing a number of new people.

The massive — Great Dane-sized — baby Steeder stopped and looked over the group with its eight glittering eyes.

[[patches|Patches]] backed away quickly, terrified of the spider, but quickly recognized it as a Duergar beast of burden. She explained this to the group, and indeed the others had sensed no hostility from the beast.

[[hemeth|Hemeth]] introduced his "little girl" Alli, the baby Steeder he had purchased and considered an investment for a future trade route.

[[hemeth|Hemeth]] was a weapons trader who had come to Sloodublop a few days earlier seeking quick profit, selling weapons to both sides.

Things had gone south and he was captured and scheduled to be sacrificed.

His Derro servant Inlebilly had been killed earlier that day.

[[patches|Patches]] noted that the dagger she had given to [[hemeth|Hemeth]] — a venomous blade — was extremely valuable, and the Dwarf agreed, thinking it fitting payment to ferry them across the [[darklake|Darklake]] to the dark iron city of [[gracksuluth|Gracksuluth]].

Not wanting to tarry, the group prepared to leave, bidding farewell to [[shuushar|Shuushar]].

Worried about a demonic insurrection left behind them, [[patches|Patches]] climbed into one of the paddle-steamer wheelhouses, lined up a shot with her heavy crossbow on Blop — Ploop's cult-leading daughter — and fired.

Climbing back up the other side of the boat, only [[doc|Doc B]] noticed the Drow returning, and thought it rather odd.

Stool shifted a little in [[patches|Patches]]'s pack, content to be ferried around and none the wiser.

Meanwhile, the group had done a quick inspection of the ship and noted a few things: the hold was completely empty, there was a single locked cabin — the captain's cabin — and the only other room was the engine room.

Noting the empty hold, [[hemeth|Hemeth]] asked for help retrieving his goods from the Kuo-Toa, and the group spent the next hour ferrying heavy crates back into the hold.

As they moved the crates of ordinary weapons, they came across one smaller Zurkhwood crate.

[[tark|Tark]] smelled something familiar and the group asked [[hemeth|Hemeth]] what was inside.

The Duergar did not answer at first.

When the crate was safely in the hold, they asked again, and they noticed the Duergar growing nervous.

They opened the crate and found pounds of Halfling leaf and Orcish Rudgrub along with a dozen art pieces and decorative clothing.

Contraband for Duergar.

[[patches|Patches]] explained the Duergar aversion to art.

The Drow pocketed quite a bit of the Halfling Leaf and [[hemeth|Hemeth]] did not protest as the group agreed to assist in his smuggling.

During this conversation, the group noticed a few interesting things about [[hemeth|Hemeth]] — he was wearing beard extensions and was much younger than he appeared.

Indeed, once this was figured out, the Duergar removed the fake beard, revealing whiskers shorter than [[tark|Tark]] even.

[[hemeth|Hemeth]] was only in his mid-twenties — barely past childhood for a Dwarf — and was very close to being clanless. He needed to make a name for himself as a trader or face being cast out.

He explained that profit and business success were everything to the Duergar and that punishment came in only two flavors: death, or fines.

He mentioned he had a few leads on the way to [[gracksuluth|Gracksuluth]] that could help him significantly — a rumored iron deposit, and a scrap of a map that he had paid a wizard to scry on for him.

The group agreed they could check these things out, as long as they were on the way.

[[hemeth|Hemeth]] swore that using the Duergar Locks, he could have them to [[gracksuluth|Gracksuluth]] in six days, including the detours.

Now keen to get underway, the conversation turned to [[snorbul|Snorbul]].

Realizing [[snorbul|Snorbul]] was only tangentially with the group, [[hemeth|Hemeth]] pulled out his dagger and made clear he intended to put the Deep Gnome to work.

[[hemeth|Hemeth]] headed up to the deck and most of the group followed, though [[tark|Tark]] and [[gundren-rockseeker|Gundren]] got distracted.

[[tark|Tark]] looked around the engine room, noting an old and dirty bed, chamber pot, large boiler, and a huge barrel of coal.

Examining the dirty bed, [[tark|Tark]] found a journal hidden underneath a loose plank — the Derro servant's journal.

Reading it proved complicated, as it appeared to be mostly crude pictures. Deciphering it, [[tark|Tark]] got a sense of a life of travel and trade, but one that had not gone well.

[[gundren-rockseeker|Gundren]] entered the engine room and started taking notes on the mechanics, nodding at the obvious design. He did find some interesting runecraft though and began noting the sound and heat-muffling properties of the runes.

Meanwhile, [[tark|Tark]] headed back upstairs, puzzled by the piles of dirty rocks in the engine room.

The use of the coal was explained to [[tark|Tark]] just as the group was deciding what to do about [[snorbul|Snorbul]].

The conversation went surprisingly well given it started with coercion, and ended with [[hemeth|Hemeth]] taking [[snorbul|Snorbul]] on as the engineer for the boiler room.

Not certain what the gnome was lying about, only that he was a bit shifty, the group pressed [[snorbul|Snorbul]] with a few hard questions.

With some pressure, he spilled — he was not wealthy, but he had leads on treasure in some of the older, unsettled parts of [[blingdenstone|Blingdenstone]]. If the group got him there, he would cut them in.

Mildly put out, [[tark|Tark]] swore that no one would shovel more coal than him and returned to the engine room, determined to out-do [[snorbul|Snorbul]], who did not complain.

Finally, the ship shuddered to life as the boiler was fed a steady supply of coal, and the wheeled Duergar vessel churned the waters, bellowing black smoke, heading out into the darkness of the underground lake.

## Sailing the Darklake

The ship steamed ahead, quickly putting the phosphorescent lights of Sloodublop in the distance, the noise of the ship muffled by its magical construction.

[[gundren-rockseeker|Gundren]] went around the ship doing what he could to fix the old bucket of bolts. Coming across the deck cannon, the old artificer noted the barrel was cracked and went about mending it.

Before long, more of the group had gathered to figure out if they had any powder and shot.

[[patches|Patches]] pulled a dozen pounds of black powder from her pack, already packed in small charges, and asked if they would work.

The group looked at the Drow infiltrator, flabbergasted that she had been carrying around so many explosives.

[[patches|Patches]] shrugged.

With the black powder, and hundreds of ball-bearings provided by [[gundren-rockseeker|Gundren]], they cobbled together six good shots for the aft gun.

The first day of travel went by quite uneventfully and, compared to traversing the tunnels of the [[underdark|Underdark]], it was far more relaxing.

[[hemeth|Hemeth]] had the group on lookout at the front of the ship to call out any stalactites or hidden rocks, but he was a good pilot despite his age and everything was avoided easily.

The group took the day for some much-needed rest.

[[fey|Fey]] dug through Strahd's spellbook, [[patches|Patches]] made bombs, [[tark|Tark]] shoveled coal with [[snorbul|Snorbul]] — who went on about the Duergar's terrible mining practices — [[cirrus-al-kayan|Cirrus]] took watch, JmJm posed on the bow of the ship, and [[doc|Doc]] tried to peer into the water for local wildlife.

After eight hours of solid progress across the dark waters, [[hemeth|Hemeth]] announced he needed a rest.

[[tark|Tark]] took the wheel and [[cirrus-al-kayan|Cirrus]] took watch. Between the two of them, they avoided the rocks and kept their heading straight.

The only thing of note was that [[tark|Tark]] spotted a small Kuo-Toa raft hiding behind a rock pillar, giving the Duergar steamboat a wide berth.

The ship continued through the "night," [[hemeth|Hemeth]] taking the wheel back after a few hours of rest.

The sleeping arrangements were less than stellar, and the group decided they needed proper relaxation.

They found a place to moor the ship and [[fey|Fey'Anon]] cast his Magnificent Mansion, [[snorbul|Snorbul]] and [[hemeth|Hemeth]] joining the group in the luxury of the extra-dimensional pocket — once again being truly astounded by the group's ability.

The group took turns watching the boat in the material plane while the others rested.

Over the night, [[tark|Tark]] and [[patches|Patches]] both noted flickering of bioluminescence and investigated, finding schools of fish darting around rock formations beneath the surface.

Realizing the danger of straying from the group alone in the dark, no one investigated further, and the night was uneventful.

The second day of travel began, and with the group on lookout and assisting [[hemeth|Hemeth]], the early hours were rather boring, though some phosphorescent fungi and fish broke up the dark monotony.

Later, [[hemeth|Hemeth]] remarked they were getting close to the location of the first detour — the map fragment.

After an hour or so, a massive stone pillar loomed out of the darkness. [[hemeth|Hemeth]] circled it and found a good place to moor.

The group disembarked to look around for something.

Twenty minutes went by.

[[gundren-rockseeker|Gundren]] and [[fey|Fey]] found a piece of stonework inlaid with ancient bronze — reminiscent of old Netherese — but there was no intact stonework around the base of the pillar.

On a hunch, [[patches|Patches]] climbed the pillar, and sure enough, noted ancient architecture wedged in a crack along the cavern ceiling above.

[[patches|Patches]] anchored herself with her climbing kit, called down to the group, and dropped a rope — though even its full length did not quite reach the cavern floor.

[[fey|Fey]], [[gundren-rockseeker|Gundren]], and [[patches|Patches]] headed up to investigate the ancient ruins in the ceiling while everyone else remained below to guard the ship.

Twenty minutes went by. [[tark|Tark]] noted a strange amphibious creature paddling a small raft through the darkness, and [[cirrus-al-kayan|Cirrus]] expounded on the virtues of the moon goddess to two [[underdark|Underdark]] dwellers who had never seen the moon.

[[doc|Doc B]] then remembered he could get [[tark|Tark]] and [[cirrus-al-kayan|Cirrus]] up to the rope easily. He shaped the earth into a ramp and told his companions that he and JmJm would secure the boat — they should go help the others.

They agreed and climbed up to the ceiling and the ancient ruins contained there.

## The Tomb

The ancient ruins were unsettling and off-kilter, held aloft in the cavern ceiling by some ancient seismic event.

[[patches|Patches]], [[gundren-rockseeker|Gundren]], and [[fey|Fey]] investigated the place, finding ancient magical traps and murals depicting a sorcerer of Netheril, its servants bowing before it.

Heading deeper into the ruins, they found a large chamber with artwork recently gouged and vandalized, and an altar of grey marble smashed to dust.

Another trap was found and dismantled in the middle of the room, and two secret doors were discovered — one leading to a short hallway, the other to a burial chamber with four sarcophagi.

[[tark|Tark]] and [[cirrus-al-kayan|Cirrus]] caught up at this point and assisted in investigating the room.

The group quickly determined that the four sarcophagi contained undead, and that a small urn — when rotated — would open the sarcophagi as well as reveal a passage to another room.

[[fey|Fey]] magically lightened one of the lids and the group triggered the urn after trapping the remaining stone containers.

Wraiths and shadows rose from the sarcophagi but the group, well-practiced against the undead, annihilated them quickly and without injury.

The group moved to the swiveling wall section revealed by the switch and triggered it, stepping into another room — this one untouched and pristine, though coated in thick dust.

A large white marble sarcophagus covered in copper relief, depicting a sorcerer on its stone lid, took up most of one thirty-foot wall.

As the group moved in, they noticed a perfect area of dustless stone by the foot of the sarcophagus.

[[fey|Fey]] sensed an enchantment and determined it to be an invisible chest.

He went over and opened the top, catching the glitter of treasure within — but heard the grind of stone as the slab atop the sepulcher began to move.

A linen-wrapped hand, covered in rings and jewelry, gripped the edge of the sarcophagus, and the mummy contained within began to rise.
