## The Party

The group won the battle in the fighting pits and headed back into the ready-room.

As they left the arena, several of them noticed a hulking figure in a heavy black cloak watching them from the crowd, and then leave.

Relaxing and recuperating in the ready-room, they discussed what to do next as the Duergar handed them their winnings.

While relaxing, a knock at the door alerted them. They checked it out and saw the Drow Wizard that had just fought them.

Dano Barrison Del Armgo proposed a job for the group — mercenary escorts for the noble entourage and their "purchases" back to [[menzoberranzan|Menzoberranzan]].

He took his leave, giving the group four days to think it over and 100gp a day as a retainer to be delivered to the Town House.

[[cirrus-al-kayan|Cirrus]], [[snorbul|Snorbul]] and [[gundren-rockseeker|Gundren]] decided to celebrate with their winnings.

As they looked over the massive amount of money, Fey rushed forward with the depositing wand and whisked it away.

Patches yelled at Fey as they didn't have a lot of cash on hand and she needed the platinum for a Bag of Holding — she was currently holding the Artifact, which made her very uncomfortable.

The group split up, with Patches looking to gamble to make money, Cirrus, [[gundren-rockseeker|Gundren]] and [[snorbul|Snorbul]] going on a bender with Tark as chaperone, and Fey deciding to go and talk to [[themberchaud|Themberchaud]] the dragon.

Seeing Fey going off alone, Patches decided to follow the Wizard at first.

Heading upstairs, the party group found the shady part of the Shattered Spire and quickly perused and ordered a number of different drugs.

[[gundren-rockseeker|Gundren]] was strapped down and took some Rage while the others engaged in [[cirrus-al-kayan|Cirrus]]' habit of inhaled drugs.

Thinking it a fantastic idea to go to the fighting pits again, they began to untie [[gundren-rockseeker|Gundren]] but were quickly discouraged by the Duergar around them. Instead, Gundren was taken to the pits to be released.

[[cirrus-al-kayan|Cirrus]], [[snorbul|Snorbul]] and Tark headed down to the main floor.

Thinking that they needed Patches, [[snorbul|Snorbul]] rushed out the main door and began across the bridge to the main city, stopping by several Drow and asking "Patches?".

Eventually he caught up to Fey and Patches, both talking on the bridge and watching the little Gnome's progress.

Patches headed back to assist Tark in chaperoning and Fey continued on alone, flagging down a rickshaw and heading deeper into the city.

Looking for something interesting, the group explored the establishment until they found what they were looking for — a back room for higher-stakes gambling.

Being let in by the guards due to their good showing and some effective persuasion, the group headed deeper into the establishment.

In the back room they found a huge chamber with numerous activities: fighting, card games, dice and a high-stakes Duergar trading game.

Settling in to watch, it was apparent that the main game was too complicated for them to pick up in a single night, so they settled on betting instead.

Patches ended up searching for a game she could play and cheat at, finding the card tables and noting the Drow noble retinue from earlier relaxing in one corner of the room.

She avoided the other Drow and began to play, cheating fabulously and getting some decent winnings.

## The Orc

Fey meanwhile was heading toward Laduager's Furrow, moving through the Duergar section of the city.

Up ahead, a number of carts suffered from a collision and his rickshaw was diverted down an alleyway.

Noting this, Fey was immediately put on guard and unsurprised when the rickshaw puller was ripped away from the cart, causing it to tip and deposit the Wizard.

Fey rolled and quickly came to his feet, ready.

He watched as two tentacles, covered in grasping tusks and fangs, ripped the rickshaw driver apart.

The massive cloaked figure from the Shattered Spire attacked, tentacles striking out from under the cloak to tear at Fey.

He raised a shield and extricated himself quickly, hasting himself and running as fast as he could back to the main thoroughfare.

An ethereal creature pulled itself out of the wall as he passed and the Incubus attempted to Charm Fey. The Incubus laughed and introduced the cloaked figure as [[ront|Ront]], the Orc they had abandoned back in Sludubloop that had escaped Velkynvelve with them.

The wizard stumbled but shook the mental assault and continued running, making it back to the main avenue with no sign of pursuit.

Noting a large patrol of Devils, Fey pointed them down the alleyway to take care of a demonic incursion.

Fey rushed back toward the rest of the group, unwilling to risk travelling further by himself.

Back in the Shattered Spire, the group remembered that [[gundren-rockseeker|Gundren]] had gone to the fighting pits alone.

They headed to the pits in time to see [[gundren-rockseeker|Gundren]] crushing Goblins with his bare hands and getting absolutely covered in blood.

The Rage began to fade after a few rounds and the exhausted Gundren rejoined his friends, their buzz beginning to wane.

Fey found them down by the ready-room again, bloodied himself.

Now back together, they re-applied [[gundren-rockseeker|Gundren]]'s makeup, [[cirrus-al-kayan|Cirrus]] went and vomited, and Fey began to tell them what had happened to him when trying to cross the city.

Discussing the attack, they remembered [[ront|Ront]] and figured that somehow it was related to [[demogorgon|Demogorgon]].

The group decided that they would head back to their lodgings to rest, but Fey declined, saying he had to make his appointment in the Hall of Sacred Scrolls.

[[cirrus-al-kayan|Cirrus]] sent a tiny messenger to the Hall of Scrolls to postpone the meeting to translate [[iron-tome|the Iron Tome]], but the response made it clear that this would be the only time such an appointment could be kept.

Tired, the group decided to go together and wait while Fey had his appointment.

## The Tome

They headed through the city to the Hall of Sacred Scrolls and rented a small reading room, though [[gundren-rockseeker|Gundren]] and Tark peeled off to go and work on Gwen at the Workshop.

The group took a short rest while they waited and then Fey was taken to his appointment.

Entering a massive office and personal library of sorts, Fey saw a strange, yet familiar creature.

A fox-faced and fur-covered Arcanaloth from the plane of Gehenna greeted him, introducing itself as Lolanaroth, or Lola.

Fey took out [[iron-tome|the Iron Tome]] and they began to negotiate a price for the translation.

After some haggling, Fey traded information on Barovia and the new location of Castle Ravenloft, and Lola began to help.

Over the next hour, with their combined intellect and magic, they managed to decipher the tome.

Reading the first page of [[iron-tome|the Iron Tome]], the red leather cover changed, eleven eyes opening on the cover and looking around.

The sentient magical artifact was a spellbook and began to ask after its old master.

With its old master long dead, the link was non-existent and with an influx of power, Fey caused the book to bond with him, claiming [[iron-tome|the Iron Tome]] as his own.

He thanked Lola for her work and they parted ways, Fey returning to the reading room where he collected the others and they headed back to the Townhouse to get some proper rest.

## The Alchemist

Back at the Townhouse, Tark was already there sleeping on the couch, the Cave Bear cub asleep on his chest.

Doc greeted the group and JmJm stormed from his room right up to Fey and demanded to return to the surface as he could no longer stand [[gracksuluth|Gracksuluth]] and its dour mood.

Fey and Doc decided to try and find a tree for some other means of quick traversal, but the idea lost steam when they realized where they were and that the Duergar city was exceedingly tree-less.

With no high-level spells to get anyone topside, the group rested and slept through to the next day, though they were disturbed by nightmares.

Waking up early, Patches took the lead to head to the Muzgardt Brewery and find her old contact [[claudine|Claudine]].

Wanting to peruse the potions of the famous Muzgardt Clan, the group headed out with Patches to the clan compound.

Arriving without incident, [[cirrus-al-kayan|Cirrus]] provided a vocal distraction as a high-paying client to be shown the goods, giving Patches the opening to slip away to find [[claudine|Claudine]].

Perusing the alchemy shops themselves, it didn't take long to find the one with an older female human behind the counter.

The twenty years showed heavily on her human features, but it was unmistakable — Patches recognized her old friend.

Patches entered the shop and asked to peruse. [[claudine|Claudine]] did not recognize the Drow.

The old human let out a wracking cough and pulled away a handkerchief with specks of blood on it.

As the old lady moved out to assist Patches in perusing the alchemical supplies and potions, a ruckus from the back of the store got the Rogue's attention.

Looking back, she saw a half-Drow girl exit the backroom carrying a large tray of healing potions to restock the shelves. The girl called to [[claudine|Claudine]], "Ma, where do you want this batch?"

[[claudine|Claudine]] turned and motioned to a shelf, saying "Over there Zella."

Patches looked back to the human in front of her, pulled the platinum mask from her face, and [[claudine|Claudine]] hid her shock well.

Leaning in, [[claudine|Claudine]] asked, "...Alyah?..."
