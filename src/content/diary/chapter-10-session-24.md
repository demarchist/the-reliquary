The group spent two weeks in the [[selunia|Barony]], attending to their duties as lords of the region.

## Two Weeks of Rest

[[feanon-serinde|Feanon]] researched the [[mythallar|Mythallar]] and had a breakthrough — the device was not damaged but simply unfinished. It would need additional power and more Prismeum to be completed. [[tarkathee-daru|Tark]] disappeared into the forest for a fortnight, occasionally meeting [[horatio-von-bumidere|Buumi]], who brought him baked treats. [[cirrus-al-kayan|Cirrus]] spent the time walking through the town, providing coin for the upcoming Spring Planting Festival. [[horatio-von-bumidere|Buumi]] and [[ezmerelda|Ezmerelda]] began researching the Plane of Fire and the Efreeti, hoping to save the prisoners left behind during the raid. [[gundren-rockseeker|Gundren]] adjusted the Efreeti Chain armour and maintained his network — adding the [[baldurs-gate|Baldur's Gate]] barge into the growing Rockseeker trade empire.

Word arrived from [[scornubel|Scornubel]] — the Efreeti had pulled back after the theft. [[shal-bartok|Shal Bartok]], disgraced by losing the armour, had been recalled to the [[city-of-brass|City of Brass]] in shame. His vassals withdrew from the front lines with their accumulated prisoners and treasures, giving a tremendous boost to the Allied war effort.

[[willow-aldencroft|Willow Aldencroft]] visited [[horatio-von-bumidere|Buumi]] for a meal and stayed overnight at [[aliyah-faen-tlabbar|Patches']] establishment before returning to [[silverymoon|Silverymoon]]. [[horatio-von-bumidere|Buumi]] suspected he was being plied for information, though the conversation had been innocent enough — talk of home, of [[neverwinter|Neverwinter]].

[[jack-mayer-johnson-mathews|JmJm]] returned from [[silverymoon|Silverymoon]] with [[gneiss|Gneiss]] and [[chani|Chani]]. The two retainers sought out [[tarkathee-daru|Tark]] and reported what they had found in the East — two dead monks of the Yellow Rose who had come west from [[damara|Damara]].

## The Spring Planting Festival

[[feanon-serinde|Feanon]] acquired a scroll of Hallucinatory Terrain from Jin the Illusionist and cast it over the [[selunia|Barony]]. For one glorious day, miles of land transformed into a blossoming paradise of gardens and growing things. [[cirrus-al-kayan|Cirrus]] had no idea what was happening and panicked before becoming thoroughly annoyed with [[feanon-serinde|Feanon]]. [[aliyah-faen-tlabbar|Patches]] meditated in her sanctum, savouring the spectacle.

The group went up to [[kossenberg|Kossenberg]] for the festival. Moon Elves emerged from the forest to join the celebration — [[aliyah-faen-tlabbar|Patches]] recognising the Moon Elf druid Kieran from an encounter decades past. Ale flowed, pigs were roasted, and winter wines were brought out under the starry sky as the sun set and the full moon rose.

The whole town processed to the Old Stone Druid grove, where [[doc|Doc]] attended the Moon Elves in blessing the lands. [[cirrus-al-kayan|Cirrus]] addressed the crowd and was showered with adoration as he venerated the moon and the local customs. They returned to the village centre and danced around a Maypole until the small hours, the Elves weaving particularly dazzling patterns, all under the bioluminescent glow of [[feanon-serinde|Feanon's]] enchantment.

## The Calling of Moonfire

Not wanting to miss another celebration, the group gathered to [[cirrus-al-kayan|Cirrus]] and plane shifted to [[svartalfheim|Svartalfheim]].

In [[dokkar|Dokkar]], under a blazing full moon, the city was subdued in veneration. They were directed to the public gardens on the first tier — a space whose dimensions seemed to stretch and change to accommodate the gathering. Dark Elves and guests reclined on blankets, basking in the calm glow. [[aliyah-faen-tlabbar|Patches]] met up with [[finn|Finn]].

At the heart of the gardens, some two hundred ancient [[drow-faction|Dark Elf]] masters stood in a circle, each in pure white robes, their hands raised to the sky. A beam of light rose from them to the moon, motes of starlight and moonlight floating within. Every so often, a mote exploded like a tiny supernova, its energy drizzling down like soft rainfall onto the outstretched hands of one of the masters — blazing as white fire before cooling to flowing liquid mercury.

Each master used their called Moonfire to perform a miracle. Repairing a magical artefact. Healing a vicious wound. Raising someone from the dead. Finishing a work of art.

[[cirrus-al-kayan|Cirrus]] looked on in open jealousy, wondering why he did not have Moonfire. [[horatio-von-bumidere|Buumi]] put a hand on his shoulder and told him it would happen. Have faith.

[[aliyah-faen-tlabbar|Patches]], meanwhile, realised something. She summoned [[eclipse|Eclipse]] into her hand and willed it to blaze with white fire — identical to the Moonfire before them. Several nearby [[drow-faction|Dark Elves]] turned curious eyes upon her. [[cirrus-al-kayan|Cirrus]] was livid.

## The Legend of the Last Word

During the ceremony, [[feanon-serinde|Feanon]] cast Legend Lore — asking the universe about [[the-last-word|The Last Word]] in this place of [[selune|Selûne]] and [[eilistraee|Eilistraee]].

He witnessed the birth of the universe and the last fragment of primordial power coalescing into the Word. It floated in the void for an eternity before being found by ancient alien beings — possibly precursors to the Aboleths — and remained at the heart of their empire for another eternity, until that civilisation fell into darkness and decay.

There it lay, forgotten, until a brutally wounded [[orcus|Orcus]] — fleeing across the planes from Primus, God of Order — stumbled across the ruins and took up [[the-last-word|The Last Word]]. He struck down Primus and destroyed the god, though temporarily. The power was so vast that it consumed [[orcus|Orcus]] in a blaze of glory, the other gods simply biding their time. He was nearly obliterated but eventually reformed in the Abyss and reclaimed his dominion.

The Word was split apart by the Greater Pantheon. Its fragments were scattered across the multiverse:

One fell to [[faerun|Faerûn]], smashing the continent and creating the Spine of the World, its power drawing the Primordial Maegera to the area in the early epochs. Another floated in the Astral Plane for millennia before being found by the Archmages of [[silverymoon|Silverymoon]] and used in a powerful binding. Another fell between the fabric of reality and vanished from sight. Another was found by humans in a far-off land — possibly another world — at the heart of a magocracy that rivalled the [[netherese|Netherese]] in scope and dwarfed them in scale, until that empire fell to a dark outside force and sank into the ocean. From the depths, a Gold Dragon retrieved it and delivered it to Celestia and the kingdom of [[bahamut|Bahamut]]. Another fell into the Lower Planes, swallowed by fire, eventually finding its way to the Yugoloths of Gehenna and then to the Abyss, where the lesser goddess Kiaransalee, the Banshee Queen, tried to use it against [[orcus|Orcus]] — and failed.

The last fragment was seen plummeting to a world, leaving a great crater. Industrial-looking humans plucked it from the silt of the sea that formed there and began to experiment on it using nothing more than alchemy and science. The final image showed them constructing a chamber and placing the fragment inside.

Then the Legend Lore simply ended.

[[feanon-serinde|Feanon]] opened his eyes, absorbing what he had witnessed.

## Into the Labyrinth

The group returned to [[faerun|Faerûn]] and took a day to absorb their pilgrimage. Then they gathered in the town centre, stocked with supplies and potions. [[jack-mayer-johnson-mathews|JmJm]] assembled his own party — himself, [[doc|Doc]], [[gneiss|Gneiss]], [[chani|Chani]], Jin, and [[ezmerelda|Ezmerelda]] — bound south to the war, to search for JmJm's Stephen and to free as many slaves from the [[amnians|Amnians]], [[red-wizards-of-thay|Thayans]], and Efreeti as they could.

The two adventuring parties wished each other luck. [[doc|Doc]] cast Wind Walk, and his group dissolved into mist and swept away to the south.

[[veygar|Veygar]] approached, armed and ready. He explained that they were heading to [[the-labyrinth|the Labyrinth]], to seek another Fragment of [[the-last-word|the Word]] in ancient ruins discovered at its heart.

They teleported to the edge of [[the-labyrinth|the Labyrinth]] and headed into the darkness of the [[underdark|Underdark]]. Without the abyssal corruption of their previous visits, the place was positively pleasant — threats spotted and avoided with ease. [[gundren-rockseeker|Gundren]] found an intricate piece of Modron machinery that seized his attention so completely it alarmed [[feanon-serinde|Feanon]], who began to watch the Dwarf as he stared, unmoving, at the metal piece for long stretches.

After two days of travel — never lost, thanks to the [[drow-faction|Drow]] pathfinder and [[veygar|Veygar's]] memory — they began to see architecture. Distinctly foreign in shape and truly ancient, the walls showed signs of being worked with unusual complexity. Panels and lines bore occasional metal fittings.

[[horatio-von-bumidere|Buumi]] barged forward, overconfident, and triggered a trap. A nearby vent opened and dozens of shining, hissing steam-powered beetles swarmed forth. The group destroyed them quickly but were shocked by the complexity of the automatons — similar in make to the mechanical spiders they had fought so long ago beneath the Troll Hills north of [[baldurs-gate|Baldur's Gate]].

They pressed deeper, disarming some traps and triggering others — [[veygar|Veygar]] deactivating the latter with a key. [[aliyah-faen-tlabbar|Patches]] marked a pressure plate at an intersection, spotting another vent ahead.

Intrigued by the ruins and their mechanical guardians, the group paused to consider their options as they began to breach the outer defences.
