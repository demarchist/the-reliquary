## The Sleigh Ride and the Dream

The sleigh whispered over alpine snow, making a rapid descent down the slopes of [[Shatterpeak]]. Exhausted, the group lounged in the back, taking turns at the reins. Healing prayers were murmured. Bandages passed around freely. One healer's kit was spent entirely.

An hour into the ride, a shockwave overtook the sleigh — shaking trees, dumping snow from branches. They looked back to see avalanches cascading down the mountain range, the rumble rolling after. The detonation of the [[Sanctum of Krasus]] was lost in the low cloud cover of a winter storm raging over the peaks, but the shockwave left no doubt.

Over the next eight hours they travelled uneventfully. [[Fëanon Serinde]] studied the new spells and spellbooks. [[Cirrus Al Kayan]] prayed to [[Selûne]], identifying the magical items they had procured. The haul proved valuable, and the group distributed gear amongst themselves. [[Megno Slatesunder]] declined a share — his prize was the trove of books, and that was what the dwarves of [[Sundabar]] had paid for. Fëanon was disappointed at losing the tomes but accepted Megno's promise of translated copies.

On the second day, they watched a wild wyvern swoop down and catch a giant elk — marvelling at the scale of two great beasts struggling to survive. That evening, the group settled in to sleep, the moose augmented by summoned mounts to pull the sleigh.

[[Tarkathee Daru]] fell asleep and dreamed of his rescue from the barrow in [[Vaasa]], where his family was murdered and [[Gaddock]] had saved him. But in the dream, the heroes took a different boy. Tark was left behind, staring at the corpse of Li Xian, the necromancer who had killed his family. Minutes turned to hours in the dark, the smell of blood and viscera overwhelming.

Then Li Xian's corpse moved. Wounds closed. The man stood. At the barrow entrance, a wood elf appeared — Neero, the Oni who had attacked them at the farm outside [[Silverymoon]]. The disguised Oni transformed into his hulking purple form and approached Li Xian, who morphed into an Oni as well. Tark recognised the features. He saw the revealed Oni pick up a wicked flint knife and place it on his belt.

The truth crystallised. Li Xian — the man, the Oni — who had murdered Tark's family in the service of [[Zhengyi]] was the same Oni they had just faced in the Sanctum. The dream ended with the two Oni conversing in an unknown tongue. Tark woke shaking and sweating, called out to the group, and told them everything, tears falling.

They could not undo what he had learned. But it confirmed the Oni attacks were connected to Zhengyi. Tark retreated under a blanket and went invisible.

## Payday and Investigations

The group reached Sundabar as night fell. Megno returned the sleigh and two very tired moose to the stables outside the East Gate. They passed through the snowy Uppercity, now half-rebuilt and bustling despite winter. This time, their passage drew attention. The party was becoming recognisable — word of their role in stopping the coup in Silverymoon had spread, coupled with the dwarf patrol rescue, the shattered castle in the desert, and tales from Baldur's Gate. People followed, whispering and pointing.

Buumi unfurled his new wings — granted by a magical feather from the Sanctum — to impress the crowd with the glory of Helm. Unfortunately, he whacked a small child (saved by Patches) and knocked most of the group down as he spun in place. He sheepishly put them away. Tark stepped in and began telling the group's story from the very beginning. Two hours later, he looked around to find the crowd dispersed. The last listener, a deaf old man, wandered off. Tark headed to Megno's workshop.

Meanwhile, the rest had settled into the workshop. Molly had them at the Everstew. Megno checked in with the Explorers League. When Tark arrived, Molly handed Patches a letter and mentioned that a woman from "The Guild" had come looking for Fëanon earlier and would return the next day.

Cirrus settled into a plush chair and pulled out an analgesic grenade. The group intervened — fed up with his addiction and the helpless state it induced. Patches grabbed the grenade, punctured it with the Luck Blade, and hurled the hissing thing out the window. The group staged an intervention. Cirrus pointed out everyone else's vices, but no one relented — the paralysing effects were far worse than drinking or smoking. Cirrus agreed to stop. He did not mention the four additional grenades in his pack.

Tark headed into the city looking for trouble and found himself outside [[The Short and Curlies]], where guards milled about and a dwarven cleric was emerging. Eavesdropping, Tark learned there had been three murders — the Zhentarim agents Patches had killed — and the investigation was clean; none of the spirits could identify their killer. The guard captain recognised Tark and informed him that he and Patches would be questioned the next day, as both had been placed at the scene of the bank heist.

Back at the workshop, Tark burst in and told everyone about the murder investigation. Patches sighed, made some calculations, then came clean — she had killed them on orders from [[Lady Faye]]. They were Zhentarim agents, and she was Faye's sword. Tark was horrified. Buumi took issue, insisting on due process, but knowing Faye was a Baldur's Gate noble and trusting Patches' account, he let it rest. Megno brushed the investigation under the rug through the Intelligence Office.

## Neverwinter

The next morning, a bald, caramel-skinned elf woman in wizard's robes arrived — Ossea, a representative of The Guild. She carried the sigil for [[Neverwinter]]'s teleportation gate, paid for by Esimir Von Bumidere. The condition: Fëanon must join The Guild. Two hundred platinum per year, formal responsibilities — but they would build a teleportation ring at a location of his choosing and waive all travel fees. Fëanon found it manipulative but agreed.

A second visitor arrived shortly after — Muldren Shaw, a very short, wide, raspy-voiced dwarven Inspector. He was not here to punish anyone for the murders. He wanted to hire Patches for two kills at the [[Council of Helm's Hold]]: Abdul Al Selemchant, the Amnian ambassador and cousin to [[Shaahir]], and [[S'Olaa Al Kayan]], Shaahir's apprentice and Cirrus's brother. Cooperation would mean the investigation in Sundabar stalled. Tark, unaware of the specifics, tried to throw the dwarf out a window. Buumi restrained him.

Outside, Patches listened but told the group only that she had been asked to protect Abdul at the Council. Everyone believed her — except Tark, who suspected something.

The group packed, bid Megno and Molly farewell, and convinced Megno that his testimony about the Sanctum would strengthen the Council. At the Guild teleportation circle, Fëanon — a new member — was presented with wine and hot towelettes. They powered the circle and stepped through.

They arrived separated. Fëanon, Patches, Gwen, and Buumi appeared in the Bluelake District — the upscale quarter, near Buumi's family estate. Fëanon realised the split was a safety precaution; he had teleported the same instant as another traveller. He recognised the energy signature and sighed. [[Mordenkainen]].

Tark and Gundren found themselves in an alleyway of a shantytown outside the city walls — not a pleasant neighbourhood.

Cirrus stood alone at the rift, sensing an energy that filled him with emotion. Another warlock of [[Ceregorach]]. The Tooth Fairy's creature — his prey — was near.
