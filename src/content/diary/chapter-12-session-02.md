## Acid and Exhaustion

[[Cirrus Al-Kayan|Cirrus]] remained awake through the night, and by morning his exhaustion was visible to everyone.

The group set off into rising temperatures, donning hot-weather gear and rationing water carefully. As the hours of hiking the scorching hills drew on, they could see the coastline ahead — easier terrain at last. So relieved were they that no one noticed the creatures creeping down from the rocky slopes above.

The pack struck without warning. The largest spewed great torrents of burning acid that showered the group in agony. Cirrus took a blast full to the face as he tried instinctively to conjure an ice shield — but his magic did not answer. A Kuthrik stalked forward and drove a long [[Talon]] through the warlock's body.

Cirrus shook in agony but did not scream.

[[Tarkathee Daru|Tark]] forced the creature back, and Cirrus stumbled behind a boulder, sobbing in pain. With [[Feanon Serinde|Feanon]] and [[Gundren Rockseeker|Gundren]] wielding [[Saurian Firelance|firelances]] alongside [[Snorbul]] and Tark, they destroyed the largest Kuthrik and sent the rest fleeing.

[[Horatio Von Bumidere|Buumi]] felt useless — stripped of his magical belt, he had realized how weak he had become. He resolved to get fit again. For now, he carried the horribly wounded and exhausted Cirrus back to the group. [[Liara]] healed the worst of the damage, and Tark patched the rest with his healer's kit.

## Three Nights Without Sleep

They made it out of the badlands and into the rolling coastal hills. As the sun dipped, they found a defensible camp. Cirrus once again refused to sleep.

The group tried everything. Liara sang him a charming lullaby about the moon being chased by the smaller, rapidly orbiting star of Al Askar's binary system. Cirrus enjoyed the moon-related song. He resisted and stayed awake.

That night, behind the hollow stare and the fractured conviction, there flickered a rare moment of insight — a glimmer of sanity still burning in there, holding on, but barely.

Feanon watched the warlock with something approaching concern — but definitely not concern — and wondered if his Deep Slumber spell could be modified with pre-programmed dreams to prevent nightmares. He unfolded his [[Iron Tome]], its metal pages covered in arcane script, and began to research.

Liara looked on with apprehension and warned him: spellcasters did not display their arcane nature so brazenly on Al Askar. Magic had broken the world. To flaunt it invited violence. Feanon reacted with condescension and went back to his research into repairing the broken weave.

## The Road to Ras Alkan

The next morning, Buumi rose early and grilled meat harvested from the previous day's kills to perfection. Everyone ate well — except Cirrus, who was barely conscious.

Snorbul grew excited when he learned that [[Ras Alkan]] might be near the brilliant reef from his vision — the one showing a source of Sovereign Glue. They also discovered that a Ruby Dragon was known to frequent the area, though it had not been seen for weeks.

Both suns climbed high and the heat grew savage, scorching across the dusty red hills with the ocean glittering under the green sky. Cirrus practically fell over. The group decided they should travel at night going forward.

They sheltered through the worst of the heat, then pressed on as temperatures dropped. Tark scouted ahead and spotted a white stone spire — an architectural shape that Liara identified as a trading post and safe haven. An oasis.

Exhausted and eager for civilization, they made for the Spire, taking turns carrying Cirrus. But Tark's next scouting run revealed trouble: a destroyed caravan, corpses scattered and being gnawed by feral beasts.

The group considered avoiding the fight entirely. Buumi disagreed. These creatures would hurt someone else if left alive, and killing them would ingratiate the party with the locals. Gundren pointed out they could loot the merchant caravan too — clearly "finders keepers" law out here.

They found a box canyon, set up an ambush, and Tark lured the beasts in with hurled darts. The creatures bounded after the monk into the killing ground, and the group was ready and waiting.
