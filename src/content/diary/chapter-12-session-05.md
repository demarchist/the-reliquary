## House Taurus

The party was brought into a villa of pale stone, rich wooden furniture, and a courtyard fountain — an obscene display of wealth in a world where water was life itself. Hamda and her mother engaged in a brief argument before the girl stormed off to her room.

The group noted the number of guards and servants — more than a house of this size warranted. Inquiries revealed that House Taurus had fallen on harder times and recently downsized, though the Lady maintained appearances with considerable skill.

[[Horatio Von Bumidere|Buumi]] excused himself to freshen up and change into his nobleman's garb. [[Cirrus Al-Kayan|Cirrus]], meanwhile, insinuated himself with their hostess at every opportunity, schmoozing with practised charm over hors d'oeuvres of strange but delicious foods.

[[Lady Taurus]] reappeared wreathed in expensive silks, every inch the gracious hostess. Buumi returned looking quite gaudy and — to the Lady's visible shock — wearing metal. [[Liara]] covered quickly, explaining that they hailed from a far-off city and something called "The Crucible."

## The Missing Ship

Over the course of dinner, Lady Taurus revealed her true purpose. She was grateful for her daughter's return and had already arranged alternative transport for the girl's journey to [[Iyum]]. In exchange, she would house the party at the villa during her absence — provided they accepted a paying job.

House Taurus was down to its last merchant ship, and it had gone missing near the reef. A pirate band called the Demon Claws was suspected. Find the ship, and the arrangement was sealed.

The group agreed, though they privately wondered if they were being set up to loot someone else's vessel under false pretences and take the blame as outlanders. [[Snorbul]] had his own reason for accepting: the reef matched his vision of the legendary Sovereign Glue, and he reminded the others of that prophecy with barely contained excitement.

## Faith Confirmed

That night, Cirrus was choked out again — at this point, routine — while much of the group stayed up drinking fermented bug nectar. Buumi tended to the warlock's bruised neck and healed the damage, his divine magic answering without hesitation. The proof was undeniable: if his actions worshipped Helm's tenets, prayers would be answered, even on a world where Helm went by another name.

## The Critereon

The next morning, the group headed into [[Ras Alkan]] proper, first to the harbour-master in the trade district. They confirmed that the missing ship was indeed registered to House Taurus — the Lady was telling the truth.

With that verified, they made for the Fisherman's Wharf, where House Taurus had rented a cheaper vessel to ferry them out to the reef. But their route took them past the Critereon — the famous arena of the city — and the roar of the cheering crowd proved irresistible.

They went to look inside.
