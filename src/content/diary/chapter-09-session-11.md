The last amniotic sacks were opened and the released prisoners sat recovering on the shore as evening settled over Lake [[selunia|Selunia]]. The Sovereignty's storm was entirely gone; the barony's calm had reasserted itself, and the water lay mirror-still in the growing dark.

[[topsy-and-turvy|Turvy]] began to hum — a haunting, melodic thing she had heard while sleeping inside the sack. The others who had been taken each nodded in recognition. Uira said she had heard it too, felt it moving through her. No one could name it.

Uira, [[topsy-and-turvy|Topsy and Turvy]], and the two very confused villagers made for the boats. Most of the party went with them. [[feanon-serinde|Fey]] walked back up through the tower wreckage with Uira, checking the cloning tanks on the way and conferring briefly with [[lady-faye|Lady Faye]] and [[lemerlais|Lemerlais]] before heading to Over the Moon.

[[tarkathee-daru|Tark]], halfway across the lake by boat, suddenly froze. He hadn't checked on the cave bear cub all day. He leapt from the boat and ran across the water toward his treehouse. The cub was asleep in the rafters. The space had not benefited from his absence. He fetched a shovel, cleaned up, then led the waking animal in a gliding run through the canopy to the lakeshore, where they both bathed. The bear went off on its own. Tark went to town.

---

At the tavern the group took stock of where things stood. The Sovereignty weregild went straight to work: [[cirrus-al-kayan|Cirrus]] and [[gundren-rockseeker|Gundren]] sat down with the Dwarven architects and began drafting fortification plans for the barony — the attack had exposed real gaps in the outer perimeter. [[snorbul|Snorbul]] watched with barely contained impatience. The group confirmed it: Blingdenstone next, one day's delay to finish [[thorna|Thorna]]'s hammer in Sundabar.

[[aliyah-faen-tlabbar|Patches]] sent [[pickles|Mr. Pickles]] to find Samid. He returned: the Bedine merchant was in the Dwarven basement, winning steadily at cards. She passed word to [[doc|Doc]] to arrange Samid's return to his people when he was ready. She also learned that two associates of Abalaba from Sundabar would be coming to the barony shortly.

---

[[lemerlais|Lemerlais]] was waiting in [[feanon-serinde|Fey]]'s rooms when he returned to the tower. Her warning was direct: cloning had long-term effects that had only become apparent through Manshoon's later history. The man had cloned himself repeatedly over the decades, making copies of copies, until cumulative degradation had driven him fully insane. Feanon's elven lifespan introduced additional unknowns that no one had properly studied. She recommended caution. Feanon thanked her and retired.

---

[[cirrus-al-kayan|Cirrus]] looked in on [[topsy-and-turvy|Topsy and Turvy]] and found them speaking quietly together. They both wanted to go home — to the [[underdark|Underdark]], to their own people. Cirrus promised to make it happen.

[[beryan|Beryan]] had found him earlier with the information he had requested on [[orcus|Orcus]]: the Palace of Everlost, in the frozen bone wastes of Oblivion's End. He filed it away, went to his inner sanctum, and checked the [[well-of-many-worlds|Well of Many Worlds]] before beginning his prayers.

He cast it against the wall.

An impossibly vast fortification filled his vision — white stone ascending thousands of feet and extending to infinity in both directions, every stone covered in glowing runes and symbols. Armored figures rushed along the ramparts and the sound of battle came from the far side: vast, constant, terrible. Cirrus was still trying to comprehend the scale when a figure dropped out of the air and landed before the portal.

Nine feet tall. Blue-skinned. Wings folding behind it. A Planetar looked at him through the opening.

Cirrus introduced himself and offered to step through and help.

The Planetar looked him over, noting the symbols of [[selune|Selune]]. It thanked the Warlock with apparent warmth but declined — mortals engaging at the Outer Gates was not recommended. Cirrus asked its name. *Luthiel*, it said. Then it reached out, grasped the Well of Many Worlds, and tugged. The portal closed.

Cirrus stood in the dark sanctum for a long time, thinking about what he had just seen.

---

[[tarkathee-daru|Tark]] knocked on [[gundren-rockseeker|Gundren]]'s door at the Manufactory, woke him, and walked in when the door opened. He had a commission: a locking mechanism for a [[bag-of-holding|Bag of Holding]]. Gundren added it to the list, right below the planned work on [[aliyah-faen-tlabbar|Patches]]' crossbow. Materials from Sundabar, tomorrow.

---

In the morning, [[aliyah-faen-tlabbar|Patches]], [[gundren-rockseeker|Gundren]], [[tarkathee-daru|Tark]], and [[thorna|Thorna]] teleported to [[sundabar|Sundabar]] with Gundren's brothers carrying an Adamantite chest between them. They went through the Everfire district and north to the Artificers Terrace.

Slagbelcher met them at his forge and handed Patches a box of ten bullets he had been working on, with apologies for not having more ready.

Inside, the forge was set up for the work. A large electroplating tank held the hammer head submerged in solution, two massive electrodes jutting upward, with a harness for the Glacium chit beside it. The handle, a sapphire, and the fittings waited on the bench. [[gundren-rockseeker|Gundren]] fitted the handle first, pressing the Aurum — enchanted gold — into place. [[barthen|Barthen]] fitted the sapphire with his brother Nundro's help. The hammer was assembled with the head still in the solution, and the Glacium was slotted into its harness, frost already falling from the enchanted Mithril.

Gundren pulled on rubber gloves and picked up the copper cables. He asked [[thorna|Thorna]] to raise a storm. She wasn't sure she could — she summoned her old hammer instead, and it appeared in her hand with a crack of sparks. She raised it aloft. The air in the forge charged. Over the next ten minutes something built overhead, and a small thunderstorm roiled in the vaulted space above them.

Thorna called the lightning down to the electrodes. Gundren connected the cables to the tank.

The flash was total. Everyone was blinded; Gundren was thrown hard against the far wall. When the light faded and the smoke cleared, the electroplating tank had cracked, but the Glacium harness was empty. Frost and ice were forming around the softly glowing owl-faced head of the hammer hanging in the solution.

[[stormvordur|Stormvordur]] was complete.

[[thorna|Thorna]] lifted it from the tank and tested the weight, the grin spreading across her face before she could stop it. [[gundren-rockseeker|Gundren]] embraced his brothers and shook Slagbelcher's hand with both of his own, then spent the next few minutes convincing the smith to pack up and come to the barony workshop. Slagbelcher accepted. At Tark's and Patches' request he also agreed to source Adamantite and Mithril for their personal projects and bring the materials when he came.

The group stopped at an alchemy shop on the way out — a Potion of Reduce for the stocks — and [[aliyah-faen-tlabbar|Patches]] helped herself to a Potion of Ogre Strength as well. They returned to the barony.

---

While the Sundabar group was away, [[feanon-serinde|Fey]] had spent the day in spell research with [[theodemir|Theodemir]] and the [[iron-tome|Iron Tome]], working on a Distortion spell. [[cirrus-al-kayan|Cirrus]] and [[snorbul|Snorbul]] had gone to the Dreaming chamber in the [[fane-of-selune|Fane]] to contact [[gruttag|Gruttag]]. They waited through the morning and into the afternoon. Gruttag took his mid-afternoon nap just long enough to make contact.

In the dreamscape, Snorbul sat with his uncle and ate. Gruttag's report was grim: Blingdenstone was tested every day. Ghosts in the ruins, ooze attacks increasing, demon raids becoming more frequent. People lost daily. The attrition would eventually finish what the Drow invasion had started. And some people were acting strangely — distant, quiet, in a way that seemed wrong to him, as if something were working on them.

---

The group reconvened in the evening and finalized their approach. Rather than trek from the barony directly into the [[underdark|Underdark]], they would return to [[sundabar|Sundabar]] and use the Dwarven Runegate to reach Mithril Hall — a closer starting point with the route better documented.

At dinner they found Magistrate Li eating with Elira in Over the Moon and told her the plan. Li acknowledged it and mentioned that [[silverymoon|Silverymoon]] had been informed of the Aboleth incursion; in response, a mercenary band called Bohd's Band had been contracted to reinforce the barony's defenses. She placed a crimson envelope sealed with black wax on the table — from the Netherese — and went back to her dinner.

That evening, [[feanon-serinde|Fey]] read it. [[lady-avelyere|Lady Avelyere]] of [[shade-enclave|Shade Enclave]] would be visiting in two weeks to tour the barony and discuss arcane matters.

---

In the morning the group teleported to [[sundabar|Sundabar]] and made their way to the Veterans' District and the fortress housing the Runegate. The Heralds' reputation opened the first gate. Dwarven Juggernauts checked their authorization papers at the entry to the long fortified corridor, and checked them again at the far end. Then, without ceremony, they were in Mithril Hall.

Without the Everfire at its heart the place felt entirely different — cooler, quieter, the undercity filling a vast V-shaped cavern with tier upon tier of stonework rising above them. An elevator took them several floors up to the Hall of Ancestors beside the Temple of [[moradin|Moradin]], where the clan elders produced an old account from a [[blingdenstone|Blingdenstone]] Burrow Warden refugee written over a century past — a first-hand description of the route to the Svirfneblin city. They descended back through the undercity and the lower mines, passed checkpoint after checkpoint, and stepped out past the last Dwarven fortification into the dark.

---

[[thorna|Thorna]] was nearly panicking in the lightless tunnels, almost completely blind. [[gundren-rockseeker|Gundren]] summoned his Tenser's Disk and she rode it in silence, the magical construct following twenty feet behind him throughout.

The first day was uneventful. They navigated by the Burrow Warden's landmarks, found a chamber to rest in, and summoned Feanon's Mansion. During the night [[tarkathee-daru|Tark]] picked up movement nearby and investigated: ten Goblins in black leather armor with wicked blades, accompanied by six Chimeras. He returned to report. [[aliyah-faen-tlabbar|Patches]] dressed in full Lolth priestess regalia — spider jewelry, Tentacle Rod — and walked alone into the cavern to confront them. The Goblins dispersed into the tunnels without incident.

The second day passed quietly until evening, when the ground cracked and Bulettes erupted — six of them, carapaces deep cobalt blue. The group fought them off and [[gundren-rockseeker|Gundren]] attempted to butcher one; the anatomy was entirely foreign and he gave up. [[aliyah-faen-tlabbar|Patches]] caught a Goblin slinking away into the dark and recognized it from the night before.

On the third day, [[thorna|Thorna]] glanced into a side passage and froze. A Kobold stared back at her, clutching a large purple leathery egg. They held the look for a moment — then the Kobold bolted. The sound of mining picks grew louder ahead. [[snorbul|Snorbul]] took the lead.

---

They emerged into a hub cavern and found a Deep Gnome Foreman. He was cautiously welcoming until [[aliyah-faen-tlabbar|Patches]] came through, at which point other gnomes called out "Drow!" — [[snorbul|Snorbul]] vouched for her and they held. The Foreman then questioned whether Snorbul was actually a Deep Gnome; Snorbul challenged him to a wrestling match, won without apparent effort, and more gnomes stepped from concealment in the walls. Snorbul mentioned [[gruttag|Gruttag]]'s name and they relaxed further.

The group was escorted into the Pickshine mines — past mining operations with clothes neatly folded at workstations and notably few visible guards. Both [[aliyah-faen-tlabbar|Patches]] and [[snorbul|Snorbul]] noted it and suspected a significant Earth Elemental presence handling security from inside the stone itself.

They entered a large common hall where dozens of Deep Gnomes were eating and resting. At a far table sat [[gruttag|Gruttag]] and three cousins. [[snorbul|Snorbul]] called out and crossed the hall in a rush; his uncle rose to meet him. They embraced. Gruttag looked the group over before answering his nephew's questions, gauging what kind of help these surface-worlders might provide. The mine leader entered the chamber as they were talking, and Gruttag rose to begin the introductions.

[[snorbul|Snorbul]] turned back to smile at his cousins — and found his cousin Yuri staring at him with blank, empty eyes. A chill went up his spine.
