## Aftermath

The mansion burned as the second floor collapsed. Behind the manor, [[Horatio von Bumidere]] moved to re-enter the building. [[Ernst Von Bumidere]] grabbed his brother and told him they were gone. Patches agreed — their mother, father, and brother could not be saved. Buumi sagged. Helm would not grant another stay of death that night.

He noticed the unconscious assassin at Patches' feet and rushed forward, violence in his heart. Patches held out her hands. She asked Buumi to give her a hand carrying the man instead. The anger faded, replaced by confusion and grief. The big man scooped up the stripped, hooded prisoner.

Around front, [[Fëanon Serinde]] floated down near [[Cirrus Al Kayan]], who had decided to nap in the half-disintegrated fountain. Fëanon and [[Doc]] examined the warlock and realised [[Corpsetaker]]'s parting gift: a feeblemind enchantment. Cirrus was essentially comatose.

Above the docks, [[Aurinax]] and [[Anaxtrasa]] harried the massive red dragon until it broke free and teleported away in short hops, the two adults giving chase. [[Tarkathee Daru]]'s blindness faded. Twenty city guard rushed down the estate drive, led by a hulking Dragonborn who introduced himself as Kliss. Tensions rose when Tark mentioned the Faerun Defence Force — a name no one recognised — until Fëanon produced his writ of nobility.

The group convened. [[Chani]] rushed to [[Gneiss]]'s body at [[Jack Mayer Johnson Mathews]]'s feet. [[Gundren Rockseeker]], caked in soot and blood, rounded the manor and called to Buumi: "Dinner sucked!"

Doc held his arms to the storm and called on Mab, Queen of Air and Darkness. A bolt of lightning struck his right hand. Trembling, he placed it on Cirrus's chest. The warlock jolted awake, healed in body and mind. His fur standing on end from the static, Doc sagged in exhaustion.

Cirrus leapt up and grabbed Fëanon. "Sundabar!" The wizard understood. He enacted Arcane Recall and the small lead box containing the artifact appeared in his hand, whisked from the vault hundreds of miles away. He peeked inside — the perfect cube of obsidian with a gold bar through its centre. Still there. He deposited it into his [[Bag of Holding]].

They told Buumi about [[Cercei]] — that the real girl was alive but possessed by something calling itself Corpsetaker. Cherry on top of the worst night of his life. He went silent.

## The Keep

The group headed to the [[Keep of Thunder Bluff]] with the city guard. Descending the hill from the [[Bluelake District]], they saw fourteen patches of fire across the city, the docks almost entirely incinerated, ships burning in the harbour. Wizards worked to contain the blazes with walls of ice and water.

At the keep, they found a triage tent run by dwarves. The old dwarf in charge confirmed fourteen fireball detonations, the docks lost, and the keep and estate targeted as primary objectives. The bombings in populated areas were the distraction. [[Gauntlgrym]] had sent reinforcements through their gate to [[Sundabar]], where there had been an attack too — outcome unknown.

Inside the keep, the audience chamber was devastated. Water dripped through a fifteen-foot hole punched through the roof. A massive, still-glowing iron golem lay slagged in the centre of the room, surrounded by dozens of corpses and unidentifiable gore. Near the throne, [[Mordenkainen]] stared at a large pile of rubble. His right arm was compound-fractured, his pate bloody, and [[Delly Griplim]]'s cat Alfred was clutched to his chest, yowling.

Stick spoke into their minds: Mordy was addled and concussed but kept waving away the clerics. Cirrus cast greater restoration. Buumi laid on hands. The wizard's eyes focused.

Mordenkainen told them what happened. He and Delly had been eating dinner when an iron golem smashed through the ceiling, followed by four Amnian wizards and a second golem of unfamiliar design — white marble threaded with silver, with a featureless head. Wherever that head looked, magic ceased, like a beholder's anti-magic cone. That was what got Delly. The gaze negated her defences. She fell beneath the rubble.

The group shifted stone until they uncovered the marble golem's head and shoulders. Mordenkainen's voice cracked as he handed Alfred to Fëanon. The cat needed to sleep. Fëanon willed the distressed creature into a slumber.

Revivify was too late — more than a minute had passed. JmJm could reincarnate her with the massive diamond, but Mordenkainen said she would need to be "whole" first. Reassembly. As dark as that sounded.

## Interrogation

Patches found a sergeant and the group was led to the keep dungeons — a perfect cube of stone, seamless and featureless save for a glass wall. An anti-magic cell. They strapped the assassin into the shackled chair.

Tark demanded they refrain from torture. Patches negotiated. Fëanon studied the assassin's intricate arcane tattoos — spell equations for gaseous form and misty step. The source of their powers.

The prisoner revealed that he was one of many Shadowthieves of [[Amn]], none knowing who else was on the job. The contract came from [[Shaahir]]. His cell was three operatives, with an escape route by boat upriver.

Cirrus asked to be left alone with the prisoner. He asked the man if he was innocent. The assassin chuckled and showed a tattoo dedicating himself to Bhaal, god of murder. Cirrus stabbed him to death with a small dagger, then captured the dying man's soul in a cage. He prestidigitated away the blood.

From the soul, Cirrus learned the escape route led to [[Thundertree]] in the Neverwinter Woods. The cell members' names: Geld Havarafam and Jeof Jeef.

[[Megno Slatesunder]] contacted Fëanon. The [[Sundabar]] vault had been attacked by eight Oni with bags of holding filled with undead. All eight were killed with heavy losses, but the last two had combined their bags of holding to tear a rift into the [[Astral Plane]]. The portal could not be closed and appeared to be growing.

Cirrus stopped in his tracks. The [[Crystal Tuning Fork]]. He had learned plane shift that very night. This was too much of a coincidence — [[Selune]] was pointing the way. He had to attune the fork while the portal existed.

## The Rift

Fëanon inscribed a teleportation circle and they arrived in Sundabar under martial law — the city deserted save for twenty-man patrols, many accompanied by stone golems. Cirrus sprinted through the streets, medallion held aloft, the group barely keeping up.

At the northern vault, a glowing crack in the doors pulled wind inward. Blue caustic light played across the entrance. Inside, a great hole in the universe showed the Astral Sea — purple nebulae and infinite stars. Cirrus dropped to his knees beneath it and held up the crystal fork.

Megno handed Fëanon ruby spectacles. Through them, the wizard saw cracks in the planar boundaries — the same as beneath [[Helm's Hold]]. Megno confirmed they couldn't close the portal. Cirrus snapped that even if they could, they shouldn't. The attuning would take twenty-four hours.

The group settled in among grim-faced dwarven veterans. The vault was wrecked — gouges in granite, jagged frost, destroyed Oni and ghoul corpses strewn about, the tunnel to the vault collapsed. Eight hours passed without incident. Everyone rested save Cirrus, prostrate before the rift. At dawn, Mordenkainen sent word: return to [[Neverwinter]] for a briefing.

## The War

They left Cirrus with Megno and teleported back. The keep was cleaned up — rubble gone, bodies removed, only bloodstains and damaged stone remaining. Acolytes prestidigitated away the stains.

A strange man sat on the steps of the throne. Fat, with a head shaped like a potato and small beady eyes, striking in his bad looks. Hugh, [[Lord Neverember]]'s spymaster.

They followed Hugh to the lord's rooms. Neverember lay in a massive bed, leg propped and bandaged, eyes wrapped in bloody cloth. He growled and cursed the group for not protecting his family, then caught himself — the anger was misplaced.

Hugh laid out the briefing over a map of [[Faerun]]:

Thay had invaded Thesk and Aglarond, conquering the former. [[Vaasa]] was gripped by a terrible blizzard with white dragons sighted. The Thayan fleet had sailed from port. The Amnian army had marched north and taken Berdusk with a green dragon's help. A coup in Amn had put [[Shaahir]] on top, endorsing a new religion — the Church of the Nightmother. The Balduran First Navy was outnumbered two-to-one against a pirate armada backed by three black dragons. [[Baldur's Gate]] had suffered smaller fireball attacks. [[Waterdeep]] had been hit hard — Amnian simulacra detonated an enchanted item inside the College of Magists, unleashing a massive cloudkill. [[The Moonwood]] was burning under a Drow assault. Tethyr, Narfell, and the Efreeti of Calimshan had joined with the Amnians and Thayans. War had been formally declared on the North.

The number of dragons troubled Hugh most. Tark wondered about [[Zhengyi]]'s connection. Fëanon questioned how the extraplanar threats factored into this mortal conflict. Hugh noted that every attack made strategic sense save one: the Drow assault on the Moonwood. Nothing of value was there.

Patches studied the map and sighed heavily. She thought the Drow attack was personal. She quoted Corpsetaker's booming voice from above the burning manor: "I will take everything you love. Nothing is safe." She believed her family had kidnapped her son, who had been residing in the Moonwood.
