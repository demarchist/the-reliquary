## The Bluelake District

[[Aliyah Faen Tlabbar]], [[Fëanon Serinde]], [[Horatio von Bumidere]], and [[Gwen]] found themselves in [[Neverwinter]]'s Bluelake District — the wealthy quarter, surrounded by servants and merchants preparing the city for the coming Council. Two guards noted their sudden appearance and questioned them. Fëanon explained the teleport mishap and dropped Buumi's family name. The guards apologised and backed off.

They tried the castle first, hoping to lodge a complaint with the Guild about the teleportation, but found it locked down for the Council. A guard told them to return the next day. The group turned toward Buumi's family estate — a large mansion with sizable gardens in the Bluelake District, bustling with carts and servants delivering preparations.

At the gate stood six guards, including a lean, vaguely Arabic-looking man in leather armour with two swords, and a large copper dragonborn in full plate. Buumi called out — "VAL!" — and embraced Val Tengu, the captain of the house guard. Val could not promise lodging in the main estate without Esimir's approval, but offered the guest house. The group declined, saying they would return when the family was available. Val promised to spread word that Buumi was alive and in the city.

The dragonborn bowed and mentioned that two hundred of her kind now served [[Lord Neverember]] as city guard. Fëanon recalled that decades earlier, a colony of the creatures had been deposited from another world in the southeast. They had since made their way across [[Faerun]] as expert mercenaries, known for honour and truthfulness.

As the group left, a tiny messenger-Cirrus with a box appeared in their carriage — a letter detailing that Cirrus was in the Rift district, tracking the warlock of [[Ceregorach]], and they should meet him at Della's General Goods.

## The Rift

[[Cirrus Al Kayan]] found himself alone in the Rift district, sensing the other warlock nearby. Guards directed him toward a guard house at the transition to the Protector's District, across from the [[Fallen Tower Inn]]. The guards were tense — a series of murders perpetrated by the warlock had the city on edge with the Council approaching.

Cirrus disguised himself as the creature he had seen in his vision — a pallid, stringy-haired halfling with massive pale eyes and teeth filed to points — and asked if they had seen anything like it. They had not but promised to circulate the description. At the guard house, Lieutenant Nanling told him the most recent victim was Boris Goodsman, husband of Della at the general store. Another group of adventurers had gone looking for the killer — the "Red Grin Killer" — and never returned.

## The Red Belt

[[Tarkathee Daru]] and [[Gundren Rockseeker]] found themselves in a decrepit alleyway surrounded by shanties. Being easily distracted, they headed for the food stalls. A gang of youths appeared, brandishing makeshift weapons and demanding a toll. Tark offered cash. Gundren pushed his hand away with a sneer.

A larger figure moved in behind — Billy the Butcher, wielding two long knives. The youths' clubs shattered on Gundren's armour. Billy scored a hit on Tark. Surprised, the monk dropped a globe of darkness while Gundren greased the alley, sending every youngster scrambling. In the dark, Tark broke Billy with a kick to the sternum that smashed the man halfway through the wooden wall. They walked out unscathed — save a small cut on Tark's forehead — and ordered kebabs and moonshine from a dwarf running a nearby stall.

They learned they were in the Red Belt, a refugee slum that had grown over the past year as people were displaced by the undead mists. One of the youths stumbled out, wiping grease from his eyes, and warned them they were "really in it now" — Billy was dead, his ribcage collapsed. Tark refused to simply leave. He carried the body deeper into the slum, looking for the Ghoulfangs.

They found a bar with thugs wearing ghoul-tooth necklaces. Tark presented the body and the necklace and apologised. The thugs howled and charged. Gundren's thundercannon obliterated two. Tark dodged and countered, snapping wrists and cracking ankles — non-lethally. A second wave poured from the bar, better armed, led by a half-ogre called Tusker. Tark fought with such grace that no one could land a blow. Gundren took hits but hacked down attacker after attacker. Tark finished Tusker with a strike to the temple. The last two swordsmen looked at one another, looked at the unharmed monk standing over eight bodies, and bolted.

The locals surged into the bar. In the cellar — cages, and prisoners being freed. Slaves the gang had been gathering. Beyond the cages, a makeshift tunnel led through the dark and eventually connected to Neverwinter's sewers. Tark and Gundren climbed a ladder to the Tower District, reported the smuggling tunnel to a guard, and were met by another tiny messenger-Cirrus directing them to Della's General Goods.

## Meeting at Della's

The group converged at the general store. Della confirmed that Boris had suffered night terrors for days before his death — vivid dreams of being burned alive, an old childhood trauma. All forty-three victims had been found with their teeth ripped out and their lips missing.

Cirrus theorised that Meenlocks — servants of Ceregorach — were terrorising victims before the warlock struck. Using the description of the killer, Fëanon scried with a strand of stringy hair found in the alley where Boris died. His vision sped through the streets to a plaza near the guard post and the gate to the Protector's District. He saw the [[Fallen Tower Inn]] and a small human urchin staring from an alleyway — the warlock in disguise.

With Buumi carrying Fëanon as he maintained the scry, the group hurried to the plaza.

## The Fallen Tower Inn

The urchin was still fixated on the inn. Cirrus went in alone to try diplomacy. Fëanon scried again and watched the creature sense something, then lock eyes on Cirrus crossing the plaza. It stood its ground, sneering.

The two warlocks faced each other in the alleyway. The creature did not give its name but knew Cirrus — who he was, what he had done, that he had sacrificed companions and betrayed the Tooth Fairy. It proclaimed everything Cirrus had done meaningless, that [[Ceregorach]] had a plan, was not dead, and was plotting even now. Then it vanished in a flash of arcane energy — far step. Cirrus's respect for his adversary increased.

The trail led to the inn itself. Inside — a bustling tavern, dozens of patrons, a stage with a performer. Patches spotted scratches in Thieves' Cant and realised the Fallen Tower was an Establishment — a headquarters of the Dead Rats, the local thieves' guild. Cirrus commandeered the stage, magically enhanced his voice, and announced that the killer was stalking someone here — someone terrorised by bad dreams. If they were having night terrors, they should meet him at the bar.

Minutes later, the bard from the stage — a man named Jake, with a truly stunning smile and perfect teeth — came forward. He had been having terrible nightmares. The group convinced him they could help and headed to his lodgings to plan.

Tark remained at the bar. A middle-aged man leaned over and introduced himself as [[Galen Marek]]. He knew more than he let on. After some probing, he leaned in and whispered that he was a spy, looking for help securing the city. Tark said he would think about it. As Galen slipped into the crowd, Tark grabbed his sleeve. The spy turned, surprised, appreciated the monk's skills, and promised they would talk soon.

Upstairs, the group set the trap. Half would wait with Jake, the other half in an adjacent room. The warlock and his Meenlocks would come for the bard — and the group would be waiting.
