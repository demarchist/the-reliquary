[[snorbul|Snorbul]] finished up repairing the bathroom in the townhouse after a few hours of work. Oddly, he had a pile of leftover rock pieces which he piled in a corner and placed some flowers (from Doc) on top.

Unbeknownst to Snorbul, he had repaired the bathroom to the scale of a Deep Gnome, and not the original dimensions.

Satisfied with a job well done, Snorbul felt like he had to make a good impression with his new friends and help Tark out, so he hit the town looking for a way to cheer up the Monk.

He wandered for about an hour, noting the occasional argument or scuffle in the marketplace, and ended up over at the [[gracksuluth|Shattered Spire]].

Entering the fighting pit area, Snorbul was entranced by the sights and smells, watching an Orc and Goblin team slay a group of Giant scorpions.

The crowd cheered them on and the Deep Gnome beelined toward the bookies for gambling.

Approaching a grey-bearded Duergar, Snorbul put his money down on the next fight — a battle with Bolekk Barrison Del Armgo, the same Drow noble the group was slated to fight the next day.

Not realizing that he had placed a bet, and was not due to actually fight the Elf, Snorbul leapt into the ring after taking his pants off (for the freedom of movement).

The crowd was shocked — the previous bout having been cleared away — at this half-naked Deep Gnome with a massive axe leaping to the sands of the arena.

The Duergar bookie rushed over, looked into the arena, yelled at Snorbul, explained the mix-up, and demanded he put his pants back on.

Snorbul thought he was signing up to fight the Drow and still wished to do that. The Duergar rushed away to ask Bolekk if he would fight the Gnome.

Across the arena, one of the large doors opened and three Drow started to walk out before they were stopped by the Duergar and informed of the situation.

The lead Drow, encased in ornate plate armor — sleek, with no visible gaps, a chain wrapped around his torso and a longsword on his hip — grinned at [[snorbul|Snorbul]] and nodded.

To the roar of the crowd, Bolekk advanced into the arena, placing his helm on his head. Snorbul steadied himself as the Elf whipped his weapons through a showy routine.

A gong sounded after each had been introduced and they rushed at one another, the Drow sprinting forward to engage, casting Faerie Fire on the Deep Gnome and lining him in harmless green flame.

Snorbul leapt a good twelve feet off the ground, coming down with a massive chop. The Drow barely deflected it and sparks flew from the heavy armor.

Responding in a flurry, Bolekk jabbed at [[snorbul|Snorbul]] with the sword, the cuts burning the small Barbarian with poison. Whipping his other hand out, the chain unwrapped from Bolekk's torso and darted out, a mace head at its end crackling with lightning and striking Snorbul in the side of the head.

Snorbul got control of his spasming muscles and fought back, though already smarting badly from the poisoned cuts of the longsword.

The Drow laughed in his helm and laid into the Barbarian again, the poisoned blade burning badly. The electrified chain-mace struck Snorbul again and he was knocked to the ground, everything going dark.

Some time later, consciousness returned to the Deep Gnome and he awoke in pain.

Looking around, he saw a Duergar cleric finishing up a healing spell. The cleric threw Snorbul his coin, including a little bonus from the Drow, who had been impressed by the Gnome's strength.

Bruised and bloodied, Snorbul took his coin, drank a massive amount of beer, and then headed back out into the town, still looking for a gift for Tark.

## The Sanctum

Meanwhile, in Bilrigger's Sanctum the group split with Tark and Patches rushing from the hidden home out into the Whorlstone Caverns in pursuit of the Dimension Door-ing Warlock, the only remaining minion of Milgre in the area.

Tark caught a whiff of the warlock and they began to track her, rushing as fast as they possibly could in the direction of the scents, stealth be damned.

Hitting a fork in the tunnels, Patches investigated and noted the tracks not on the floor, but on the walls and ceiling — a Spider-Climb to try and throw them off. Knowing what to look for, they pursued.

Enacting his blindfold, Tark noted the planar cracks they had been seeing throughout the [[underdark|Underdark]].

Completely eschewing stealth though, Tark was easily heard by the fleeing Warlock. Just as they entered a small chamber, they saw the female Derro — clad in black leather armor and with a blood-red hood covering her head — vanish in another Dimension Door spell.

Cursing their ill fortune, Patches and Tark reluctantly returned to Bilrigger's hideout and the two Myconid Sprouts Patches had left behind.

In the meantime, [[cirrus-al-kayan|Cirrus]] had rushed into the basement of the Sanctum and found the corpse of the Warlock of [[ceregorach|Ceregorach]].

The Warlock's head had been cleanly split in two, sliced up through the jaw, cutting the Green Molar in half. Cirrus quickly took in the rest of the chamber, barely noting the workshop and torture racks with three dead victims.

The blood pooling around the dead Warlock was congealed and old. Whatever killed him had happened hours ago.

He carried the corpse of Bilrigger up into the foyer and showed it to [[droki|Droki]], who was immediately cowed into accepting a ceremony to dedicate himself to [[selune|Selune]], for the promise of power from the Moon Goddess.

Cirrus began the hour-long ritual, [[droki|Droki]] somewhat reluctantly going along with it.

[[cirrus-al-kayan|Fey'Anon]] and [[gundren-rockseeker|Gundren]] began to search the abode.

The two Myconid Sprouts continued to watch in silence, the small two-foot creatures occasionally puffing out small clouds of spores at one another.

Much of the place had been destroyed and looted. The signs of a previous battle from before they arrived were obvious, the pools of blood congealed and cold.

The two of them determined that there were two secret rooms flanking the foyer, but both were clearly trapped and warded.

Fey approached the first and began to look over the warding glyph — one of Petrification. He shattered the magic but found the lock behind it to be intricate and decided to wait for Patches to return.

Moving through the area, Fey'Anon found a small triangular cut of a silken robe that he believed could be from a sleeve. He tagged it with the relevant information and sequestered it away before moving on to the corpse of Rilka, slain by Tark.

Looking over the headless corpse, he picked up a bag of loose coins and a potion of Expeditious Retreat and began to identify a magical breastplate on the corpse.

## The Vault

Back in the main room, Patches had returned and begun working on the locks behind the glyphed door. She had rarely come across such complicated machinery and it took her a while due to the sheer number of false locks and mechanisms, but she finally unlocked the final mechanism and the stone slab slid away, revealing a small vault of treasure.

Hearing the sliding stone wall and fearing that his Rogue friend would take all the good stuff, Fey quaffed the potion of Expeditious Retreat, threw the breastplate to [[gundren-rockseeker|Gundren]], and rushed back to the foyer in time to see Patches enter a chamber the size of a large walk-in closet.

Within they were greeted with a small treasure trove — four chests of coins trapped with glyphs, a platinum tea-set on velvet pillows covered in contact poison, and a glass display case containing a massive Red Dragon horn, the tip capped in gold with a red sling and red wax capping the open end of the horn.

Investigating each carefully, Fey noted a Planeshift on the glass of the display case.

Patches defeated the contact poison carefully with the mystical power of gloves.

Hurt and unwilling to risk himself for treasure, Fey backed up and began to summon unseen servants to trigger the glyphs, dispelling them as they recharged to avoid any potential backlash.

It took time, but they broke through the defending magic and looted the chests of coin easily, depositing them with the wand in Sundabar.

Fey smashed the glass panel protecting the Dragon horn and took the treasure for himself — the four-foot horn a deep burgundy, fading to black at the tips though covered in gold.

He and Patches moved across the room to the other chamber to begin again and noted a scuffle occurring between Tark and [[droki|Droki]].

They grabbed the corpse of Rilka to throw into the Glyph of Petrification, disarming it as the body fell and shattered into stone chunks.

## Tark and Droki

The monk, incensed by the betrayal from [[kenshi|Kenshi]] and the ever-looming threat of Orcus and his minions, had been questioning the poor Derro since Cirrus finished his ceremony.

The snap of bone distracted Patches and she saw Tark snap the Derro's arm. Cirrus healed it, but he snapped the other and Patches rushed forward to splint it and try to calm her monk friend.

Tark learned from [[droki|Droki]] that the Council of Savants was made up of many Warlocks with patrons ranging from Archfey to Demons and Devils. They worked together when it was convenient, but otherwise would stab one another in the back.

Tark asked where he could find more information on the minions of Orcus and Droki said the only place would be O'Alla, the Beholder mediator for the Savants — the one they had already questioned.

Frustrated by this dismal place, Tark stormed out of the Sanctum and into the nearby tunnels.

He began to head back the way the group came, back to the Savant meeting chambers, but stopped when he neared the fungal pools where the dancing Myconids had been.

He heard a cacophony of voices.

Getting closer, Tark slipped into the cavern and saw a large group of Demons — a mix of multi-headed Barlgura and Chasme demons — discussing their plans.

Listening, Tark determined that the demons were gathering in the caverns below, looking for a way up into the city.

He returned to the group to tell them what he had seen.

## The Second Vault

Though it took significant time and effort, Fey and Patches worked their way through unlocking and disarming the other treasure vault. Inside they found a cursed necklace of a massive golden tooth, a set of Rotheskin moccasins, another set of coin chests, and a massive portrait of [[ceregorach|Ceregorach]].

As Patches neared the portrait, it began to slowly move, reaching from the canvas to grab her.

She backed away quickly, and then further thinking on it, looted the room and avoided the painting altogether.

[[droki|Droki]] meanwhile was whimpering nearby, cradling his broken arms, despondent and rejected, no longer having any warlock powers after the ritual by Cirrus.

During the looting of the place, Cirrus had returned to the basement chamber and cut an eye from the decapitated head of Bilrigger. He plopped it in a vial of alcohol and began to think.

Depressed and feeling like a failure, Cirrus sat there for many minutes.

Eventually, he made his way back to the foyer, just as Tark entered and recounted the issue up ahead with the demons.

The two of them, the saddest of the party, began to shuffle out of the Sanctum and back toward the fungal cave.

Patches and [[gundren-rockseeker|Gundren]] joined them, but Fey'Anon stayed behind, fascinated by the moving painting of [[ceregorach|Ceregorach]] trying to stretch from the canvas.

Cirrus eventually went back to check on him, Patches unwilling to tell him why Fey remained.

Cirrus approached the small vault and stopped, eyes going wide as he saw the painting of his old master — a saggy-skinned, thin creature with drooping pale flesh and massive, milky eyes, its jaw far too large and wide and filled with thousands of jagged teeth.

Cirrus rushed forward and yanked Fey back to safety.

The two of them decided to remain and study the painting. The others let them and began to return to the city above.

## Back to the City

[[droki|Droki]] ended up fleeing into the tunnels, away from the group, though Patches convinced him to return for more healing and to discuss the ex-warlock's future. He agreed reluctantly.

Patches, Tark and [[gundren-rockseeker|Gundren]] made it back to the fungal cavern, expecting demons, but the group Tark had seen earlier was nowhere to be found and they continued on.

Though it took the better part of two hours, they ascended without incident back to the Savant chambers and beyond, out into the city proper and back to the Townhouse.

As they went, Patches tried to talk to Tark. She told him that she knew what it was like to lose a master and a family member.

Her brother Marzell had been her teacher and her only friend, killed by their mother. She empathized with Tark but warned him not to lose himself to vengeance or darkness.

Feeling awkward sharing so much information, Patches rushed ahead of her friends for a while to let her embarrassment fade.

Tark got her attention and pulled out the Artifact, handing it to Patches and saying he could not be trusted. Patches asked why she would be any better but agreed, taking it until Tark was ready to carry it again.

To try and distract from the handoff, [[gundren-rockseeker|Gundren]] ignited his cannon and waved it about. Nearby civilians watching quickly rushed away.

Exhausted, they all headed in to rest. Patches checked the Drow embassy and noted guards from House Baenre, Fey-Branche, Barrison Del Armgo and Xorlarrin.

[[gundren-rockseeker|Gundren]] immediately headed to bed.

## The Bear Cub

Tark beelined toward his room but stopped as [[snorbul|Snorbul]] sat up on the couch, addressing the beleaguered monk.

Snorbul took out his gift for Tark.

A small Cave Bear cub on a rope was walked from around the back of the couch. About the same size as Tark and incredibly fuzzy, with massive dark eyes and strange floppy skin between its limbs and a big bushy tail, the creature looked more like a monstrous flying squirrel than a bear.

The creature rolled on the ground and Patches rubbed its belly. It let out little chirps of happiness.

Tark stepped around the creature and walked to his room. The cub followed, but Tark closed the door before it could enter.

A few moments later, the door opened enough to let it inside and the cub joined Tark.

Patches retired for the night, showing Mr. Pickles her new ornate platinum tea set and having him brew up a calming cup of tea.

## Sundabar

Cirrus and Fey were alone in the creepy and destroyed Sanctum.

Wishing to rest, but not there, Fey extended his senses and felt the lack of Wild Magic in the area. The Sanctum was shielded and would work as a launching point.

Unwilling to leave the powerful painting, Fey teleported the item to the vaults in Sundabar and then followed with a Teleportation Circle, bringing Cirrus with him back to the surface.

Leaving the [[underdark|Underdark]], a great mental weight immediately lifted from the two of them.

The difference was so profound that neither of them could pretend it was natural.

Greeted in the guild teleportation room by a smiling face and a glass of sparkling wine, it was good to be back amongst friendly races.

The two of them headed through the bustling Dwarven undercity, the difference between Sundabar and [[gracksuluth|Gracksuluth]] striking even more sharply.

There was no choking smog here. The laughter of happy people and the sounds of performers and music reached their ears. There was no sulfurous stench. It was a place of happiness and homeliness, not repression and industry.

They passed the massive lava pool of the Everfire, feeling the intense heat not as oppressive, but as a life-giving warmth.

Arriving at the bank, they were seen quickly, though Goldhold was not available to see them.

They informed the clerk of their issue with the magical painting. The Dwarf was a little taken aback that they would bring it here.

They mentioned that they probably wanted to find a way to destroy it, to which the clerk asked why they didn't just throw it in the Everfire.

They asked the clerk to look into that and then took their leave, heading to the Uppercity to see the stars.

Taking the short elevator ride, they arrived in the chill, early springtime air of the Uppercity, a crescent moon greeting them amongst the glittering stars.

Cirrus walked a little ways, found a nice bench, and parked himself there staring up at the moon, his heart lifting.

Fey'Anon bid his friend goodnight and walked the newly constructed streets, the city in much better condition than the last time they were here — the industrious Dwarves working quickly to rebuild.

Asking directions, the Wizard made his way over to an Elven inn, ordered good Elven food, and rented the nicest room they had available, a warm bath waiting for him upstairs.
