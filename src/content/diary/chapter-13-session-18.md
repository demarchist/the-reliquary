## The Saurian Tunnels

The artificial caverns of the [[Obsidian Order]] gave way to older architecture — a massive underground roadway built by the [[The Saurians|Saurians]] long before any elf had set foot in this world. [[Ahmahd]] led them through the facility at a brisk pace. The elves had been raiding it for supplies for some time, and the only serious threat was the local population of Nasnas — simian brutes that infested the tunnels in considerable numbers.

The group assessed their collective capacity for stealth — a mixed party of varying sizes, clanking with arms and armour — and concluded there was little point in pretending. Those who could move silently did so. The rest walked openly through the Saurian corridors beneath the flickering Sourcelight.

Ahmahd was appalled. She took up the rear, invisible, positioning herself to flee when these foreigners were inevitably overrun.

[[Cirrus Al-Kayan|Cirrus]] could feel the pull of [[Fawden]] — a low, constant ache that gave them their heading. The wild magic that plagued the surface was almost absent this deep underground, which was one small mercy.

They descended a makeshift ladder of lashed bones and hair, and pushed deeper.

## The Horde

[[Aliyah Faen Tlabbar|Patches]] heard the commotion first — the guttural shouting and crash of movement from a gatehouse ahead. She crept to the entrance and peered inside.

Ape-like creatures filled the chamber, pale-skinned and heavy-jawed, with tiny beady eyes and oversized canines. They were snatching up crude weapons and working themselves into a frenzy. Patches counted over a dozen, with the sounds of many more echoing from adjacent passages.

She fell back and warned the group just as the Nasnas burst from the ruin and charged.

The group was ready. [[Feanon Serinde|Feanon]] sealed the tunnel with a wall of ice, funnelling the horde into a killing zone, and laid a Sickening Radiance across the bottleneck. The Nasnas hammered at the barrier, dying in waves as the radiant light ate through their flesh.

A flanking force found a crack in the wall and poured through. Fireballs turned them to ash.

[[Jack Mayer-Johnson Mathews|JmJm]] rode [[Steden]] through the [[Ethereal Plane]] to scout beyond the ice wall and found another horde rushing toward the sound of battle. He burst from the Ethereal and unleashed twin lightning bolts from [[Gwen]], cutting down all but the toughest. Those survivors he dispatched with blade and lance.

Still they came — wave after wave pressing against the bottleneck, climbing over the charred remains of the dead. After fifty or more had fallen, the Nasnas finally broke. The survivors scattered into the dark tunnels, howling.

## The Bridge and the Shaft

The group advanced cautiously through the carnage. A few hundred feet further, they found a rope bridge spanning a deep chasm — freshly cut.

Patches sighted Nasnas on the far side and dropped them with precise crossbow shots while JmJm used Steden, rope, and Gwen to retrieve the severed bridge. As the group began to cross, Patches sprinted ahead like a shadow given purpose and killed the Nasnas waiting on the far side to cut the ropes mid-crossing. The group made it across safely.

Ahmahd, still alive and now somewhat impressed, admitted she might have been wrong about these outlanders.

They pushed on through the Saurian facility, passing makeshift shrines — crude assemblies of bone, hide, and scavenged metal. One had a Machina device at its core, which Cirrus pocketed after [[Gundren Rockseeker|Gundren]] identified it as a motor of some kind.

The tunnel ended at a large chamber with an ancient Saurian door — a massive portal barricaded shut with the accumulated refuse of the Nasnas clans. The area was thick with shrines and totems. Whatever lay beyond this door was sacred to them.

Feanon found a Saurian console. The door led to an elevator shaft descending hundreds of feet. The mechanism required several components — one of which was the motor they had just recovered. But the others were missing, scattered among the Nasnas warrens.

JmJm offered a faster solution. He would ferry everyone through the Ethereal Plane, bypassing the door entirely and dropping them at the bottom of the shaft.

He began immediately. As he worked, the Nasnas clans launched another assault, pouring from side tunnels toward the chamber. Feanon dropped a Sickening Radiance across the main approach, but the creatures were flanking through passages the group had not mapped.

JmJm shuttled the last of them through just as the first wave rounded the corner. They descended the elevator shaft through the spirit world — dark echoes of ancient shades and swirling energies drifted past, but nothing attacked — and stepped back into the Material Plane, safe and far below the reach of the horde.

## The Mines

The tunnels here were different. Ancient and silent, thick with dust, and clearly less travelled. They had entered the mines.

Bones littered the floor — many of them human. Slaves of the Saurian Empire, worked to death and left where they fell a thousand years ago.

Ahmahd admitted she had never been this deep. She knew the general direction, but the detailed maps ended above. They were heading for the [[Disphotic Forest]].

They pressed downward through narrow passages, moving quietly now — veins of corruption and signs of Alghouls warned them that the Nasnas were not the only predators in the depths. Wild magic was almost nonexistent this far underground, and the silence was oppressive.

Hours passed. Defunct mine carts rusted on ancient tracks. Stonework shored up the ceilings. Then they found a vein of Sourcestone — raw ore, glowing with inner light, wisps of luminous gas rising from its surface.

Someone touched it.

The screams of [[Fawden]] ripped through their minds. The very life-force of the world shuddered beneath their fingers — discordant, corrupted, and failing. The ground shook. Stones clattered from the ceiling.

They pulled away, shaken, and pressed on.

The tunnels wore on. Unlike the lightless stretches of the Underdark, the depths here were softly illuminated by flickering energies and glowing Sourcestone deposits. Patches detected something stalking them, and the group doubled back, found a concealed alcove, and waited in silence beneath concealing magic until whatever followed them moved on.

## The Disphotic Forest

More hours of descent. The mines gave way to natural tunnels — wet with condensation, growing steadily hotter. The passage narrowed to a fissure barely two feet wide, dropping at a steep angle. Gleaming Sourcestone pinpricks dotted the walls like tiny stars. Water poured through in a torrent, and a strong floral scent filled the air.

The ground shuddered again. Cirrus winced — he could hear Fawden's cries clearly now. They were close.

Feanon scouted the crack in gaseous form and returned to report a way through. He transformed the group, and they flowed through the fissure as easily as the water.

The passage opened without warning. They found themselves expelled from a waterfall, hundreds of feet above the floor of a cavern that defied comprehension.

The [[Disphotic Forest]] stretched before them in every direction, lost to fog and diffused blue-white light. Great pillars of stone rose from the mists to the ceiling far above, their surfaces threaded with glowing veins of Sourcestone and festooned with living ecosystems. Flora and fauna flourished in impossible abundance — schools of floating jellyfish-like creatures drifted between the columns, coral and anemones carpeted the stone, and bioluminescent mosses painted every surface in soft light.

The waterfall disgorged into a small lake atop a plateau on one of the great pillars. The group reconstituted on its banks, the mist of the falls settling over them.

They had been travelling for over twelve hours. There was no wild magic here at all. Feanon summoned his Magnificent Mansion, and the exhausted group filed inside.

Those who preferred the open air turned to survey the alien landscape — the subterranean cliffs and mountains contained within the crust of the world, all teeming with life — and tried to comprehend the scale of what lay before them.
