## The Cost of the Ritual

The previous day, [[Cirrus Al-Kayan|Cirrus]] had addressed the crowds, healed the sick, and visited his [[Fane of Selune|Fane]], where he met with his top acolytes — [[Temura]], [[Topsy and Turvy|Topsy, and Turvy]]. He proclaimed a week-long Moon Festival to follow the High Sun celebrations, paid for out of his own coffers. For her devotion, he True Polymorphed Turvy into a being of pure silver and chrome, radiating soft light. The transformed Svirfneblin left to praise [[Selune]] in the Fane proper, luminous and ecstatic.

The next morning, Cirrus reached for a glass of water and his fingers met the glass without feeling. He looked at his hands. The skin was ashen and flaking.

He communed with Selune. The ritual that had destroyed [[Fawden]] and brought [[Selune]] to Al Askar had wounded him. She had shielded him as best she could, but the toll was severe. If he wished to be healed, he must come to her — via the Towers of Twilight.

Cirrus hid his affliction beneath his illusions and went to see [[Doc|Doctor Breakfast]], who cast Heal without question. Then he headed to [[The Establishment]] to announce the celebrations.

## The Witch King's Curse

The group convened at The Establishment over breakfast. The two Vaasan monks — Minnow and Goro — relayed their warning about [[Zhengyi]], and [[Tarkathee Daru|Tark]] told the group he intended to leave for the Bloodstone Lands within seven days. He *must* deal with his family's killer. [[Horatio Von Bumidere|Buumi]] swore to help. [[Aliyah Faen Tlabbar|Patches]] agreed — if Tark would help her with her mother in return.

[[Feanon Serinde|Feanon]] looked terrible. [[Jack Mayer-Johnson Mathews|JmJm]] explained the cursed tome, and the monks confirmed the stories — Zhengyi had consumed spellcasters this way for centuries.

The group debated how to remove the curse. They needed a target to shunt it to. The more moral members protested — no one in the Barony deserved such a fate, and the prison held only a single petty thief. [[Gundren Rockseeker|Gundren]] suggested finding a target elsewhere. Cirrus suggested *making* one.

Feanon and Cirrus retired to a private room. There, Cirrus True Polymorphed a chair into an old man — an approximation of what Feanon imagined Zhengyi looked like. The elderly figure blinked into existence, a completely blank slate, dazed and bewildered.

Feanon slapped him in the face — hurting his own hand in the process — and then Wished for the curse to be transferred. With the most powerful Dispel Magic he had ever channelled, the curse leapt from Feanon to the nearest unprotected soul.

Cirrus opened a Demiplane. They shoved the confused, exhausted man-who-had-been-a-chair through the portal and sealed it behind him. Alone.

Feanon's exhaustion lifted immediately. He sighed in relief.

They returned to the bar below. Feanon felt a twinge of something he did not care to examine too closely.

## Vaketh

Tark walked the forest paths to the top of Red Hill, needing answers. He meditated, reaching for his connection to [[Bahamut]]. The link was strong here in [[Faerûn]], and through it he sensed the Lich's power stretching out like a dark web, harming people even here.

Then he felt an unexpected presence. [[Vaketh]], the god of Power from Al Askar, was with him — hitchhiking on the monk's soul, as per their agreement. The god offered power. Tark asked the price. Vaketh wanted only to come along for the ride, having spent an eternity imprisoned by [[Vecna]].

With the nascent god of Power and the focus of Bahamut, Tark began to reshape his prosthetic. The white wood of his staff moulded and contorted, threads of reddish metal winding through the masterwork as a wooden hand grew to fit his wounded arm — elegant, powerful, and infused with divine energy.

He sat on the hilltop and watched two youths run a footrace up the slope, leaping from the hundred-foot cliffs into the summer water below, their laughter echoing up the cliffsides.

## The Anauroch

Gundren, meanwhile, had discovered that all the rare materials for [[Aliyah Faen Tlabbar|Patches]]'s crossbow had arrived — save one: Bloodrinker Wyvern bone. He had a solid lead on a source: the High Plateau of the northern [[Anauroch]] desert. A two-day journey, and he could craft a masterpiece.

The group agreed. Cirrus's Moon celebration could not begin until High Sun ended in two days, and JmJm and Feanon both perked up at the prospect of venturing into old Netherese territory.

Doc gathered the group at his grove and cast Wind Walk, speeding them thousands of miles across the landscape — past dunes and the ruins of [[Castle Ravenloft|Ravenloft]] — to the high plateau of the Anauroch. Gundren deployed the [[Delzoun Instant Fortress]] and they made camp within its adamantine walls.

Feanon and JmJm went for an evening walk across the desolation. JmJm bent down and picked up a beautifully ornate ring worth thousands of gold that he *Wished* into existence. They returned to the tower uneventfully.

Winter wolves howled in the darkness, but none approached the adamantine walls. The next morning, they set out across the windswept plateau, heading north toward the glaciers and the Wyvern they had spotted in the distance.
