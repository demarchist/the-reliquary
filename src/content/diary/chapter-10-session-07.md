[[cirrus-al-kayan|Cirrus]] popped back into the material world, reappearing in his sanctum room from his extraplanar jaunt. He found [[feanon-serinde|Feanon]] there, surrounded by papers, equations, and apparatus for measuring the planar walls. The wizard turned at the displaced air and nodded.

[[cirrus-al-kayan|Cirrus]] was excited — he wished to tell everyone what he had discovered. They headed to Over the Moon, finding [[tarkathee-daru|Tark]] out on Lake [[selunia|Selunia]] playing with local children, catapulting them over the water by capsizing boats for fun. [[gundren-rockseeker|Gundren]] was checking on the production of a Hill Giant Belt before heading to the town centre to meet with the alchemists.

## Tales of Svartalfheim

The group convened in [[aliyah-faen-tlabbar|Patches']] establishment at their usual table, and [[cirrus-al-kayan|Cirrus]] began to recount his adventure in intricate detail. He described the plane of Ysgard and [[svartalfheim|Svartalfheim]] — the long-dead good [[drow-faction|Drow]] who lived there, their city, and his belief that it was the residence of [[eilistraee|Eilistraee]]. He showed them the gold planar fork and mentioned two human wizards from Sigil he had met in the city.

[[aliyah-faen-tlabbar|Patches]] expressed scepticism. She did not know how to feel about the plane of her goddess — it made everything suddenly very real. [[cirrus-al-kayan|Cirrus]] told the story over and over between bites of lunch.

They decided to take [[vizerion-devir|Vizerion]] up on his offer and assist with the ritual spell — for now. Everyone agreed the [[drow-faction|Drow]] wizard had ulterior motives. [[feanon-serinde|Feanon]], after consulting the [[iron-tome|Iron Tome]], confirmed that the ritual was complex and hiding magical undercurrents — specifically a necromantic drain whose target he could not yet pinpoint. He suspected the group themselves might be the intended victims.

Before departing, [[feanon-serinde|Feanon]] checked on [[theodemir|Theodemir]], finding him gaining control of the new body, eating soup with shaking hands. He also sent copies of [[vizerion-devir|Vizerion's]] spell notes to [[lemerlais|Lemerlais]] via [[lady-faye|Lady Faye]], and to [[megno-slatesunder|Megno]]. Between himself, [[theodemir|Theodemir]], [[lemerlais|Lemerlais]], and [[megno-slatesunder|Megno]], he was confident they could fully decrypt the spell.

## Into the Labyrinth

The group convened atop [[feanons-tower|Feanon's tower]]. Using a small stone given by [[vizerion-devir|Vizerion]], [[feanon-serinde|Feanon]] teleported them to the depths of the [[underdark|Underdark]]. They found themselves looking into a large cavern filled with mist. At its heart sat an island surrounded by chasms, accessible by a narrow bridge of natural stone. [[vizerion-devir|Vizerion's]] tower of dark stone rose from the island's centre, dimly lit by [[faerzress|Faerzress]] crystals — though strangely, there was no wild magic.

The front doors opened and a figure emerged — not [[vizerion-devir|Vizerion]], but a younger [[drow-faction|Drow]] who introduced himself as [[grein-osstyl|Grein Osstyl]], from a minor house of no import, as [[aliyah-faen-tlabbar|Patches]] recognised. The apprentice explained that his master was working on the spell and had sent him to ferry the group to [[the-labyrinth|the Labyrinth]]. He produced a spell-gem of teleportation and a small stone from the destination. They gathered around. [[grein-osstyl|Grein]] smashed the gemstone with a hammer, and they were transported hundreds of miles across the [[underdark|Underdark]].

They found themselves in a tunnel of natural stone and absolute darkness. [[horatio-von-bumidere|Buumi]] swore quietly and lowered the faceplate on his helmet for some darkvision. [[grein-osstyl|Grein]] explained that the Gallery of Angels lay deep within [[the-labyrinth|the Labyrinth]], then handed over a pendant that would grow warmer as they neared their goal. He stepped away and began inscribing a teleportation circle to return to his master.

The group headed into the ancient, convoluted tunnels.

## Yeenoghu

They hiked for hours through alternating terrain — natural tunnels, caverns, ancient mortal-made architecture — making good time with [[aliyah-faen-tlabbar|Patches]] leading. Then she stopped, hand raised. The echoing barks, yips, and chittering of Gnolls.

They moved toward the sound, intent on finding a guide or, failing that, some Gnolls to crush. The ruckus proved to be a battle, and as they crept into cover, they witnessed a titanic struggle.

A massive Goristro Demon — a towering minotaur-like beast — was locked in combat with the largest Gnoll any of them had ever seen. The creature's jaws distended to reveal an impossibly wide maw lined with circles of sharklike teeth. It bit into the Goristro as its multi-headed flail crashed in, scoring and tearing flesh. Around them, hulking skull-adorned minotaurs battled massive Gnolls and a horde of hyenas.

The group recognised not one but two objectives within reach: the heart of a Goristro Demon and the blood of a Demon Lord — [[yeenoghu|Yeenoghu]], Lord of the Hunt.

The opportunity was too good to pass up.

[[aliyah-faen-tlabbar|Patches]] went in first, slipping into the [[ethereal-plane|Ethereal Plane]] with [[tarkathee-daru|Tark's]] Pass Without Trace. As she approached, the Demon Lord's eyes darted toward her and she leapt behind a stalagmite on instinct. She darted out, slipped from the [[ethereal-plane|Ethereal Plane]], and plunged her daggers home. The flesh and hide of [[yeenoghu|Yeenoghu]] was like iron — her strikes, though true, dealt less damage than hoped. But she surged with adrenaline, tried again, and managed to fill a vial with thick, molasses-like blood.

She rushed back to the group as her friends waded into the fray. [[gundren-rockseeker|Gundren]] began blasting, [[feanon-serinde|Feanon]] wove magical defences, and [[cirrus-al-kayan|Cirrus]] unleashed Eldritch energy. As [[yeenoghu|Yeenoghu]] and the Goristro exchanged vicious blows, [[feanon-serinde|Feanon]] decided to simplify the equation — a Hypnotic Pattern washed over the Goristro and many of its minions, stunning them. [[yeenoghu|Yeenoghu]] was unaffected and roared, charging the wizard — though not before [[tarkathee-daru|Tark]] leapt onto its back, smashing away.

[[feanon-serinde|Feanon]], seeing the Demon Lord's cackling hyena-laugh up close, could not resist the opportunity and hit the creature with a Hideous Laughter dweomer. The Lord's hyena nature could not fight it. The group savaged the beast while it convulsed, hurting it badly before it broke free. In a rage, [[yeenoghu|Yeenoghu]] smashed [[tarkathee-daru|Tark]] with his demonic flail — paralysing the monk — and swatted [[aliyah-faen-tlabbar|Patches]] away.

The minion creatures were dropping in the background, leaving [[yeenoghu|Yeenoghu]] as the greatest threat — and the Goristro stumbling, still stunned, behind it.
