## The Remembrancer

The group packed their things at *Her Gift* and prepared to descend toward [[Yggward]]. [[Feanon Serinde|Feanon]] noticed [[Tarkathee Daru|Tark]] reading a book called *Tres Liches* and peered over the halfling's shoulder. Three names stared back: [[Kangaxx]], [[Zhengyi]], and [[Vecna]].

Then Feanon went pale. A prickling sensation crept across his mind — the unmistakable psychic pressure of an Illithid. Not distant. Not passing. Downstairs, in this very inn.

He decided to confront it. The wizard led the group down to the common area while they hung back, ready if he needed them.

The Mind Flayer sat at the bar in flowing purple robes, seemingly absorbed in conversation with the barkeep — a gorgeous, androgynous Drow of statuesque beauty. As Feanon approached, a telepathic greeting brushed against his thoughts. Amicable. Almost warm.

Disarmed by the thing's apparent friendliness, Feanon accepted the invitation to drink. The barkeep served a concoction laced with a coalesced memory — a sunset, warm and melancholy, that bloomed behind the eyes as you swallowed. The Illithid responded to the emotions the drink generated, drinking in the psychic resonance like wine.

The rest of the group drifted over — all save [[Aliyah Faen Tlabbar|Patches]], who was doing alchemy upstairs — and ordered their own memory-laced drinks. The Illithid savoured the cascading emotional energy of six adventurers experiencing extraplanar cocktails for the first time.

Feanon attempted Detect Thoughts. The spell was immediately counterspelled by the city's unseen wardens — aggressive magic against a citizen of [[Dokkar]] was not tolerated. The wizard switched to intuition and conversation instead.

The creature's name, it said, was *The Euphoria One Feels When Besting an Inferior*.

The group shortened it to Euphoria.

The Mind Flayer was a Remembrancer — a collector of memory and emotion, gathering data for the colony's Long Sleep between universal cycles. The Illithid believed that [[Atropos]] and [[Orcus]] could not be stopped. Their kind intended to flee to the farthest reaches of the cosmological background and hibernate until a new universe formed. For that hibernation, they needed as much data as possible.

The group found this motivation strangely unthreatening. They enjoyed the strange creature's company. Before they left, Euphoria made one last request — to travel with them a short while, collecting a better sense of the interesting group.

Tark remembered the Githzerai monk who had been stalking this very Illithid. Wary of tricks, they extracted a binding promise from Euphoria to abide by the laws of Dokkar even beyond the city's borders. The Mind Flayer agreed.

## The Caravan

The group descended to the second tier to find Krilin, the Drow ranger [[Gundren Rockseeker|Gundren]] had spoken to at the Hall of Hunters. They found him in the lodge, regaling a group of drinking gnomes with a tale of daring.

The ranger stopped mid-sentence when he saw Gundren arrive with a small army of absurdly well-equipped adventurers. A huge grin erupted across his face.

Krilin needed an escort for a treasure caravan heading to [[The Crossroads]] — the junction just before [[Nidavellir]] and [[Yggward]]. The group accepted. True to his word, Krilin promised a single item drawn at random from the haul as payment.

He led them across the city to a menagerie of celestial creatures in stables at the lowest tier. There he introduced Youree — a Celestial Bison, easily ten feet tall at the shoulder, with sweeping white horns, pearlescent fur, and glowing white eyes. Krilin ruffled her ears. *Who's my best girl?*

*Oh, I am,* Youree replied.

[[Jack Mayer-Johnson Mathews|JmJm]] started. *You can talk?*

Youree laughed and began to walk from her stable, massive treasure chests jingling on her flanks, heading toward the city exit. She called back for JmJm to catch up.

## The Descent

The sloping tunnels corkscrewed down from [[Svartalfheim]] toward [[Nidavellir]], and the journey was unlike anything in the [[Underdark]]. Time and space bent and shifted in the tunnels — Patches noted that her survival instincts were thrown off by the unnatural nature of the plane.

As they descended, the chaos of the plane increased. Great cracks and crevasses split the stone, creating ever-wider rents in the living rock. Floating islands of crystal drifted in defiance of gravity. The flora and fauna resembled the Underdark but operated on emotion and resonance rather than any recognisable law of nature.

Youree and JmJm hit it off wonderfully, their laughter echoing through crystal tunnels. The celestial bison plodded along making small talk, treasure chests jingling, untroubled by the increasingly shattered landscape. When the rents grew too severe for walking, the group took to their various means of flight while Youree picked her way along paths that should not have supported her weight.

## The Cathedral of Twilight

They reached a vast floating island and found the pathway split at a crossroad. One path looped down the cliff face into the rock below. The other led across a slowly corkscrewing arch of stone to another island hanging in the void.

Built into that floating stone, suspended above a nebula-filled sky, stood a grand cathedral of onyx. Its great spire descended and twisted — warped by a black hole far beneath it, floating in the [[Astral Plane|Astral Sea]]. Through gaps in the shattered rock, the Sea glimmered in every direction.

This was [[The Crossroads]] — where the [[Shadowfell]], the Astral Sea, [[Svartalfheim]], and [[Nidavellir]] met.

Krilin shimmered into visibility as his Greater Invisibility ended. He clapped his hands and thanked the group for the escort. True to his word, he opened one of the great treasure chests and drew an item at random.

He pulled out a glorious golden mace with gleaming gemstones set into the head and haft.

The ranger groaned in dismay as he realised what he had promised. Gundren reached out and took it. Krilin resisted — for a moment — before relinquishing the weapon with a sigh and a wry smile. He chalked it up to a lucky roll of fate's dice.

It was a newly forged Weapon of Lordly Might.

Gundren peered into the gleaming gems and saw his own reflection wearing the bejewelled crown. He thought the weapon really brought the outfit together.

JmJm and Youree shared a tearful goodbye. JmJm promised to visit, or bring her to the Material Plane for a holiday. Then Krilin and Youree took the path toward the chaotic floating rocks of Nidavellir, leaving the more solid rock of Svartalfheim above.

The group turned to the twisting bridge that led to the Cathedral of Twilight — the entrance to [[Yggward]], necropolis of the Dark Elves.

They crossed the arch, gravity moving with them as the bridge corkscrewed a full revolution. Patches went invisible, nerves taut with the sensation of being watched. The group let out gasps of surprise, thinking she had simply become *that* stealthy.

The great black shimmering doors swung silently inward. Inside, pairs of robed elves moved through the cathedral — one of each pair wearing a serene mask of silver, the other a solemn mask of onyx. Clerics of the Twinborn Goddesses — [[Selune]] and [[Shar]].

The cathedral interior was impossibly vast, far too large for the structure seen from outside. At its centre, a great hole descended to Yggward and the [[Shadowfell]]. Twelve mirrors of perfect darkness floated by the precipice, some attended by an elf and two priests — the elf meditating, the clerics waiting in silence.

A pair of clerics approached the group, and [[Cirrus Al-Kayan|Cirrus]] peeled away without explanation. They caught a snippet of the clerics addressing the warlock as a *Child of Twilight*. He walked some distance away, clearly desiring privacy.

## The Mirror

Another pair of clerics appeared beside the group, seemingly from nowhere. Patches bowed and greeted them, and they bowed in return, welcoming her by name. One served [[Selune]], the other [[Shar]].

A flash of light marked the arrival of a Celestial across the chamber — a radiant figure who began to speak with Cirrus, though her shadow was notably dark and intense.

Patches stated her purpose: she sought the blessing of her ancestors to found a new house, free of [[Lolth]]'s corruption. Both clerics turned and gestured toward a mirror. They told her to reach out and touch its surface — to visit a dreaming ancestor and ask for this blessing herself.

Patches extended her hand, then hesitated, looking back at her friends.

The clerics said her companions were welcome to touch the mirror and bear witness.

Patches reached out. Her fingertips met the darkness.

Her friends followed.

The vision resolved into a sweeping moonlit vista — a grass-covered bluff rising over the steppe, dotted with trees, capped by a henge of elven monoliths. Snow gleamed on distant peaks. Tark felt a tinge of familiarity he could not place.

A full silver moon bathed the world in twilight.

Thirty elves rushed up the final slope to the standing stones, children among them. An albino elk trailed behind, bearing an elven woman at full gallop. She wielded a long silvery glaive with a gracefully curving blade and wore flowing robes of shimmering white silk. Moonlight gleamed off delicate armour beneath the robes. Her white hair and lilac skin seemed to glow.

Her eyes were piercing orange. They looked just like Patches'.

As the elk crossed the threshold of the standing stones, a Drow arcanist within the holy site raised a dome of force around the perimeter. The woman backflipped from the elk as it barely made it inside, the barrier separating beast and rider.

The arcanist and the woman shared a long, mournful look. She turned from the hilltop, from her fleeing people, and looked back down the slope.

Two massive hulking forms rushed up through the darkness — Drider-like, with spider legs and twisted Drow bodies wreathed in purple flame. One arm of each split at the wrist into a mass of writhing snake-headed tentacles. In the other, they gripped long wicked swords that flickered with lightning.

Patches' ancestor raised her glaive. It burst into holy light. She cried forth a word of power.

The dome of force blazed with moonlit runes, reinforcing the barrier. The Balors let out shrill, mocking laughs and advanced.

Cirrus felt a familiar presence in the darkness and shivered.

Patches — suspecting she was about to watch her ancestor die — willed herself to intervene. She could not sit idle. She found herself joining the vision in physical form, manifesting beside the ancient woman. She summoned [[Eclipse]] from quicksilver, and the ancestor recognised the blade as Moonfire of [[Eilistraee]].

The ancient woman smiled and nodded. Then she looked on in surprise as the rest of the group willed themselves to join, materialising one by one on the moonlit hilltop.

They arrayed themselves before the gleaming wall of force and the ancient standing stones to meet the demons' charge.
