## Neverwinter

[[jack-mayer-johnson-mathews|JmJm]], [[doc|Doc]], [[gneiss|Gneiss]], and [[chani|Chani]] were in the rooms provided for them by Lord Neverember.

Gneiss's corpse lay out on a stone slab, prepared for [[jack-mayer-johnson-mathews|JmJm]]'s casting. [[chani|Chani]] waited nearby, watching with [[doc|Doc]].

Taking out the massive diamond [[horatio-von-bumidere|Buumi]] — and [[aliyah-faen-tlabbar|Patches]] — had provided, [[jack-mayer-johnson-mathews|JmJm]] placed it on the big man's chest and began his spellcasting.

As the spell went off, [[jack-mayer-johnson-mathews|JmJm]]'s vision darkened and the world seemed to drop away, leaving him alone with [[gneiss|Gneiss]], looking down at a glowing light beneath his hand.

Another hand came over [[jack-mayer-johnson-mathews|JmJm]]'s from someone standing behind him, and he heard a voice in his ear — one he had not heard in years: the purring, sultry tone of the goddess Sharess.

She thought bringing back [[gneiss|Gneiss]] was a grand idea, that the man would be most useful. With a surge of divine power the spell completed and [[jack-mayer-johnson-mathews|JmJm]]'s view of the world returned.

Beneath his hand, half the diamond's mass crumbled to dust. [[gneiss|Gneiss]] shuddered and sat up stiffly.

[[chani|Chani]] rushed over and hugged the man, sobbing into his arms. She looked up at [[jack-mayer-johnson-mathews|JmJm]] and asked why he would help them. He looked off wistfully.

Exhausted, [[gneiss|Gneiss]] and [[jack-mayer-johnson-mathews|JmJm]] took some time to rest.

Meanwhile, [[doc|Doc]] received a message from [[feanon-serinde|Fëanon]], asking him to Wind Walk to [[the-moonwood|the Moonwood]] and collect Mr. Pickles along the way, at the keep.

The little druid gave everyone time to gather themselves, told them of the summons, and they headed out.

[[doc|Doc]] took out a small vial of oddly shaped glass filled with storm water and uncorked it. Taking a long drag off his pipe, he blew the smoke over the vial, and it created a swirling cloud of steam as the water was consumed. The clouds grew and swirled, filling the room as [[doc|Doc]] continued to exhale. As the last breath left him, everyone in the room was swept up in the swirling wind and they rushed across the Sword Coast, the land blurring beneath them.

Traveling as the wind three hundred miles to the east, the breeze rushed through [[keep-of-thunder-bluff|Thunder Bluff]] and its half-constructed keep, picking up Mr. Pickles, and continuing north into [[the-moonwood|the Moonwood]].

Only a few hours after being cast, the spell deposited [[doc|Doc]], [[jack-mayer-johnson-mathews|JmJm]], Mr. Pickles, [[gneiss|Gneiss]], and [[chani|Chani]] directly behind the rest of the party as they looked over a ruined Elven village, smoke still hazy and thick in the air.

Hearing the crunch of snow, the group overlooking the village spun and saw their companions had arrived.

## Moondancer Village

As everyone settled from the surprise of five people showing up — and Mr. Pickles' swearing at not being warned — they turned back to the destroyed village and noted movement down below.

The three elves with them were not waiting. They hopped off the Tenser's Disc and began running and limping down to their homes, grief plain on their faces.

The party followed, save for [[gundren-rockseeker|Gundren]] who stayed on the ridge with his Thundercannon to cover them.

The village had been arranged in a circular pattern, buildings surrounding a large central clearing. The structures around the clearing were almost completely destroyed, many of them worked into massive trees that were now charred husks. The clearing itself was in worse shape.

Covering it were hundreds of dismembered pieces of elves — Moon, Drow, and other skin tones the group did not recognize. In the center of the clearing was a single head on a pike. It appeared to be moving.

The elves began to walk through the carnage, pausing to wail when they found someone they knew.

[[cirrus-al-kayan|Cirrus]] sent up his owl for a bird's-eye view when the others noticed what looked like a pattern in the arrangement. Sure enough, as the owl looked down, [[cirrus-al-kayan|Cirrus]] saw the dismembered remains arranged in a massive web pattern, spiraling out from the head at the center.

[[tarkathee-daru|Tark]] rushed toward the center, determined to take the head off the spike. [[jack-mayer-johnson-mathews|JmJm]] and [[cirrus-al-kayan|Cirrus]] followed at a more considered pace. At the edge of the village, three Moon Elf Rangers were making their way toward the group.

As [[tarkathee-daru|Tark]] approached the pike, he heard the head rambling and saw it moving. Covered in small spiders, the dismembered elf head looked at him as he drew close and a wave of dark energy flowed from it at the halfling.

[[tarkathee-daru|Tark]] fell back as he began to feel hundreds of small creatures burrowing beneath his skin. He concentrated and got his body under control, preventing the curse from spreading further, just as [[cirrus-al-kayan|Cirrus]] rushed the last thirty feet and cast a Greater Restoration.

Everyone backed away from the head.

The three Moon Elf Rangers reached the group, with one introducing herself as Menriel Boughrunner and addressing [[aliyah-faen-tlabbar|Patches]] by the name Alyah. The other rangers moved off toward their surviving kin.

Menriel told them the village had been home to free Drow and Moon Elves both, and that other than the three survivors the group had found in the forest, only four others had survived the massacre.

Shaken by the scale of the attack, [[aliyah-faen-tlabbar|Patches]] began investigating and found massive blade-like tracks in the earth. The only thing she knew that made tracks like these was a Retriever — a demonic, spider-like construct.

Menriel pointed to the head on the spike and told Patches it was the head of Findaal's wizardry teacher. The attack had been targeted at [[finn|Finn]], though the rangers did not blame [[aliyah-faen-tlabbar|Patches]] or her son.

[[gundren-rockseeker|Gundren]] walked down from the ridge and decided he had heard enough of the screaming cursed head. He tried to shoot it, only for his gun to jam. He walked off to fix it, came back, and blew it apart.

The head exploded. The cursed ground remained. [[horatio-von-bumidere|Buumi]] and [[feanon-serinde|Fëanon]] were unable to dispel or remove the curse from the land.

[[aliyah-faen-tlabbar|Patches]] moved through the village looking for anything salvageable but found nothing. The Drow raiders had been thorough.

[[tarkathee-daru|Tark]], concerned about Oni infiltrators, mentioned how they had saved the three elves in the woods and wanted to escort the survivors and rangers back to their camp. Menriel agreed — the cave to the [[underdark|Underdark]] was north, and the group would be traveling near their camp regardless.

They walked away from the ruined, cursed village and traveled two hours north, to a large Elven camp of igloos packed from snow.

## Goodbye for Now

At the camp, the group rested, even though the mood was dour.

Surrounded by Elven Rangers — all kin of those who had died in the massacre — the camp was quiet and subdued.

The group helped themselves to the stew over the central fire and began to discuss next steps.

[[doc|Doc]] walked the perimeter for an hour, searching for Oni with a detection spell, but found nothing. He returned to the camp satisfied that none were nearby.

[[horatio-von-bumidere|Buumi]] thought it was probably the right time for him to head back to the keep with [[gneiss|Gneiss]], [[chani|Chani]], and Menriel, who had expressed interest in seeing the Barony.

[[horatio-von-bumidere|Buumi]] said his goodbyes, hugging his friends and making them promise to call him when they needed him. He said the last year had been absolutely awful, but that without each other, they would have all died a dozen times over.

[[tarkathee-daru|Tark]] rushed after him and gave him another hug, saying [[horatio-von-bumidere|Buumi]] was his best friend. The paladin replied, tearfully, that [[tarkathee-daru|Tark]] was his.

[[horatio-von-bumidere|Buumi]], [[gneiss|Gneiss]], [[chani|Chani]], and Menriel walked off through the snow-covered woods, heading south toward the Barony.

The group rested, getting much-needed sleep and preparing for what waited below.

[[jack-mayer-johnson-mathews|JmJm]] began to sing softly, playing his lute and telling the story of the group. Over time, many of the elves gathered around the fire to listen as the sun set.

[[aliyah-faen-tlabbar|Patches]] got out her bomb-making kit, knowing what the [[underdark|Underdark]] demanded. Mr. Pickles handed her a small chest of supplies, saying they were from Abalaba — much needed for the trip.

[[feanon-serinde|Fëanon]], meanwhile, had found a quiet spot and taken out the second golden scroll. He activated its runes and began to read it over. He confirmed it was another High-Magic ritual and recognized a name: Proctiv. Wracking his brain — the name was familiar — [[feanon-serinde|Fëanon]] went through his notes until he found the passage he was looking for. Proctiv, Archmage of Netheril, known for the spell "Move Mountain" — the magic that had allowed the Netherese to rip mountains from the ground and build their cities atop them. [[feanon-serinde|Fëanon]] hid his excitement well, but his mind was already racing.

After resting, night had truly fallen and the snow-covered forest was eerily silent. The group took their leave from the elves and headed north, toward the spot [[aliyah-faen-tlabbar|Patches]] had last entered the [[underdark|Underdark]] more than twenty years before.

## The Descent

Traveling through the snowy forest was easy enough. The group was experienced with winter travel by now and made quick time north.

While moving through the trees, they heard a large crash and the groan of wood. Everyone kept walking save for [[doc|Doc]], who stopped to listen.

Hearing it again, the Druid recognized the moan of a Treant and decided to investigate with [[jack-mayer-johnson-mathews|JmJm]]. The others realized [[doc|Doc]] had split off and waited, impatiently.

[[doc|Doc]] walked through the trees for ten minutes until he found the source: a Treant.

The Treant was doing its best to deal with a strange situation. In a small dell with a mostly frozen stream, the Treant stood above a pile of organic mush, and whenever the pile stirred — its regeneration attempting to knit it back together — the Treant slammed down a fist and splattered it flat again. Without fire or acid, the Troll would always survive.

As they watched, one of the Troll's hands began to crawl away.

[[doc|Doc]] walked toward it, pulled his Scimitar from its sheath, and called up its fire. He touched the burning blade to the crawling hand and reduced it to ash. Moving to the main pile of gore, he communicated his intent to the Treant, which backed off, and the Druid did what was necessary. Moments later, the Troll was truly dead and the Treant was ambling off into the forest, tree-hopping through the distance until it was gone.

[[jack-mayer-johnson-mathews|JmJm]] and [[doc|Doc]] followed their footprints back and rejoined the group. An impatient [[aliyah-faen-tlabbar|Patches]] got them moving again.

After another hour, [[aliyah-faen-tlabbar|Patches]] spotted the small forested hillock hiding a dark passageway down into the lightless [[underdark|Underdark]].

They went around to the north side and found a small cliff face with a sliver of a crack in it. [[aliyah-faen-tlabbar|Patches]] pointed. That was the entrance.

She took a moment to lay out three rules of the [[underdark|Underdark]]: be silent at all times, reveal no light, and if something looked welcoming, it was either a trap or already claimed.

[[tarkathee-daru|Tark]] mentioned it wouldn't be so bad with [[jack-mayer-johnson-mathews|JmJm]]'s magical box. The bard went quiet and told the group it had been stolen in [[athkatla|Athkatla]]. He was fairly certain [[shaahir|Shaahir]] was behind the theft. Yet another reason to despise the Archmage.

With nothing else for it, the group headed to the crack and began to squeeze through, single file.

The crack was barely large enough for them. They shimmied into the darkness, cold wet stone pressing close on both sides.

The light diminished quickly. Before long, everyone was relying on their darkvision, painting the world in shades of grey. Only [[aliyah-faen-tlabbar|Patches]] and [[cirrus-al-kayan|Cirrus]] seemed unaffected, their superior sight giving them an advantage in the dark.

The thin crack seemed to go on forever. For at least two hours the group moved slowly through the tunnel, descending further and further into the cold earth.

Eventually, they reached a small chamber — the crack depositing them into a space just large enough for three people before the tunnel continued, this time close to the floor, requiring everyone to belly crawl.

For the next hour, they crawled in absolute darkness, thousands of tons of stone pressing in overhead.

If not for [[aliyah-faen-tlabbar|Patches]] guiding them, panic would have been reasonable.

Finally, after what felt like an eternity, the group crawled out into a larger tunnel. Eons ago, water had channeled this passage through the rock, but it had long since dried to a trickle.

[[aliyah-faen-tlabbar|Patches]] took point with [[tarkathee-daru|Tark]] and [[gundren-rockseeker|Gundren]] brought up the rear. They continued their trek into the [[underdark|Underdark]].

Hours passed in silence. The smooth limestone gave the walls an oddly amorphous look. [[aliyah-faen-tlabbar|Patches]] pointed out the wedge-shaped cuts in the stone marking the route, and the group assisted in finding the correct path.

## Spores

Turning down a left-hand tunnel, the group continued until [[aliyah-faen-tlabbar|Patches]] signaled a stop.

Moving in to investigate, she found a large fungal growth covering one side of the damp limestone tunnel.

The rest of the group moved in close enough to see. [[doc|Doc]] took a look at the growth but pulled back quickly as it puffed a cloud of spores at him.

[[gundren-rockseeker|Gundren]] and [[jack-mayer-johnson-mathews|JmJm]] put on the gas masks they had taken from the assassins in [[neverwinter|Neverwinter]] and moved in to investigate. They found a gruesome sight.

The fungal growth was coming from a humanoid body — the spores had erupted from the head and chest of a human prisoner and grown outward to consume it. As they searched the thing, it puffed spore clouds over both of them.

Finding nothing of use, they left the body and walked to catch up with the group.

[[gundren-rockseeker|Gundren]] took off his mask and began scratching at his skin. [[jack-mayer-johnson-mathews|JmJm]] stopped and stared at the dwarf, certain he had seen movement around [[gundren-rockseeker|Gundren]]'s collar.

They continued on.

After sixteen hours of travel, the group risked exhaustion and doubled back to a small chamber they had found a few minutes earlier. They began to set up camp, and [[aliyah-faen-tlabbar|Patches]] scouted the surrounding area.

As they prepared for rest, [[tarkathee-daru|Tark]] noticed [[gundren-rockseeker|Gundren]] scratching — and drawing blood.

Concerned, he called over [[doc|Doc]] and they began to examine the dwarf. [[jack-mayer-johnson-mathews|JmJm]] called out in a panic, swearing he had just seen something leave the self-inflicted wound.

Out on patrol, [[aliyah-faen-tlabbar|Patches]] stopped suddenly, hearing the commotion back at camp. Then she felt a vibration in the stone around her.

She rushed back to find [[gundren-rockseeker|Gundren]] scratching maniacally and [[jack-mayer-johnson-mathews|JmJm]] hissing about worms.

She opened her mouth to tell them to be quiet — just as the wall exploded nearby and three massive chitinous forms rushed into the chamber.
