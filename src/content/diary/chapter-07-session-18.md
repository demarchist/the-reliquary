## The Attack

The fires of the explosion caught tapestries and ceiling beams alight. Fëanon slammed his staff down and a crystalline wall of ice formed across the burning windows, the light cascading through it momentarily beautiful. [[Lord Neverember]]'s bodyguards clasped pendants and dimension-doored their lord to safety.

A voice spoke into [[Fëanon Serinde]]'s mind — the dwarven banker Goldhold from [[Sundabar]]. The vault was under attack from blue-skinned giants that appeared from nowhere. Oni.

Patches rushed into the hall and found Abdul's burned corpse splayed in the foyer. She searched him quickly, pocketing signet rings and valuables before tossing the poisoned tobacco pouch away. It slid across the marble floor to the booted feet of two figures. She darted behind the fountain. Two assassins rounded the corner — red cloaks, beaked gas masks, dark leather armour, and the venomous daggers from the [[Thayan Drop Box]]es.

The house shuddered under more explosions. Smoke filled the corridors. Servants screamed on other floors.

In the kitchens, [[Gundren Rockseeker]] patted flames from his cloak as two assassins rushed through the burning doorway. The first stabbed wildly — poison burning in the wounds, then instantly neutralised by [[Doc]]'s Heroes' Feast still in their blood. Breakfast was the most important meal of the day. One assassin exploded into smoke and vanished. Outside the back door, Gundren spotted Amnian veterans and a smaller figure — arrows thudding into the table above him.

[[Jack Mayer Johnson Mathews]] healed [[Chani]] and took cover. A hissing ball of gas materialised in the dining hall — Cloudkill. [[Kurt Von Bumidere]], burned and prone, gagged on the caustic fog and spasmed. Dead.

[[Sera Von Bumidere]] cried out in anguish as she helped [[Ernst Von Bumidere]] up. He downed a potion, creating force armour around his ruined noble clothes. [[Cirrus Al Kayan]] rushed to Sera's side and raised a Globe of Invulnerability from his staff. Wherever it moved, the Cloudkill simply ceased to exist. Civilians crowded into the bubble. [[Horatio von Bumidere]] collected Kurt's body, calling on Helm for an Aura of Vitality.

The assassins in the hall threw fireball gems at the group, but they shattered harmlessly against the globe. [[Gneiss]] grabbed Fëanon and plowed through the ballroom doors, where two more assassins with repeating crossbows opened fire. Poison bolts — neutralised by the feast.

Upstairs, [[Tarkathee Daru]] rushed from his room. Through a third-floor window he saw chaos: a red-robed figure floating sixty feet up with orbs of fire orbiting its head, two of which lanced out and struck the manor. Below stood Farrim the Amnian wizard, Salama the cleric, and Dellig the halfling archer — the same group from [[Silverymoon]]. Beyond the estate, fires burned throughout the city. In the harbour, an ancient red dragon strafed the docks with a jet of flame.

Tark leapt from the window, shadowstepping the rest of the way to strike the floating figure. He stunned it and smashed into its form — but the robes were insubstantial, an illusion, and he fell. As he looked up, he saw a familiar face beneath the hood. [[Cercei]]. Cold eyes looked back at him, and in a flash of insight Tark recognised possession. Something had taken control of her body.

## The Burning Manor

Combat raged on every front. In the foyer, [[Li Xian]] appeared from invisibility and turned a bag inside out. Two zombies tumbled free — Georg and Marta. The Oni called to the group, lamenting he had prepared them for the children, but the group would suffice. He vanished around a corner and unleashed a Circle of Death. [[Sera Von Bumidere]] fell dead — a desiccated husk — the Globe of Invulnerability powerless against the necrotic magic.

Ernst and Buumi both cried out.

The zombies shambled forward. Patches fought through the halls, surrounded by assassins. Li Xian struck her with rays of frost as she was flanked. Gundren wrestled assassins in the kitchens, then ripped a diamond from his helmet and unleashed a prismatic spray that burned and electrocuted his attackers while [[Gwen]] savaged them with tooth and claw.

Outside, the entity in Cercei's body — [[Corpsetaker]] — called lightning down on JmJm, Tark, and the arcane hand. JmJm had smashed the manor doors off their hinges with the massive construct and was pressing the attack on Salama. Doc called down bolt after bolt from the storm above.

Fëanon hasted Gneiss and contacted Goldhold by sending stone. The dwarves were holding the vault entrance but were engaged in a pitched battle. Fëanon's firewall trapped an assassin behind the kitchens who tried to leap through and was incinerated.

Through smoke and collapsing roof beams, the battle ground on. Tark flew up to engage Corpsetaker, calling out to the creature, asking who it was. The cold voice — unmistakably Cercei's but strangely modulated — responded that it knew Tark, that he should "feel" his family, as it consumed them to awaken so long ago. In a rage, Tark unleashed everything he had. The wizard below dispelled his flight and the monk fell, landing in a crouch.

In the ballroom, Gneiss cleaved down an assassin with his lightning halberd. Chani deflected a volley of crossbow bolts as Ernst braced a door beside her. Buumi thunderstepped in with his wounded brother, a thunderclap detonating behind them on the stairs. The assassins' necklaces of fireballs filled the ballroom with fire, scorching everyone — including the assassins who misjudged the blast radius.

Buumi laid into the assassin before him, crying out for his fallen family. Chani and Ernst overwhelmed another. Patches pumped her last rounds into the one facing Buumi and he staggered, fleeing through the hole Fëanon's fireball had blown in the wall.

In the foyer, Cirrus blasted the zombies with eldritch energy. They stood back up. Li Xian flew across the room wreathed in purple flame, flint knife slashing at the warlock. Cirrus called on [[Selune]] and unleashed an explosion of psychic static — glowing needles stabbing into the minds of his enemies.

Gneiss rushed across the foyer toward the fleeing assassin but took three bolts from a repeating crossbow. The poison, no longer countered by the feast's diminishing magic, found its mark. The big man fell dead.

The third floor collapsed inward. The house was dying. Li Xian's wounds closed as he regenerated, and above it all, Corpsetaker flew high, launching burning orbs into the fray.
