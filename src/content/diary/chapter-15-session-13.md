## The Second Wave

The [[vampire-knight-of-frostmoore|Vampire Knight]] came for [[horatio-von-bumidere|Buumi]] next — materialising from invisibility at the paladin's side, pressing the attack with the black blade while the paladins were occupied with the Balor. The [[red-wizards-of-thay|Thayan]] Necromancer and Abjurer Dimension Doored across the battlefield in tandem, angling to take [[gundren-rockseeker|Gundren]] out of the fight. Gundren attacked them both immediately and failed to put either down. The Abjurer raised one hand and Dominated him — the dwarf's eyes going flat and obedient — and turned him on [[lady-isabel|Izmakella]].

The invisible [[red-wizards-of-thay|Thayan Archwizard]] — the Zulkhir Atlan, the leader — unleashed a Prismatic Spray through the centre of the battle. The beam swept across the paladins and the Dragon, dealing heavy damage to both. The group surrounding the Balor was, by some fortune, largely unaffected. The Archwizard held back and reassessed.

An Oni gathered the remaining ghouls and charged [[jack-mayer-johnson-mathews|JmJm]]. The other Oni Cone of Colded across the group and [[tarkathee-daru|Tark]] went down again — the monk's body at the absolute end of what it could sustain. A Mass Cure Wounds from [[horatio-von-bumidere|Buumi]] pulled him back from the edge and kept him breathing.

The Balor strode through the chaos with evident joy. Its belly-mouths cackled. Its hammer found [[jack-mayer-johnson-mathews|JmJm]] and the electrified head cracked across the bard with enough force to stagger a much larger man. The Thayan Evoker floated forward and added a Cone of Cold to the same cluster, freezing [[tarkathee-daru|Tark]] solid and sending him down for a third time in the same night.

[[cirrus-al-kayan|Cirrus]] Hellish Rebuked the Evoker and forced him to burn his reaction on an Absorb Elements — even so, the man was badly burned, arrows from [[cassian|Cassian]] already stuck through his robes. [[lady-isabel|Izmakella]] bit and tore at the Balor from above, the copper scales bright against the demon's burning hide, until the great hammer found her flank and wrenched a roar of pain from the dragon.

The Evoker Cone of Colded the group again. [[cirrus-al-kayan|Cirrus]] Hellish Rebuked him again. The man was running out of reactions.

## The Archwizard's Taunt

The invisible Zulkhir Atlan had been watching. Now he spoke — his voice coming from no visible body, rolling across the battlefield with the theatrical confidence of someone who believed himself untouchable.

He addressed [[cirrus-al-kayan|Cirrus]] and [[feanon-serinde|Fëanon]] together. The warlock, he said, would be a sacrifice along his road to godhood — his teeth offered to their lord. The wizard he dismissed as [[mordenkainen|Mordenkainen]]'s newest apprentice, a title apparently meant as an insult.

[[aliyah-faen-tlabbar|Patches]] answered him with a Faerie Fire. The spell bloomed across the Archwizard's position and his invisible form lit up in silver-green outline — the greater invisibility rendered useless in an instant, the man suddenly visible to every archer on the field. The shots that followed were immediate.

The Thayan Evoker took two more arrows from [[cassian|Cassian]] and stumbled backward, bleeding from several wounds. He did not recover. He fell.

[[lord-aimu|Lord Aimu]]'s paladins filled the gap with a Mass Cure Wounds that steadied the whole line. The Necromancer caught sight of [[gundren-rockseeker|Gundren]]'s familiar and sealed [[gwen|Gwen]] in a Resilient Sphere before she could intervene. Both Oni were caught in [[feanon-serinde|Fëanon]]'s overlapping Hypnotic Patterns and charmed into unconsciousness.

Dominated, [[gundren-rockseeker|Gundren]] turned and shot [[lady-isabel|Izmakella]].

The Copper Dragon took the bolt with the expression of an adult who has been hit by a child — pained, and not especially impressed — and warned the group between attacks that the Balor would explode on death.

## The Banishment

[[lord-aimu|Lord Aimu]] had been trying to Banish the greater demon since the moment it stepped through the Gate. The Balor was powerful enough that the spell kept failing — the demon's will pushing back against the working, breaking the concentration required to hold it. The lord tried again. And again. His griffon circled, his mace held forward, the prayer forming and collapsing and forming again with the patient discipline of a man who had been doing this his entire life and was not going to stop because it was hard.

On the ninth casting, it held.

The Balor roared — a sound of genuine outrage, the belly-mouths screaming in chorus — and vanished. The silence it left behind was almost physical.

[[aliyah-faen-tlabbar|Patches]] moved immediately, crossing the field to the remaining ghouls and slaughtering them with the efficient economy of someone clearing an obstacle. Her crossbow found the Zulkhir Atlan next, and the Archwizard raised his remaining defenses and answered with a Cone of Cold before beginning the words of a Teleport.

[[cirrus-al-kayan|Cirrus]] and [[horatio-von-bumidere|Buumi]] both healed themselves. The Thayan leader saw the shape of what was coming — his Evoker dead, his Oni unconscious, his Balor banished, his greater invisibility stripped — and finished the Teleport. He was gone before anyone could close the distance.

The Necromancer and Abjurer turned to flee. [[feanon-serinde|Fëanon]] Counterspelled their Dimension Door. They tried a Teleport. He Counterspelled that too. The group overwhelmed them in moments.

The echoes of battle faded across the valley. The estate was in ruins. The vineyard was ash. But the field was theirs.

## The Blood

They looted the fallen Thayans methodically — staffs, robes, rings, the collected equipment of several wizards of considerable power. The group divided it with the practised efficiency of people who had done this many times.

[[cirrus-al-kayan|Cirrus]] was not methodical. He was manic.

He moved through the battlefield searching for any trace of the Zulkhir Atlan — a hair, a fragment of robe, anything that had touched the man directly. He found what he was looking for in a scorch mark on the grass where the Archwizard had been standing when [[aliyah-faen-tlabbar|Patches]]' Faerie Fire had lit him up. A few drops. He collected them carefully.

The blood was black. Not dark red, not the brown of dried blood — black, and thick, with the consistency of tar. It did not behave as human blood behaved. Whatever the Zulkhir Atlan had been, or had done to himself along his road to godhood, it had not left him human.

[[cirrus-al-kayan|Cirrus]] pocketed the sample without explanation. The others did not ask.

[[lord-aimu|Lord Aimu]] and his remaining paladins spent the next hour recovering what they could from the ruins of the estate — relics, heirlooms, the pieces of a household that had stood in this valley for a generation and would not stand again. They worked in silence and took what could be carried.

## Going Home

[[lady-isabel|Izmakella]] shifted back to her human form as the work finished, Lady Isabel once more in a borrowed gown, and found [[jack-mayer-johnson-mathews|JmJm]] to inform him, with the directness of a dragon who had decided on a course of action, that she intended to come to the Barony for a while. She had heard about the theater. The bard was interesting company. She would impose on his hospitality if he had no objection.

[[jack-mayer-johnson-mathews|JmJm]] had no objection.

[[lord-aimu|Lord Aimu]] requested the same — refuge at the Barony while they planned next steps, his order displaced and his estate destroyed, the political situation in [[helioglabulus|Helios]] too unstable to return to without a clearer picture. The group agreed without hesitation.

They distributed their remaining healing, took stock of what they were carrying, and [[feanon-serinde|Fëanon]] opened the Teleport Circle.

It was past midnight when they stepped out onto the great teleportation platform at the top of [[feanon-serinde|Fëanon]]'s tower, the silver door closing behind the last of them. The lake glittered far below in the summer moonlight. The sounds of the forest and the distant river carried up through the warm night air even at this height, and the lights of the Barony spread across the valley in clusters — the Keep, the Fane, the Establishment, the market buildings, the orcish encampment along the moor with its low-burning fires.

After the ruin of [[frostmoore-vale|Frostmoore Vale]], the quiet of it was almost difficult to process.

[[lord-aimu|Lord Aimu]] accepted the offer of lodging at [[the-establishment|The Establishment]] and declined it. He and his men would camp near the Fane of [[selune|Selûne]] that night, beneath the open sky. They went down into the darkness with their gear, the lord moving with the unhurried composure of a man who had lost his home that evening and was not yet ready to be inside walls.

[[lady-isabel|Izmakella]] and [[jack-mayer-johnson-mathews|JmJm]] headed to the theater. [[cassian|Cassian]] walked with [[gundren-rockseeker|Gundren]] toward the Keep until the dwarf peeled off at the entrance to his manufactory — pausing at the door long enough to note the shapes of [[bolg|Bolg]] and [[surtr|Surtr]] visible through the workshop windows, the giants still at their work in the small hours. [[aliyah-faen-tlabbar|Patches]] vanished into the night without ceremony. [[horatio-von-bumidere|Buumi]] walked [[tarkathee-daru|Tark]] to his door to make sure the monk was not more damaged than he was admitting to.

## The Dojo

[[cassian|Cassian]] did not go to the Keep.

He had a clear enough sense of the Barony now — the lake, the forest line, the way the lights arranged themselves across the valley — to navigate without a guide. He climbed back onto [[uni|Uni]], rose into the moonlit air above the settlement, and turned north toward the expanse of forest where the Dojo sat. A hub of heroes and trade and accumulated power, the Barony. A good place to begin his search. He filed the observation away and let Uni find her own pace through the night air.

He found the Dojo in the wee hours — a structure set back into the treeline, closed up and dark, the kind of quiet that suggested everyone inside had gone to bed some time ago. He landed, dismounted, and knocked.

He waited. He knocked again.

The door opened.

The man who answered was not what [[cassian|Cassian]] had been expecting. He was large, with the particular broadness of someone who had spent a great deal of time thinking about being large, and he regarded the ranger on his doorstep with the blinking suspicion of a man pulled from sleep by someone he had never agreed to know. He wore the expression of a person who considered himself important and was perpetually aggrieved that the universe had not fully caught up with this assessment.

He introduced himself as Rex Kwon Do.

[[cassian|Cassian]] introduced himself in return and asked after [[tarkathee-daru|Tark]].

Rex Kwon Do told him, with the authority of a man who ran things around here, that Tark was unavailable. Did the ranger have an appointment?
