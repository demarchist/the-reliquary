## The Ambush Sprung

[[Tarkathee Daru|Tark]] rushed down the small canyon, keeping just far enough ahead of the beasts to maintain the chase without losing them. He hit his mark and the trap sprung. A boulder tumbled down the cliffside, crushing two of the creatures flat. [[Gundren Rockseeker|Gundren]] and [[Feanon Serinde|Feanon]] opened fire with firelances from above.

[[Cirrus Al-Kayan|Cirrus]] charged down the hill with a tired yell — tripped — and tumbled the last ten feet. [[Snorbul]] scooped him up before plunging into the fight. [[Horatio Von Bumidere|Buumi]] tried to follow but stumbled as well, clattering down the cliffside in his armour before hauling himself upright and joining the melee.

As the first beast fell, [[Liara]] raised its corpse to fight alongside the party — a casual act of necromancy that startled both Buumi and Tark. They made short work of the remaining creatures and then turned to the priestess, who released her spell without comment. The risen corpse collapsed.

Tark demanded to know which god Liara served.

Zalaam. God of death.

The party went on guard immediately — the cult that had dragged them to this world served Zalaam. Liara's eyes flashed with anger and she called the [[Cult of Zalaam|Black Skull]] heretics. Their way was false and must be destroyed. She served the true aspect of Zalaam: death as balance, as natural order, not as corruption and domination.

Buumi recounted how a similar cult had once infiltrated his own order — Paladins at Helm's Hold led astray by a devil that had killed the Grandmaster. That cult had been rooted out and destroyed. Liara nodded. The parallel was exact.

The group let it go, for now. Tark kept watching.

Liara sanctified the beasts' corpses — which had once been human.

## The Oasis

The party salvaged the wrecked caravan, recovering a shipment of alcohol and a wagon their Khonsfa beetle could pull. They loaded Cirrus into the back and made the last few miles to the Stone Spire.

Numerous caravans were parked outside the walls, crews gathered around campfires. The guards informed them: two hundred Durhams per person, unless they had goods to trade. The alcohol earned them free passage, and the guards ushered them inside with raised eyebrows at the exhausted warlock slumped in the wagon.

The group refilled their water supplies at the oasis and met with [[Chrysalis]], the Spire's leader. It turned out her own guards had stolen the alcohol shipment. She thanked the party for its return and offered two thousand Durhams — which they took as a lump sum, preferring to push on to [[Ras Alkan]] rather than accept mercenary work in the hills.

## The Dream

That night, the group enacted their plan to save Cirrus from himself.

Feanon cast Deep Slumber, shielding the warlock's mind from nightmares. Cirrus resisted. His eyes snapped to Liara — who stood nearby with her lullaby ready — and he lunged at her with a knife. Snorbul and Buumi tackled him to the ground, subduing the warlock as he pleaded. They knocked him unconscious and made him as comfortable as they could.

In the darkness behind his eyes, Cirrus dreamed. He floated in a vast body of water, a starry nebula filling the sky above, the moon of Al Askar visible. Terror gripped him — but a great voice told him not to worry. He was safe here. He could rest. He pushed himself too much.

The entity said Cirrus had great power within, if only he would accept it.

Cirrus rejected the voice, terrified that this was a dark nightmare luring him from [[Selune]] into another pact like the one that had enslaved him to Ceregorach. The entity withdrew, stating it wished him no harm, and Cirrus rested in dreamless peace for the remainder of the night.

## The Usurpers

While Cirrus slept, Liara shared more of her history over fermented bug nectar. She belonged to a group called the [[Usurpers of Rudjek]], who had overthrown the ruler of the [[Iyum]] — a creature she called a Shaa'barid, The Cold One. Her descriptions made the party suspect he had been some kind of vampire: killed only by being stabbed in the heart with a magic weapon in sunlight. She had followed his Vizier — a member of the Black Skull — out to the mountains, which is where she had encountered the party.

Buumi offered that their own group was called the [[Faerun Defense Force]], though some knew them as the Heralds of Woe.

Come morning, Cirrus awoke surrounded by concerned faces. Furious at his treatment, he stormed away. Buumi followed, apologizing profusely. Cirrus called it assault. Buumi accepted the charge, saying he hadn't known what else to do to save his friend's life.

As the two returned to the group, a rough vagrant offered to tell their fortune. They refused.

## Teleporting Turtles

The group packed up and headed north through scrublands and sage desert — much easier terrain than the Red Hills. Near the end of the first day, trouble found them.

Two massive turtle-like creatures materialized on the trail — teleporting around the battlefield and unleashing devastating psychic attacks. The party rallied but quickly realized these Musulhuf were extraordinarily tough, their thick shells turning aside blow after blow.
