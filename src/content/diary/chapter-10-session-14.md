The group began their trek back across the city, but halfway through they stopped by the Westrift to debate whether they should simply leave. Nose, their [[bregan-daerthe|Bregan D'Aerthe]] escort, appeared to ask why they had halted. [[aliyah-faen-tlabbar|Patches]] stated she would like to stay and see how things progressed, in case matters went badly. The decision was cemented when Nose mentioned that [[jarlaxle|Jarlaxle]] still needed a distraction — and would owe them a favour.

## Traversing the City

They took a northerly route through the [[drow-faction|Drow]] districts, avoiding patrols, priestesses, and gangs of displaced hooligans. They managed to traverse a good portion of the city without incident, reaching the edge of Tier Breche where the great parade grounds were being patrolled. [[feanon-serinde|Feanon]] looked up at the tower of Sorcere rising above the plateau and felt the magic emanations and the power of the wards. He reluctantly turned away, and the group skirted the Academy, heading south.

The neighbourhood grew rougher as they neared the Clawrift and the devastation wrought by [[demogorgon|Demogorgon]]. [[aliyah-faen-tlabbar|Patches]] noticed they were being followed — [[grein-osstyl|Grein]], [[vizerion-devir|Vizerion's]] apprentice. [[tarkathee-daru|Tark]] went invisible, stalked the wizard, and stunned him. They tied the unconscious [[drow-faction|Drow]] up and carried him back to the inn in [[horatio-von-bumidere|Buumi's]] arms, looking like a drunk being hauled home.

They passed the fighting pits of the Duthcloim. [[cirrus-al-kayan|Cirrus]] felt a tug on his soul — he could feel the Marilith there. [[aliyah-faen-tlabbar|Patches]] mentioned she might want to check out the pits, but [[cirrus-al-kayan|Cirrus]] recounted his dark history with the Demon. The group honoured his wishes and did not stop. Near Narbondellyn's Shadow, they ran afoul of a Priestess, but proper deference and grovelling saw them through without incident.

## Grein's Interrogation

Back at the inn, they found the other guests had left. They took the unconscious [[grein-osstyl|Grein]] upstairs. They could not kill him — [[vizerion-devir|Vizerion]] would know instantly. Instead, they placed a Geas on the apprentice, preventing him from speaking of anything involving the group. [[pickles|Mr. Pickles]], on lookout, reported that [[vizerion-devir|Vizerion's]] Death Slaad [[kleve|Kleve]] was downstairs looking for [[grein-osstyl|Grein]]. They released the wizard, who rushed away. The group was certain they would have to deal with [[vizerion-devir|Vizerion]] eventually, but hopefully this would buy them time.

## Dark Tourism

[[cirrus-al-kayan|Cirrus]] slipped out alone to the fighting pits, wrestling with his conscience before giving in. Inside, he saw nothing but *her*. The Marilith lounged on a stage above the arena floor, her serpentine body draped over a hundred severed heads. She scanned the crowd with an amused smirk, fangs and tusks protruding from a face that was far too pretty for a demon. Her scales glistened with blood and golden jewellery. Her gaze swept toward him and [[cirrus-al-kayan|Cirrus]] ducked into cover, heart pounding, and fled the arena.

[[aliyah-faen-tlabbar|Patches]], [[tarkathee-daru|Tark]], and [[horatio-von-bumidere|Buumi]] decided to investigate the brothel supposedly taken over by [[grazzt|Graz'zt]], Demon Lord of Pleasure. They reached the pleasure houses without issue. The largest had a perimeter of sorts — not to stop entry, but to issue a warning. Three [[drow-faction|Drow]] stumbled out in a daze, injured in bizarre ways.

[[tarkathee-daru|Tark]] scaled the building to investigate. On the pinnacle, he found an orgy of excess in every sense. People gorging themselves on food until they choked, drowning themselves on drink, dancing and coupling until they collapsed. Bodies lay among the revelry — clearly killed by the vices on display. Past the writhing victims being tortured by demons, sitting at the centre on a bizarre throne of sculpted muscle and flesh, was a figure both beautiful and terrible: [[grazzt|Graz'zt]].

[[tarkathee-daru|Tark]] felt himself being pulled in, his mind twisting under the demonic assault. He closed his eyes, centred himself, and leapt away. When he returned to his friends in the alley, they asked what he had seen. He would not answer.

## Kimmuriel's Proposition

Back at Narbondellyn's Shadow, a [[drow-faction|Drow]] entered the establishment and approached them — the same incredibly bland-looking fellow they had seen with [[jarlaxle|Jarlaxle]]. [[aliyah-faen-tlabbar|Patches]] asked his name. [[kimmuriel|Kimmuriel]].

[[feanon-serinde|Feanon]] remembered [[lodan|Lo'Dan]] calling out to this [[kimmuriel|Kimmuriel]] during the battle with [[kangaxx|Kangaxx]] — the one who had teleported them to the middle of the ocean in pursuit. [[feanon-serinde|Feanon]] inquired about his power and discovered a [[drow-faction|Drow]] psionicist of extraordinary ability.

[[kimmuriel|Kimmuriel]] had come with purpose. [[house-baenre|House Baenre]] had leapt upon the plan. [[gromph-baenre|Gromph Baenre]] had returned to the city and had the spell in hand. The prisoner exchange would happen within twenty-four hours and the armies were already being mustered. The [[drow-faction|Drow]] wanted the Demon Lords resolved as soon as possible. [[house-baenre|House Baenre]] eagerly awaited the spell components.

The group told [[kimmuriel|Kimmuriel]] they needed a Purple Worm Egg, and he dispatched Nose to retrieve one from the Bazaar at any cost. As soon as [[finn|Finn]] was back in the [[selunia|Barony]], they would hand over the rest.

Then [[kimmuriel|Kimmuriel]] raised the matter of the distraction. With the Demon Lord issue being handled, [[jarlaxle|Jarlaxle]] needed cover to extract his own VIP from the Do'Urden compound — a reason to deploy his soldiers into the city and create an opening.

He suggested the West-Wall Slave Market. Close enough to Do'Urden for [[jarlaxle|Jarlaxle]] to send troops, far enough from the noble houses to be vulnerable, and filled with slaves to be freed. The group was intrigued.

The market was a circular open plaza with a temple of [[lolth|Lolth]] at its centre — the nerve centre of the slave trade, garrisoned by four Priestesses and twenty-two soldiers, with half patrolling the pens at any given time, supported by packs of lesser demons. The slave pens existed below the plaza in pie-shaped wedges, accessible only through the central temple. Underground, fifteen feet of solid stone separated the pens from the stench-streets. [[kimmuriel|Kimmuriel]] estimated at least six hundred slaves were being held.

When asked how they were supposed to extract the slaves, [[kimmuriel|Kimmuriel]] shrugged — not his problem. He suggested arming the Goblin and Orc slaves and having them riot out into the nearby city.

The group began to plan.
