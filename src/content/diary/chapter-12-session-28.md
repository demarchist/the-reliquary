## The Tome's Last Gift

The group retired to their extravagant palace rooms. Alone, [[Feanon Serinde|Feanon]] felt the [[Tome of Al Azif]] reach out to him. Unable to resist its pull, he took the book from its titanium box. The immediate relief he felt confirmed what he already suspected — he was addicted. The knowledge was too valuable to abandon. He opened the dark pages and learned the spell: Torture.

With effort, he returned the book to the box and settled into Reverie.

## Vecna's Offer

The vision came. Feanon opened his eyes to find a dark figure seated at his table — shrouded in wispy blackness, a single cyclopean purple eye gleaming beneath a cavernous hood. [[Vecna]].

The dark god gestured to the table. He wished to talk.

Feanon sat.

Vecna wanted the group gone from his world. He promised safe passage through a Ringed Veil and the information needed to stop Atropos, if they left this world to him. Feanon demanded assurances and specifics, but without a binding promise, Vecna danced around the details.

The god did reveal certain truths. He had almost achieved everything he wanted from this world. He merely wished to remain here, secure in his power, and not venture forth to be a slave to [[Orcus]]. He told Feanon that [[The Last Word]] was not the only way to stop Atropos — any sufficiently powerful Greater god could reforge the chains that once bound it in the [[Far Realm]]. Vecna neared that power here, with his Fragment of the Word and the divine essence of consumed gods. He could work with Feanon toward a similar goal.

Feanon said he could not promise anything, but would think on it. Vecna said he would not give the group a breather just because Feanon was thinking. Feanon understood.

He woke and said nothing to the others. Not yet.

## The Tower's Defences

The group decided to breach the [[Golden Tower]] the next morning. Confident in their power, they triggered the outer wards. Arcane energies lashed from crystals embedded in the white marble facade, summoning four massive elementals — earth, water, fire, and wind.

A pulse of wild magic reversed gravity beneath the earth elemental, flinging it skyward. The water elemental was thrown from the bridge, plummeting five hundred feet to the city below — unharmed, but removed from the fight. With two temporarily gone, the group concentrated on the remainder. As they assaulted the wind elemental, it split into three smaller ones — the wards were more complex than expected.

## The Throne Room

They destroyed the air elementals in a series of shockwaves, and [[Horatio Von Bumidere|Buumi]] enclosed the returning water elemental in a Resilient Sphere, dropping it off the bridge again. [[Aliyah Faen Tlabbar|Patches]] spotted a crystal on the tower charging for another summon and shot it with a magical bullet. Feanon identified three more crystals, each preparing to call additional elementals. The group destroyed them all.

[[Jack Mayer-Johnson Mathews|JmJm]] mounted his Nightmare steed and soared above the city, gaining a magnificent view of the ancient Saurian High Road stretching to the horizon. Below, the others found the front door sealed by powerful magic. JmJm returned and, with a flourish of power, dispelled the wards.

The door swung open onto a copper-lined tunnel flanked by bronze statues with a larger one above the inner door. JmJm walked straight in. Every statue thrust out a hand and blasted him with lightning. The entire group caught the discharge. Buumi rushed around healing. JmJm used a Bigsby's Hand to smash the inner doors, deactivating the statues.

Beyond lay a throne room. A golden dais held a crystal throne — the tower's control seat. Two alcoves contained dormant golems. [[Cirrus Al-Kayan|Cirrus]] sat in the throne and was attacked by the tower's security enchantments. He failed the mental challenge and was hurled ten feet, bleeding from his eyes and ears.

The golems stirred. The group had one more attempt before the tower attacked in force.

Buumi sat on the throne, praying to Helm as his companions infused him with protective magics. He broke the security spells. [[Tarkathee Daru|Tark]] attuned to the tower over the next hour, learning its inner workings: a dangerous basement, trapped guest rooms designed by [[Rudjek]] for torture, two penthouse suites obscured by separate enchantments, and hidden elevator shafts. Tark commanded the guest-room death traps deleted. The golems obeyed his voice.

In the basement, they found the source of [[Iyum]]'s plague — hundreds of cages containing wailing masses of corrupt flesh, oozing disease into the aquifer below. Cirrus burned it all away with sickening radiance. Tark deleted the room behind them as they ascended.

The first penthouse revealed [[Kumuzu Isa]]'s chambers: lush with drapes and carpets on every surface, a dais of clear marble with a slab of dark crystal at its centre, and three rings of metal set into the far wall — small Ringed Veils, one with an active portal. Everything in the room was a trap. Animate rugs attacked. The group flew and jumped to the crystal slab, which Feanon identified as an Efreeti bottle.

Then a drape wrapped around Cirrus and hauled him through the illusionary ceiling. A long tentacle carried him toward a dripping maw embedded above — something vast and hungry hidden in the architecture.
