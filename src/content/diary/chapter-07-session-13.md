## The Freed Slaves

The group settled aboard [[The Silent Virtue]], taking a much-needed breather. Twenty minutes later, one of the newly freed slaves came to find them. Below decks, thirty-two people sat around — hungry, uncertain, and with nowhere to go.

The group decided a feast was in order. They poured out the Ewer of Feasting and supplemented the conjured banquet with stores from the ship's hold. As the grateful folk ate, an older half-orc woman approached and introduced herself as Keerna. She told them plainly that the freed slaves had very little and no idea what to do next — they had all been grabbed from the slums.

The group brought up the possibility of working on a temple of [[Selune]] within the city, but Keerna was sceptical. Then someone mentioned that the Barony could always use more people. [[Cirrus Al Kayan]] jumped on the idea and the others agreed, telling the freed slaves of opportunities in the east at [[Selunia]], the Barony at [[Thunder Bluff]].

Keerna liked the idea but worried about crossing [[the Evermoors]] in winter with no financing. [[Tarkathee Daru]] pulled out a hundred and twenty gold and handed it over. Keerna took the fortune — the most money she had ever seen — and teared up. The others gathered and began to cheer, swearing to form a caravan and head east. They mentioned that with that kind of gold, they could probably convince a full group from the slums to emigrate. The group told them to seek out Li Tan upon arrival.

## The Sallow Manatee

A whistle from the docks drew them topside. [[Captain Lendeer]], a city guard captain, had arrived at the wharf. He rowed over and climbed aboard, mentioning he had heard The Silent Virtue had been captured by the Heralds. Enough eyes watched the docks that any hope of controlling the information was already moot. He urged the group to follow their remaining leads now, before word spread further.

The group told him about [[Petey Greene]] and the mysterious box delivered to someone called "Priss" at the [[Sallow Manatee]]. Lendeer used a sending stone to call in troops to cut off the city block — twenty minutes to get in position. Too slow for the group's taste.

They headed for the inn, leaving Lendeer to his logistics. Approaching the darkened two-storey building, they saw two lit windows on the upper floor. Patches scaled the outside and peered through — one unoccupied room with a low-burning lamp, another with an old man asleep. She dropped back down and reported.

Checking the front door — noting the boarded-over hole from Gundren's earlier shotgun blast — Patches found the entrance wired with a black powder charge. Halfway through the disarm, she realised it was a false fuse and dove clear just as the charge detonated, blasting the door and a chunk of wall from its hinges.

The group rushed inside, [[Gundren Rockseeker]] with his [[Thundercannon]] aflame. The darkened common room was empty but noise came from the cellar stairs. Cirrus disguised himself as [[Vaas Redblade]] and called down, doing an excellent impression — telling Petey the gig was up.

The barrel of a blunderbuss poked around the corner, followed by the fat, balding head of Petey Greene. He spotted the heavily armed group, went pale, locked eyes with [[Fëanon Serinde]] — and dropped unconscious.

Patches climbed back to the second floor and investigated the rooms. Behind one door she smelled burnt paper. Inside, the room was completely tossed — mattress on the floor, desk flipped, a cot in one corner, a stack of ash in the fireplace. A half-burned letter lay at the edge of the hearth. The window was open to the alley. A large quantity of metallic purple dust was scattered near the overturned desk.

Patches snatched the letter. It detailed fragments of a shadowy plan — mentions of items provided by a benefactor, payment in advance, something about a lord's head, eyes on targets, agents who would give no quarter if discovered, and a sign-off addressed to "Stalker."

Downstairs, the group found a disguised trapdoor in the cellar leading to a second basement. Tark peered over the edge and caught a crossbow bolt for his trouble — fired wildly by the large Orog from earlier, Gleg. Tark dropped down and told the creature to drop the crossbow, which he did. Sensing Gleg was not the sharpest knife in the drawer, Tark told him to "watch the crossbow." The Orog drew a shortsword and began eyeing the dropped weapon intently.

Across the room, Jenny — the redheaded bouncer — was pocketing silver ingots into a sack. She surrendered when Gundren and [[Gwen]] arrived. After some negotiation, Jenny traded information for freedom. She explained they had nothing to do with the people trafficking — they moved the money, melting down gold and silver from the slave trade and pirate coin into ingots. Their goldsmith was Blek Blekson in the Tower District. She knew nothing of the mysterious box.

They let Jenny flee into the sewers. Gleg, having decided the crossbow had ceased its shenanigans, was sent off to the docks to join Keerna's caravan to Thunder Bluff.

## The Thayan Drop Box

The group headed to the Tower District by carriage, arriving past midnight. A guard directed them to Blek Blekson's shop — a two-storey blue building with a cellar entrance. Patches and Tark took the back. Cirrus, disguised as Vaas, and Gundren took the front. Fëanon remained in the carriage, reading.

Blek answered the door — a sleepy, annoyed dwarf with scraggly black hair and a braided beard. As he grew suspicious, Gundren smashed the butt of his cannon into the dwarf's face and dropped him cold. Inside, Patches and Tark found a journal in Dwarvish. Gundren flipped through it, discovering Blek's petty rivalry with a nearby watchmaker named Leister Bana — and more importantly, that Blek's spies had observed a strange delivery to the watchmaker: a metallic purple box, seamless, delivered in the dead of night. It matched the description of the box Vaas had mentioned.

Four houses down, they found Leister's shop with its ornate clockwork sign. Patches picked the back door, spotted and disarmed a trap, and slipped inside. After searching every box in the storage room, she found it — a smaller box covered in a white sheet, glittering purple metal, seamless and strangely heavy for its size. She left a decoy in its place and hauled the [[Thayan Drop Box]] back to the group.

Fëanon examined it in the carriage, sensing strong abjuration, transmutation, and enchantment magic. He had no idea how to open it. Patches went back in and brought the sleeping watchmaker down for questioning. The panicking man knew nothing — he had been paid a hundred gold to store the box and hand it to someone later that day.

The group told Leister to take a vacation. They would man his shop and entrap whoever came for the box. Tark handed the man a hundred and ninety gold; Patches took back a hundred. Leister left with his wife, bewildered.

Come morning, Cirrus posed as Leister at the workbench while Patches, disguised as Mrs. Bana, ran the front. Tark hid inside a grandfather clock. Gundren waited upstairs with his gun. After handling a few genuine customers, a halfling entered — features obscured by a wide-brimmed hat. Patches approached. The man said he was window shopping, handed her a card reading simply "Holst," and left. The rest of the morning passed uneventfully. The group suspected Holst had somehow seen through their disguises.

A sending from [[Horatio von Bumidere]] arrived — he had expected them for breakfast. The [[Amnians]] delegation had arrived: [[S'Olaa]] Al Kayan, Abdul Al Selemchant, Leigham Al Selemchant, [[Gneiss]], [[Chani]], and Whistler. Buumi wanted Cirrus to brief them.

## Return to the Estate

Heading back, Patches spotted a halfling matching Holst's description entering a haberdashery. When the man removed his hat — blond hair, cherubic features — she recognised him as the halfling from the Bumidere estate. Thessalt. She called out "Holst" but the halfling kept walking. Frustrated, she rejoined the group.

Meanwhile, the others sent the Thayan Drop Box to [[Mordenkainen]] via Instant Messenger — much to Fëanon's dismay. Word came back: a Thayan Drop Box, accessible only with a password. Any other attempt would trigger a Glyph of Power Word Kill.

Fëanon attempted to scry on Holst using the card. The spell was resisted.

At the estate, the group noticed Mr. Shon teaching the younger children by a fountain — reading them the history of [[Neverwinter]], the children actually paying attention. Patches asked what the man was doing. "Teaching," he replied with an arrogant sneer, "to avoid repeating the errors of history."

Inside, Buumi, his brothers [[Ernst Von Bumidere]] and [[Kurt Von Bumidere]], and [[Esimir Von Bumidere]] were at brunch. The group learned the Amnians were delayed, heading straight to [[Helm's Hold]] before coming to the estate. The names were repeated and the group recognised two retainers — Gneiss and Chani — as the monk and halberd fighter they had fought in [[Silverymoon]].

The group recounted the night's events. When they mentioned the Thayan Drop Box, Esimir noted he had heard of such devices and that the Thayans had been invited to the Council but never responded. He also mentioned the Thayans had tried to conquer the city eighty years prior.

Patches excused herself to pen a letter to [[Lady Faye]] via spanreed, inquiring about Thayan activity. As she moved through the house, she spotted [[Val Tengu]] checking every window — a security sweep. Without looking back, he said "Drow, eh?" and initiated a conversation. He had read Buumi's letters. He was fairly sure Patches was one of the good ones. But he told her plainly — don't hurt Buumi. Then he walked off.

As the group prepared their teleport circle, Patches spotted a halfling enter the estate — nearly identical to the one she had followed in the Tower District. He let out a series of hacking coughs, proclaimed he didn't feel well, and retreated to "his rooms." Patches thought the coughing was faked and followed, peering through the keyhole to watch the halfling sit at a desk, remove his hat, and begin writing a letter.

At brunch, [[Kurt Von Bumidere]] mentioned he needed to check on his boyfriend — a halfling named Thessalt. The pieces clicked into place. The group decided to keep an eye on him rather than confront him directly. They suspected an assassination plot was in motion. Outside, Patches saw [[Cercei]] some distance from the other children, berating Mr. Shon — the older man hanging his head in apparent shame. The little girl wore a flowing red dress with an elaborate bow at her waist.

Buumi was told about the potential threat to his father. Val and [[Sera Von Bumidere]] were both warned. Then Fëanon finished the teleport circle, activated it, and the group stepped through.

## Helm's Hold

They materialised atop a massive tower — the teleport chamber of [[Helm's Hold]], some four hundred feet in the air. Armoured paladins, clerics, and three golden golems stood guard. The flood plains of the coast stretched east, [[Neverwinter]] was visible on the horizon to the north, a forest lay to the south, and the sea glittered to the west.

A cleric led them to a spiralling exterior staircase and then into a mechanical elevator that dropped them to the ground floor of the Cathedral — a truly massive stone chamber with a ninety-foot ceiling covered in paintings of celestials protecting innocents. White marble floors gleamed with colour from hundreds of stained-glass windows. The air smelled of incense and wildflowers. Ahead, a waterfall poured from above into a vast platinum-lined pool — seemingly endless but never overflowing.

A huge man in gold-trimmed plate armour approached. Dark-skinned, amber-eyed, with short curling hair and a pointed goatee — stern and serious, in his mid-thirties. [[Javen Tarkimos]], Leader of the [[Watchers of Helm]]. He questioned Buumi's intent, noting that the [[Trials of the Crusader]] had not been performed in over two centuries. The records were so incomplete they had to bring in an old dragon who had witnessed the last one. He wondered aloud if this was merely a bid for celebrity.

The group defended their comrade's character. Buumi said he had no idea why he had called for the Trials — only that he felt it was Helm calling him. Javen studied him, cracked a smile, and clapped him on the shoulders, satisfied. He led them to the waterfall and the platinum pool where an odd-looking dwarf stood waiting. Javen introduced him as [[Aurinax]] and took his leave.

Aurinax turned, smiling. Shorter than Gundren and of indeterminate age, the dwarf wore robes of glittering gold and orange. A short-cropped metallic gold beard and a mane of well-kept hair framed a friendly face that glittered faintly. Two sets of gold, cat-slit eyes squinted out at the party. In his hand was a staff of gold — two twisting dragons wound around one another.

"Ah, good," he said. "I've been expecting you."
