## The Villa

Georg's villa outside Silverymoon sat on a gentle hill above the River Rauvin, surrounded by snow-laden apple orchards and a low stone wall that had been built for beauty rather than defence. Smoke curled from three chimneys. As the group approached, the front door burst open and two of Georg's younger children sprinted out, hurling snowballs at each other. They stopped when they saw the group and stared.

Buumi waved. The children waved back and then resumed their war.

Inside, the villa was everything the group's inns and campsites were not — warm, clean, and full of noise. Toys littered the hallway. Sheet music covered every flat surface. A pianoforte dominated the sitting room, flanked by a violin stand and a small harp. The smell of baking bread drifted from the kitchen.

Georg greeted them at the door in shirtsleeves, a dish towel over one shoulder, and ushered the group inside with the efficiency of a man who had marshalled seven children through breakfast. Buumi introduced the rest of the party. Georg shook each hand firmly, pausing at Tark — he had never seen a halfling move with such contained violence — and again at Fëanon, whose mask he studied for a long moment before deciding not to ask.

"You are all welcome," Georg said. "But if you break anything, you fix it."

## The Governess

The children materialised in waves — the older ones curious and polite, the younger ones climbing on furniture and staring. Among them moved a young woman the group had not met before. She was perhaps twenty-five, with an open, sun-freckled face and chestnut hair that refused to stay pinned. She wore the simple grey robes of a cleric of Helm with the sleeves rolled up past her elbows and flour on her hands.

"This is [[Maria]]," Georg said. "She was sent from the [[House Invincible]] as governess for the children."

Maria smiled at the group and immediately began making tea for everyone without being asked. She had the natural warmth of someone who liked people on principle and the quiet competence of someone who had learned to manage seven children through charm rather than discipline. The youngest boy — the one who had hidden from Cercei — clung to Maria's skirt and watched the strangers from behind her.

Cercei, who had been examining the piano with intense interest, looked at Maria, looked at the boy, and walked over. She sat down on the floor next to him and began talking about dragons. Within five minutes, the boy was sitting in her lap asking questions, and Maria was staring at Cercei with undisguised delight.

## Amnian Flags

Over dinner, Georg spoke about what had been troubling him.

Amnian influence in Silverymoon had been growing for months. A merchant lord named [[Rama Al Kayan]] — a distant relation of Cyrrus's family, the group noted with discomfort — had established a trade mission in the city and was funding infrastructure projects throughout the region. Roads, bridges, granaries. Generous terms. No obvious strings.

"Except for the flags," Georg said.

Amnian banners had begun appearing on public buildings, trade offices, and even some private estates. The message was clear: Amn was investing in Silverymoon, and it expected recognition. Georg — who had served in the northern fleet and had fought Amnian pirates off the Sword Coast — refused to fly one. He was, he said, a citizen of Silverymoon. Not a franchisee.

Fëanon listened with growing unease. The Amnian presence he had noticed in the city — the fit young men with military bearing, the weapons caravans — now had a face and a name.

"How many soldiers does Rama have?" Fëanon asked.

Georg shook his head. "He calls them merchants. Trade guards. Infrastructure workers. But I know soldiers when I see them." He paused. "And I am not the only one who has noticed."

## The Party

A week later, Georg hosted a gathering at the villa — old friends, fellow officers, and members of Silverymoon's civic establishment. The house was transformed with candles, greenery and music. Georg's oldest daughter played the violin while Maria sang — a clear, unaffected alto that silenced the room. Buumi sat in the corner watching his brother-in-law's face as Georg listened to Maria's voice, and saw something he recognised.

Cyrrus, never one to miss a social opportunity, charmed a cluster of Silverymoon merchants with tales of the group's exploits — heavily embellished and starring himself. Tark found a quiet corner and was approached by an old soldier who recognised a fighter's hands and spent the evening exchanging stories. Patches circulated the room, collecting information with the ease of someone who could be anyone.

The evening took a sharp turn when [[Rama Al Kayan]] arrived uninvited.

He was a large man — broad, bearded, expensively dressed — with the confident stride of someone who believed money could open any door. Two guards flanked him. He crossed the room to Georg and embraced him like an old friend, though the two had met only once.

"A fine home, Captain," Rama said, loud enough for the room to hear. "But I notice you have not yet raised our flag. An oversight, I am sure."

Georg's jaw tightened. "No oversight."

The room went quiet. Rama studied Georg for a long moment, his smile fixed, and then laughed and clapped the captain on the shoulder. "We will discuss it another time." He helped himself to wine and moved into the crowd, leaving Georg standing very still.

Buumi crossed the room to his brother-in-law. "You should leave the city," he said quietly.

Georg shook his head. "This is my home."

## Maria

In the days that followed, Buumi noticed what the others had already seen: Georg and Maria were falling in love. It was the quiet kind — built on shared glances over the children's heads, on the way Georg's rigid posture softened when she entered a room, on Maria's habit of resting her hand on his arm when she spoke. Neither acknowledged it. Both were aware.

[[Baroness Elsa]] noticed too. She was Georg's intended — a socialite from one of Silverymoon's old families, beautiful and sharp and entirely unsuited to a house full of children. She had been courting Georg for over a year with the patience of a woman who knew her own worth. When she visited the villa and saw the way Maria moved through the household — the way the children ran to her, the way Georg watched her — Elsa understood.

She found Maria in the garden and told her plainly. Maria, stricken, packed a small bag that evening and returned to the House Invincible without saying goodbye. Georg came downstairs the next morning and found the children confused and Maria's room empty. He stood in the doorway for a long time.

The house grew quieter. The children moped. Georg threw himself into his work with the kind of intensity that men use to avoid thinking. Elsa visited more frequently, graceful and composed, and if she felt any satisfaction it did not show.

After two weeks, Maria came back. She arrived at the front door in the rain, her grey robes soaked, her hair plastered to her face, and told Georg that she had prayed to Helm for guidance and the answer had been clear. She belonged here. Georg took her hand and pulled her inside.

Elsa came to the villa that afternoon. She and Georg spoke privately in the study for an hour. When the door opened, both were composed. Elsa kissed Georg on the cheek, nodded to Maria, and left. She did not return.

Georg and Maria married quietly, with Buumi as witness and the seven children filling the front pew of a small chapel near the villa. Cyrrus officiated — claiming dubious but enthusiastic authority — and even Fëanon attended, sitting in the back row with his arms crossed and the faintest suggestion of a smile.

## The Message

The warmth did not last.

A sealed letter arrived at the villa bearing the Amnian trade seal. Georg opened it at the breakfast table and read it in silence. Maria watched his face change.

Rama Al Kayan, on behalf of the Amnian Trade Mission, was pleased to offer Captain Georg von Strapp a position of honour in the newly formed Amnian Civic Militia — a force dedicated to protecting Silverymoon's trade infrastructure. The letter praised Georg's distinguished military record and expressed confidence that his experience would be invaluable. A uniform and posting details would follow.

It was not an offer. It was a conscription notice wrapped in silk.

Georg set the letter on the table and looked at Buumi. "Now will you help me leave?"

## The Assassination

The group had been watching Rama Al Kayan for weeks. Cyrrus, who understood Amnian politics better than anyone, had identified the merchant lord as the linchpin of the operation in Silverymoon — remove him and the infrastructure of influence would take months to rebuild.

Patches made the suggestion. Cyrrus considered it. Fëanon said nothing, which was as close to approval as the wizard ever gave.

It happened on a cold evening outside the Amnian trade offices. Rama was leaving a dinner engagement, flanked by his usual guards, when Cyrrus stepped out of a side street and intercepted him. The warlock engaged Rama in conversation — something about trade tariffs — drawing the merchant lord's attention while the guards shifted to cover the obvious threat.

Nobody saw Patches. She came from above — a shadow against the roofline — and dropped behind the rearmost guard without a sound. By the time Rama turned at the noise, it was already done. A single precise cut, surgical and silent. The merchant lord's eyes went wide, his hand reaching for a throat that was no longer intact. He collapsed into the snow.

Cyrrus caught the body and lowered it to the ground. Patches vanished. The guards stood frozen for three heartbeats before the first one shouted. By then, Cyrrus was already gone — a bamf of displaced air and he was two streets away, walking calmly through the evening crowds.

## Retaliation

Rama Al Kayan's second-in-command — a hard-eyed captain named Hafeez — responded within hours. Amnian soldiers dropped any pretence of being merchants and began locking down the trade quarter. Checkpoints appeared on major streets. Armed patrols swept the residential districts. The official story was that Rama had been killed by bandits, but Hafeez's fury suggested he knew better.

Georg's villa was visited that night by a squad of Amnian soldiers demanding to search the premises. Georg met them at the door in full uniform — the dress blues of the Silverymoon Northern Fleet — and refused entry. The squad leader hesitated. Georg von Strapp was known, and his reputation carried weight even among the Amnians. They left, but the threat was clear.

"Tomorrow," Georg said when they had gone. "We leave tomorrow."

## The Festival

Fate provided cover. The annual Midwinter Folk Festival was the next evening — a celebration of music, dance and regional traditions held in the Market Square. Every family in the residential district would attend. The streets would be packed. The checkpoints would be overwhelmed.

The plan came together quickly. Georg and Maria would enter the festival with all seven children, dressed in traditional Luruaran folk costume. They would perform in the family ensemble competition — a tradition Georg's family had upheld for years — and then, in the chaos of the festival's finale, slip out through the western gate with pre-packed supplies and horses stabled at a farm two miles outside the walls.

The group would run interference.

Buumi helped Georg pack through the night — two bags, nothing more. Maria dressed the children and explained, in careful terms appropriate for each age, that they were going on an adventure. The oldest understood and said nothing. The youngest was excited.

At dusk, the family walked to the Market Square. The festival was already in full swing — bonfires, music, vendors selling roasted chestnuts and mulled wine. Amnian patrols circled the perimeter but the crowd was thick and the mood was festive. No one looked twice at a family in folk costume carrying instruments.

Georg and his children took the stage for their performance. Maria stood with them, the youngest on her hip, and together they played a folk arrangement that drew applause and tears from the crowd. Georg played the guitar while the children sang in harmony — seven voices, from the teenager's clear soprano to the four-year-old's tuneless enthusiasm. Maria's voice carried above them all.

Backstage, Buumi watched the exits. Tark perched on a rooftop overlooking the square. Fëanon sat at a café table near the western gate, ostensibly reading, his fingers resting on his staff beneath the table. Cyrrus mingled with the crowd, ready to create a distraction. Patches was nowhere to be seen, which meant she was everywhere.

The performance ended to a standing ovation. Georg bowed, gathered his children, and walked toward the western edge of the square. An Amnian patrol stepped into his path — two soldiers, checking papers.

Cyrrus detonated a series of minor illusions at the eastern end of the square — fireworks, coloured smoke, the sound of a fight. The crowd surged. The patrol turned. Georg and his family slipped between the bodies and through the western gate without breaking stride.

At the stables, horses were saddled and waiting. Georg lifted each child onto a horse, mounted the last one himself, and turned to Buumi.

"Thank you, brother," he said.

"Keep them safe," Buumi said.

Georg nodded. Maria pulled her cloak around the youngest child, kicked her horse to a trot, and the Von Strapp family disappeared into the winter darkness beyond the city walls.
