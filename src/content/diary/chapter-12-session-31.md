## The Last Whisper

The group shaped the [[Golden Tower]] to their preferences during a long rest. That night, [[Vecna]]'s whispers found [[Feanon Serinde|Feanon]] — just one more spell in the book. The wizard gave in but failed to decipher the complex spell. Frustrated, he returned the [[Tome of Al Azif|tome]] to its titanium box.

## Pasha

The next morning, [[Gundren Rockseeker|Gundren]] summoned [[Sultan Bashir]], who arrived with his Grand Vizier and four Royal Guard in grown-metal armour. The group informed him that they had cleansed the tower, including the source of [[Iyum]]'s plague — corrupt flesh festering in the aquifer beneath [[Kumuzu Isa]]'s basement.

Bashir was shocked. He had never suspected the plague's origin was within the tower. Grateful, he foresaw problems with the nobility over an outlander controlling a major city structure and had his Grand Vizier bestow the title of Pasha on [[Tarkathee Daru|Tark]]. The monk was confused but accepted.

## Into the Swamp

[[Jareem]] returned from meeting his [[Obsidian Order]] contacts. The Order's agents would assist against Vecna and begin scouting the [[Thueba Swamp]] for the ancient [[Machina Factory]]. The group told Jareem to remain in the Golden Tower as its guardian — there was much he could orchestrate from Iyum.

In Kumuzu Isa's old chambers, Feanon and Gundren spent hours deciphering the Ringed Veil with the mud-tracked floor. They reactivated the gate and connected it to its configured partner. [[Cirrus Al-Kayan|Cirrus]] summoned a powerful celestial and sent it through with orders to secure the area and report back via notepad.

The notepad returned covered in beautiful drawings rendered in red ink — a [[Cult of Zalaam|Cult of the Black Skull]] encampment, slaughtered by the celestial, its walls covered in gore. The drawings were done in blood.

The group stepped through. Over thirty dead cultists lay across a fortified ancient Aarakocran eyrie, high in the mountains overlooking the swamp. They secured the gateway and descended the barren mountainside under the glaring twin suns.

On the way down, they encountered a massive Fire Giant punching craters into the rock, searching for titanium — the sacred metal of his people. Tark offered his spear. The giant was shocked to see holy metal in the hands of a halfling but refused it, saying the monk would need the weapon where he was going. The giant wanted to be left alone, and Tark obliged.

By late afternoon they reached the swamp's edge — a tropical quagmire of gnarled trees, dense vegetation, and rising mist. A six-hundred-foot canal separated them from the interior, and a shipwreck across the water drew their attention. They flew over to investigate.

A chorus of ghostly howls split the air. The waters churned. A legion of undead rose from below — and the wisps of cloud clinging to the swamp twisted into the forms of ghostly giants floating among the vapour.

The group stopped short, a vast army of the dead arrayed before them, the [[Thueba Swamp]] guarding its secrets with teeth and spectral fury.
