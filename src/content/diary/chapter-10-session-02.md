[[tarkathee-daru|Tark]] gazed upon the Stone Giant standing beneath the magnificent crystal heart of the library. The librarian introduced himself as [[ustova|Ustova]], Archivist of the Future — easily twenty feet tall, clad in elegant purple robes that matched the amethyst chandelier above. The giant smiled and invited [[tarkathee-daru|Tark]] to touch the great crystal and peer into what was to come, though he warned that the experience would be hard on both body and mind.

[[tarkathee-daru|Tark]] asked what he would see. "Possibilities," the giant replied.

The monk settled into meditation and attuned to the heart of the great library.

## Visions of the Future

The meditation came easily to [[tarkathee-daru|Tark]] — he had spent a lifetime in such discipline. But the library's presence was something new, buoying him with power and intent. He pushed through the first window into the future, which spawned more windows, then more, then more — an infinite cascade of possibility. His mind began to fracture under the strain.

It took every ounce of discipline to remain focused. With the library's aid, he steadied himself and began to see the futures he sought.

He saw the future [[zhengyi|Zhengyi]] was driving toward: a conquered and failing world, the great northern cities in ruins, the last survivors pushed back to the Spine of the World and the Dwarven strongholds within. A dark humanoid figure floated forward — multiple crimson arms sprouting from its back, an iron crown atop its head — and with a gesture ripped the life from a battalion of defenders. A host of undead monstrosities, [[amnians|Amnians]], and Thayans stood at its back. [[zhengyi|Zhengyi]].

The vision ended and [[tarkathee-daru|Tark's]] mind reeled. Could they win? Could he trust his friends? How long until betrayal? His mind raced down this avenue — but every future showed his companions standing by his side. They did not betray him.

Pleasantly surprised, he turned to the question of actual victory. The web of possibilities expanded before him. In one, every member of the group wielded a fragment of [[the-last-word|the Word]], battling a monstrous necrotic abomination at the heart of a dead world. In another, only one of them held the artefact whole — the power was staggering, but it was killing the wielder. He saw variation after variation: each of them wielding it, each burned away by the power. Another vision showed the group leading a heavenly host, empowered by the artefact. In yet another, [[atropos|Atropos]] was dragged in massive chains back to the [[far-realm|Far Realm]] and re-imprisoned. And in one more, the group stood over the burned-out husk of [[orcus|Orcus]], the Demon Lord's heart ripped from his chest at the centre of a great ritual in Thanatos.

There was hope. They could win.

## The Demonweb Pits

In the chamber of the Present, [[aliyah-faen-tlabbar|Patches]] gazed upon the [[abyss|Abyss]]. She saw the Demonweb Pits — the domain of [[lolth|Lolth]]. An infinite layer filled with weblike structures and hazy ambient light filtering through billions of strands of silk. It was deserted.

She watched for some time before spotting movement in the distance: a host of spider-like demons clad in glistening black carapace, rushing along a particularly thick strand of webbing. As the small army passed beneath her vantage, [[aliyah-faen-tlabbar|Patches]] followed them toward a portal at the edge of the layer. Two female Drow stood flanking the tear between layers, and as each demon passed, they reached out and touched it — granting Drow bodies and weapons before the host moved through onto a plain of frozen bones. She could not see the Drow clearly, but suspected they were Yochlol, Handmaidens of [[lolth|Lolth]].

The vision ended and [[aliyah-faen-tlabbar|Patches]] woke, exhaustion washing over her. She told [[horatio-von-bumidere|Buumi]] what she had seen.

## The Iron Horn and Efreeti Chain

Nearby, [[gundren-rockseeker|Gundren]] was deep in his own visions. He searched first for an artefact from the legends of the Uthgardt Barbarians — the Iron Horn. The vision raced north to the peaks of the Spine of the World, to a massive glacier held within a basin of mountains. A shattered peak barely rose above the ice, and a cave descended deep into the stone beneath. Enormous ravens with twenty-foot wingspans circled the entrance. Raven Rock — the rumours from his kin were confirmed. The Barbarian burial site held significant treasure.

He shifted focus and the sensor rushed south for what felt like hours, past the Heartlands and Baldur's Gate, to a city he did not recognise built along the River Chionthar — [[scornubel|Scornubel]], he thought. The poor city was under siege: a massive force of [[amnians|Amnians]] and Calishites across the river, with northern forces from Baldur's Gate defending. Farther upriver, a fleet of black ships flew the crimson colours of the [[red-wizards-of-thay|Red Wizards of Thay]], two Green Dragons circling above.

The vision descended among the Calishite ranks and showed [[gundren-rockseeker|Gundren]] what he had been looking for: Efreeti Chain. The legendary armour was currently being worn — by the Efreeti themselves. The fiery Barons led the Calishite host, seven feet tall and crimson-skinned, built like weightlifters, their arms and armour wrought of brass, much of it glowing red-hot or smouldering.

[[aliyah-faen-tlabbar|Patches]] also scried on her mother and watched her family conspiring to capture her — setting bait with a convoy to Ched Nasad led by her two eldest sisters. And [[gundren-rockseeker|Gundren]], in a separate vision, witnessed Modrons constructing a [[delzoun-instant-fortress|Delzoun Instant Fortress]] inside a large Modron structure.

## Creation and Entropy

In the chamber of the Past, [[feanon-serinde|Feanon's]] mind raced to the beginning of the multiverse itself. Though much was beyond comprehension, he was learned enough — and possessed enough psionic power — to grasp what the library showed him.

He witnessed an over-deity shaping the universe, watching over it, letting it grow. Fragments of its power broke off and changed, creating deities, races, and entities. Life coalesced from raw energy. A particularly brilliant entity was birthed — Corellon, perhaps, the Lord of the Elves — and [[feanon-serinde|Feanon]] felt the creation resonate within him. As more beings emerged and the universe took on a life of its own, the raw energies grew chaotic. The over-deity took them and broke off a piece of itself to govern and order them, creating the first Weave of Magic and the first god of magic.

Ever curious, [[feanon-serinde|Feanon]] sent his questions into the far future, to the end of all things. It ended in silence and ice. A frozen rock — the last planet. Nautiloid Illithid vessels barely survived beneath the ice, huddled near the core where some heat remained. As the heat death of the universe reached inward to snuff out what little life was left, the [[illithid-faction|Illithids]] activated their only hope. The device scattered them across time and space as the last planet died.

## Artefacts of Selune and the Last Word

[[cirrus-al-kayan|Cirrus]] searched the Present for artefacts of [[selune|Selune]]. The first vision carried him to the elven island of [[evermeet|Evermeet]], to a mountain temple where an elven cleric wore armour that shone like luminous fish scales, his white silk robes dull by comparison. The vision raced beneath the waves and into the [[underdark|Underdark's]] darkness, stopping at a subterranean river where an ancient skeleton lay beneath a large boulder, its skull wearing a silver circlet set with a single white gemstone. He caught a glimpse of the surface — several hundred miles southeast of [[selunia|Selunia]]. The sensor rushed north to [[silverymoon|Silverymoon]], into the temple of [[selune|Selune]], where ornate white robes trimmed in gold rested upon a mannequin at the heart of a great shrine. As the vision faded, a play of colour shimmered across them like an aurora borealis.

Exhausted but driven, [[cirrus-al-kayan|Cirrus]] turned his mind to [[the-last-word|the Last Word]] — the fragments of power that could help them defeat [[orcus|Orcus]] and [[atropos|Atropos]].

The first vision took him to the foot of a great mountain on the shores of a twilight lake reflecting millions of stars. Great winged humanoids and ethereal lights flew among the skies, and classical structures of gleaming marble and starlight shone among ancient trees. Celestia. The vision entered a massive fortress of crystal and precious metals, gliding down glittering hallways lined with ornate armour and beautiful works of art, until the architecture opened into a vast treasure chamber. Atop the hoard rested the most magnificent dragon [[cirrus-al-kayan|Cirrus]] had ever seen — [[bahamut|Bahamut]], the King of Dragons — reading from a great tome set upon a stand of gold. The Lord of Dragons looked up, directly at the sensor, and smiled.

The vision shifted. A great pyramid of black stone and bone under a red-tinged sky, swirling clouds, and great deluges of raining blood. The [[abyss|Abyss]]. [[cirrus-al-kayan|Cirrus]] recognized the Necropolis of Everlost — the seat of [[orcus|Orcus]]. A vast city sprawled beneath the pyramid throne, its sides flowing with rivers of freezing blood, layer upon waxy layer. At the base roiled a host of undeath, and four Hierophants of Annihilation — the greatest Balors of [[orcus|Orcus]] — guarded the Obsidian Blood Throne. Just as the vision faded, he spotted it: a glowing point of warmth amid the cold, the fragment of [[the-last-word|the Word]] shining atop [[orcus|Orcus']] throne.

A third vision was murky, as if seen through deep water. The bottom of a great pit, oozing with decay, bones littering the ground. Something slithered. A vast section of the corruption moved, and through it the light of [[the-last-word|the Word]] shone faintly.

The last was even more indistinct — a wall of blue-glowing crystal, apparently wrought by mortal hands, above a worked metal-plated floor. Then nothing.

## Council in the Library

Sixteen hours had passed. Exhausted beyond measure, the group reconvened in the foyer, settling into the comfortable furnishings near the roaring firepit. Nearby, two of the giant custodians ate and talked. Wanting privacy, the group touched the mineral veins in the table and were enclosed in a private room. They summoned food, a second [[tarkathee-daru|Tark]] to enjoy it with, and began to compare notes.

The picture was not hopeless. [[cirrus-al-kayan|Cirrus]] argued that with [[lolth|Lolth]] possibly assaulting [[orcus|Orcus']] layer of the [[abyss|Abyss]] while he was away, the time was ripe to steal the fragment of [[the-last-word|the Word]] from Everlost. They would need more research, as the place did not seem particularly survivable for mortals — [[gundren-rockseeker|Gundren]] thought he could modify the Masks of Clear Breath to help. But the indistinct pit in [[cirrus-al-kayan|Cirrus']] vision bore similarities to [[lady-faye|Lady Faye's]] lead, and they resolved to investigate further.

Exhausted, they wrapped up their meals and retired for the night.
