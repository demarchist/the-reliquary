## The Iridescent Crystals

The Gauth lay where it had fallen, ichor pooling beneath its tentacles. Crystals had spilled from its grip across the stone floor of the chamber, scattered like a child's marbles — clear, faintly luminescent, refracting the bioluminescent fungal light into shifting prismatic colour.

[[aliyah-faen-tlabbar|Patches]] crouched beside the nearest shard and reached out a gloved hand.

The cold hit her before her fingertips made contact. Not the cold of stone in winter, not even the cold of metal at altitude — something deeper, a leaching wrongness that pulled heat from her hand the way a wound pulled blood. She drew back. The leather of her glove had crusted at the tip with a brittle frost.

[[horatio-von-bumidere|Buumi]] tried with a bare finger and nearly lost the skin of it. The crystal did not simply burn — it took. His flesh adhered to the iridescent facet as though welded, and when he wrenched his hand back, he left a thin disc of skin behind. The wound was bloodless. The cold had cauterised it instantly.

Worse, the crystal was *growing*. The shard he had touched extended a hair-thin filament toward his wrist, reaching, and the discarded fragments on the floor were doing the same — slowly, almost imperceptibly, but unmistakably. Wherever flesh met crystal, the crystal began to encompass it.

[[jack-mayer-johnson-mathews|JmJm]] cast Mage Hand and lifted three of the shards into a [[bag-of-holding|Bag of Holding]] without further comment. Whatever the things were, they were going somewhere they could not be touched until someone with more knowledge could examine them safely.

## The Sleeping Dreamer

While the others worked, Patches slipped ahead alone to scout.

She moved through a side passage and into a small alcove cut from the crystal-veined stone. A man lay on his side in the centre of the room — human or something that had once been human, dressed in the tattered remains of what looked like miner's clothing. He was deeply asleep. His chest rose and fell with the slow rhythm of profound dreaming.

Reality bent around him.

The walls of the alcove rippled outward in slow concentric waves, like a stone dropped into still water — except the stone was the man, and the water was the world. The flagstones beneath him stretched and contracted in time with his breathing. Crystal spurs grew from the floor at the edges of his sleeping body, then withdrew, then grew again somewhere else. The ceiling above him sagged half a foot lower than the rest of the room and held there, defying gravity by sheer weight of dream.

His head was wrong. Beneath his hair, eyeballs shifted — moving across the surface of his scalp like beads under cloth, rolling around to the back of his head and forward again. Patches counted at least a dozen of them before she stopped counting.

She did not wake him. She did not breathe louder than she had to. She backed slowly out of the alcove, set a tripwire across the threshold rigged to a bag of explosives, and returned to the group. Whatever the thing was, it could wait. If it followed them down, the trap would announce itself loudly enough that they would have warning.

## The Crystal Cathedral

The party pressed deeper.

The tunnel widened into a vast chamber threaded with veins of crystal and mineral deposits like marbled rock — a geode opened up to the size of a cathedral. Mining gear lay scattered across the floor: pickaxes, abandoned helmets, a wheelbarrow on its side with its cargo of rock spilled across the stone. There was no sign of the miners themselves.

JmJm urged [[steden|Steden]] up the curving wall and onto the cavern's vaulted ceiling — the Nightmare's hooves striking sparks from the crystal — and from that vantage attempted to slip into the [[ethereal-plane|Ethereal Plane]] for a clearer view.

The veil refused him.

He pressed against it and felt only resistance — a smooth, unyielding pressure where there should have been the familiar grey mist. The Ethereal was sealed off from this place, walled away by whatever influence saturated the crystal-ridden depths. JmJm withdrew, frowning, and brought Steden back down to the chamber floor.

Then, all around them, the eyes opened.

Dozens of lids flicked back along the walls of the cavern, embedded in the crystal-veined stone — wet, lidded orbs that swivelled in unison to fix on the intruders. The group raised weapons. Bows nocked. Patches dropped into a crouch, blades drawn.

[[cirrus-al-kayan|Cirrus]] raised a hand.

"Greetings," he called out into the watching dark, his voice carrying with the easy resonance of a man who genuinely believed every aberration in the room would want to hear him out. "We come bearing words for the master of this place."

The eyes did not fire. They watched.

Movement caught their attention down a far tunnel — something approaching from the deeper passages, slow and bobbing.

## The Gauth Embassy

Two Gauth beholderkin drifted into the chamber.

The lead creature was the larger of the two, its central eye glittering with curiosity and its crown of stalks weaving with each word. It addressed the group in a voice like polished glass — formal, almost courtly, expressing genuine pleasure at meeting visitors who had behaved with such admirable restraint. Would the honoured guests care to be escorted to the master? He would so enjoy meeting them.

The second Gauth said nothing pleasant. It hung back behind its companion, eye fixed on the group with naked hunger, lips peeled back from too many teeth. Whatever fiction of hospitality the first creature was performing, the second clearly considered it a thin sheet of paper between the visitors and the meal.

The party agreed to the escort. Patches did not.

While the others followed the two Gauth deeper into the mine, Patches melted into shadow and tracked them stealthily — fifty paces behind, eye on the rear of the column, hand near her bandolier of poisons. If the welcome turned, she would not be where the welcomers expected her to be.

## The Netherese City Beneath

The route descended through a vast crystal shaft.

What had been mine tunnels gave way, gradually and then all at once, to *architecture*. Pillars rose along the shaft's walls — first as suggestions in the rock, then as defined columns, then as elaborately fluted shafts of milky crystal capped with the hexagonal motifs of an empire two thousand years dead. Niches had been carved at regular intervals to hold statuary. Lintels arched over passageways no humanoid hand had cut. The crystals of the deep mine were not merely growing — they were *building*, repeating the geometries of an ancient civilisation as though the stone itself remembered.

[[cirrus-al-kayan|Cirrus]] recognised the style. So did the Gauth, who confirmed it with audible pride: *Netherese*.

At the bottom of the shaft the party emerged into a cavernous space the size of a city plaza. Crystal archways spanned the chamber. Crystal facades flanked a central avenue — windows, balconies, doorways carved into faces of crystal, an entire street of buildings preserved beneath the earth. It was as though a Netherese enclave had crashed here in some forgotten age and the mountain had digested it slowly over centuries, replacing every stone with crystalline equivalent until the ruin was perfectly remembered in glass.

[[cassian|Cassian]] — riding in formation behind the lead Gauth — said something to the smaller of the two beholderkin under his breath. The exact words did not survive the moment. The substance of them was an insult.

The smaller Gauth reacted instantly. A beam lanced from one of its eyestalks. Cassian dove sideways, the bolt missing him by inches, and the party drew steel.

The larger Gauth recoiled in horror — not at the violence, but at the *breach of protocol*. These were guests of the master. To strike at them was to strike at hospitality itself. He hovered uncertainly as his sibling pressed the attack alone, made no move to assist, and watched in growing distress as the group cut his brother apart in three seconds. The lesser Gauth fell from the air and lay still on the crystal flagstones.

The eyes embedded in the walls did not act. They simply watched, recording, neutral. The master, it seemed, valued protocol — and the dead Gauth had broken it.

The remaining Gauth gathered itself with what might have been a sigh and gestured for the group to follow. The breach had been resolved. The audience could continue.

## The False Portal

Ahead of them, at the far end of the crystal avenue, a vast portal blazed.

It was a circle of swirling energies twenty feet across, crackling with the chromatic violence of planar transit — a maw of light and motion that could have led anywhere. The group slowed. JmJm, with the practiced eye of a stage performer, studied the effect for a half-breath and announced it for what it was.

Illusion.

The portal was not a portal at all. It was a door, dressed up in the costume of a portal. The Elder Orb's sense of theatre, perhaps, or his sense of humour. Either way, the group walked through the lightshow as though pushing through a curtain — and entered the lair.

## Audience with the Elder Orb

The chamber was enormous.

A grand circular floor opened beneath a domed ceiling of crystal facets, with a raised balcony wrapping the back third of the room. The walls were a staggered montage of crystal panels and angled mirrors, casting reflections of the party at every height and depth. Bioluminescence spilled from veins in the walls in pulses that quickened when the [[elder-orb|Elder Orb]] spoke, dimmed when it paused.

The creature itself floated at the room's centre.

It was massive — a sphere of pink, fleshy folds the size of a small wagon, sagging and wobbling as it hovered, the slack skin pendulous and stretched. From its body sprouted long, fingerlike eyestalks, jointed twice along their lengths, ending in eyes that were each a perfect black sphere — no whites, no irises, no pupils, only ink. Its central eye dominated its lower half, lidded in flesh that resembled a vast frog's mouth, and beneath that mouth were *human teeth*. White, even, set in pink gums. Smiling.

It welcomed them with apparent warmth.

The group, almost as one, understood three things in quick succession. The first was that the warmth was a mask. The second was that the creature regarded them as one regards an interesting plate of food at a banquet — the precise moment to lift the fork was the only question. The third was that all five of its smaller companions — the Gauth, three Spectators with their flat eye-bodies, and the dreaming sentinels behind the mirrors — had positioned themselves at the perimeter of the chamber while the conversation occupied the centre.

Cirrus made eye contact with the Elder Orb. The Orb made eye contact back. Something shifted in the air between them.

"You are *interesting*," the Orb purred, in a voice like a coil of wet rope dragged across glass. "Tiefling. Sorcerer. Devotee of *moons*. Tell me about yourself."

Cirrus told it. Cirrus would have told a brick wall about himself. The Orb listened, asked questions — clever ones, probing ones — and gradually offered fragments of its own story in exchange.

It had not been born in these mines.

It had come from a Netherese ruin trapped in ancient ice, far to the north — an enclave that had crashed in the fall of [[netherese|Netheril]] and been preserved beneath the glaciers ever since. It had lived there for centuries, drinking the dormant magic of the ruin, becoming what it now was. And then a *thing* had come — a thing of bone and old fury, a spellcaster long past its mortal death, with the patience of stone and an army at its back. The thing had driven the Orb out. The ruin and its magic were now the spellcaster's. The Orb had fled south, finding the disused dwarven mines beneath [[und|Und]] and a smaller node of Netherese power to feed upon — humbled, displaced, but alive.

Buumi, very quietly, asked the Elder Orb whether the spellcaster's name had been [[zhengyi|Zhengyi]].

The eye stalks all turned. Every black orb fixed on Buumi at once.

"Yes," said the Elder Orb. "That was him."

The room held still. Outside, beyond the chamber's mirrored walls, the dreaming eyes blinked open.

The Orb smiled, all those human teeth showing. "I see we share an enemy. How fortunate. Now — please. Set down your weapons and let us *discuss*."

The group attacked.

## The Battle

The Elder Orb had been waiting for it.

Cassian and Buumi broke right toward the nearest two Spectators — the flat, four-armed beholderkin hovering like watchful discs at the chamber's edge. Buumi closed the distance in two strides and hammered the first into the floor with his greatsword. Cassian's bow sang twice, the arrows finding the second Spectator's central eye, and the creature collapsed in a heap of deflated flesh before it could bring a single eyestalk to bear.

Cirrus hurled Banish at the Elder Orb. The spell struck the central eye and *slid off* — the cone of the great eye's antimagic gaze had already turned toward him, and the enchantment dissolved in midair. He pivoted and threw the Banish at the larger Gauth instead, and the courtly beholderkin vanished with a startled chirp into a momentary planar pocket.

The Elder Orb turned its central eye sweepingly across the chamber. Wherever the cone fell, magic *ceased*. Active enchantments guttered. Buumi's blessing wavered. Patches' boots — enchanted to muffle her footsteps — made a sudden audible scuff before she danced back out of the cone.

Then the eyestalks fired.

A dozen rays lanced from the creature in a single rolling barrage. Beams struck the floor and ripped a chunk of crystal flooring upward into the air, then flung it telekinetically across the chamber. Other rays caught Buumi with a wave of overwhelming dread that froze him mid-charge; another caught Patches with a slow petrifying ray that crawled grey across her shoulder before she shrugged it off. Massive damage rolled through the group like a wave breaking.

JmJm answered.

He spurred Steden into a charge across the open floor, the Nightmare's hooves trailing fire across the crystal, and as he closed he drew the [[inkblade|Inkblade]] — the singing weapon that had hung at his hip since long before any of his current companions had known him. The blade keened on the air, a tuning-fork hum that cut through the Orb's ambient warping. JmJm vaulted from the saddle, struck the great central body twice, and tore long ragged wounds across the slack pink flesh.

The Orb screamed and turned.

A barrage of eyebeams struck Steden centre-mass.

The Nightmare's fiery hide crumbled inward. His legs buckled. His form began to dissolve, dragged back toward the [[the-nine-hells|Nine Hells]] from which he came — and JmJm, mid-leap, suddenly had no mount beneath him.

He fell thirty feet.

Halfway down, his hand closed around something at his belt.

The glove.

The worn white leather thing he had carried at his belt for as long as any of his current companions had known him — the [[mysterious-glove|Mysterious Glove From His Past]], as he had been calling it for years with the deadpan precision of a man who had decided this was what the bit was. He pulled it onto his right hand in the long second of the fall, and the air beside him *thickened* — coalescing into a translucent disembodied hand the size of a horse, ten fingers spread wide. The Arcane Hand caught him a foot above the floor, lifted him, and set him down upright as though catching a child off a wall. JmJm landed on his feet without so much as a stumble.

He did not look at the glove. He did not look at the place where Steden had been. He drew the Inkblade across his palm to renew its song and turned back to the fight.

## Eclipse and the Stealthy Pair

Patches and Cassian worked the perimeter — the rogue and the ranger, both moving in and out of cover behind crystal pillars and behind chunks of the floor that the Elder Orb's telekinesis kept ripping up and spinning around the chamber.

Arrows and bolts lanced across the room in a steady, lethal rhythm. They were not dramatic shots — they were efficient ones, working in steady erosion at the Spectators that remained, peeling off another, and another, while the Orb's attention was fixed on the louder threats.

When an opening came, Patches took it.

She stepped out of cover, planted her feet, and called Steelwind.

Reality blurred around her. She vanished — and reappeared at the Elder Orb's flank, [[eclipse|Eclipse]] already in motion. The obsidian blade swept through the pink flesh of the great body, opening a clean, bloodless cut along one whole side. Patches teleported again — a second strike from above, the blade carving across the shoulder of the great central eye. A third — into the underside, angling up toward the cluster of stalks. Eclipse cut where Eclipse always cut. The sentient blade *did* damage where ordinary steel could not.

But the *enchantments* on her follow-up attacks died on contact.

She had bound divine fire to her strikes — a moonfire blessing from Eilistraee for engagements precisely like this one. The fire did not catch. It simply was not there. The Elder Orb's body shrugged off any spell that targeted it directly the way oilcloth shrugged off rain. The cuts were real. The magic that should have followed the cuts was nothing.

Patches landed, rolled, and disappeared back into cover. Cassian was already drawing the next arrow.

The Elder Orb retaliated. A death ray — pure necrotic black — lanced from its largest stalk and struck JmJm full in the chest. The bard staggered, all colour draining from him, and only the tightening of his jaw kept him from going down. Another beam tore at the fabric of reality and *dragged* a fresh Spectator into being from a fold of spacetime, the new aberration popping into the room with an audible whump and immediately opening fire.

## The Beholder's Mirror

Cirrus took a step back from the chaos and lifted both arms.

He spoke to Selûne — not aloud, not in the room, but somewhere behind his eyes — and called True Polymorph.

The transformation was instantaneous and grotesque.

His body inflated. His skin turned a smooth, fleshy pink. His face withdrew and his eyestalks erupted from his scalp in a writhing crown. His central eye, vast and lidded, opened in the place where his chest had been. Within seconds Cirrus was no longer Cirrus — he was a Beholder, a perfect mirror of the Elder Orb, hovering in the air across from his counterpart with all the stalk-eyes weaving and ready.

The Elder Orb *howled*.

The sound was not pain. It was *insult*. That a lowly mortal — a *tiefling*, a *worshipper of a moon* — would dare to assume the form of an Elder, would dare to *imitate the perfect shape*, was an obscenity beyond the violence. The Elder Orb's eyestalks all snapped toward Cirrus at once. Every other foe in the chamber, for that crucial half-second, ceased to exist.

Cirrus opened his central eye and turned his own antimagic cone toward the Elder Orb.

What followed was a dogfight.

Two great fleshy spheres tilted and drifted around each other in mid-air, each desperately working to keep its central eye pointed at the other, each desperately working to keep itself out of the other's antimagic cone. The Elder Orb fired eye rays from the stalks not occluded by Cirrus's cone. Cirrus fired eye rays of his own — Beholder eye rays, conjured into being by his polymorphed body. Beams crossed the chamber in tangled arcs. A telekinetic ray ripped a chunk of the balcony loose and hurled it at Cirrus, who slewed sideways and let it shatter against a mirror that disintegrated into glittering dust.

The Elder Orb tried to reposition. Cirrus *tracked it*. Wherever the great pink body drifted, Cirrus drifted with it, his cone fixed on the central eye, denying it the ability to negate the spells of his companions, denying it the ability to slough off the damage.

JmJm closed in beneath them. The Inkblade rose and fell. Patches' Eclipse cut another wound across the back. Cassian's bow sang. Buumi's hammer rang against the underside of the great body and something inside it cracked.

The Elder Orb began to list.

It tried, in its last seconds, to bring its central eye onto Cirrus and end the impertinence — and Cirrus held the cone, jaw of his enormous central mouth set, pinning the creature like a fisherman pinning a marlin. The combined damage of the four mortals on the floor and the one polymorphed lunatic hovering in the air took its toll.

The Elder Orb sagged. Its stalks went slack. Its central eye filmed over.

It dropped to the chamber floor with a wet, heavy sound, and lay still.

## The Loot

The reality-warping pressure in the room receded immediately. The mirrors stopped reflecting things they had not been pointed at. The crystals stopped growing. The dreaming eyes in the walls slid closed and did not reopen.

Cirrus shifted back into his own body with a small private smile and brushed nonexistent dust from his coat.

The group approached the corpse. There was loot to take.

The eyestalks were the most valuable harvest — eight of them salvageable, each requiring a careful extraction at the joint to preserve the magic locked in the orb. Patches and Cassian worked the cuts. The central eye was unsalvageable, ruined by Cirrus's last antimagic punch — the very thing that had let them kill the creature had also rendered the most valuable component inert. They took what they could. A swatch of [[elder-orb-hide|Elder Orb hide]] came free in a long strip across the underside, thick and rubbery and unmistakably enchanted, and went into the [[bag-of-holding|Bag of Holding]] for later use.

Tucked into a small ornate chest beneath the balcony, they found the Orb's portable hoard: a [[potion-of-supreme-healing|Potion of Supreme Healing]], a [[scroll-of-demiplane|Scroll of Demiplane]], and an [[instrument-of-the-bards|Instrument of the Bards]] — which JmJm picked up and tucked under one arm without breaking eye contact with anyone.

It was, all things considered, a small treasure for an Elder Orb.

The conversation about that began as they searched the rest of the lair.

Elder Orbs hoarded across centuries. This one had been displaced — driven out of his original home, the Netherese ruin in the ice. Whatever the Orb had carried with him was the rump of his treasury. The bulk of his wealth, his accumulated centuries of magic and craft, was wherever he had been driven *from* — and the thing that had driven him from it now possessed it.

[[zhengyi|Zhengyi]] had the rest. Or the lair sat somewhere in the deep north, under glaciers, waiting for whoever could find it. Either possibility was useful. Both possibilities pointed the same direction.

The group stood for a long moment in the silent chamber.

The lair had lost its glow. The crystals creaked once as they settled, and then stopped. The bioluminescence in the walls dimmed by half and held there, faded to a dull amber. The stench of the corpses began to seep into the still air.

The Elder Orb of Und was dead. The glaciers of the north held a Netherese ruin that had once been his. And a lich was sitting in it.

The group gathered their loot and turned for the surface.
