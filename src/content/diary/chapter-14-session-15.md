## The Good Soul

[[Aliyah Faen Tlabbar|Patches]] contacted [[Lady Faye]], offering magical goods for trade. [[Feanon Serinde|Feanon]] returned to his tower while [[Jack Mayer-Johnson Mathews|JmJm]], Patches, and [[Cirrus Al-Kayan|Cirrus]] headed to the [[Fane of Selune|Fane]] to question the headless man.

[[Gundren Rockseeker|Gundren]], meanwhile, had been investigating the Modrons that had appeared around the Barony. Since the dragon attack, the things had been performing routine maintenance unbidden and seemed to follow simple tasks by reading his mind. He put them to work in the Foundry, but noticed they had begun assembling some kind of mechanical contraption in the middle of the castle courtyard. The lads were taking bets.

At the Fane, a ritual of reincarnation began — the man had no head, so they would simply grow him a new body. JmJm stripped naked in the ritual circle and anointed himself and the corpse with ritual oils. The body disintegrated and mingled with the unguents, but did not form anew. The soul did not wish to return.

Calling upon divine intervention, Cirrus and JmJm forced the spirit to answer five questions — a gift from [[Selune]].

The dead man's name was Letji. His master was Samonosuke, a Warlock of Ceregorach. He had been sent to spy on Cirrus with presumably murderous intent.

Cirrus did not ask his final question. Instead, he offered the soul to [[Crisserei]] the Planetar, to bring to Selune — to save this dark soul.

The Planetar shook her head. Cirrus did not understand.

Selune had not *forced* this soul to answer. It had *chosen* to when petitioned by the goddess. This was the soul of a good person, not an evildoer.

Cirrus was aghast. He demanded JmJm end the spell. He was shaken to his core.

Who was this man, truly?

## The City of Sails

The group questioned their security. Patches had one lead on the assassination contract against her: a fixer in [[Luskan]] named Morik the Rogue. JmJm pointed out that [[Doc]] could Wind Walk them there.

Patches, Cirrus, Gundren, JmJm, and Doc materialised on a hillock overlooking the city at nightfall.

Luskan had seen far better days. The exterior wall was in dire disrepair, with collapsed sections shored up by wooden scaffolding. Stone structures within had crumbled, wooden shanties propping up among the wreckage. The docks dominated the city — hundreds of wharves and piers jutting into a crowded bay of countless ships, lit by thousands of torches and lanterns. The sound of revelry drifted up. Ruffians manned the main gate, partying.

Patches had been here before. She laid out what she knew: the pirate lords and their Ships ran the streets. The ruling Ship was Ship Kurth. [[Jarlaxle]] was somehow in control of it. [[Bregan D'Aerthe]]'s headquarters was *One Eyed Jacks*, along the main wharf at the top of Carnival Hill. Drow were not uncommon. The [[Zhentarim]] ran a field office here, and many gangs were Zhent-affiliated.

They paid their way through the gate. Their charisma secured an escort through the streets. The Ships were back from mercenary work in the war — a lull in the fighting — and the city was celebrating. They passed the ruined Host Tower of the Arcane, a century defunct.

Two young ruffians tried their luck when JmJm openly flaunted his wealth, slipping a ring from his finger. Patches pickpocketed them right back and returned the ring. When the youth discovered his prize was gone, JmJm pounced, forcing the boy at sword-point to serve as their chauffeur.

## One Eyed Jacks

The finest and largest intact building they had seen — One Eyed Jacks was packed with ruffians of every sort. Drinking, gambling, and violence in equal measure.

Patches spotted two Drow she recognised from her time in Bregan D'Aerthe — one at the bar, one gambling. She approached the one at the table. His body language immediately went rigid.

Rude words and thinly veiled barbs were exchanged. Patches demanded satisfaction and threw a wooden dagger on the table. The Drow sneered. The whole table rose, moving to the back alley. The Drow began to whistle a repetitive tune.

With a flash of insight, Patches realised where she knew this man. Not from Bregan D'Aerthe — from her own noble house. One of her mother's elite warriors.

The ruffians pulled weapons. Other Dark Elves leapt from the shadows, assaulting the group with poisoned darts. Cirrus and JmJm were peppered and staggered with exhaustion before finding a second wind. [[Arkto]] and [[Steden]] were summoned. JmJm pulled on a mysterious glove from his past.

The battle was short and furious. The group left only one survivor — a Drow soldier.

The noise had not gone unnoticed. Movement echoed from streets all around.

The group looked at the bloodstained bodies and wondered just what they had gotten themselves into.
