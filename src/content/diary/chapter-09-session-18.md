The group retired to the tavern and rested in the real world — for the first time in [[blingdenstone|Blingdenstone]], no one was affected by the abyssal energies. Everyone slept soundly.

The next morning, [[gurnik|Gurnik]] and Chief Diggermattock met them as [[feanon-serinde|Feanon]] prepared to teleport the party home. The Deep Gnomes would recall their troops and spend a week summoning Earth Elementals. In seven days the group would return, and together they would launch an assault north to push the oozes — and potentially the Demon Lord [[juiblex|Juiblex]] — out of the city. [[snorbul|Snorbul]] and [[thorna|Thorna]] had gone north to parley with the wererat warren; [[cirrus-al-kayan|Cirrus]] mentioned his own wererat acolytes and promised to speak with both groups.

The group stepped into the teleportation circle and vanished in a flash of arcane brilliance.

## Homecoming

They appeared without incident at [[feanons-tower|Feanon's Tower]]. The oppressive weight of the [[underdark|Underdark]]'s abyssal energies lifted immediately, replaced by the brisk spring morning air of northern [[faerun|Faerûn]]. The sun was rising, casting the Barony in brilliant light — the towers and walls of the [[keep-of-thunder-bluff|keep]] across the valley gleaming.

Feanon peeled off into his laboratory. Everyone else headed to the boats and across the lake. [[tarkathee-daru|Tark]] sprinted across the water toward his treehouse.

They noticed it quickly: more guards than usual, many of them strangers. A mercenary company called [[bohds-band|Bohd's Band]] had been hired by [[silverymoon|Silverymoon]] to shore up the Barony's defences.

## The Fane and the Far Realm

[[cirrus-al-kayan|Cirrus]] made it to the [[fane-of-selune|Fane]] and spoke with [[topsy-and-turvy|Topsy and Turvy]]. He asked if they were still lonely and mentioned bringing the wererats of Blingdenstone to the Barony. The two were unsure but wished to at least meet their kin. Cirrus agreed and retired to his [[sanctum-of-krasus|Sanctum]] to pray.

That night he used the [[well-of-many-worlds|Well of Many Worlds]], opening a portal to the [[ethereal-plane|Ethereal Plane]]. Uninterested, he closed it and returned to meditation.

## Bohd and Miranda

[[aliyah-faen-tlabbar|Patches]] arrived at [[the-establishment|the Establishment]] and received a report from [[pickles|Mr. Pickles]]. Two of the mercenary leaders were currently on the premises: [[bohd|Bohd]] himself and [[miranda|Miranda]], a Priestess of Sune.

Using the wall tunnels, Patches eavesdropped on Bohd — a half-orc with a mane of crimson hair, red and gold armour, and a massive battleaxe. He was telling a story competently enough, but Patches had no patience for bravado and lost interest quickly.

She headed upstairs to find Miranda practically moving in, with several trunks being hauled by villagers. The priestess was a human woman in her twenties — sharp, thin features, dark complexion, shockingly bright eyes, and face-paint depicting sigils of Sune. She wore an elegant gown with a large jewelled dagger on one hip and a prayer book on the other.

Miranda explained her position: she served as Bohd's Band's warpriest, performing healing magic in the field. The rest of the time, she encouraged passion in all its forms — including escort services for the wealthy, the money from which was funnelled to less fortunate artists and craftspeople. Anyone with a passion to be nurtured.

Patches said she would think it over — and took Miranda's five hundred gold weekly rent.

## The Manufactory

[[gundren-rockseeker|Gundren]] headed to the [[rockseeker-manufactory|Manufactory]] to show his brothers the [[chardalyn|Chardalyn]] resonant bells. They were suitably impressed and mentioned the mercenary band's company of Dwarven Sappers. Gundren invited Bohd to training and sent for the sappers. All the requested experts from [[sundabar|Sundabar]] had arrived and nearly finished setting up their workstations.

## The Week

With the weapons master finally arrived from [[silverymoon|Silverymoon]], Patches and Gundren spent the week training under [[master-koh|Master Koh]], a High Elf.

[[cirrus-al-kayan|Cirrus]] continued opening portal after portal with the [[well-of-many-worlds|Well of Many Worlds]], desperate to find the plane where [[selune|Selûne]] resided. He got the [[ethereal-plane|Ethereal]] several more times, one to Gehenna, another to the Outer Gates of Reality — which he closed immediately — until something intrigued him.

A rift of white light flooded into the [[sanctum-of-krasus|Sanctum]]. Through it: an endless expanse of white fog and flat crystal ground. Great hunched figures were visible in the distance. Cirrus stepped through and closed the portal behind him.

The silence was eerie. It smelled of nothing. Water — or something water-like — coalesced on the ground and rained upward. Cirrus walked for twenty minutes toward the nearest figure before catching movement in his peripheral vision. He looked down — and in the reflections on the crystal ground, the hunched figures were moving toward his reflection, surrounding him in a ring. They appeared to be made of writhing masses of rubbery tendrils.

One extended an appendage toward him.

Cirrus Planeshifted away. Only back in reality did the true foreignness of the place hit him. Unnerved, he went to [[beryan|Beryan]] the Sage. She had never heard of such a place, but that itself was telling — it might indicate the Far Realm, a place outside of reality entirely. She added that someone the group had been researching had visited it before: Abraxim, the Elven archmage from the Chardalyn mine ruins. He had traversed the Outer Gates and returned infected — an Elder Evil called Halashnar, the Pallid Prince, lodged in his mind. It had taken Sehanine Moonbow, an aspect of [[selune|Selûne]], to drive the creature from the world and cause Abraxim to flee.

---

[[snorbul|Snorbul]] and [[thorna|Thorna]] were teleported back and reported that negotiations between the Deep Gnomes and wererats had begun, though the formal meeting awaited the group's return. The northern caverns were confirmed infested with oozes and ooze-origin demons.

## Sparring

On the third day, [[bohd|Bohd]] joined the training grounds at [[gundren-rockseeker|Gundren]]'s request. [[aliyah-faen-tlabbar|Patches]] invited him to step into the arena.

In a flurry of movement, Patches swept Bohd to the ground and slammed him with her weapon. He roared, stood, and came swinging with his practice axe — good enough to glance her repeatedly, though never landing solidly. Patches tripped him again. He came back harder. They went back and forth for nearly a minute until Patches tried for a third trip. This time Bohd leapt the strike — but took a pommel to the face. He swung back; she riposted and laid him out, bloodied and breathing hard.

He growled, grinned, and yielded. [[master-koh|Master Koh]] handed them both healing potions. Bohd was good — though no match for any of the Heralds. A solid addition to the [[faerun-defense-force|FDF]].

## Kim the Wyvern

Bohd headed to [[the-establishment|Over the Moon]] for lunch and ran into [[cirrus-al-kayan|Cirrus]]. Over drinks, Bohd mentioned his mount — a blood-drinker wyvern named Kim. Cirrus asked to see her.

The next morning, Cirrus arrived at the [[keep-of-thunder-bluff|keep]]'s courtyard. Bohd stood in a crimson suit, sword at hip, leading a sheep on a leash. Part of the copper roof of the [[temple-of-helm|Temple of Helm]] had opened into a makeshift aviary. Basking in the morning sun, its crimson feathers catching the light, was the wyvern.

Cirrus slaughtered the sheep. The massive reptilian creature roared, took flight, and glided down to devour the kill. It lapped up the blood as Bohd brushed and groomed it. A natural creature — no magic, just natural excellence. Cirrus thanked him. Bohd mentioned he could extract venom from Kim on request.

## The Jade Invitation

One evening, the front doors of Over the Moon swung open and in walked a bejewelled dwarf-lord flanked by two bodyguards. He spotted [[gundren-rockseeker|Gundren]] and strode forward — [[dogan|Dogan]] of Clan Battlehammer, Smith and Artificer.

They clasped hands. Gundren bowed slightly. Dogan explained he had invitations to deliver and handed over an ornate granite box. Inside, nestled in velvet, was a brick of jade carved and engraved with the runes of Clan Battlehammer, [[gauntlgrym|Gauntlgrym]], and the Delzoun Empire.

In one month, ten invitees were to meet in Gauntlgrym and present an invention, work of artifice, or enchantment to aid the northern nations in their war with [[amn|Amn]] and Thay. The winner would receive unfettered access to the forges of Gauntlgrym and the Dawn Titan Maegera.

Dogan eyed the [[thundercannon|Thundercannon]] and [[gwen|Gwen]], smiled, and said he looked forward to seeing what the [[rockseeker-manufactory|Rockseeker Manufactory]] would produce. Then he heard about the goblin fights and Dwarven blackjack downstairs and was led to the basement by Gundren — the Dwarven miners roaring their approval when informed their bar tab was being covered by the Dwarven Empire.

The rest of the week passed quietly. For the first time in months, the group could finally catch their breath.
