## The Stilled Crystal

They lingered in the chamber for a long time after the [[elder-orb|Elder Orb]] stopped speaking.

The crystals had ceased their slow geometric growth. The dreaming eyes in the walls were closed. The bioluminescence in the veins of the chamber held at a dull amber, no longer pulsing with the Orb's voice — and in that quieter light, the architecture of the place stood revealed as something the group had been too occupied to study while it was still alive.

The pillars were not stalactites that had grown into pillar-shape. They had been *carved* — and not by any hand of the dwarven miners or the Beholder's thralls. The lintels, the fluted shafts, the hexagonal capitals, the regular niches cut to hold statuary that had long since vanished or been replaced with raw crystal facets: every detail of it bore the architectural vocabulary of a single civilisation.

"Netherese," [[cirrus-al-kayan|Cirrus]] said, and [[feanon-serinde|Fëanon]] — who had been quiet for some time — finally raised his head and confirmed it.

Late Netherese. Not the proto-imperial work of the early centuries but the high decadence of the final era. The geometries, the proportions, the trailing scrollwork at the capital of each shaft — these were features of the last architectural movement of the Empire, the period of refinement that ended abruptly when [[karsus|Karsus]] reached for the goddess and the floating cities fell. Fëanon ran one gloved hand down the nearest column and listened to the silence of two thousand years of buried memory.

He spoke without turning.

"There was a city," he said, "christened by the Netherese mere months before the Fall. It was called Ythryn. The records of it are fragmentary. The ruins of it have never been found."

He let the word hang.

"Could it be trapped in the ice north of here?"

The group considered the implication in silence. The [[elder-orb|Elder Orb]] had told them where he had come from: a [[netherese|Netherese]] ruin preserved beneath ancient glaciers far to the north, where he had drunk dormant magic for centuries until [[zhengyi|Zhengyi]] arrived with an army and drove him south. The Orb had used the word *ruin* and given them no name. Now Fëanon offered one.

Ythryn. The lost city of the late Empire. A buried enclave under the ice. A lich sitting in it.

It made too much sense to be coincidence. None of them said so aloud.

## The Sleeping Dreamer Awakened

They climbed the long shaft back toward the surface.

On the way up, [[aliyah-faen-tlabbar|Patches]] paused at the side-alcove she had rigged with a tripwire and a bag of explosives on the descent. The dreaming man was still there. She held a finger to her lips, eased past her own trap, and looked in.

The man slept on. But the eyes on his scalp were gone. The flesh of his head was smooth and unbroken, and the room around him no longer rippled. The flagstones lay flat. The ceiling did not sag. Whatever the [[elder-orb|Orb]] had done to him — whatever dream-channel it had opened through his head — had closed with the Orb's death, and he had simply gone on sleeping.

She disarmed the trap and waved the others in.

[[horatio-von-bumidere|Buumi]] crouched beside the man and shook him gently. He groaned. His eyes — both of them, ordinary and unsplit — fluttered open. He stared at the ceiling for a moment without comprehension and then, slowly, sat up.

He had been asleep for weeks. He remembered nothing. He was, by his own immediate report, starving.

They walked him out of the mine.

Up the long shaft, through the silent crystal cathedral that had once watched them with a hundred eyes, past the dead Gauth, and into the daylight of the abandoned village of [[und|Und]]. The sun was high. The wind moved through empty doorways. The villagers had not yet returned from the hillside camps where they had fled when the Beholder's influence had crept down the valley.

The group sat the man on a bench in the village square, broke out rations from the [[bag-of-holding|Bag of Holding]], and watched him eat. [[jack-mayer-johnson-mathews|JmJm]] dispatched a runner down the hill with word that it was safe to come home.

Then they shouldered their packs and set their faces north.

## The Black Dragon's Work

The road climbed.

By midmorning they were navigating a small rockslide that had partially blocked the pass — fresh, the scree still tumbling in places, the air full of dust. A long, low roar rolled across the peaks somewhere to the east. It was the third such roar they had heard since they had crossed into the Galena Mountains, and none of the previous two had come from anything they could see.

Around midday the road brought them to a crossroads.

The pass climbed straight up and over the saddle of the mountain ahead — and atop the saddle, dominating the high notch like a black tooth, stood a ruined fortress of dark stone. The group stopped and looked at it for a long moment.

They had heard of this fortress. [[ironfang-keep|Ironfang Keep]] — a stronghold of the [[shar|Sharran]] worshippers, supposedly held by a small order of fanatics who answered to the Mistress of the Night. *Supposedly held* was the operative phrase. Whatever order had once been there was not there now. The fortress was a wreck. There was, as Patches observed flatly, *not much left to run*.

"I want to look at it," [[tarkathee-daru|Tark]] said.

He and Patches broke from the road and climbed the last switchback to the ruin while the others continued cautiously along the main pass below.

The destruction was *recent*. There was no smouldering, no smoke, no corpses they could find — but there was a heavy, sickly-sweet scent hanging over the melted stone, and the stone *had* melted. Long runnels of vitrified rock streaked the broken walls where something corrosive had washed across them and run downhill before cooling. Great rents had been torn through the curtain wall. Claw-marks the length of a man's forearm scored the inner courtyard. The damage was consistent with the breath of a Black Dragon, and no other source they could think of.

Patches sifted the rubble while Tark stood watch.

Among the loose stones at the foot of a half-collapsed tower, she found a single shard of scale — black, faceted, slightly curved, the size of a fingernail. She turned it in the light and pocketed it. They turned up a handful of other trinkets — a melted ingot, a scorched ring, a fragment of an iron holy symbol of [[shar|Shar]] half-buried in slag — but no bodies, no survivors, and no sign of who or what had been here when the dragon came.

They rejoined the group on the road and reported.

The conversation that followed was uncomfortable. White and grey dragons had been circling distant peaks for days now. A Black Dragon had torn this mountain fortress apart within recent days. And they were riding into a high valley to retrieve a stolen [[ashbringer|Sunsword]] from a paladin who did not know any of this was happening. The threads did not match. Something larger was moving across these mountains, and the group's quiet errand to recover a holy weapon was running directly into the middle of it.

They made haste.

## The Vale

By late afternoon the road brought them up onto a ridge and they looked down into [[frostmoore-vale|Frostmoore Vale]] for the first time.

It was beautiful.

A wooded valley nestled between two mountain shoulders, with a noble estate at its back and a cascade of small waterfalls running down through terraced gardens to a glittering lake. The slopes around the manor were cultivated — vineyards on the southern terraces, neat squares of vegetable gardens, an orchard near the lake. The house itself was modest by the standards of the city nobility, two stories of fine stone and slate, with no curtain wall and only a low garden hedge between the front door and the world. A larger, more ornate chapel building stood apart from the main house, its slate roof set with a Lathanderian sunburst, its windows narrow and tall.

The road switchbacked down off the ridge. The group descended.

They reached the estate as the sun began to kiss the mountaintops, and as they did, one of the distant white dragons turned in its high arc and began to drift toward the valley.

## At the Door

Movement flickered behind the estate windows as they approached. No one came out to meet them.

They walked up to the front door and knocked.

A peephole slid open at eye-height — a small grate of ornate metal protecting the face of a human man with the suspicious set of someone who had been instructed not to open the door. He looked them over once and asked, in the polite tone of a servant whose patience was already thin, what their business was.

JmJm took the lead.

*Travelling performers,* the Bard explained, with the warmth of a man who had once persuaded a marilith to take a seat. A *troupe*. Bringing entertainment to the noble lord's residence on the off-chance of a kind reception and an evening meal. The man behind the grate raised one eyebrow in a way that conveyed, without speaking, the precise degree to which he could *see* the swords and bows and enchanted armour the group was wearing.

He asked them to wait on the porch. He spoke with his lord. The grate slid shut.

The group turned to one another and began to bicker, quietly, in the porch's shade.

Patches did not bicker. She walked away around the corner, out of the line of any guard, and put her gloved hands against the wall.

She climbed.

The estate was solid — the windows were small, leaded, locked from inside, the shutters reinforced with iron strapping. Whoever had readied this house had readied it well. She inspected each window in turn from the second-floor eaves and was about to drop back down when something moved on the mountainside above the valley.

Something *fast*.

She froze, hung from the eaves, and watched.

Patches called down softly to the group, alerted them, and dropped from the wall in a roll. She did not wait for an answer. She broke into a run, up the slope above the estate, toward whatever it was the trees were hiding.

Behind her, the white dragon they had spotted earlier was now beelining directly for the valley. The remaining group banged on the front door a second time.

The peephole slid open. JmJm pointed urgently at the incoming dragon, at the commotion among the mountain trees, at the fading light.

The door opened.

Six well-equipped knights stood inside, in plate and silk surcoats of the [[crimson-dawn-knights|Order of the Crimson Dawn]]. The lead knight — taller than the rest, plate-clad to the gorget, with the look of a man who had served before he had pledged — stepped onto the porch with a brass spyglass in one hand and put it to his eye.

He introduced himself as Ishin.

He swept the spyglass across the slopes. He found Patches. He found the figure she was running toward. He held the glass on what he was seeing for several long seconds, then lowered it slowly, his face arranging itself into an expression the group could only describe as *thoroughly confused*.

## The Woman on the Mountainside

Up the slope, Patches had closed the distance.

The shape she had seen through the trees resolved into a woman. A *human* woman — voluptuous and sturdy, in a low-cut evening gown of fine silk that was now tattered and stained at the hem, sprinting barefoot down the mountainside at a speed no human had any business achieving. She was covered in bruises and small cuts. Part of her gleaming, curled hair was caked dark with blood. In one hand she carried a smaller figure — a toddler-sized form, draped limp, held by the back of the clothing like a suitcase.

Patches moved fast.

She ducked behind a tree, threw on the disguise of Nora — the older shopkeep face she sometimes wore in cities — and stepped out into the path of the running woman with both hands raised.

The woman did not slow. She *flung* herself sideways, a controlled tumble at sprinting speed, and her shoulder struck a fairly large tree trunk full force. The trunk *splintered*. The tree came down. The woman rolled twice across the leaf litter and was on her feet again before she had finished rolling, seemingly unharmed.

She rounded on the disguised rogue with a glare that was about to become a shout.

Then she sniffed the air.

The glare paused. Curiosity flickered through it. She tilted her head — not far, just a degree or two — and her nostrils flared once.

"You've been in my shop," she said.

Patches said nothing for a half-second. Then the woman lifted the limp figure she was carrying and Patches saw the rest of the situation: a small, foreign-looking [[gnome-of-the-vale|Gnome]], unconscious, her head lolling. Two puncture marks on her neck, oozing slowly, the wounds black around the edges.

A vampire bite. Patches knew it on sight.

"It is not safe here," the woman said. Her voice was steady despite the breath, low and faintly accented, with the cadence of someone used to giving orders. "We must get to the Chapel. Hallowed ground. As fast as you can run, drow."

She did not wait for an answer. She turned and continued down the slope.

Patches matched her stride.

## The Sky Fills

The pursuing dragon nearing the valley roared.

It was answered from the ridge — by a second roar, then a third. Patches and the woman were running flat-out down the slope when another shape crested the ridge above them. Then another. Then another. The sky above [[frostmoore-vale|Frostmoore Vale]] began to fill with the pale, sickly-grey wings of [[ashen-white-dragon|Ashen White Dragons]] — the same eight Patches had seen circling at distance days earlier, now stooping in formation toward the valley floor.

Atop the largest of them, a figure rode.

Crimson robes. Pallid skin, almost grey. A bald, shaved scalp marked with dark, intricate tattooed runes that ran from his temples down across the back of his neck and into the collar of the robes. He carried a wand-like focus in one hand and what looked, even at distance, like a great deal of confidence in the other.

A [[red-wizards-of-thay|Red Wizard of Thay]].

On the porch, Ishin lowered the spyglass and turned to his men.

"Lady Isabel," he said. "From [[helioglabulus|Helios]]." His voice was flat with disbelief. The Lady Isabel he knew — a wealthy merchant and respected socialite of the capital — was many things, but she was not capable of sprinting down a mountainside at the speed of a fleeing deer, or of putting her shoulder through a tree.

He did not finish the thought aloud.

The House Guard had come on alert behind him without waiting for the order — ornate bows being strung, enchanted blades being drawn from sheaths, a runner already gone for the lord. Ishin turned back to JmJm and the others.

"Defend the estate," he said.

The sun dipped below the rim of the mountains. The valley plunged into shadow.

The battle began.

## The Skies, and the Spear

[[tarkathee-daru|Tark]] moved first.

He plucked a spear from the nearest house guard's hands with a murmured apology and a small bow, coiled his right arm, and the filigree along his white-wood prosthetic flared to life — a deep, slow red, threads of [[vaketh|Vaketh]]'s power waking along the grain. He launched himself bodily into the air with a burst of otherworldly might and then *shadowstepped* through the dimming light.

He reappeared close enough to the lead dragon to throw.

The spear left his hand. The lead Ashen Dragon's mount-rider — the Red Wizard — saw it coming and raised one hand and a wall of *air* hardened in front of him; the spear deflected and ploughed harmlessly into the trees. But Tark was already drifting toward the formation, calling out — half a parlay, half a warning, in the universal tongue of someone trying to give an enemy one chance to back down.

The Thayan looked at the floating halfling as one looks at an insect that has settled on a sleeve.

He spoke a word.

Arcane wrath struck Tark across the chest like a falling beam. The monk's flight collapsed in mid-air. He pitched, twisted, and slammed into the slope below the estate.

[[cassian|Cassian]] was already drawing.

The ranger's longbow sang in a fast, accurate rhythm — arrow after arrow loosed at the Thayan and his mount, the cluster of shots tight enough to thread a needle. The first three bounced off an invisible shield the Wizard had set up at the edge of his saddle. The fourth and fifth got through and bit into the dragon's pale hide. The dragon screamed and twisted, throwing off the line of the Wizard's next casting.

The smaller Ashen Dragons split off and screamed past [[horatio-von-bumidere|Buumi]], who had launched himself into the air on his [[cloak-of-wings|Cloak of Wings]] to support Tark — blasting the Paladin with frost as they passed, then diving toward the slope where Patches and the woman in the gown were nearly to the estate, the unconscious Gnome bouncing in the woman's grip.

Patches did not stop running. She drew, mid-stride, and pivoted at her belt — and a poisoned crossbow bolt sang up out of the dark *past* the diving smaller dragons and into the saddle of the largest. It took the Red Wizard cleanly in the thigh.

The poison was the bandolier mixture — fast, paralytic, insidious. The Thayan's grip on his focus *jerked*. His next casting unravelled mid-syllable. The pale dragon under him rolled defensively to shield its rider, but the damage was already done — the man in the saddle had locked rigid, mouth still open in the shape of a word he could not finish.

## The Vampire Knight

[[horatio-von-bumidere|Buumi]] was halfway through the air on the Cloak of Wings, shouting encouragement to Tark, when the air *cracked* beside the paralysed Thayan and his mount.

A new shape teleported into being.

It was a dragon — adult, white, sickly-grey at the throat, larger than any of the other Ashen Dragons in the flight by half again. The displaced air thundered outward in a ring that shook the slate of the chapel roof. And on its back, in a tall war-saddle of black steel, sat a figure that even from the porch made the hair on the guards' necks rise.

Black plate armour, fluted and asymmetrical. A long lance — easily ten feet, a piece of unmistakably *not-current* technology mounted at its head, where the tip split slightly to bare twin filaments. A tower shield of black steel braced on the other arm. The helm had no faceplate, only a deep T-slit through which two red eyes glowed softly. Below the slit, pallid lips parted in a faint smile.

Delicate fangs.

The Vampire Knight raised his lance in formal salute toward Tark.

Then he lowered it. He tilted forward in his saddle — and the lance's filaments split wider, a ribbon of crackling electricity washing along their length, and a brilliant flash of light tore the dimming valley momentarily white.

A *Lightning Bolt* blazed from the lance.

The Halfling Monk in the air dropped sideways with the casual fluency of a man who had spent a lifetime dodging worse — the bolt missed him by feet. But the bolt did not stop. It continued past Tark, slammed into the side of the estate behind him, ploughed a smouldering rent through three walls and a balcony, and at the end of its path caught Lady Isabel full across the shoulder as she sprinted into the door yard with Patches at her heels.

The group recognised the technology immediately. JmJm cursed. Cirrus's eyes narrowed.

A Saurian Lance-weapon. The kind they had pulled from the ruins of [[al-askar|Al Askar]] half a world away. The kind that *should not have existed* in the hands of a Damaran nobleman, or any Faerûnian, or anyone at all on this side of the [[ringed-veils|Ringed Veil]].

The Vampire had a [[saurian-firelance|Saurian]] [[saurian-lance|lance]].

## The Copper

Patches grabbed Lady Isabel by the shoulder and hauled her behind the porch's stone column for cover.

"Get *down*," she said. "I will get you to the chapel."

Isabel turned the bruised side of her face to the rogue and smiled in a way that the rogue had not expected at all.

"No," she said. "I think I can finally act."

She handed the unconscious Gnome forward.

JmJm was there already, on Steden — the Nightmare's fiery hide newly reformed from cinders, summoned that morning at JmJm's quiet whistle, the Bard not yet willing to explain how. He swept the small body up in one practiced motion and tucked her in the crook of his arm.

"To the chapel," he said. "I have her."

The two house guards at the door drew weapons and shouted protest, but neither of them moved to stop a man riding for a holy site with a dying child. JmJm spurred Steden into the estate, hooves striking sparks across the polished floor, and disappeared through an archway in the direction Ishin's pointed glove indicated.

Patches turned back to argue with Isabel about cover.

The woman in the tattered evening gown shifted.

There was no warning. There was only a glitter of warm metal in the failing light — a long ripple of *change* that began at her bare feet and ran up the length of her body, dress and bruises and curled hair unmaking themselves into something else. Her shoulders broadened. Her neck lengthened. Her skin became scales the colour of a freshly struck copper coin, gleaming and warm.

An adult Copper Dragon stood where Lady Isabel had stood.

She turned her serpentine head toward the diving formation of Ashen White Dragons, drew breath, and exhaled.

Slow Breath rolled up the slope in a thick golden haze. The two nearest Ashen Dragons hit it in mid-stoop and lurched, their wing-beats suddenly dragging through invisible syrup, their dive collapsing into a fall. The Copper roared after them with the casual fury of a dragon who had, until very recently, been carrying a dying friend through the woods in a torn evening gown.

Patches stared at her for two full seconds.

Then she went back to shooting at the Red Wizard.

## The Wizard Falls

The group focused fire.

The Thayan was still paralysed — slumped sideways in his saddle, the poison riding through him with cold efficiency — and the rest of the party concentrated everything they had on closing the gap before he could shake it off. Cassian put two more arrows into the great Ashen Dragon beneath him; the mount thrashed and tried to put distance between itself and the [[saurian-lance|lance-throwing]] Vampire Knight, who was suddenly fighting alone on its other flank.

The Vampire saw the trajectory.

He kicked off his own saddle with the grace of a man who weighed nothing and *flew*.

The black-armoured shape sailed across the gap of air between his mount and the Red Wizard's, lance and shield gathering up behind him like a banner. He reached the Wizard just as the paralysed body began to slide from the dragon's back — caught the falling man bodily, twisted in the air to absorb the impact through his own shoulder, and arrested the descent.

There was a crack of breaking bone. The Wizard's neck or shoulder; impossible to tell from the porch.

The Vampire began to angle back toward his own mount with the unconscious Thayan in his arms.

Half a dozen arrows and crossbow bolts hit him simultaneously.

Cassian's bow had not stopped singing. Patches had switched to her hand crossbow the moment the Vampire moved. Buumi, mid-air on the Cloak of Wings, had taken half a heartbeat to draw, line, and loose. Tark had thrown his short blade from the slope. Even Cirrus — who had been working through the chant for something larger and had been forced to abort it — had loosed a Fire Bolt in the same instant.

The Vampire Knight took every shot.

The bolts and arrows passed *through* the Red Wizard.

The paralysed Thayan body in the Vampire's arms jerked once with the impact, and a half-dozen new entry wounds opened in his crimson robes. The Vampire did not seem to register the body's death. He continued on his arc to his dragon — and then, when he reached the saddle, paused only long enough to look down at the riddled corpse in his arms and let out a sound that the entire valley heard.

It was a roar.

It was the worst sound any of them had heard in some time. It was the cold, deliberate fury of an undead lord whose plans had just been disrupted by mortals who would not, by rights, have been in a position to disrupt anything.

The Vampire opened his arms.

A sphere of cold darkness expanded outward from his saddle — vast, lightless, swallowing the sound and the air and the colour of the world inside its radius. The dragon mount and the rider and the corpse of the Red Wizard all disappeared into it.

When the darkness collapsed in on itself a moment later, there was nothing left.

The Vampire was gone. The dragon mount was gone. The body of the Thayan was gone.

The eight Ashen White Dragons left in the sky paused in their formation, screamed in something that sounded like dismay, and *split*.

Half of them folded their wings and dove on the Copper. Half pulled hard to the side and came around for Tark and Buumi.

## The Chapel

[[jack-mayer-johnson-mathews|JmJm]] burst through the chapel doors on horseback.

The chapel was as he had been told: smaller than the manor, but ornate, vaulted, with high narrow windows and a long aisle that led to a single altar. The altar held a *blade*.

A blade of pure radiance, projecting upward from a hilt of bone-white metal — the blade quiet now, banked low, but unmistakable. The Sunsword. The Ashbringer. The same celestial weapon the group had reforged in [[krezk|Krezk]] from the Planetar's sacrifice, the same weapon they had donated to the Church of [[lathander|Lathander]] in [[neverwinter|Neverwinter]], the same weapon catalogued under the name *Sunsword* by clergy who preferred the institutional terminology to the bloody truth.

A man knelt before the altar.

He wore a silk-and-linen warrior's hakama in soft cream and deep brown, his head bowed, hands folded — and he turned, calmly, at the sound of JmJm's entrance. He did not rise. He did not reach for the sword. He simply looked at the bard as one looks at an unexpected but not unwelcome guest, with eyes that were neither surprised nor afraid.

[[lord-aimu|Lord Aimu]].

The Bard pulled up short. Steden's hooves clattered to a halt on the chapel flagstones. The unconscious Gnome — small, foreign-looking, bite-marked — sagged in JmJm's other arm.

One of the house guards burst in behind him.

"Dragons, my lord! And Lady Isabel! We are *confounded*!"

Lord Aimu looked at the Gnome in JmJm's arm, then at the bard's face, then beyond him through the door at the flashing battle in the sky. His expression did not change.

"What is your friend's wound?" he asked.

JmJm told him in two sentences. Dragons. A Red Wizard. A Vampire with a Saurian lance. A bite on the neck of a small woman who needed hallowed ground.

JmJm placed the Gnome gently on the floor at the lord's feet.

Lord Aimu rose, knelt again — beside the Gnome this time — and laid both hands across her chest. He did not pray aloud. The radiance that spilled from his palms was the steady, sourceless light of a man who had spent his life in this work: Lay on Hands, channelled through a paladin's discipline. Every bruise on the small body faded. Every cut closed. Colour returned to her cheeks.

The puncture marks on her neck remained, black-edged and unhealed.

Lord Aimu sat back on his heels.

"There is little I can do for the bite," he said quietly. "Hallowed ground will hold the corruption at bay. But the strength I had to summon to mend the rest of her —" He paused, looking down at the small unconscious face. " — was not the strength I would need to heal a Gnome."

JmJm's eyes narrowed.

The lord did not elaborate. He stood, smoothing the front of his hakama with the absent precision of a man preparing for what came next. Outside, another lightning strike from the Vampire's vanished position — or perhaps from a lingering effect — flickered through the high chapel windows. The walls vibrated with the impact of something large striking the manor's outer wall.

JmJm strode to the altar.

He set his hand on the hilt of the Sunsword and lifted.

The blade rose. The hilt felt right in his palm, balanced and quiet, the weapon ready — but the radiance of the blade did not change. The light remained banked, dormant. The Sunsword had been bonded to its wielder, and JmJm was not it.

"Attuned," he said, more to himself than to anyone else.

Lord Aimu stepped forward, straightening the lapels of his hakama, and extended one hand. JmJm placed the hilt in it without comment.

The Sunsword *lit*.

The radiance flared from banked to brilliant the instant the lord's fingers closed on the grip — the entire chapel filling with a clean, sourceless daylight that made the high windows redundant. Lord Aimu held the sword in front of him for the space of a single breath and then turned his eyes toward the doors and the battle beyond.

He did not ask the bard's name. He did not ask why the Sunsword had been recovered by a stranger and not the Crimson Dawn knights at his door. He simply nodded once, in the small economical way of a man who had accepted that what was happening was happening, and beckoned to his two guards.

"With me," he said.

JmJm followed.

## What They Found Outside

They emerged from the chapel into the sound of dragon wings beating against the slope.

The valley was full of fire and frost. The estate's east wing was burning where the Vampire's lightning bolt had ripped through it. The Copper Dragon — once Lady Isabel — was fighting two of the Ashen Whites in a tangle of necks and wings at the base of the cascade waterfalls, her copper hide bright against their sickly grey. Tark was airborne and trading blows with another. Buumi was airborne and trading blows with another. Cassian was on the ground with Patches behind a low garden wall, bowstring drawing and loosing in a rhythm so steady it sounded like an instrument.

The Vampire Lord, his dragon mount, and the corpse of the Red Wizard were nowhere to be seen.

JmJm understood without needing to ask: the Wizard had fallen, and the Vampire had vanished into a globe of inky darkness an instant later, taking his ally's body with him into wherever vampires went when they did not wish to be found. The arrow shafts that had killed the Thayan had all been the group's. The bolt that had paralysed him had been Patches's. They had taken down a Red Wizard of Thay before he had survived the poison.

It had cost them an east wing and a tree and possibly a vineyard.

Lord Aimu walked past JmJm with the [[ashbringer|Sunsword]] in his hand, and the radiance of the blade fell across the garden ahead like the unexpected return of the sun. The two nearest Ashen White Dragons, mid-stoop on the Copper, lifted their heads at the brightness and *flinched*.

The battle was not over. The lord stepped into it. The Bard, half a step behind, reached for the [[inkblade|Inkblade]] at his belt.

The note of the singing sword rose.

Above them, the remaining six Ashen Whites screamed in a chorus that the valley walls threw back as eight, and the second half of the battle began.
