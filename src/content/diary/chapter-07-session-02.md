## The Giant's Hospitality

The old giant regarded the group with a stoic, unreadable expression. It took a few minutes for the others to cross the ice, and as they approached, the party got a clear look at them — all blue-skinned and pale-eyed, wearing thick leathers adorned with mammoth-tusk shoulder pieces and mammoth-fur cloaks. Every one of them bore small wounds from the gnolls.

There was a female giant with bob-cut silver hair and a mammoth calf in her arms. A young, beardless male carrying a large chest on his back with iron chains. An adult male — huge, with light blue hair and a handlebar moustache. And the old one, massive beard and dark eyes, who had nearly fallen to the pack.

The giants exchanged words in their own tongue. The female set down the baby mammoth and began casting healing spells on it. [[Tarkathee Daru]] pulled out his healing kit and tended to the larger mammoth, then the calf — both appreciating the help and further warming the giants' disposition toward the group.

The female cast a quick translation spell and introduced herself as [[Meljir]]. She named the others — [[Mimor]], the old giant; [[Ljokjar]], the one with blue hair and the handlebar moustache; and [[Fen]], the young one. They were her family, travelling to a feast celebrating Ljokjar's marriage to the Skuldir Tribe's new matriarch.

Meljir told the group that honour demanded hospitality be extended to them. They would be fed and given shelter. The giants turned and began walking across the frozen lake, beckoning the sleigh to follow.

## The Village on the Mountain

Over the next hour, the group ascended [[Shatterpeak]] alongside the giants, Meljir asking questions in her deep, rumbling voice. A heavy snowfall rolled in and the wind picked up, obscuring the mountain and cutting visibility to nothing. The sleigh had trouble on the steeper sections, but every time it slowed, one of the giants gave it a push.

Eventually the ascent levelled out and the village appeared through the blowing snow — a massive standing stone covered in whorls and designs, and two hundred feet beyond it, a great hall made of ice slabs tapering to interlaced points like skeletal fingers. The hall looked large enough to house twenty giants comfortably. Around it stood smaller abodes of ice. Giants going about their business stopped to watch the newcomers. Those closest scowled at the small folk and their strange vehicle, though none approached.

The weather parted just long enough for [[Aliyah Faen Tlabbar]] to raise her spyglass and spot something higher up — herds of giant goats on the rock faces, the massive forms of mammoths, and a colossal staircase cut into the mountainside, ascending into cloud.

Mimor's family headed into the massive hall and the group followed on foot, leaving the sleigh behind on Meljir's assurance. [[Megno Slatesunder]] trailed behind, silent and apprehensive.

Inside, a huge bonfire roared in the centre of the cavernous ice chamber. Massive tables of stone and ice flanked the blaze. When Meljir was asked if they worried about the place melting, she chuckled — this ice would never melt. Giants moved about, setting tables and preparing for the wedding feast. An elderly giantess, bowed with age, directed the preparations. In a chair at the back of the hall sat an elderly male giant, his beard reaching the floor, a mammoth-tusk walking stick cradled in his arms, a tartan robe secured at one shoulder by a massive silver brooch. He was snoring — softly for a giant, which is to say extremely loudly.

The elderly giantess turned to greet Mimor and was surprised to see the party. Words were exchanged, and the old matriarch addressed the group in Common, welcoming them. She introduced herself as [[Ingrbotha]] and explained that the fight against the gnolls demanded repayment — they were invited to stay for the festivities.

## Drinks with Meljir

Meljir led the group to a side passage and into a huge, sparsely furnished chamber — a giant-sized bedroom. They filed inside for some rest, all except Patches, who veered off to scout the hall.

She moved room to room unnoticed by the bustling giants and found valuables — all giant-sized, nothing she could carry. On her way back, she glanced into the main hall and saw a huge, shaved-headed and moustached young giant arguing with Ingrbotha. The matriarch slapped him across the face and pointed toward the door. The furious giant stormed out, leaving Ingrbotha shaking her head.

Patches rejoined the group and found them drinking with Meljir, who sat on the bed watching the small folk and interjecting with the odd word. She had poured herself a cup of Eyrak — fermented mammoth milk and fruit — from a massive ice pitcher. [[Cirrus Al Kayan]] asked to try it and liked it more than whiskey. Tark tried next and the strong liquor hit him fast. Then Buumi, [[Gundren Rockseeker]], Megno, and finally Patches.

Before long, the inebriated group was laughing and chatting freely — bringing up [[Cercei]], which led to discussing Buumi's inter-species romances and earning a raised eyebrow from Meljir. Tark, realising this was the first time he had seen Patches drunk, decided to probe her past. The rest of the group jumped in eagerly.

Normally tight-lipped, Patches answered some questions. Her name was indeed Alya Faen Tlabbar. She was the seventh daughter of Matron Mother Vadalma of [[House Faen Tlabbar]], third house of [[Menzoberranzan]]. [[Lo'Dan]] was not the father of any child, but he had helped Patches escape the [[Underdark]]. She had worked for [[Bregan D'Aerthe]] — Lo'Dan being her contact — and operated on the surface for them for years before starting work for [[Lady Faye]] in [[Baldur's Gate]] because they were a "good fit."

They chatted for hours but got little more from her. Eventually it was time for the feast, and Meljir kicked them out so she could get ready.

## The Wedding Feast

The main hall was transformed. Giants sat at two massive tables, eating and drinking and laughing — the spitting image of any northern rural celebration, only enormous. Few took notice of the group, and those who did either smiled, averted their gaze, or scowled. None approached.

Patches kept an eye out for the angry bald giant and spotted him with an entourage, drinking with the newly-wed Ljokjar. Meanwhile, Cirrus, Gundren, and Fëanon engaged Ingrbotha in conversation. She told them of a stone archway further up the mountain that the great stair avoided — the giants could never shape the stone there. They knew a tower sat atop the highest peak but could never see it directly, only catching it in peripheral vision or as a mirage. To reach the archway, the group would need to climb the stair to a massive bridge, cross it to a plateau infested with Remorhaz — including a colossal polar worm named Merrumjundr.

Buumi, meanwhile, continued drinking heavily and made a pass at Meljir. She rebuffed him.

Patches climbed onto the table where the male giants were drinking, slipping behind a mug of Eyrak. When the mug was lifted, she was revealed — a Drow sitting cross-legged in plain sight. The giants reeled back. "See," one rumbled, "I told you they can do that." Patches declared herself a warrior. The bald giant laughed and demanded proof.

## The Competitions

He let out a shout that echoed around the hall. The giants filtered outside into the snowy night and issued a series of challenges: a foot race to the standing stone and back, a drinking competition, a javelin throw, a wrestling match, and something called "The Cheese Sniff."

Patches took the foot race against a female giant who actually smiled before the start. At the crack of a titanic clap, Patches zipped off into the night, running atop the snow and easily outpacing the giant's massive strides. Within thirty feet of the stone, she misty-stepped to it, spun, and sprinted back — passing the surprised giantess on the return. Her hair glowed with moonlight as she crossed the finish. Meljir asked if any magic was used, noting it would be dishonourable and "unfortunate." Patches said it had not been, and the group sighed in relief as Meljir nodded, convinced.

Two massive tusks filled with Eyrak were brought out for the drinking contest. A pudgy giant stepped forward. Cirrus decided [[Selûne]] was with him and accepted the challenge. The group grew nervous at the thought of the skinniest among them trying to outdrink a giant. A beam of moonlight broke through the clouds and illuminated Cirrus. The muscles in his throat relaxed and he poured the liquor down as if filling an empty vessel. The horn was emptied — no one could fathom where he was putting it. The giant finished his own tusk and moved to strike at the impudent little creature, but the bald, moustached giant delivered a blow to the drinker's jaw, dropping him to the ground.

For the javelin throw, Buumi eagerly stepped forward against the bald giant — Suttung. The ice javelins were enormous, and Buumi struggled with their weight. Cirrus quietly cast levitate on the first one, just enough for an easier grip. Both competitors hit on the first throw, missed the next two, and on the fourth, Suttung missed while Buumi's javelin smashed into the standing stone. The giants roared in delight. Suttung regarded the group coldly, then turned and walked into the night.

Meljir stepped forward for the wrestling match, looking for fun. Tark volunteered. He darted between her legs and struck the back of her knees, bringing the giantess crashing down, then leapt onto her back. She grabbed him and squeezed, but Tark pushed against her hand and shrank, using the sudden space to break free. She rolled and swatted — Tark dodged, feinted a fish-hook, leapt, and came down on her diaphragm with an elbow. Meljir chuckled on the ground, conceding she'd had too much to drink. Buumi tried his luck with her again. She rebuffed him again.

For the final event, the old patriarch emerged from the hall — [[Aegir]], the ancient fellow with the great beard, walking stick, and tartan sash. Eighteen wheels of cheese were set out. Each contestant was presented with three sets of three cheeses — one good in each set, the other two rotten. After selection, the cheese had to be eaten. Only smell and rind could guide the choice. Gundren stepped forward as the group's foremost expert in foul odours. He picked badly in the first round, nearly vomiting through dwarven constitution, but Aegir also picked wrong — a draw. Both chose correctly in the second round. In the third, Gundren picked right and Aegir did not.

The giants roared and congratulated the group. Gundren and Aegir returned to the hall to discuss cheese at length.

## Departures in the Dark

As the night wore on and giants passed out or drifted home, Patches and Tark both felt something watching from the darkness. Tark spotted an old orcish woman walking through the snow and shadowstepped out to her. The woman — [[Dregthana]] — had come to deliver Rudgrub as a gift. They walked back together, and Dregthana soon had several giants gathered around her, preparing pipes.

Megno stumbled through the front doors, utterly drunk, and collapsed in a pile of furs. Patches settled in alongside Cirrus and his analgesic, and the group slept in the main hall, warm under mammoth furs near the bonfire.

## The Climb

Morning brought terrible hangovers. Buumi went around neutralising the poison in everyone's systems, then they ate leftovers from the feast and gathered their things. They found Meljir passed out under a table and nudged her awake to ask for a guide up the mountain. She declined but assured them the giants would keep their sleigh safe, then went back to sleep.

The sleigh and moose were already being tended in a stable. The group decided to go on foot — the stair was only an hour's walk. They set out into the wind, light snow falling, and arrived at the base of the staircase: three-foot steps, twelve feet wide, encrusted in snow and ice.

The climb was gruelling. Gaps thirty feet wide from landslides required rope and grapples. Patches of ice demanded climbing gear. A bolt of lightning discharged above them, raining stone and ice until Cirrus threw up a wall of force. Heavy fog rolled in and Patches scouted ahead, finding a large archway half-buried in snow that the stair curved around.

The group caught up, examined the arch, and charged it with cantrips. They passed through — and as the clouds parted, they saw their destination revealed: a dark stone tower rising from the highest peak, encrusted in centuries of ice.

They pressed on, roping themselves together against the wind. A massive eagle cry split the snowstorm and they hunkered down as a Roc circled the mountain, snatching a bleating goat from the lower slopes before wheeling away. The cold was biting and exhaustion setting in by the time they reached the top of the stair. A massive bridge of ice planks fixed by huge iron chains spanned the gap between the mountain's halves, leading to the plateau of Merrumjundr.

Patches crossed first, anchoring rope behind her. The group followed — slow, tedious, several slips but no falls greater than ten feet. On the plateau, her spyglass revealed patches of moving snow and dozens of sets of giant bones. Rather than tangle with polar worms, the group cast flight spells and simply flew across, carrying those who could not fly themselves. Cirrus tried to lift Megno but lacked the strength and dropped the dwarf — Tark swooped in and grabbed him just as a smaller worm burst from the ground, jaws snapping at the ascending dwarf's feet.

On the far side, Patches spotted a human-sized staircase cut immaculately into the mountainside, completely free of ice and snow. The group swooped down and followed it. Moments later they stood before the massive black-stoned tower rising from the highest peak, the wind howling, blowing ice stinging exposed skin.

A great black iron door stood in a stone frame thick with ice. Fëanon and Megno began hurling firebolts into the ice, cracking and melting it away until the door stood free.
