## Claudine and the Alchemy Shop

Patches looked at [[claudine|Claudine]] and nodded slightly, confirming the old woman's suspicions.

[[claudine|Claudine]]'s eyes went a little wide and she turned to Zella, ordering her into the backroom.

The young half-Elf sighed but did as she was told.

Patches pointedly didn't ask about the parentage of the girl, but did ask if she had any contacts still in Faen Tlabbar.

[[claudine|Claudine]] was nervous and asked to walk and talk, as her boss would be angry if she just chatted.

They moved around the shop and Patches perused, buying several potions and receiving them in a nice box.

[[claudine|Claudine]] told Patches that no, she had no contacts in Faen Tlabbar, but she did have information.

They would meet after the third day's work shift, at the Slave Quarters of the Muzgardt clan, a set of barracks and apartments nearby.

As they hashed out the details, a Duergar woman approached — Seke, the owner of this store.

Seke immediately tried to order [[claudine|Claudine]] to start polishing the metal counter-tops as she spotted some corrosion, then tried to take over the conversation with Patches.

Patches was taken aback and immediately said she had nothing to say to Seke but was talking to [[claudine|Claudine]].

Seke's face contorted with anger before she got it under control when she noted the Dragon badge, but asked that Patches leave, immediately.

Patches left and heard Seke screaming at [[claudine|Claudine]] as the rogue walked away to find the rest of the group.

## The Brewery Tour

The rest of the group had been getting a tour of the Brewery after pretending to be high-rollers looking to engage in trade.

They got a lot of information, seeing the Cave Fisher stables and the creatures harvested for their filament for beer and alchemy.

[[snorbul|Snorbul]] was very intrigued — his family had bred Cave Fishers as guards for the city, and he had no idea they could be used this way. He resolved himself to try and start a ranch.

The group also discovered that Muzgardt currently traded with the Zhentarim in Mantol Derrith, which was then brought to the surface. Any other surface deals had to go through the Zhentarim, or match the bribery clause.

Patches met up with the group after they exited the stables and they all headed to get tastings of the many potions.

In the masterfully crafted bar-like area, the group saw many kegs and barrels behind a countertop of pure gold. A strange spider-like contraption whirred behind the bar, serving drinks and small tastes of potions.

The group was interested and remarked that it did not look like Duergar make.

The Duergar confirmed and instead said that a Gnome engineer named Dr. Hojo Finklespark, Inventor Extraordinaire, had been responsible for the work of artifice.

They then inquired about purchasing a large supply of healing and other potions, but when told the price, made excuses and left.

Fey complained they were cash poor and Patches cocked an eyebrow at him.

Patches told the group she had an appointment later in the day but her tasks were currently done.

They headed back to the Townhouse.

## The Townhouse

Pulling up to the Gholbrorn's Lair, the group exited the rickshaws and headed into the Foreigner's Complex.

As they walked over to the elevator to take them to their floor, they spotted a young Duergar hustling toward them with a long black box under his arm.

They recognized [[hemeth|Hemeth]] and the Duergar gave them a hearty hello, telling them that he had their order and gesturing to the box under his arm.

Understanding that [[hemeth|Hemeth]] had information for them, they all headed up to the Townhouse and the relative privacy of their rooms.

Once inside, [[hemeth|Hemeth]] let them know that the box was just pastries — the group chowed down — but that he had information for them.

House Baenre of [[menzoberranzan|Menzoberranzan]] traded with the Zhentarim to the north at Mantol Derrith, but no one had heard from the outpost in some time.

They had found someone who knew the personnel on the Golem Sale: a Drow Agent of Bregan D'Aerthe named Shin. He was one of the guards of the Drow Embassy and disguised as a Mizzryn guard. He could get the group a name.

The group thanked [[hemeth|Hemeth]] and he took his leave.

The group then discussed what to do next in the city, and whether they should even remain.

They deliberated and decided to head to Cairngorm Cavern and speak with the Stone Giants about Gravenhollow.

## Cairngorm Cavern

They headed into the city, getting rickshaws as usual — with so many trips now, several waited nearby.

As they left the [[darklake|Darklake]] district and headed into the Duergar section, a scuffle on the market edge caught their attention.

They watched as a group of five Derro all pulled weapons and attacked a lone Duergar Merchant.

They bore the man to the ground, hacking and stabbing with abandon.

In their single-minded bloodlust they didn't even register when three Duergar Stoneguard all came out of invisibility and began to cut them down.

In a matter of a few bloody seconds, six corpses lay bleeding on the street.

The rickshaws trundled on. The group did not stop.

Crossing the great furrow that divided the city, the chemical and industrial chimneys belching a thick stream of smog into the air, the group came to a strange juxtaposition.

A set of massive, natural stone doors covered in intricate whorls and carved knots.

Sitting out front of them was a Stone Giant with a hide wrap made of a massive red lizard. A menhir stone sat next to him.

The giant smiled down at the approaching group and rumbled a greeting.

No one spoke Giant, so he then looked at Patches and continued in Undercommon.

After a brief conversation, the giant understood they were visiting at the behest of Dorhun, the partner of the dead mutant from the day prior.

The giant touched his massive club and the whorls lit up, matching the massive door, and with a rumble they slowly opened.

The group left their rickshaws behind and began down the massive, natural limestone tunnel ahead of them.

Large enough for two giants abreast, the group felt dwarfed by the massive natural landscape, the place glittering with mineral deposits.

It had a clean smell to the place, neither dry nor humid, and it felt clean and cared for.

The natural shapes seemed molded and it felt much like a Japanese garden.

They saw several giants tending to the cavern and [[snorbul|Snorbul]] approached one and asked what he was doing.

He did not respond at first.

Patches, loving the place, asked if she could meditate here and the giant was amicable.

They found that the massive creatures were rather slow of purpose and dismissive of any of the smaller races.

They didn't get much from the gardener and continued on, making it to the main chamber.

The noise of a waterfall filled the place and the massive limestone cavern shone with reflected water.

Several giants could be seen sitting in the chamber, washing clothes, gathering water or relaxing.

The bright light source in the chamber, casting the reflection, lounged down by the water's edge.

A radiant figure, shining with dawn light, noticed the group and sat up, showing a slender Eladrin-esque appearance, though a massive seven feet tall — a Summer An Sidhe.

The radiant being called out to the group just as [[cirrus-al-kayan|Cirrus]] began to freak out.

Already uncomfortable by the proximity of this place to the Fae Wilds, coming face to face with an Archfey unsettled the Warlock immensely.

The Sidhe called out in Common, naming many of the group and saying that he wished them no harm — and indeed promised it under the Guest Rites of the Fae, a binding word.

[[cirrus-al-kayan|Cirrus]] began to walk away quickly.

Patches caught up and after a brief conversation, Cirrus took a massive hit of drugs to calm his nerves and headed back in.

The radiant figure introduced himself as Araele the Dawn Knight, one of twelve of the Summer Court of the Fae.

On guard, the group began to interrogate the Dawn Knight while giving up little of their own purpose.

They gathered that Araele had been drawn to the Prime Material by accident when Hgraam summoned a Fire elemental recently and the portal expanded.

Being nearby and hunting the same elemental in the Plane of Fire, the Knight had been drawn into the same portal by the unstable cracks in the cosmos.

He was now trapped in Cairngorm Cavern as he would need an invitation to leave into the greater city — much like a Vampire — and only the King or similar noble could grant such a thing.

The King and the Lairds had not responded to any of the messengers sent by the Stone Giants. Odd.

He offered to buy one of the Dragon Badges, or gain an introduction to the dragon himself.

The group was cagey and they began to negotiate from a place of strength, realizing that Araele needed them desperately.

While the negotiations were happening, Patches noted the cubby that the Archfey had cut into the stone while staying in the cavern.

The cuts were ridiculously sharp and precise, like the cubby had been cut by the same thing as Bilrigger's Sanctum.

Returning, she asked the Dawn Knight about it and he drew his sword.

Appearing from a blazing scar, a huge weapon materialized into his outstretched hand.

The [[underdark|Underdark]] dwellers all hissed in pain as their eyes were burned by the flash and several giants swore and moved away, muttering.

[[snorbul|Snorbul]] looked them over and approached one.

Araele introduced the Fae Blade — a monstrously powerful type of weapon of which few existed, and even fewer existed without a current master.

The group put it together and realized that Bilrigger's Sanctum had been attacked by a wielder of one of these blades, though Araele swore he had not left the cavern.

The negotiations ended with a few offers and counteroffers: a scroll of Planeshift or an introduction to the Lady of Summer and being taken to the gates of the Bifrost — the road to Ysgard and the domain of [[selune|Selune]]. All this in exchange for his freedom.

Meanwhile, [[snorbul|Snorbul]] chatted with the nearby giant and discovered that Araele had been a headache for them to deal with.

A stone giant approached from one of the nearby tunnels, heading toward [[snorbul|Snorbul]].

The huge giant, with a crown of crystals and a cloak of moss, introduced himself as Hgraam, leader of the giants here.

The group took their leave from Araele, saying they would think it over, and headed to join [[snorbul|Snorbul]].

The giant offered them some information on Gravenhollow, confirming that with a crystal key, the library could be drawn to the wielder.

He also confirmed [[snorbul|Snorbul]]'s suspicions that half of that key resided in Blingdenstone, having been given to the gnomes many eons ago.

The giant spoke a cryptic warning of the dangerous powers growing in the [[underdark|Underdark]] and that the very caverns quaked.

He gave the group his half of the key to Gravenhollow and implored them to seek out the Library.

The group took the large crystal and turned to leave Cairngorm Cavern, unsure how to deal with the Summer Sidhe, and with Patches needing to make her evening appointment with [[claudine|Claudine]].
