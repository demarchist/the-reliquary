## The Hunter Becomes the Hunted

The party emerged from the satellite pyramid bearing all three crests, but the victory rang hollow against the knowledge that something still stalked them through the dark. [[Hazzim]] named the creature an Alghoul — a thing of legend among [[Al-Askar|Al Askar's]] scattered survivors, a source of lesser ghouls that dragged the unwary into lightless caves to feed its brood.

[[Horatio Von Bumidere|Buumi]] would not suffer such a predator to roam while people sheltered under his protection. He insisted they lay an ambush before pressing deeper into the mines.

They chose the nearest pyramid as their killing ground. [[Snorbul]] burrowed beneath the stone floor and waited with the patience of earth itself, while [[Tarkathee Daru|Tark]] stood alone in the open — bait dangling before the dark.

An hour crept past in silence before the creature struck. It erupted from concealment in a blur of muscle and claw, lashing at Tark with predatory precision. His cloak of displacement saved him, the attack shearing through empty air where his image wavered, and he sprinted for the pyramid door, hammering against the stone.

The trap sprang. The party surged from the doorway as Snorbul burst upward through the ground, and they surrounded the Alghoul in a ring of steel and spell. Every wound they carved sprayed caustic acid across the attackers, burning flesh as savagely as the creature's talons gouged it. With a shriek of rage, the Alghoul bounded free and vanished — its mottled hide blending seamlessly against the cavern wall.

[[Feanon Serinde|Feanon]] lashed the cliff face with a blast of freezing cold, but found nothing. Then [[Cirrus Al-Kayan|Cirrus]] raised his hand and called forth a sickening radiance, and there — beneath the overhang — a faint, sickly glow betrayed the creature's hiding place. Tark leapt without hesitation. [[Eclipse]] bit deep, and the Alghoul died in a spray of hissing blood.

## Whispers of Zalaam

They hauled the twisted corpse back to level ground and examined it — a grotesque mass of claw and sinew, its flesh threaded with the same dark corruption that webbed the mine tunnels. Exhausted, the party retreated into the pyramid, sealed the door with traps and alarms, and conjured a Magnificent Mansion for the night.

During the watches, Feanon observed a swarm of machina beetles threading through the mine, scanning the passages with mechanical purpose. He let them pass undisturbed.

Inside the mansion, Buumi drew Hazzim into conversation about the dark god [[Cult of Zalaam|Zalaam]]. The young man knew only legends — that Zalaam had once been a god of death and darkness held in balance with creation, until the [[Saurians]] shattered that equilibrium a thousand years ago. Since then, the dark god had been in ascendance, his corruption spreading unchecked.

While Buumi baked and chatted, [[Aliyah Faen Tlabbar|Patches]] arrived with her own concerns. She had noticed Cirrus's resentment that she possessed Moonfire while the warlock seemingly did not. Together, she and Buumi theorized that Cirrus's strange, luminous eyes — a gift from the goddess — were themselves a manifestation of Moonfire. Patches went to confront the warlock directly, and after some reluctant denial, Cirrus conceded the point.

Patches then sought out Feanon, telling him she had discovered his first clone. She asked if the path of arcane duplication was worth it, and the wizard answered without hesitation that it was. She warned him of Manshoon and the madness that had consumed that archmage's fractured copies, but Feanon waved the caution aside — history was the best teacher, and he would not repeat those mistakes. No one in earshot seemed convinced.

## The Hatchery

Come morning, the party packed camp and moved back toward the central pyramid and its control console. Along the way, they discovered a side passage sealed by a heavy metal door fitted with a mechanical contraption. Tark and Patches investigated while the others held the tunnel mouth.

The device proved to be an airlock. They cycled through it and found a chamber of pure horror.

Dark webs choked the space. Black tar pooled between hundreds of egg-sacs that pulsed with foul life, and among them shuffled hunched figures — ghouls, tending the brood like grotesque nursemaids. One stood out from the rest: the burned and twisted form of the cultist the party had taken to calling Baconbitz, his humanity consumed entirely by the corruption.

Tark and Patches sealed the door and retreated. Behind them, the ghouls sensed the intrusion and began forcing the airlock open.

The party turned the tunnel into a killing ground — traps laid, spells readied, chokepoints established. When the ghouls burst through and surged forward, they met a wall of coordinated violence. These were no ordinary undead; they struck with lashing tentacles and corrupted magic, far tougher than any ghouls the party had faced. But Cirrus conjured another sickening radiance while the fighters held the line, and one by one the creatures fell.

They burned the corpses and let the radiance scour the hatchery clean, destroying every egg and strand of corruption. In the mine carts beyond, they scavenged more sourcestones and a handful of runestones, which [[Hasrala]] identified for them.

## Into the Deep

With the crests in hand, the party returned to the central pyramid and inserted them into the console. The platform hummed with gathering magical energy, arcane light spiralling upward, and they ascended the dais together.

The teleportation deposited them in a deeper section of the mines — perhaps miles from where they had stood moments before. The tunnels here were well-crafted and meticulously maintained, a stark contrast to the crumbling passages above. The party advanced on full alert, searching for traps as they went and spotting several warding glyphs which they carefully avoided.

As they entered a larger chamber, Patches called out a warning — but not quickly enough. Hidden cultists struck from the shadows, lashing out with dark magic. The party overwhelmed three of them in moments, but when the survivors turned to flee, only one was cut down. The other two bolted down separate corridors.

Buumi and Tark ran one to ground, but the third escaped into the depths of the complex, carrying word of the intruders with him.

The hunt was far from over.
