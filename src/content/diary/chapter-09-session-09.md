The Aboleth's psionics crashed against [[feanon-serinde|Fey]]'s will like a tide. [[theodemir|Theodemir]] cried out from the [[iron-tome|Iron Tome]], throwing everything he had into shoring up Fey's defenses — but both were overwhelmed by the vastness of the Sovereignty pressing in from every direction. Feanon began to change, the creature's mucus rewriting him at a cellular level.

Something tore at the back of his head — a sudden warmth, like a tendon giving way. The psionic pressure collapsed. The Aboleth recoiled and released a pulse of raw rage that hammered everyone still in the water.

High above in the lake, the Kraken — already swimming back from the Plane of Water — charged its lightning again.

---

[[aliyah-faen-tlabbar|Patches]] drove poisoned bolts into the minions restraining Feanon; he broke free. [[thorna|Thorna]] snapped out of her daze at the water's edge and reached out with Control Water, dragging the Aboleth and Feanon toward the surface. [[tarkathee-daru|Tark]] plunged in. [[snorbul|Snorbul]] commandeered a nearby boat, secured a rope from himself to the hull, and dived over the side with his axe.

Snorbul crashed into the Aboleth and swung. The creature's mucus splashed across him and his flesh shifted immediately — color draining, skin turning translucent. The Aboleth flinched under the assault but kept working. Tark struck it repeatedly without effect on himself. [[cirrus-al-kayan|Cirrus]] destroyed an illusory duplicate the beast conjured to buy itself distance.

The Aboleth snatched [[feanon-serinde|Fey]] and Tark in its tentacles and fled for the depths, spinning more phantasms as it went. Thorna dragged at it again with Control Water, slowing its flight — but it reached deep water, the surface a dim void of light overhead. A vast shape rose ahead: the Kraken, electricity crackling across its hide.

[[feanon-serinde|Fey]] teleported.

He and [[tarkathee-daru|Tark]] appeared in a flash in the tower's teleportation chamber, soaking wet and covered in mucus. Fey sprinted down the corridor holding his breath, his lungs burning, his skin already craving water. He called the elevator. It opened immediately. They descended to the laboratory, where a large holding tank had been prepared for the Aboleth's hypothetical visit. Fey flung the hatch open and splashed in, gulping a lungful of cold water with relief. He communicated telepathically to Tark — but when he opened his eyes, Tark had already gone, shadowstepping back to the battle below.

---

Up on the surface, [[gundren-rockseeker|Gundren]] had been working the fight from [[arkto|Arkto]] when a column of lightning stole his vision. The Kraken, far below, had fired through the mutated Storm Giant, which redirected the full bolt upward into [[horatio-von-bumidere|Buumi]] — hovering above the lake on summoned wings. The valley lit up for a moment. Buumi screamed and barely caught himself, dropping hard onto the docks.

[[cirrus-al-kayan|Cirrus]], still deep below, saw the Kraken charging again. [[gundren-rockseeker|Gundren]] hammered the Storm Giant with the [[thundercannon|Thundercannon]]; torn and bleeding, the giant dove and retreated. The Kraken fired regardless — the sinking giant redirected it upward at [[snorbul|Snorbul]]. The blast boiled the water around the Gnome and slammed into him. He went limp, his axe slipping from his hand and settling on the lake bed, and drifted.

[[horatio-von-bumidere|Buumi]] healed himself with a swift prayer to Helm, flew to the drifting boat, and began hauling on Snorbul's rope.

[[cirrus-al-kayan|Cirrus]] reached the Kraken. He drove the Iron Fork into its hide and tore a hole in reality — the Kraken was ripped into [[abyss|the Abyss]]. The water collapsed inward with a boom and then went still.

Above, [[thorna|Thorna]] called down an Ice Storm on the remaining minions, already mired in [[aliyah-faen-tlabbar|Patches]]' tar and half-frozen by the ice. [[gundren-rockseeker|Gundren]] picked off the stragglers. The Aboleth and the mutated Storm Giant retreated together into the lightless depths and did not surface again.

---

The storm died almost immediately, the barony's magically calm weather reasserting itself. Stars appeared. The waning moon reflected off the puddles across the docks.

Snorbul gasped awake from Buumi's healing — then gagged, his lungs refusing the air, and dropped back into the water. His transformation had taken.

From across the lake came a boat laden with Dragonborn knights, soldiers, [[doc|Doc]], and [[jack-mayer-johnson-mathews|JmJm]], rowing hard from the [[feanons-tower|Kossenberg]] side. A moment later a fiery comet blazed from the forest shoreline and arced toward the tower island; at the last moment it detonated, and [[surtr|Surtr]] dropped from the fireball, catching his hammer in a running stop.

[[tarkathee-daru|Tark]] descended from the tower above. The group gathered on the docks, asking after Feanon. [[doc|Doc]] knelt over Snorbul and began working — within minutes the mutagenic disease was reversed and the Gnome could breathe air again. They headed up to the lab.

---

[[cirrus-al-kayan|Cirrus]] got there first. He closed the door behind him and wedged a chair under the handle, then crossed to the tank.

Through the glass, [[feanon-serinde|Fey]] watched him approach.

Cirrus admonished him at length. The Aboleth hadn't been summoned by the dinner conversation — it had been coming long before the evening's truce was struck. That much was obvious. But the consequences were real: citizens were dead and taken, Uira was missing, and the trust the group had just carefully rebuilt was in pieces. Feanon's arrogance had a cost that other people were paying.

[[aliyah-faen-tlabbar|Patches]] and [[snorbul|Snorbul]] arrived to find the door barred. Patches produced the stolen keycard, unlocked the lab, and Snorbul hit the door at full speed. The chair exploded into splinters. Patches crossed the lab in three strides, [[eclipse|Eclipse]] forming in her hand, and slashed at the tank. Sparks flew; the reinforced glass held. Snorbul swung his recovered axe; more sparks, no damage.

[[feanon-serinde|Fey]] called for calm and, uncharacteristically, offered it unprompted: a full admission of fault, an apology, and two concrete concessions — a magistrate of Tyr to serve as ongoing oversight, and a standing invitation for the Mind Hunters to act as a check on his decisions. Tempers gradually cooled.

Then Fey told them the Abolethic Sovereignty was contacting him.

The terms: eight hostages — citizens of the barony — held alive. The Sovereignty wanted to send a Surgeon to examine how Fey had resisted absorption. In exchange, everyone came home.

Fey refused. He promised an alternative solution.

The Aboleths ended communication.

The group settled in for a long, paranoid, and much-needed rest.
