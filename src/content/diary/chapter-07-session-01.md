## Planning the Expedition

[[Megno Slatesunder]] looked up from his reading with a gleam in his eye and asked [[Fëanon Serinde]] if he remembered much about the ancient Netheril Empire. Fëanon said he did, and Megno pressed on — the book described the [[Sanctum of Krasus]], built by the most powerful mage to have ever lived. More importantly, it placed the Sanctum barely fifty miles southeast, hidden within the Nether Mountains at a split peak called [[Shatterpeak]]. The mountain itself was a well-known landmark, but according to the text, the only way to find the Sanctum was to pass through a stone archway on the north slope.

The two wizards agreed at once — an expedition had to be mounted immediately. Megno headed downstairs and put his apprentice Molly to work seeing to the group's needs while [[Gundren Rockseeker]] continued his repairs on [[Gwen]]. Then the dwarf rushed off to secure permission from his clan head in the Explorers League, despite Fëanon's misgivings about spreading the information.

Fëanon rejoined the group in the workshop to find [[Horatio von Bumidere]] showing off his latest purchase — cooled saddlebags for food storage. [[Aliyah Faen Tlabbar]] tried on her enchanted platinum mask, projecting a silent image of herself and drifting it across the room. It took [[Tarkathee Daru]] an embarrassingly long time to notice.

A knock at the open front door interrupted their chatter. In the doorway stood Forgefather Halfhand alongside [[Helga Stoneheart]], the mason assigned to oversee construction of the castle at the Barony. The group laid out their specifications, mentioning the situation with the orcs. The dwarves were mildly annoyed but agreed to work alongside them and produced a ledger for signature. Patches ordered [[Pickles]] to lead the mining scouts back to the Barony, accompanied by Kitty and Aurora. The dwarves were taken aback by the sight of a blue goblin, but after some muttered assurances, accepted that it was simply another client.

Shortly after, Megno returned, locked his door, raised his wards, and announced that he had secured permission to lead an expedition to the Sanctum. Due to the sensitivity of the information, he was authorised to hire only the group and had to accompany them himself. He handed over a cheque for ten thousand gold for supplies, promising another ten thousand upon confirmation of the Sanctum's discovery.

## Shopping in Sundabar

The group headed out into [[Sundabar]] to gather supplies. Patches veered off alone to follow a lead on the bank heist — the halfling spy. Buumi stopped at the Northern Vault to deposit the Artifact, and Tark picked up several healer's kits.

They made their way to the Ward of Artifice and found a particularly fine alchemy shop — [[Gilbert's Gumptions]]. Inside, they met Gilbert Bubblegump, an elderly and remarkably short dwarven alchemist. The group browsed the shelves, picking up an assortment of potions and haggling the price down. In exchange for a permanent twenty-percent discount, they agreed to give Gilbert first right of refusal on any rare potion recipes and reagents they found.

[[Cirrus Al Kayan]] then remarked that it was probably time he acquired some armour — a comment that left everyone flabbergasted when they realised he had not been wearing any. Gilbert recommended his brother's establishment, [[Albert's Armor]], and the group trooped over. Albert was the spitting image of Gilbert, though considerably taller and sporting a fancy mithril waistcoat. Cirrus found an excellent suit of enchanted leather and negotiated a significant discount by trading away plate armour and a few grenades.

## Foiling the Heist

Meanwhile, Patches had tracked the halfling spy to an inn in the residential quarter of the Undercity — [[The Short and Curlies]]. Through the window she spotted her mark chatting with three figures: a gnome male, a half-elf female, and a human male. Patches slipped inside, took a seat at the bar, and listened.

The heist was set for midnight. The gnome mage, Frumple Binswaddle, would handle the actual infiltration using potions to render himself invisible and levitating. The others would cover him outside in case something went wrong. The thieves broke up for the remainder of the day.

Patches returned to the workshop. Gundren had finished repairing and upgrading Gwen with adamantite — though he knew any further improvements would require something more powerful than an enchanted gemstone for a core. Megno announced he was ready to leave, but Patches asked for a private word. She told the group about the heist and her orders from [[Lady Faye]] to stop it. They agreed unanimously — the robbery of the Southern Vault could not be allowed.

The group split. Buumi, Gundren, and Fëanon headed to a bar near the bank in the southern military district. Cirrus transformed his owl familiar into a spider — complete with Cirrus-face patterning — and meditated in the workshop, seeing through its eyes. The spider was meant for Patches, but her absolute revulsion at the creature nearly caused a panic, so it went to Tark instead.

Patches, Tark, and the spider-familiar staked out the vault. Around midnight, the halfling spy appeared near the entrance, her belly conspicuously swollen as though pregnant. On cue, she stumbled and cried out in pain. Two guards rushed to assist, and the momentary distraction was enough. Patches saw nothing else happen and realised the gnome must have slipped inside invisible.

Tark activated his blindfold's truesight and confirmed it — a floating gnome shining with golden light was drifting through the bank entrance. They hurried inside, trying to devise a plan. Tark reported the gnome was about to pass the guards into the vault tunnel itself. Thinking fast, Patches pulled a card from her [[Deck of Illusions]], hesitated, then threw it the moment Tark said the gnome was in the tunnel.

The card spun down and touched the floor. A night hag materialised in the centre of the vault, cackling and menacing. The bank erupted — patrons screaming, guards lowering halberds in a clatter of plate. The front doors boomed shut. The guard nearest the vault tunnel slapped a hidden panel on the wall and the entire tunnel ceiling dropped. Several thousand pounds of stone sealed the passage, and poor Frumple was obliterated in a spray of gore that jetted across the bank floor.

Tark and Patches stood wide-eyed in the panicking crowd. A young dwarf woman in gold robes and ruby spectacles rushed forward, scooped up the card, and dismissed the illusion. After twenty minutes, patrons were allowed to leave and the story began spreading through the Undercity. The group convened at the pub. Patches and Tark's account earned a round of laughs. Back at the workshop, Cirrus celebrated his own fashion — huffing an analgesic grenade in the corner.

They reported the security vulnerabilities to Goldhold at the Northern Vault. The banker laughed about the "Gusher" and assured them the concerns would be addressed. Patches wrote to Lady Faye with her enchanted quill, then returned to The Short and Curlies. The three surviving thieves sat at their usual table, toasting their fallen comrade and taking comfort that nothing could be traced back to them.

Patches waited for the bartender to turn away, then slipped poison into each of the mugs heading for their table. Within fifteen minutes, two complained of illness and retreated to their rooms. The half-elf felt drunk rather than sick and headed upstairs to sleep.

Patches followed. She waited fifteen minutes, then picked the lock and slipped inside. The half-elf was unconscious on the bed. Patches relocked the door, crossed the room, and drove the [[Luck Blade]] through the woman's throat into her brain — killing her instantly. Heavy knocks interrupted her search of the room. The human thief was at the door, begging for the antidotes he believed the half-elf kept. Patches opened the door and pressed herself into the corner. The man stumbled inside, saw his dead companion, and before he could react, Patches stepped behind him and cut his throat.

She checked herself for blood, found none, and walked calmly down the stairs. The proprietor asked if she had heard someone calling about poison. Patches said she had not, paid her tab, and left.

## On the Road

An excited Megno roused the group at dawn. Buumi cooked eggs and bacon while they packed. Megno left the workshop in Molly's hands, and the party took the elevator to the Uppercity — finding it snowy and bustling, the rubble almost entirely cleared, half-rebuilt structures rising everywhere despite winter.

Outside the East Gate, a large covered sleigh waited, pulled by two moose. The vehicle was the size of a covered wagon — thin sheet metal riveted onto a wooden frame, insulated, with massive curving runners. Its aerodynamic silhouette coupled with the enormous moose was more than a little eye-catching. Megno took the reins, Tark perched atop the sled grinning like an idiot, and they set off due east.

The first day was easy going across snowy rolling fields and sparse trees. They spotted a herd of wild pigs snuffling in the snow, startled briefly by the sleigh before returning to frolicking after a wheel of cheese Tark hurled into the snowfield. As sunset brought the real cold, the thickening forest slowed their progress and they made camp.

Under the stars, the group talked. Buumi asked about Cirrus's childhood in [[Amn]], raised by tieflings. The conversation turned to Buumi's long-dead cousin — the little girl he had accidentally killed — and his hope of one day finding the power to undo such a thing, or save another in a similar situation. He admitted he would love to save [[Cercei]] from the evil fate of chromatic dragons. Fëanon cautioned that such a thing would mean blocking a goddess from one of her own, but everyone agreed that in this world, anything seemed possible.

Fëanon took first watch, studying his acquired spellbooks. In a flash of insight, knowledge from [[Theodemir]] unpacked in his mind and he began jotting down the foundations of a new spell centred on manipulating time and space. Cirrus's owl spotted an orcish hunter tracking deer in the snow, but the morning came without incident. Tark tried to motivate one of the moose with a feedbag, but the beast simply stood over him and stuck its head into the bag, lifting the halfling off the ground.

The Nether Mountains were clearly visible ahead, and one peak in particular caught every eye — a mountain split in half as if by a sword stroke, the edges weathered but the cut still unmistakably unnatural. Cirrus's owl scouted ahead and found a settlement by a steaming, ice-free lake — dozens of huts and yurts with figures moving among them. The group decided to blow past at speed, meeting any threat with overwhelming force. Gundren popped the top hatch as a turret, Gwen beside him, Patches at the rear with her crossbow. Cirrus cast an illusion making the moose appear to be aflame, thus starting the legend of the Flaming-Moose Sleigh of Winter.

The village turned out to be orcs co-habitating with barbarians. A few came to the water's edge to watch, but no war parties formed. The sleigh rounded the hot-spring-fed lake and pushed into the foothills around Shatterpeak.

## The Giants and the Gnolls

The sleigh burst from the treeline onto a flat, frozen lake beneath the split mountain. Across the ice, four Frost Giants were running — massive and blue-skinned, a huge mammoth at their side. The lead giant was female, clutching a baby mammoth in her arms. Behind them, a pack of twenty gnolls was closing fast.

The trailing giant — an older male with a silver beard — and his mammoth companion took the brunt of it as spears and arrows hammered into their backs. The group knew when to pitch in. The sleigh accelerated across the ice. Patches and Gundren took firing positions atop the sled and opened up, dropping gnolls with each volley. Fëanon launched a lance of popping fire from the front, blasting the pack apart in a shower of bone and debris. A second gnoll pack appeared on a flanking route across the ice and Fëanon redirected his next fireball to meet them.

The old giant and his mammoth turned to fight, sliding to a halt in a shower of snow. The gnoll pack slammed into them. The sleigh stopped and the group disembarked — Tark leaping directly from the sled into the gnolls at the giant's feet. The party fanned out to defend the sleigh as the rear of the pack turned on them.

The gnolls fell quickly under fireballs, thundercannon shots, and crossbow bolts, but their blades cut deep into Gundren when he pushed too far forward. Tark defended the old giant ferociously, dropping gnoll after gnoll, until the pack leader recognised his skill and engaged him directly, slashing with a black, notched blade. At the front of the second pack, a massive demonic gnoll with glowing red eyes tore into the group with abandon — until a burst of synaptic static split the air, killing half a dozen gnolls where they stood.

Patches fan-fired her revolver into the undead witherlings and dove in with her blades. The pack leader fell between her and Tark. The fight by the sleigh ended shortly after in an overwhelming flurry of spells and steel. The demonic gnoll was destroyed.

As the dust settled, the old giant eyed the group suspiciously. Bleeding heavily, he addressed them in a voice like a rolling boulder. "What now?"

Cirrus stepped forward and healed the giant. Buumi did the same. The tension eased. Across the ice, the other giants had stopped and turned, beginning to head back as the old one waved them over.
