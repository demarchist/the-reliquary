## After the Kill

The party stood over the corpse of Strigu, the warlock of [[Ceregorach]]. [[Galen Marek]] sent a man to escort Jake to safety, then had his people secure the perimeter and begin re-entering the half-burned [[Fallen Tower Inn]]. He sat at a table and had wine and a cheese board brought out.

[[Cirrus Al Kayan]] crouched over the body, produced a knife, and began cutting an eye from the dead warlock's skull. [[Horatio von Bumidere]] rushed forward to restrain him, but Cirrus was squirrely enough to finish the grisly work. He explained — a trophy to inspire fear in the remaining warlocks he was tasked to hunt. The group exchanged concerned glances, but Cirrus saw a connection between the loss of his own eyes and the quest from [[Selûne]].

Galen sipped his wine and invited the group to sit. [[Aliyah Faen Tlabbar]] took a glass, checked for poison by habit, and drank. [[Gundren Rockseeker]] demolished handfuls of cheese. [[Tarkathee Daru]] joined the assault on the platter.

Galen mentioned the cost of repairs and the slavers killed in the Red Belt — they were only part of the problem. The captives had been taken to the docks, bound for markets elsewhere. He had little intelligence, as the opposing guild was skilled at avoiding surveillance. As thanks for saving Jake and killing the Red Grin Killer, Galen offered a private room at [[The Long House]], an establishment in the Protector's Enclave. He told Gundren that The Pit in the Tower District would be more his speed, and the dwarf took him up on it.

## The Long House

The remaining group called a summoned mount and rode through the city. After asking directions from some Lathandarian acolytes — crime was suspiciously quiet with the Council approaching — they found The Long House, marked by a green door with a golden lion knocker.

Inside — a marble room with plush seating around a fountain filled with rose-petal water. A middle-aged woman in white silk, Rei, asked that everyone remove their shoes. She led them past mineral pools and treatment areas to a suite: a large bubbling mineral pool, four smaller adjunct baths, plush couches, recessed cushions, a fireplace, a sauna, and two king-sized bedrooms.

Patches claimed a room, changed, and headed for the steam room to meditate with the mercury of [[Eclipse]]. Everyone else stripped and sank into the mineral pool. Tark collected all the laundry, pestering Patches for hers. Cirrus ordered a "pick-me-up" from the crystal — presented with a platter of substances, he selected Kestrel-White, an inhaled powder. Patches emerged in a thick bath gown, grabbed the halfling leaf, and began smoking.

Cirrus spent the next hour pacing naked and sketching tower designs, amped up, while [[Fëanon Serinde]] watched in amusement and studied his books. Eventually Cirrus wound down and began drifting from pool to pool. In one of the baths, eyes closed, he saw brightness through his lids. He opened them to silver light — an obscured female figure within it. Selûne.

She bent down and brushed his face. Four new teeth grew into his gums. He gained ten pounds of muscle. His hair grew less patchy, his blemishes healed slightly. Cirrus gasped and professed his faith, mentioning the eye as a trophy. He sensed confusion and revulsion from the goddess at the offering. The vision ended, leaving the warlock grinning and contemplating his path.

## Lady Faye and Her Guests

At midday, the group headed to the docks. The carriage driver located the Bearded Lass II — a three-masted caravel painted blue. Tara, the half-orc, waited on the wharf beside a large blue longboat. Tark chose not to come — still processing the murders Lady Faye had ordered in Sundabar.

They rowed out and climbed aboard via rope net. Buumi hauled himself over effortlessly. Patches flipped over the railing onto the deck. Fëanon rolled over it and landed with a thump. Cirrus levitated above the railing and hung there until Buumi pulled him in with mage hand. [[Captain Dink]] descended from the poop deck, greeted the group, and led them to the captain's cabin — now Lady Faye's quarters.

Inside — a large desk with plush chairs, a screen divider, and at the back, a bed. Sitting in one of the chairs was a middle-aged redheaded woman of stark beauty in elegant, bejewelled clothing. [[Lady Faye]] smiled, extended a cream-coloured hand to each of them, and remarked how glad she was they had survived [[Baldur's Gate]]. Her blue eyes scanned the group.

An elf woman emerged from behind the divider — long blood-red hair, light caramel skin, faintly glowing green eyes, wearing a crimson gown trimmed in gold. Lady Faye introduced her as [[Lemerlais]], her new house wizard. Fëanon was enchanted — and intrigued, for he recognised the name of an elvish pirate queen from before the Spellplague, over a century ago. Cirrus kissed Lemerlais's hand and she greeted him as "Lord" Cirrus, already knowing the title.

A knife flew into the corner of the room — right by a man's head. [[Lo'Dan]] strode from the shadows, grinning, his dark purple features and white hair unmistakable. Buumi and Fëanon groaned. Lo'Dan tried to look innocent. He and Patches shared a warm greeting. Lady Faye introduced him as her liaison to [[Bregan D'Aerthe]].

They discussed the Council. Lemerlais reported that dark messengers from [[Zhengyi]] had been intercepted — promises of lichdom and undeath to any leader who would join him. Lady Faye's archaeologists had catalogued the vault beneath [[Neverwinter]] where the group had found the first fragment. Lo'Dan leaned in — agents in the [[Underdark]] had found a mural depicting an artifact matching the one beneath Neverwinter. A potential lead on another fragment of [[The Last Word]], in ruins matching those in the Troll Hills where the Alhoon had attacked.

Fëanon argued they did not need to fight — he was close to being powerful enough to simply flee. Lemerlais laid it out plainly: [[Atropos]] and [[Orcus]] would not stop with one planet. They would hunt anyone powerful enough to oppose them. Fleeing was temporary at best.

Sighing, Fëanon agreed. No one else should know about the artifacts. Lady Faye's agent would meet them in [[Gracksuluth]], the city of the Duergar. But no contact had been made with the Underdark since the Winter Solstice — clearly connected to Zhengyi's return.

The group agreed: after the [[Council of Helm's Hold]], they would descend beneath [[Faerun]] to Gracksuluth.
