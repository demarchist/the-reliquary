## The Small Hours

The group had spent the day working the land alongside Damaran farmers, earning their keep and their confidence. It was a period of peace and prosperity in the [[Bloodstone Lands]], ruled by a seemingly just King Frostmantle and Queen Concetinna. Midsummer was almost upon them, and the roads toward the capital of [[Helios]] were thick with traders come to enjoy the brief northern summer.

[[Tarkathee Daru]] woke in the small hours of the barn loft to a dread crawling up his spine and a heartbeat pulsing through the floorboards beneath him. [[Gundren Rockseeker]]'s snoring drifted up from the floor below. Tark reached into his bag of holding and withdrew the [[Flint Dagger of Li Xian]] — the cursed relic that had murdered his family. It hummed with dark power, its connection to its distant master a cold thread running out across the landscape. The thing burned his hand with cold. He redirected his Ki and turned the curse aside, then planted the weapon point-first into the barn floor, settled into a cross-legged meditation before it, and contemplated the dark implement he had carried for so long.

In the modest room given over to the Baron for the night, [[Cirrus Al Kayan]] occupied himself with prayer and an increasingly grim inspection of his deteriorating flesh, picking at flaking skin in the lamplight. In his [[Magnificent Mansion]], [[Fëanon Serinde]] sat with the [[Iron Tome]], transcribing a spell of Life Transference as he turned the problem of Cirrus's condition over in his mind. He had been thinking about the ritual in Al Askar — the invocation of the Last Word that ushered Selûne herself into the world — and had come to a troubling conclusion. The damage done to Cirrus was not merely physical. His very soul had likely been touched by it.

[[Patches]] wandered the fields alone, enjoying the soft swish of the golden wheat — a shorter variety than the tall grain of the western Sword Coast. The waxing moon broke through scattered clouds and bathed the rolling farmland in silver. She found a wide clearing of flattened wheat where something enormous had recently rested, carrying a pleasant scent she could not name — rich and warm, like buttered popcorn. Lunar butterflies drifted through the pressed stalks. She lay down in the clearing and watched the stars wheel.

High above the farmlands, a magnificent steed galloped through the night sky, its rider scanning the dark land below. The occasional warm light of a farmstead marked small islands of life in the black earth. To the north, the gathered glow of Helios lit the horizon. The rider had been drawn across the planes by dark dreams and prophetic visions — a great evil moving through these lands, a terrible bell tolling to raise the dead, and powerful allies waiting to be found. He turned his steed toward the distant city lights.

## The Shattering

Back in the barn loft, [[Vaketh]] stirred — the celestial passenger bonded in Al Askar, a shard of the god of Power — and offered to help Tark destroy the cursed weapon. Tark gathered his Ki and channeled it down through his prosthetic arm. Crimson light traced every line and joint of the white wood until it blazed. He lunged.

The strike blitzed through the [[Flint Dagger of Li Xian]] and obliterated it in an explosion of scarlet light. The blast tore out the entire back wall of the barn and sent shattered timber raining down across the moonlit fields. A spray of frost and ice from the detonation caught the silver light as the cursed blade was reduced to nothing.

Everyone woke. Cirrus shouted to keep it down and returned to his prayers. Gundren stirred, grunted a greeting at the dark, and went back to sleep. Buumi climbed from his bedroll and made his way to the ruined barn wall, looking out into the night. Fëanon noticed nothing within his mansion. JmJm did not show himself, occupied as he was with the farmer and his wife.

The rider high above had caught the flash of red light and the gleam of frost below. He dove.

A magnificent silvery Pegasus with a single spiraling horn from its brow touched down among the farm buildings in a rush of wings. Its rider slipped beneath a cloak of invisibility before the dust settled — but not before the group had caught a clear look at the mount and a glimpse of someone exceptionally well-equipped in the saddle.

Patches melted into the treeline. Tark turned invisible in the shadows of the ruined barn. Buumi stood alone in the splintered opening of the wall and felt very exposed.

Tark thought better of invisibility. He reappeared and reached out toward the Pegasus. The creature's serenity was remarkable — extraordinary, even. The blade that materialized silently at his back a moment later shattered that serenity completely. He raised his hands.

The invisible rider demanded to know why the barn had been destroyed and what harm was intended toward the innocent farmers. Tark explained, calmly: he had not meant to damage the building. He had sought only to destroy a cursed artifact. He had succeeded, if with rather more spectacle than intended. The rider cast *Detect Good and Evil*. The halfling's white-wood prosthetic arm blazed with celestial power. The Drow in the treeline bore a faint silvery celestial aura. He lowered his hood, became visible, and sheathed a beautiful filigreed blade.

He introduced himself as [[Cassian]], a distant traveler from the world of Oerth, drawn across the planes by prophetic visions of a dark apocalypse — tolling bells, rising dead, a great evil moving through a northern land. The group confirmed that they too had felt the disquiet: dark tones in the small hours, bells in dreams. The difference was that they had been living the waking version of those nightmares for some time.

Cassian introduced his mount as [[Uni]]. Buumi chuckled from the barn — she certainly was Unique. Tark accepted it as the Pegasus's name and reached out to pet her; she snorted and shuffled a step away. Patches dropped from the trees, her form shifted for speed with long gazelle-limbs, then released the spell and reassumed her normal proportions as the threat receded. She summoned [[Eclipse]], her mercury blade, as a mirror-disc and played a dot of moonlight for the great celestial horse. Cirrus finally emerged from the farmhouse, annoyed at the ongoing commotion — he gave Cassian a careful once-over, noting the worn boots and travel gear alongside unmistakably fine equipment. The business with the knife confirmed a serious set of skills. Gundren surfaced long enough to chuff a greeting.

## Broken Barn and New Company

[[Gundren]] produced the [[Ewer of Feasting]] and poured out a campfire and a full spread, and the group settled in for what remained of the night. They found Cassian was from Oerth and had heard the name of the great [[Mordenkainen]] without ever having had the misfortune of meeting the man. Fëanon raised a toast to his absence. Cassian spoke briefly of his purpose beyond this immediate crisis: he traveled the planes in search of his missing parents.

Tark gave Cassian fair warning about the objective waiting in Helios — every piece of intelligence gathered suggested that his old master [[Gaddock]] had been turned into a Vampire and was now terrorizing the Bloodstone Lands. They planned to stop him. Cassian asked to travel with them a while, to trace the source of his dark visions. The group agreed without hesitation.

Patches drifted away from the fire mid-conversation, doubled back through the dark, and crept up directly behind Cassian while he was still speaking. He gave no sign of noticing her at all. She smirked and sauntered back to her meditation, satisfied.

In the morning, the group repaired the barn. Fëanon's magical fabrication and the combined skills of the party made short work of the damage. Buumi pressed several hundred gold coins on the farmers for their trouble, and they mounted up and turned north.

## The Road to Helios

The procession that set out along the road to [[Helios]] drew stares from every traveler they passed. Wartigers. Mechanical lions. Owlbears. Nightmares. A Unicorn. Spectral steeds. Not a single ordinary horse. Uni was aghast and kept as far from JmJm's devil-horse as the road would allow. Steeden shot the celestial withering looks in return. The tension between the celestial and the fiendish was mutual and entirely undisguised.

The ride through the golden wheat fields was otherwise uneventful, the temperature climbing from comfortable to properly hot as the sun reached its zenith and Helios dominated the horizon.

The city was smaller than the great settlements of the western Sword Coast but built as an unambiguous fortress — concentric rings of grey stone shot through with the telltale green and scarlet veins of Bloodstone ore. The architecture was slab-like and brutalist, squares and right angles softened only by sweeping rooflines. At the heart of the city rose a rectangular keep bristling with dozens of turrets, each capped with colorful swirling teardrop shapes catching the summer light. Nearby, a great domed cathedral with radiant spires blazed like a second sun — the Cathedral of [[Lathander]], the Morninglord. The outskirts were thick with traders' pavilions, caravans, and tents, all come for the brief northern summer market.

Patches twisted her ring and shifted from Drow to Moon Elf. They moved through the colorful crowd, sampling exotic street food and delighting in the foreign dress and smells. Tark purchased an enormous sun hat from a vendor and massively overpaid, which caused the delighted vendor's pocket to be picked in the confusion. Cassian followed the thief silently, struck without being seen, and put an arrow through his leg. He scooped up the stolen coins and returned them to the vendor. The group slipped away before the city guard arrived.

## Into Helios

The group approached the main gate beneath the red and gold banners of the [[Crimson Dawn Knights]] and were informed entry to the city proper required papers. Cirrus produced his writ of nobility with visible affront and was directed to the courthouse to have it confirmed and his rights properly recognized. A young guard named [[Yugo]] was assigned to escort them. He proved a cheerful guide through the compact inner rings of the city, pointing out local drinking establishments and the popup restaurant tradition — vacant lots given over to clusters of small eateries for the summer market season.

As they neared the more affluent quarter, the crowd parted around a passing Palanquin. The pedestrians fell quiet and drew back to let it through, whispering in its wake. Yugo identified the occupants as the Thayans. Fëanon grinned and walked directly toward it.

A blade materialized at his neck. A [[Thayan]] warrior had appeared beside him — black plate, black cloak, a pallid bald head covered in black script tattoos matching those of the large white-robed bearers carrying the palanquin. The dark blade hovered an inch from the wizard's throat.

*"Back away, Elf."*

Fëanon backed away. The palanquin passed through the inner gate. The warrior murmured a word and vanished. Fëanon rejoined the group and turned the encounter over in his mind. The Thayans were presently enemies, and walking up to one of the Red Wizards on the street as an unknown had never been going to end differently. He would need a better approach next time.

At the courthouse, the noble line was gratifyingly shorter than the snaking peasant queue — until it became apparent the peasant queue was somehow moving faster. Cirrus waited. He waited a little longer. Then he pushed past an older, prosperous merchant woman at the front of the line. She planted herself bodily and stopped him cold. He turned the full weight of his attention on her and cowed her into submission. His writ was notarized in short order, and he formally accepted legal responsibility for his entourage.

## The Cathedral of Lathander

They turned their attention to the real work of their visit. The logical way to track a Vampire was to find its victims; the logical people to ask were priests. They headed for the Cathedral.

A crowd was being held back from the entrance by city guard. The group established that an incident had taken place the night before — [[Lord Aimu]], a Paladin of Lathander and nobleman, had broken into the Cathedral and stolen a relic. As someone divinely touched by Selûne, Cirrus had sufficient standing with the Morninglord's clergy to be admitted despite the lockdown.

Inside the great domed structure, altars and chapels sat beneath sweeping stained glass, the center dominated by a vast golden altar and intricate solar calendars traced in polished Bloodstone across the floor. A priest confirmed the nature of the stolen relic: a [[Sunsword]].

The group recognized it immediately. It was the Sunsword they had brought back from [[Barovia]] and donated to the Church of Lathander in Neverwinter. Somehow the blade had made its way east to the holy city. The vampire-killing weapon was gone.

They investigated the scene discreetly, noting the signs of a small struggle, while Cirrus offered the church their assistance in recovering Lord Aimu and the sword. The priest thanked him but explained that this was an internal matter — if he wished to involve himself, he would need to do so through the city's nobility. [[House Huron]] was hosting an event in four days. That would be the appropriate venue to make himself known.

They took their leave into the heat of the afternoon plaza, the stone radiating the day's warmth around them. The group began casting about for suitable accommodation when Tark noticed a flier on the ground at his feet and bent to pick it up.
