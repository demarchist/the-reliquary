## At the Shattered Spire

The group gathered at their table in the Shattered Spire and took stock.

The question of [[ilvara-mizzrym|Ilvara]] had been sitting unresolved since the scrying. A pursuit party led by the Drow commander was moving through the [[underdark|Underdark]], closing the distance. Attacking her directly inside [[gracksuluth|Gracksuluth]] would be disastrous — too many variables, too much noise. What the group needed was a battleground they could choose.

They turned the problem over and arrived at the same answer from several directions: Themberchaud. The fat dragon with his Flameguard grudge and his territorial temper. If they could feed him information that put Ilvara in his path, they could let those two problems solve each other.

To set it up, they needed to know where Ilvara was. The Drow scout Xalith had been watching the group from within the Spire — they had noticed her, and she had noticed them noticing. An arrangement, then.

[[aliyah-faen-tlabbar|Patches]] found Xalith at the bar enjoying a drink and approached. When the conversation turned to business, Xalith took a potion and sobered herself up quickly, the professional mask settling into place. Patches hired her for information on Ilvara's position and movements, paid a premium for discretion on top, and booked a morning appointment for further work. Ten gold for the information, five for silence, one more to hold the time.

Nearby, [[tarkathee-daru|Tark]] had been buying drinks and sampling the lagers while investigating the fighting pits the Spire maintained. He could not get drunk — his constitution made it impossible — but he was convincingly pretending otherwise when he flagged down the fight organizers. Two days hence, at what passed for noon in the lightless city: [[tarkathee-daru|Tark]] and his companions against Bolekk Barrison Del'Armgo and his retinue. Two hundred gold on the match.

[[snorbul|Snorbul]] mentioned, as the group wrapped up and prepared to leave, that he would like to go home eventually. The group confirmed: they would take him to [[blingdenstone|Blingdenstone]]. They just had other things to handle first.

## Gholbrorn's Lair

[[gundren-rockseeker|Gundren]] had left earlier to get ahead of the work on [[gwen|Gwen]], arriving back at their townhouse in the Gholbrorn's Lair before the others. He found [[jack-mayer-johnson-mathews|JmJm]] and [[doc|Doc]] already there, and [[jack-mayer-johnson-mathews|JmJm]] in a foul mood.

He had been forced to resurrect a Duergar — a circumstance the bard described at length, decrying the dreary nature of the city with considerable feeling — and the group's last large diamond was now gone.

The rest of the group arrived a few hours later. They settled in to plan.

The Ilvara problem needed a sharper approach. Luring the Drow commander into a fight with Themberchaud within the city was one option, but it meant controlling where the confrontation happened. [[aliyah-faen-tlabbar|Patches]] could arrange the setup if they had Ilvara's exact position, which Xalith would provide in the morning.

[[pickles|Mr. Pickles]] and [[snorbul|Snorbul]] ended up in conversation nearby, the Deep Gnome visibly unsettled by the cat — as most people were.

The smog outside had thickened over the past day, seeping in and making several people cough. [[gundren-rockseeker|Gundren]] began work on breathing masks, using the gas masks they had taken from the assassins in [[neverwinter|Neverwinter]] as a model. He intended to duplicate them using [[gracksuluth|Gracksuluth]]'s metallurgical resources.

## The Magnificent Mansion

[[feanon-serinde|Fëanon]], feeling hemmed in by the fixed walls of the townhouse, decided the place was insufficiently responsive to his requirements. He cast Magnificent Mansion.

There was a knock on the door shortly after.

The Gholbrorn's Lair proprietor had come to inform them that excessive spellcasting in the complex was a finable offense without a license. The group paid the fine, purchased the license, and the Duergar left. They complained about the city at some length.

The Magnificent Mansion provided a significant quality-of-life improvement. The smog of Gracksuluth could not enter. They ate well, slept in actual comfort, and awoke to a proper morning table within the extradimensional space.

## The Aboleth

The evening before, [[feanon-serinde|Fëanon]] had decided he could not pass up an opportunity that had been nagging at him since their arrival.

An Aboleth called Panoptes had taken up residence in the upper floors of the Gholbrorn's Lair — one of the establishment's more exotic guests. [[feanon-serinde|Fëanon]] wanted to speak with it about psionics. [[aliyah-faen-tlabbar|Patches]], restless herself and wanting to observe the Drow compound from the upper floors, went with him.

They entered the apartment to find the common room divided by a wall of standing water — an interior sea, shadowy humanoid shapes visible within. A thick organic smell. [[feanon-serinde|Fëanon]] approached and touched the water, sending out a mental query.

An Abolethic Thrall waded from the water and stepped out onto solid ground: a mutated humanoid, covered in mucus, its features partially dissolved into something else. It spoke on behalf of the Aboleth.

To [[aliyah-faen-tlabbar|Patches]], who stood watching, the conversation seemed to last only a moment — a brief and silent exchange. The mental communication moved faster than speech could follow.

The Aboleth was curious about the group. [[feanon-serinde|Fëanon]] was curious about learning psionic techniques. The Aboleth remained unconvinced until [[feanon-serinde|Fëanon]] mentioned [[the-last-word|The Last Word]] — and until Panoptes mentioned that the Sovereignty was already considering fleeing this reality entirely.

[[feanon-serinde|Fëanon]] said he had considered the same option himself. The difference was that [[atropos|Atropos]] would not stop at a single universe. Only The Last Word could end it. Which meant fleeing was not actually an escape.

The Aboleth went still — the particular stillness of a very old thing encountering a new thought. It told [[feanon-serinde|Fëanon]] it would speak with the others and be in contact.

The Thrall vomited a pearl onto the floor — a Sending Stone — and returned to the water.

[[aliyah-faen-tlabbar|Patches]] watched the whole thing in visible disgust.

## The Mutating Giant

After resting in the Mansion, the group set out the following morning. They had two immediate objectives: drop [[gundren-rockseeker|Gundren]] at the Anvilthew manufactory to begin properly working on [[gwen|Gwen]], and get [[aliyah-faen-tlabbar|Patches]] to her morning meeting with Xalith at the Shattered Spire.

[[gundren-rockseeker|Gundren]] went in under his Mediocris Dente disguise to avoid any complications with the local Duergar politics.

The manufactory was adjacent to a large plaza where a massive lava pool provided both light and a working heat source for the surrounding workshops. Many Duergar moved through the area, along with a detail of Stoneguard on patrol.

They were halfway across the plaza when the commotion started.

A Stone Giant came staggering around a far corner, roaring. Its hands were pressed against its head, as if trying to hold something in. It was clearly in agony.

Then it stopped trying to hold it in. It began to smash everything around it — crushing Duergar underfoot, hurling others into the lava pool. Its screaming had no words in it anymore. Just pain, and then something worse than pain.

The group rushed in to draw it away from the civilians.

As the fight developed, the giant began to change. Its face split slowly down the center, a second face pushing through. Horns and bone spines erupted from its back. The legs bent backward at the knees, snapping and reforming, the whole shape of the body reorganizing into something that no longer resembled a giant at all. Something that resembled [[demogorgon|Demogorgon]].

Attacks from the group landed harder than expected — the creature was already far past the fragile side of its constitution — but the transformation kept regenerating what should have been killing damage.

[[feanon-serinde|Fëanon]] cast Polymorph and felt the creature's will push back against the spell with considerable force. The magic took anyway. The giant compressed into the form of a Myconid Sprout, two feet tall and barely capable of nudging anyone's ankles.

[[gundren-rockseeker|Gundren]] called down [[arkto|Arkto]], swept the tiny form up, and flew it over the lava pool. He dropped it in.

The Myconid Sprout sank beneath the surface. The lava boiled. Then the Polymorph ended — the massive form of the demon-corrupted giant erupted from the molten stone, half its body blackened and cracking, roaring as it clawed its way back out. It flung handfuls of lava at the group, burning them badly, and charged.

The creature was slowing. Whatever it was becoming had spent something essential in the transformation, and every wound the group opened cost it more than it should have. It crashed through the group and slammed into the far wall, reaching for [[feanon-serinde|Fëanon]] and [[cirrus-al-kayan|Cirrus]].

[[feanon-serinde|Fëanon]] was done being methodical about it. He cast Finger of Death and the necrotic energy smashed into the thing — desiccating it, shriveling the flesh where it landed, finally dropping the massive form to the ground.

The body smoldered. The lava cooled around the edges of the scorch it had made. Stoneguard boots could be heard ringing on stone from every direction as reinforcements converged on the plaza.
