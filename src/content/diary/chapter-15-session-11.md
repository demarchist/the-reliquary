## The Crafting Layer

The bazaar of [[bytopia|Bytopia]] did not hold still.

[[cirrus-al-kayan|Cirrus]] and [[jack-mayer-johnson-mathews|JmJm]] walked through it the way one walks through a dream that has not decided what it is yet — past stalls and round-roofed cottages and whole villages stacked one atop another, the same hamlet living three times over in three different hours of the same afternoon. A bell tolled noon in one fold of the valley and dusk in the next. Children chased a hoop down a lane that, six feet on, became a lane where the children were already grown. Time did not so much pass on the Twin Paradises as *pool*, gathering in low places and running thin over the high ones, and the two travellers found that if they looked too long at any one corner of the market it began to disagree with the corner beside it.

So they chose. Somewhere beneath the idyll ran a harder seam — a layer of gear and forge-light and the patient clink of craft, where gnomish artificers bent over workbenches and the air smelled of hot metal and oiled leather. They turned their wills toward it, the way Cirrus had learned to turn his will toward a destination through the Well, and *wanted* to be there.

The plane obliged. The dreamlike layers settled by a degree, sliding into register like a lens finding focus. The crafting-layer thickened around them into something nearly solid — less a painting of a market now and more a market — and the gnomes at their benches looked up, unsurprised, and went back to their work.

They had come to shop. Rare weapons. Esoteric knowledge. The kind of thing one does not find on a shelf in [[helioglabulus|Helios]].

## The Oathbow

Hours went by — or what passed for hours in a place that kept no honest account of them — and JmJm found what he had been hunting.

It hung at the back of a weaponsmith's stall, unstrung, a longbow of dark heartwood worked with silver inlay that ran the length of each limb in a single unbroken line of script too old to read. An [[oathbow|Oathbow]]. The bard knew it the moment he saw it the way he knew a good audience from a bad one — by the weight of the silence around it.

The gnome who kept the stall did not want gold. Gold was easy. The artificers of Bytopia traded in things that were *hard*, and what the gnome wanted from JmJm was the one currency the bard had spent a life accumulating and never once been asked to part with: his legend. His bardic knowledge. The songs. The stories. The whole catalogue of a performer who had stood on the stage at the Refounding Festival and made an enemy fall in love with him in front of a roaring crowd.

JmJm gave it over. Not all of it — a man does not empty himself for a bow — but enough. He sang into the wood. He told it three stories it had never heard, and the silver script along its limbs caught a little light as he did, like ink drying.

Then he made the Oath, because that is the price the bow names and not the gnome. He swore to add to the weapon's legend — to carry it into stories worth telling, to make of it a thing future bards would haggle their own songs to hold — and, when he was through with the artifact, to give it to a wielder worthy of it, or to return it here, to this stall, to this layer, to the gnomes who had kept it. The words settled over the bow and over him both. He strung it. It fit his hand as though it had been waiting.

## The Deva's Warning

The soft gold light came up the slope toward them, and the [[callarduran-stonehands|Deva]] was with them again.

The Celestial had seen Azoo to her lair and returned, drifting over the meadow grass at the unhurried gait of a being that no longer troubled itself with walking. It greeted the pair once more in its flawless Draconic, and this time its message was plainer and less welcoming than its manner. They were needed, it said, on the Prime Material Plane. The errand that had brought them here was done. The world they had left was already turning toward something, and they should be standing in it when it arrived.

They acknowledged the angelic being. They did not argue. They simply did not, quite, *go*.

## The Witch at the Edge

[[cirrus-al-kayan|Cirrus]] had one more errand of his own.

He drifted toward the periphery of the gnomish community, where the bright market thinned into older lanes and the stalls stopped selling things one could hold. Here were the purveyors of ancient knowledge and quiet secrets — the gnomes who dealt in answers rather than goods — and Cirrus went among them with the focused, slightly furtive intensity of a man pursuing a private wound. Behind him, the Deva folded its hands and waited, its serene face acquiring, by slow degrees, the particular exasperation of a creature that has delivered a clear instruction and watched it ignored.

He found an old gnomish wise-woman at the very edge of the settlement — a sage so ancient her eyes had gone the milk-white of distance, tending a small fire that gave more light than it had any right to. To her, and only to her, Cirrus asked the question he had been carrying.

He wanted to know how to remove his shadow. To cut it away from himself entirely. To be a man who threw no darkness on the ground behind him.

The old witch was quiet for a long moment. Then she told him plainly that the thing he was asking after did not live where he thought it lived. To take a shadow from a man, one had to go and *find* it — and his had gone down deep into the [[shadowfell|Shadowfell]], into the cold mirror of the world, and waited for him there. But she warned him, in the patient way of someone who has watched fools dig their own graves with great enthusiasm, that to separate a shadow from its caster was tantamount to *killing* it. These things, she said, exist as they are meant to exist. A shadow is not a stain. It is a part. And meddling with the natural balance of one's own inner self — prying loose the dark half because one has decided to be only the bright one — normally ends badly for everyone within reach of the meddler.

Cirrus thanked the old witch. He did not promise her he would heed the warning, because he was Cirrus, and he had heard warnings before. He left her to her fire, and the Deva, with visible relief, finally got its two charges turned toward home.

## The Flaw in the Crystal

Back across the planes, the night at the [[aimu-estate|Aimu Estate]] had drawn long and quiet.

The [[crimson-dawn-knights|paladins]] held the grounds, posted in pairs at the porch and the orchard and the stable yard, and the rest of the group settled into the manor's undamaged front room to wait out the dark. [[lord-aimu|Lord Aimu]] had left the [[sunsword|Sunsword]] on the low table where he had laid it — and left it there, the party understood, as a kind of answer. The blade was theirs to take up if they would.

[[tarkathee-daru|Tark]] took it up.

He turned the [[ashbringer|Ashbringer]] over in the lamplight with the slow attention of a man re-meeting an old friend much changed. He had wielded this weapon once, at the Abbey of Krezk, when its sunbursts had chained through the ranks of the Wild Hunt and laid six inches of grey ash across the snow. He knew its weight. He let his eyes — the dead one behind the blindfold most of all — travel the length of the crystal, and for over an hour he simply *looked*.

At the end of the hour he found it. A flaw. So small it had survived every cataloguing the Church of [[lathander|Lathander]] had ever made of the relic: at the very edge of the crystal blade, near the guard, a subtle warping in the structure, like a single drop of melted glass that had cooled wrong. And set into the warp, faint as breath on a mirror, a delicate *fingerprint* — one whorl of someone's thumb pressed into the crystal at the moment it had been soft, and left there forever in a blade that was otherwise without fault.

Tark filed the detail away where he kept such things and did not speak of it. He settled instead into meditation, the Sunsword across his knees, and bent his discipline toward attuning to the blade. The radiance answered him by slow degrees over the hours that followed — recognising, perhaps, the hand that had carried it through Krezk — banking warm against his palms as the night drew on.

## The Tree to the North

It was while he meditated that [[lady-isabel|Izmakella]] put her head into the parlor.

She had taken her human shape again for the comfort of the household, Lady Isabel once more in a borrowed gown, and she lingered in the doorway watching the small monk with the frank curiosity of a dragon who has decided a thing is interesting. Her eyes went to the white-wood arm — the prosthetic hand resting open on the crystal blade, its filigree of red metal dark and quiet now.

It must have been a great gift, she remarked.

Tark agreed that it was. It had once been a staff, he told her — a gift from his old master [[moot|Moot]], reshaped around the wound where his hand had been.

The dragon-lady chuckled, low and knowing, and asked an idle-sounding question that was not idle at all. This *Moot* — had he happened to travel in the company of a number of golden canaries?

Tark confirmed that he had. Seven of them. One still rode with Tark himself.

Izmakella smiled the smile of someone watching the last piece of a thing settle into place. Then the wood, she said, must have been a gift from the Great Tree to the north — the holy heart of [[bloodstone-city|Bloodstone City]], the white tree at the highest terrace whose roots ran down into [[great-tree-of-bahamut|Yggdrasil]] itself. The [[great-tree-of-bahamut|Tree of Bahamut]]. She said the name as though it explained the canaries and the staff and the man who carried both, and in her telling it did: the white-wood Tark had worn as a hand all these months, the staff Moot had pressed on him in honour after the cult attack at the festival, had been cut, somewhere far back along its history, from the living wood of the Platinum Dragon's own tree.

She watched the monk a little longer with that same curiosity, said nothing more of it, and left him to his meditations. The implication sat in the quiet room behind her: that [[vaketh|Vaketh]]'s crimson power had been running, all this time, through a branch of the holiest tree in the [[bloodstone-lands|Bloodstone Lands]] — and that Tark's road had been bending toward Bahamut's Tree long before any of them had known the Tree existed.

## A Quiet House

The others kept their own counsel through the small hours.

[[aliyah-faen-tlabbar|Patches]] moved through the manor like a draft, soundless, opening cabinets and lifting the lids of jewel-boxes and running her fingers across every precious stone the Aimu household owned. [[eclipse|Eclipse]] was hungry. The sentient obsidian blade fed on the *Luster* of fine gems — the deep inner light that made a stone worth having — and Patches drew it out of them one by one with a touch, leaving behind dull glass and a handful of charges restored to the weapon at her hip. She was careful, and quiet, and entirely without remorse. The Aimu silver would notice the difference in the morning. By then she would be elsewhere.

[[cassian|Cassian]] spent the same hours in the stable yard with [[uni|Uni]]. The winged unicorn had taken hurts in the day's battle that the ranger would not leave untended; he had the [[crimson-dawn-knights|paladins]] lay their healing hands on her flanks until the worst of it closed, and then he stayed, brushing her down in the lamplight with the long unhurried strokes of a man who trusted his hands more than his words. The celestial mare leaned into the brush. The night was, for a little while, almost peaceful.

It did not last.

## Thunder in the Valley

A rapid series of booms rolled across the vale — a peal of distant thunder out of a clear, moonlit sky.

The paladins came on guard at once. [[aliyah-faen-tlabbar|Patches]] and [[cassian|Cassian]] left what they were doing and crossed to the windows, looking out across the dark valley toward the sound. There was nothing to see but the silver lake and the black wall of the mountains and the moon riding high and full above the pass. The booms came again, closer.

In the same breath, [[cirrus-al-kayan|Cirrus]] and [[jack-mayer-johnson-mathews|JmJm]] stepped out of a doorway of moonlight onto the estate's lawn, back from [[bytopia|Bytopia]] at last — and felt the tension in the grounds before either of them had said a word. The [[callarduran-stonehands|Deva]] had been right. Something was afoot. Something had been waiting for the world to turn, exactly as the angel had said, and it had not waited for them to be ready.

[[cassian|Cassian]] ran for [[uni|Uni]]. [[aliyah-faen-tlabbar|Patches]] gathered herself to leap out into the night and scout the disturbance, with [[tarkathee-daru|Tark]] rising from his meditation to shadowstep after her.

Then a voice came across the valley — a single chanting voice, resonant with power, rolling up out of the dark trees on the far slope and filling the whole vale with the cadence of a working being spoken aloud.

The ground cracked and heaved.

## The Quake

The [[the-thayans-attack|earthquake]] tore through the estate like a living thing.

Stone sheared. Beams snapped. The fine two-storey house that had stood at the back of [[frostmoore-vale|Frostmoore Vale]] for a generation began, in the space of seconds, to come apart — walls folding inward, floors pitching, the slate roof sliding off its rafters in a roaring cascade. Everyone in the building threw themselves toward the nearest opening and leapt, as the mansion buckled and caved behind them.

[[horatio-von-bumidere|Buumi]] did not make it clear. A section of collapsing wall came down across the big man and buried him, and he cried out from beneath the rubble. Other [[crimson-dawn-knights|paladins]] went down the same way, clobbered by falling stone and pinned in the wreckage, while [[lord-aimu|Lord Aimu]] moved among his men in the dust and the moonlight, laying on healing and blessings, hauling his order back onto its feet.

[[aliyah-faen-tlabbar|Patches]] reached Buumi first. She set her shoulder against the broken stone and heaved it off him, shifting the slabs aside to drag the monk free —

— and a lightning bolt came down out of the sky and blasted them both. Patches read it coming in the prickle of the air and rolled clear; the bolt scorched the ground where the Drow had been an instant before. Above the treeline, lit silver by the full moon, a red-robed wizard floated in the open air with a wand levelled down at the ruin of the estate. A [[red-wizards-of-thay|Red Wizard of Thay]]. He marked his work, and then he darted away into the night before anyone could close the distance.

## Gaddock

[[tarkathee-daru|Tark]] went after him.

He shadowstepped through the dark after the floating Thayan and struck — and in the same instant [[aliyah-faen-tlabbar|Patches]]' crossbow sang and a poisoned bolt took the wizard in the body, the Drow venom dropping him out of the air unconscious before he had finished his next word.

But another presence had stepped through the shadow behind the monk.

Powerful arms closed around Tark from behind — cold, terribly strong, locking him in a grapple he could not at once break. He twisted in the grip to see his assailant, and the world narrowed to a single face. Pale. Emaciated. Bearded. The face of his long-lost master, the man who had taught a small orphan how to stand and how to strike and how to be good in a world that gave him no reason to be.

[[gaddock|Gaddock]].

Tark saw two things in the same heartbeat. The first was the pain in his master's eyes — a grief so old and so total it had worn grooves in the man's face. The second was the horn. A spike of black crystal had been driven into Gaddock's skull, jutting from his head like the broken-off tip of a lance, and within the dark stone something *moved* — an inner smoke that swirled and curled against the crystal's facets, alive, patient, in command. This was the leash. This was the thing that held him.

The vampire bared his fangs. Tears ran down the emaciated face, bright in the moonlight, falling freely as he locked eyes with the student he loved — and then Gaddock bent his head to Tark's throat and bit, and began to feed.

## The Arcane Hand

[[horatio-von-bumidere|Buumi]] flew to help him.

He took to the air the instant he was free of the rubble — and was met in the dark by the [[vampire-knight-of-frostmoore|Armored Vampire]] from earlier in the evening, the lance-knight come back to the valley out of whatever shadow had carried him from the day's defeat. The two collided above the broken estate, and Buumi's path to his friend was cut.

So [[jack-mayer-johnson-mathews|JmJm]] reached for the answer he always reaches for.

He pulled the [[mysterious-glove|mysterious glove]] from his past onto his hand, finger by finger, and an enormous translucent Arcane Hand bloomed out of the night air and drove itself between [[tarkathee-daru|Tark]] and his vampire master — a wall of force the size of a horse, prying the feeding undead back off the monk's throat. The grapple broke. Tark tore free, came around on the balls of his feet, and turned every ounce of his grief into a strike meant to put his master down.

Gaddock did not let him land it. The vampire dropped — sinking away to the forest floor below, retreating into the dark of the trees — and Tark, bleeding from the throat, went down after him without a moment's hesitation. Into the mountain. Into the forest. After the man he had sworn to free or to end.

## The Valley of Battle

He came down into the opening engagement of a war.

A horde of ghouls was already pouring down the mountainside toward the estate — grey and loping and many, summoned out of the dark earth by more [[red-wizards-of-thay|Red Wizards]] working their castings somewhere back in the trees. The [[crimson-dawn-knights|paladins]] braced to meet them. [[jack-mayer-johnson-mathews|JmJm]] caught sight of an Oni stepping out from behind a tree to hurl a spell across the field before sliding back into cover — a flicker of the same shapeshifting menace that had haunted the group for chapters.

[[cirrus-al-kayan|Cirrus]] moved on the one enemy already down. He Farstepped across the field to the unconscious [[red-wizards-of-thay|Red Wizard]] Patches had dropped, and drew out a [[bag-of-devouring|grim bag]] from his vestments — not a vault but a *mouth*, a horrible thing whose interior was all jaws. He slung it over one shoulder, took the Thayan's slack hand, and fed it into the maw. The wizard was drawn in and consumed, gone, swallowed whole into the dark between somewheres.

A peal of cold laughter answered from the trees.

It rolled across the valley from no body anyone could see — and then arcane power lashed out of the dark and slammed into Cirrus, a blast from an unseen foe who had watched the priest devour an ally and found it, apparently, amusing. The vale filled with the sounds of the thing that had begun: the dry shrieking of ghouls, the chanting of the Red Wizards of [[red-wizards-of-thay|Thay]], the crack and flare of spells in the dark.

The Thayans had come to [[frostmoore-vale|Frostmoore Vale]] in the night, and the battle for the [[ashbringer|Sunsword]] was joined.
