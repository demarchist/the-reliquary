[[tarkathee-daru|Tark]] had left the group at [[the-establishment|the Establishment]] and was crossing the town square when a portal tore open in the air before him.

## The Freed Slaves

The slaves they had freed in [[menzoberranzan|Menzoberranzan]] — led to safety by [[bregan-daerthe|Bregan D'Aerthe]] scouts — poured through the rift into the [[selunia|Barony]], Red Slaad snapping at their heels. [[tarkathee-daru|Tark]] engaged both creatures just as the rest of the group rushed from [[the-establishment|the Establishment]], drawn by the commotion.

Once the last of the freed slaves stumbled through, the portal was sealed by a Wall of Force and dismissed by the caster — [[kimmuriel|Kimmuriel]]. Three Slaad were subdued and taken to [[feanons-tower|Feanon's Tower]], and a rider dispatched to the [[rockseeker-manufactory|Manufactory]] to commission adamantine cages for the beasts. The townsfolk had begun calling the tower the *Tower of Slumber*.

The newly freed slaves were seen to by Li and the local Paladins. Thirty chose to remain in [[kossenberg|Kossenberg]]. Ten Wood Elves headed north into [[the-moonwood|the Moonwood]] to join the clans there. The rest set out along the Tradeway. Mark, one of [[kossenberg|Kossenberg's]] town elders, swore that the village would help the newcomers build homes should they wish to stay and supply those who wished to move on.

Of the sixty souls freed, twenty were nearly comatose — barely comprehending their change in fortune or weeping with disbelieving joy. Half the remainder heard [[cirrus-al-kayan|Cirrus']] call and headed toward the Fane of [[selune|Selûne]] for shelter. The rest were guided to [[the-establishment|Over the Moon]], where [[aliyah-faen-tlabbar|Patches]] promised free lodging and food for the week.

[[tarkathee-daru|Tark]] watched his friends tend to the desperate folk with quiet suspicion, searching for signs of exploitation. All he found was sincerity. Perhaps his friends were not all bad.

## Kimmuriel's Report

The [[bregan-daerthe|Bregan D'Aerthe]] [[drow-faction|Drow]], having shed their disguises for those of overly handsome Elves and Humans, moved into [[the-establishment|the Establishment]] and headed downstairs to gamble against the Dwarves.

[[kimmuriel|Kimmuriel]] sat with the group and explained what had happened after their departure. [[menzoberranzan|Menzoberranzan]] was in chaos. [[vizerion-devir|Vizerion]] had attacked the city directly — distributing enchanted clay urns that continually dispensed sickening fog through the streets. The wizard had used his ritual in the Clawrift to siphon an enormous amount of energy, somehow captured it, and then fled with a veritable army of Slaad under his control.

[[cirrus-al-kayan|Cirrus]] attempted to recruit [[kimmuriel|Kimmuriel]] to the worship of [[selune|Selûne]]. The psionicist declined the religion but accepted a dwelling in the [[selunia|Barony]] as a gift — and, more practically, as a [[bregan-daerthe|Bregan D'Aerthe]] safe house. It would be known as the Twilight Lodge.

[[kimmuriel|Kimmuriel]] was invited to [[feanons-tower|Feanon's Tower]] and accepted. [[tarkathee-daru|Tark]] invited him swimming at the old dock. The psionicist did not respond. [[tarkathee-daru|Tark]] reminded him of the invitation, as did [[feanon-serinde|Feanon]]. [[kimmuriel|Kimmuriel]] said nothing.

[[aliyah-faen-tlabbar|Patches]] asked after [[jarlaxle|Jarlaxle's]] operation and was told it had been successful — in [[jarlaxle|Jarlaxle's]] favour. She took this to mean that Tiago Baenre had potentially fallen in the process.

## Patches' Truth

With the psionicist gone, [[aliyah-faen-tlabbar|Patches]] turned to the group and offered to answer any and all questions — honestly, as a show of good faith. They asked about Tiago, about [[finn|Finn]], about her feelings for [[horatio-von-bumidere|Buumi]]. She answered them all.

Afterward, she took [[finn|Finn]] on a tour of the [[selunia|Barony]], showing him the towers and the surrounds. [[tarkathee-daru|Tark]] headed north alone. [[feanon-serinde|Feanon]] returned to his tower to prepare for the arrival of Lady Avelyere and to begin work on another clone. [[cirrus-al-kayan|Cirrus]] headed to his Fane with [[finn|Finn]] and [[aliyah-faen-tlabbar|Patches]] to tend to the newly arrived refugees, speaking to each before retiring to his inner sanctum.

[[finn|Finn]] expressed concern about his safety from [[vladalma|Valdalma]]. [[aliyah-faen-tlabbar|Patches]] asked [[cirrus-al-kayan|Cirrus]] if he could take them to [[svartalfheim|Svartalfheim]]. He agreed — after he had rested.

## Tark's Afternoon

[[tarkathee-daru|Tark]] came across two children poking at a badger den with a stick. He ended up giving the children his magical staff and checked the hole himself, finding a pregnant badger. He moved the children off to leave the creature in peace — though the boy ran off with the staff, forcing [[tarkathee-daru|Tark]] to retrieve it later.

He wandered to the docks. [[feanon-serinde|Feanon]] visited the monk as a gaseous form and gave him a wand before departing without ceremony.

## The Last Mindslayer

[[aliyah-faen-tlabbar|Patches]] brought [[finn|Finn]] to [[feanons-tower|Feanon's Tower]] to properly introduce the young [[drow-faction|Drow]] to her wizard friend. [[finn|Finn]] resolved that he would learn enough to defend himself rather than relying on others.

As they climbed, [[feanon-serinde|Feanon]] was interrupted by a knock at his laboratory door. He opened it to find a man he had never met — who introduced himself as the new janitor. [[stason-jatham|Stason Jatham]].

[[feanon-serinde|Feanon]] eyed the man uneasily, recognising almost immediately what he was — the Last Mindslayer, brought by [[aliyah-faen-tlabbar|Patches]] to keep an eye on the wizard and clean up. [[aliyah-faen-tlabbar|Patches]] and [[stason-jatham|Stason]] passed one another without acknowledgement as she walked into the lab with [[finn|Finn]].

[[finn|Finn]] and [[feanon-serinde|Feanon]] spoke about apprenticeship. The young [[drow-faction|Drow]] was intrigued but non-committal.

## Kimmuriel at the Dock

With no sign of [[kimmuriel|Kimmuriel]] at the swimming hole, [[tarkathee-daru|Tark]] headed back to [[the-establishment|Over the Moon]] and found the psionicist among the crowd of gamblers in the basement. He convinced the curious [[drow-faction|Drow]] to follow him back to the old dock.

There, [[tarkathee-daru|Tark]] dived beneath the water and returned with a box — pulling out an eclectic collection of magical weapons, mundane buttons, and wondrous items. [[kimmuriel|Kimmuriel]] was thoroughly puzzled. The monk began to speak about his friends and their accomplishments, singing their praises to the bemused psionicist.

[[kimmuriel|Kimmuriel]] took his leave from the curious monk, leaving [[tarkathee-daru|Tark]] to enjoy the sunset.

## Patches and Tark

Over at the [[keep-of-thunder-bluff|Keep]], [[aliyah-faen-tlabbar|Patches]] visited [[horatio-von-bumidere|Buumi]] and asked for his help speaking with [[tarkathee-daru|Tark]]. As they walked, she asked about the arranged marriage and the debutante ball. [[horatio-von-bumidere|Buumi]] was unenthusiastic about the whole affair but cagey as to why, blushing as he looked at [[aliyah-faen-tlabbar|Patches]].

After an hour, [[tarkathee-daru|Tark's]] reverie was interrupted by the two of them arriving at the lakeside. As the sun set, [[aliyah-faen-tlabbar|Patches]] apologised for lying and promised never to do so again — though she admitted she would probably fail from time to time. She swore that if [[tarkathee-daru|Tark]] asked, she would always answer truthfully.

[[tarkathee-daru|Tark]] still felt deceived — as though he was being tricked into doing bad things by his friends. But [[horatio-von-bumidere|Buumi]] pointed toward the [[selunia|Barony]] — bustling with freed slaves and refugees — and reminded him that they had done good in the world. Could they do better? Yes. And they should strive to.

[[tarkathee-daru|Tark]] was mollified. Somewhat.

They stayed up into the small hours, talking on the lakeshore under the shining moon.

## Svartalfheim

At dawn, [[cirrus-al-kayan|Cirrus]] woke them on the shores of the lake, proclaiming he was ready for the journey. They returned to the [[selunia|Barony]] and collected the group for a *Cirruscation* — stopping by Ivan the baker for breakfast. [[tarkathee-daru|Tark]] detoured to [[kossenberg|Kossenberg]] to reclaim his staff from eight-year-old Ben, giving the boy a Wand of Lightning Bolts in exchange.

[[cirrus-al-kayan|Cirrus]] plane shifted the group to [[svartalfheim|Svartalfheim]].

They arrived split — everyone except [[aliyah-faen-tlabbar|Patches]] and [[finn|Finn]] found themselves in a black marble waiting room adorned with onyx furniture. [[aliyah-faen-tlabbar|Patches]] and [[finn|Finn]] found themselves in an office, across an onyx desk from a [[drow-faction|Drow]] man in flowing white robes.

He introduced himself as [[magistrate-delroy|Magistrate Delroy]].

His skin was plum-coloured, his eyes a vibrant pink, his hair tinged blue. The black marble architecture was threaded through with silver and mithril. [[magistrate-delroy|Delroy]] inquired about their origin and noted, with displeasure, that neither of them was dead and both hailed from [[toril|Toril]] and the [[lolth|Lolthsworn]] city of [[menzoberranzan|Menzoberranzan]]. He complained that they brought the Curse of Wendonai to the city of [[dokkar|Dokkar]].

[[aliyah-faen-tlabbar|Patches]] made her case — offering to leave while [[finn|Finn]] remained under the city's protection. [[magistrate-delroy|Delroy]] mulled the proposal and agreed that both could stay within the first level of the city and the Penitent Gardens while he consulted the elders.

To press her point, [[aliyah-faen-tlabbar|Patches]] summoned [[eclipse|Eclipse]] — a blade of moonlight and quicksilver — proving beyond doubt that she was a follower of [[eilistraee|Eilistraee]] and no servant of [[lolth|Lolth]]. [[magistrate-delroy|Delroy]] was visibly taken aback. He swore that both she and [[finn|Finn]] were his guests and under his protection.

The group gathered in the waiting room and [[cirrus-al-kayan|Cirrus]] was insufferably smug — having brought them to [[dokkar|Dokkar]]. [[magistrate-delroy|Delroy]] welcomed the group and noted that each of them was a Prime with their original body. [[tarkathee-daru|Tark]] asked how old the magistrate was. [[magistrate-delroy|Delroy]] replied that he was a long, long dead Elf and, as a Magistrate, was unaffiliated with any clan.

They followed [[cirrus-al-kayan|Cirrus]] out into the gleaming city of the Dark Elves. The population was predominantly [[drow-faction|Drow]], though Elves, Dwarves, and beings of every type walked the streets. [[aliyah-faen-tlabbar|Patches]] and [[finn|Finn]] drew stares — she returned them, juggling her knives for the onlookers.

At the base of the shining citadel, the group looked upward. The many tiers of the white marble city ascended to the ceiling of a massive cavern, the roof bisected by a great gash that opened to the stars of the heavens. Here, in the plane of Ysgard, they gazed upon the realm of [[eilistraee|Eilistraee]], goddess of moonlight.
