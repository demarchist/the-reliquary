## The Dark Iron City

[[hemeth|Hemeth]] took the vessel out onto the last stretch of the [[darklake|Darklake]] before the Duergar city.

The group asked him a number of questions and worked on [[gundren-rockseeker|Gundren]]'s disguise.

They realized that the bureaucracy of the city would most likely be the real headache and decided to lay low, using Hemeth's contacts in the Grey Ghosts to get illicit papers.

The vessel approached the Chain Towers defending the city from approach via the [[darklake|Darklake]].

Lake-floor to cavern ceiling, the massive towers were grand fortifications covered in metal nodules, each glowing softly with a spot of warm light.

Duergar could be seen patrolling the tower fortifications and massive grey-skinned giants, wearing finely crafted leathers and wielding long lances of crystal, were stationed at each of the towers — Stone Giants.

[[tark|Tark]] flashed the light signal to the closest tower as they passed, per Hemeth's instruction.

As they passed the Chain Towers, they noted correctly that massive chains could be raised from below the water to defend the bay, and that the towers themselves were covered in carvings — constructs that would come alive to defend them.

[[fey|Fey]] was impressed and a touch confused until Hemeth welcomed the group to the Duergar capital of [[gracksuluth|Gracksuluth]].

The stench of the place was overwhelming. Sulfur, oil, coal, and the harsh tang of metal and smog were everywhere. Even the water showed a disgusting sheen of chemicals on its surface as the ship approached the main docks.

Hemeth veered away from the main docks, a longing look on his face as he took his ship far away from the hustle and bustle of the Blade Bazaar, the largest market in the city.

The paddle steamer trundled through the waterways, underneath massive iron and steel cranes and past warehouses.

Hemeth took the ship to a run-down dock, out front of an abandoned warehouse.

[[snorbul|Snorbul]] leaped to the ramshackle dock and Hemeth tossed him the ropes, securing the ship.

[[cirrus-al-kayan|Cirrus]] noted a Duergar walking through the abandoned industrial park toward the ship and pointed it out to the group. They saw no one and called out to the invisible Duergar.

A Duergar with a bushy, curly beard, a perfectly bald head, a bowler hat, and a heavy overcoat and boots became visible — amber eyes glowing softly beneath bushy white eyebrows.

Hemeth called out to the newcomer, naming him Chadle.

Chadle turned out to be Hemeth's contact with the city underbelly, his fence with whom he smuggled in art and other contraband.

Chadle saw the group and saw an opportunity, and the group saw their way into the city.

They negotiated with the Duergar and purchased three sets of work papers off him immediately, and paid to have [[snorbul|Snorbul]] registered as a legitimate slave to ensure no other Duergar would capture the poor Svirfneblin.

[[snorbul|Snorbul]] was assured by [[fey|Fey]] that they would be taking him home, and the deep gnome believed him.

Chadle invited the group to dine with him that evening at the Shattered Spire, where he would deliver the rest of their papers and a middling-noble ID badge to [[gundren-rockseeker|Gundren]], whom Chadle took for a foreign Duergar.

Hemeth and Chadle headed toward the Shattered Spire, mentioning they would return shortly to move the ship to a more permanent dock.

The group headed off toward the Gholbrorn's Lair, the large complex of apartments, shops, and restaurants for foreigners in the city, as described by [[patches|Patches]] and Hemeth.

The group made good progress through the warehouse district and along the waterfront.

[[patches|Patches]] pointed out the smithing complex of Clan Anvilthew, enticing [[gundren-rockseeker|Gundren]] to come back later to fix Gwen. Patches also mentioned she had contacts in the clan and wished to speak with them.

They passed a massive ore refinery, then the smithing complex of Clan Steelshadow, the largest weapons makers in the city.

Passing these massive factories, the group noted the sheer amount of slave labor present in [[gracksuluth|Gracksuluth]].

Unlike the Drow, who appeared to take a sadistic pleasure in tormenting their slaves, the Duergar approach was far more practical.

The slaves looked dirty but well fed and quite hardy, undergoing intense manual labor but kept in reasonable condition to ensure efficiency.

The group noted that many of the slaves were working with harsh chemicals. One even fell into a large pile of some substance.

As the slave thrashed, two Duergar rushed forwards and hauled him out, dragging him quickly to a water-bath where he was drenched.

It was not due to any caring nature — it was to protect an investment and ensure efficiency.

Most of the slaves appeared to be Orcs, Goblins, and other Underdark denizens, though the occasional human, elf, or other surface dweller was spotted.

As the group moved through the city, they noticed another population — the homeless vagrants present in every city.

Strangely, that population in [[gracksuluth|Gracksuluth]] appeared to be entirely Derro, the strange, mad, mutant Dwarf-kin.

Passing the massive Duergar complexes, the group noted the deceptive scale of the place — all smaller rock formations had long been cleared away, leaving only truly massive stone support pillars reinforced by Duergar engineering. Fortifications and architectural complexes sprawled around each support pillar in an industrialization none of the party had ever witnessed, save [[patches|Patches]].

Passing through the Blade Bazaar, the group stopped at a small clothing store and picked up Duergar clothing for [[gundren-rockseeker|Gundren]] and [[tark|Tark]], who since arriving in the city had been operating under the guise of Tark-Spidermenn.

As [[fey|Fey]] spotted their destination, he pushed through the crowded market, passing Deep Gnomes with Earth Elemental bodyguards, Drow in their distinctive [[piwafwi|Piwafwi]] cloaks, and even a human man. Then he came face to face with something he had only ever heard described — he nearly walked into a large floating glass tank lined with intricate metalwork.

A great face resolved itself within the cloudy liquid of the tank — an Aboleth.

[[fey|Fey]] and the creature exchanged a few words telepathically, but the creature quickly lost interest and the floating tank moved on.

[[fey|Fey]] rushed to catch up and implored it for an audience. The creature reluctantly agreed, letting Fey know that it was staying in the Gholbrorn's Lair and would be there for several more days. A name resolved itself in Fey's mind: Panoptes of the Sovereignty.

The group pushed on and arrived at the foreigners' residences, entering the massive stone column that made up the majority of the complex.

Inside, they found a large courtyard — the interior of the pillar had been hollowed out to a height of at least 150 feet, with buildings constructed in ascending levels atop one another.

The cheapest residences occupied the lower floors; cafes and restaurants covered the ground floor with tables and seating areas arranged around warm braziers.

The sulfurous smell of the exterior city was somewhat lessened here, and foreigners could be seen enjoying food and drink.

The absence of music was stark. The surface world was filled with the sounds of street performers and bards. Down here, only the constant hum of machinery, the clang of metal, and the chatter of voices filled the air.

## Building Two

The group ascended almost to the top floor of the Gholbrorn and along one wall found a classy establishment and entered.

The interior was dark granite, immaculately crafted and trimmed with black marble. The furniture was perfectly made from stone and hardwoods — likely Zurkhwood.

A large hearth crackled against one wall, a Deep Gnome relaxing in one of the larger chairs while reading a book. A Duergar in fine clothes drank from a dark cup at one of the tables.

The common area was a large T — one wing held the hearth, tables and chairs; the other functioned as the entryway with a long counter behind which sat a female halfling with greying hair.

Down the hallway, four staircases led to townhouses.

They approached the counter and spoke with Nedda Bigheart, an enslaved halfling whose large eyes carried a weariness and whose face wore a subtle, empty smile.

The group grew concerned and discovered the situation of her enslavement — she worked for the owner of the establishment, paying off her slave debt and working toward buying her freedom. She said she still had many years to go and grew quiet when pressed for further details.

The group rented one of the townhouses from her and headed inside to drop off their things.

Doc B, JmJm, and [[cirrus-al-kayan|Cirrus]] remained behind as the others decided to head to Clan Anvilthew before dinner with Chadle.

They had Nedda summon two rickshaws for the group.

While leaving the Gholbrorn, [[patches|Patches]] spotted four Drow on the level above them, guarding an apartment complex — she recognized it as the Drow "Embassy," a permanent rental held by all major noble houses of [[menzoberranzan|Menzoberranzan]].

On the ground floor, the group got into their Orc-drawn rickshaws and began heading through the city.

As they left the Blade Bazaar, they all turned in their seats as a massive rumbling emanated behind them.

One of the massive sets of Adamantine doors separating the city's districts was opening, the engraved faces of the door covered in defensive constructs.

The dozens of guards by the gate fanned out to allow a massive creature to waddle through.

A massive Red Dragon moved forward, its body rippling oddly with fat, accompanied by many Duergar in red robes and armor.

The rickshaw driver named it as [[themberchaud|Themberchaud]] the Wyrmsmith — the dragon apparently fueling and lighting the forges of the city.

[[themberchaud|Themberchaud]] waddled into the smithing complex of Clan Steelshadow.

## Anvilthew

The rickshaws returned the group to the docks far faster than moving on foot, as they could use designated pathways for transport.

Arriving at the Anvilthew factory, an important-looking Duergar exited the building as the rickshaws approached, sensing wealthy customers.

He introduced himself as Legran an Anvilthew and led the group inside.

After a quick negotiation, [[patches|Patches]] dropped the name Bregan D'Aerthe and described what she needed for their infiltrators.

Understanding, Legran led the group to two artificers who specialized in that equipment — Patches explaining that the Duergar had developed much of her gear.

[[patches|Patches]] asked Legran to look into an old contact of hers with the clan, a skilled slave laborer named [[claudine|Claudine]].

Legran took his leave to investigate.

[[gundren-rockseeker|Gundren]] began negotiating to rent a workshop and an assistant to begin repairing Gwen, the mechanical lion.

As the resupply for [[patches|Patches]] went through, with Duergar craftsmen bringing in crates of raw materials and platinum changing hands, [[fey|Fey'Anon]] opened his bag of holding to drop the supplies in — and a blast of sand, dust, and wind hit him in the face.

The strange whirlwind of sand blew past the group, out into the hallway, and disappeared quickly from sight.

[[tark|Tark]] immediately pursued, as did [[gundren-rockseeker|Gundren]], without stopping to think.

In the workshop, the group finished packing the supplies as guards arrived at the commotion.

The bottom of [[fey|Fey]]'s stomach dropped when he realized what had happened. He called out to the guards and the Duergar that he believed a Mummy Lord had just been let loose.

[[fey|Fey]] checked the contents of his bag of holding and found it had stolen the ancient Netherese battleaxe Grace and the Aurum Spell Scrolls. Fey panicked.

[[patches|Patches]] and [[fey|Fey]] rushed after the Mummy Lord, blowing past the guards and dropping non-lethal spells behind them to slow any pursuit.

[[tark|Tark]] and [[gundren-rockseeker|Gundren]] tracked the creature to a nearby storeroom, but the door was locked.

A young Duergar came to investigate the commotion and introduced himself as Gil.

Gil was not convinced at first and tried to usher the group away and back to the storefronts and workshops.

[[gundren-rockseeker|Gundren]] finally managed to convince him to at least check the storeroom. Gil opened the door with a massive ring of keys.

[[fey|Fey]] and [[patches|Patches]] caught up to the group as the clatter of pursuit began to grow louder.

As [[tark|Tark]], [[patches|Patches]], and [[gundren-rockseeker|Gundren]] entered the storeroom, the Mummy Lord dropped from above, behind them.

The undead monster turned — loose linens flapping — wielding the leaf-shaped battleaxe Grace, with three golden scroll tubes tucked into its waist.

It embedded the axe into poor Gil, almost bisecting the Dwarf.

Then it turned to sand and rushed away.

[[tark|Tark]] shadowstepped in pursuit, and with an extraordinary act of dexterity managed to scoop the blowing trail of sand into his own bag of holding.

The guards caught up and saw the dead Duergar, readying their weapons to arrest the group.

Fortunately, the artificers who had witnessed the creature corroborated the group's account of an undead monster. The group took the amber-encased heart of the thing from their bag and handed it to the guards to destroy.

The guards tried and could not scratch the egg.

Legran returned, investigated the body, and noted that the wounds matched the story — none of the group carried a weapon that could have left them.

The group promised to resurrect Gil, and [[patches|Patches]] was sent under armed escort to collect JmJm, Doc B, and [[cirrus-al-kayan|Cirrus]] from the townhouse.

As she was leaving, with the egg still completely unharmed and the withered heart still sealed within, [[fey|Fey]] had an idea.

[[themberchaud|Themberchaud]] could breathe on the egg and destroy it.

Legran agreed, and the group exited the Anvilthew facilities to head toward the lair of the Red Dragon.

Outside they were greeted by dozens of plate-wearing armed guards and two Stone Golems, and were escorted out of the Darklake District, through a set of gargantuan doors, and into Laduguer's Furrow — the main residence area of the city for its native population.

[[patches|Patches]] made it back to the townhouse and sent Doc and JmJm to resurrect Gil, while she and [[cirrus-al-kayan|Cirrus]] were escorted to join the group heading toward the Dragon.

They crossed over the massive chasm that split the city in two, hundred-foot-wide bridges spanning it at every level, with buildings carved directly into the rock face above and below.

Many geothermal chimneys could be seen disgorging gases directly into the air.

Several of these vents had devices capping them, trapping the gases as they were expelled. Many more had Duergar held aloft by strange balloon-like devices, wielding long poles with instruments linked by hoses into the stream of gases.

The city was a bustle of activity here, no less than the Darklake District.

The main distinction was that no foreigners were normally permitted here — only Duergar and Stone Giants. The group attracted considerable attention as it was escorted through.

After over an hour of walking, the group finally arrived at the other side of the city, [[patches|Patches]] and [[cirrus-al-kayan|Cirrus]] catching up to their friends.

The air was much hotter here, cloying with humidity, burning their lungs with volcanic fumes. The smell of sulfur was overwhelming.

Before the escorted group stood a massive brass door shimmering with heat.

Red-robed and armored Duergar guarded the gates — gates large enough for the great Wyrm to pass through with ease.
