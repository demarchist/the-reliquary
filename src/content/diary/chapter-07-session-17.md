## Simulacra

As the children walked into the breakfast hall, [[Jack Mayer Johnson Mathews]] looked up from his plate and his blood ran cold. His wild-magic-gifted truesight showed the truth — [[Cercei]] and the Amnian wizard Leigham were not children and mage but clay puppets. Simulacra.

JmJm pulled [[Cirrus Al Kayan]] aside and the others convened. Fëanon approached Leigham while Patches slipped poison into Abdul's chewing tobacco. The Amnian copy, confronted, admitted he was a simulacrum — the real Leigham simply hadn't wanted to make the trip. But when [[Fëanon Serinde]] asked about the little girl, Leigham looked confused. "What girl?"

JmJm distracted the Cercei simulacrum in the ballroom with illusion-assisted dancing while the group cast detect magic and confirmed heavy illusion on both constructs. Their prime suspect was Mr. Shon. Patches and [[Gundren Rockseeker]] searched his office and found a long blue hair in the fold-out cot. The pieces clicked — Mr. Shon was [[Li Xian]], the Oni from the [[Sanctum of Krasus]].

They confronted [[Val Tengu]], who was flustered. He pulled open a drawer to show his truesight lantern — the one he used to check everyone entering the estate. It was gone. As they pressed him on his memories, Val began to seize — foam at his mouth, blood from his nose. A Geas, triggered by forcing him to confront the gaps. Cirrus cast greater restoration and the seizure stopped, but the man's past month was riddled with holes.

The children had to go. [[Sera Von Bumidere]] penned a letter to Forgefather Flamestoker in [[Sundabar]]. Fëanon inscribed a teleportation circle while JmJm kept the simulacrum occupied. As the false Cercei was taken from the room, Patches spotted her trying to see the sigil Fëanon was drawing. The real children, two guards, and Val stepped through to safety.

Doc cast locate creature and after an hour got a contact — on the estate's edge, moving away. Patches scanned with her spyglass and possibly saw a black robe disappearing into the [[Bluelake District]]. In the harbour beyond, forty ships of the Baldur's Gate Third Fleet rode at anchor.

Out front, JmJm sat with the simulacrum, but the little girl walked toward the garden hedges. Inside, the creature struck — a modify memory spell that JmJm couldn't resist. He returned dazed, telling the group the girl was fine.

[[Horatio von Bumidere]] turned away. Cirrus blasted the simulacrum with eldritch energy. It skidded through the gravel, sat up, and stared at him. He blasted it again. The body exploded into clay. JmJm sagged as his memories returned, sobbing. The group wondered how the simulacrum of a child could have wielded such power.

Sera rushed out with a note: the Dragonborn mercenaries had sensed another dragon in the city.

## The Council of Helm's Hold

The group teleported to [[Helm's Hold]]. Over fifty figures gathered around the platinum pool — calm and still today, no waterfall flowing. They recognised [[Megno Slatesunder]], [[Renaer Neverember]], [[Laeral Silverhand]], Thelm, Amarandine, [[Javen Tarkimos]], [[Aurinax]], [[Anaxtrasa]], and many more. A woman in a blue gown stared intently at Fëanon.

Amarandine called the council to session. Standing on a glass floor over the pool, Cirrus began the group's tale from [[Phandalin]]. As the story progressed, witnesses stood to corroborate — [[Mordenkainen]] joining for the Barovian chapter, Anaxtrasa confirming [[Castle Ravenloft]]'s relocation to the [[Anauroch]]. They spoke of [[Strahd Von Zarovich]], [[Rahadin]], [[Baba Lysaga]], the Illithid hive, the Eye of [[Theodemir]], [[Zhengyi]]'s rise, and [[Atropos]] freed from the Far Realm — glossing over the artifact whenever possible.

Whispers grew as the omissions became obvious. Two camps formed: the Amnians, Tethyrians, and Cormyrians demanding answers on one side, everyone else on the other. Cirrus seized the initiative before Abdul could rise. With thaumaturgy amplifying his voice, he revealed [[The Last Word]] — two pieces in Sundabar — and proposed sending them to [[Bahamut]]. Aurinax endorsed the plan. Anaxtrasa rolled her eyes. Abdul looked furious.

Mordenkainen reported that scrying the [[Thayan Drop Box]]'s chain of custody revealed only middlemen. The Oni hair hit a barrier of non-detection. [[Delly Griplim]] confirmed this was standard countermeasure.

Fëanon spoke with the woman in the blue gown — Lady Avelyere of Netheril. She recognised his robes as Netherese and was displeased to see an outsider wearing them. They discussed the resurgent empire. Fëanon was disappointed to learn Shade Enclave had devolved from meritocracy to aristocracy in the [[Shadowfell]].

Patches found [[Lady Faye]] with [[Lemerlais]] on the outskirts. Lemerlais reported that the south had been far less affected by the Mists — the undead were disparate, easily dispatched. The Mists had been focused on the north, on [[Neverwinter]].

Cirrus proposed naming the keep at [[Thunder Bluff]]: Herald's Keep. Everyone agreed. Doc got excited about a druid grove. JmJm declared he would raise a theatre named [[The Crossroads]] next to Patches' "Over the Moon" tavern.

[[Esimir Von Bumidere]] approached. The Amnians had been invited to dine at the estate. [[Lord Neverember]] would join to press the cause.

## Dinner at the Bumidere Estate

Back at the estate, Neverember worked Abdul and [[S'Olaa]] with Esimir — careful logic, sound arguments. S'Olaa seemed swayed. Abdul grew visibly worse as the poisoned tobacco took its toll.

Gundren settled into the kitchens with [[Gwen]], wanting nothing to do with nobles. Dinner was served — Underdark giant crab legs with Rothe butter and Bluecap spores. Abdul started coughing blood into his handkerchief and excused himself. Cirrus asked S'Olaa to continue.

The young Amnian picked up his cup to toast. Esimir stood near the window with Leigham. Neverember sat with steepled hands, the beginnings of a smile.

The clock chimed nine. Rain pattered against the windows, growing heavier.

The windows of the dining hall shattered inward.

Two fireballs engulfed the room. Esimir Von Bumidere was reduced to ash where he stood. The Leigham simulacrum was thrown to the ground, burning. [[Kurt Von Bumidere]] and [[Ernst Von Bumidere]] tumbled from their chairs, clothes catching fire. Sera was pushed over the table, ablaze. Neverember was knocked to the floor. S'Olaa vanished in a flash — his contingency spell triggered by the damage. Patches rolled under the table, untouched.

Fëanon registered the attack: two fireballs in the dining hall, but the house had been struck multiple times. The protective wards were doing nothing.

The estate began to burn.
