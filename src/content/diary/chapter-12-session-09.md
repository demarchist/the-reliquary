## Into the Temple

[[Cirrus Al-Kayan|Cirrus]] materialized in a dark, ominous chamber. Moments later, [[Liara]] appeared — and Cirrus, convinced she had lured him here to finish him off, attacked her. [[Horatio Von Bumidere|Buumi]] arrived seconds later to find the situation tense.

Above, the remaining party gathered around the dais. [[Feanon Serinde|Feanon]] investigated and confirmed it was a teleporter — relatively safe. [[Gundren Rockseeker|Gundren]] barged past the hesitant group, calling them pansies. The others followed and found Liara playing her stringed instrument to calm the scratched and raving Cirrus. [[Tarkathee Daru|Tark]] demanded she cease on pain of death. Liara complied, and Buumi corroborated her account: Cirrus had lashed out again.

A voice echoed through the chamber: *"May the light of Preservation guide you."*

## The Trials

The chamber was a mirror of the entryway above — monoliths descending as solid columns from the ceiling, but with rotating mirrors on pillars that could focus beams of light. The party deduced the puzzle: illuminate the symbols of gods aligned to Preservation, not Ruin. But before Buumi could coordinate, all three mirrors were moved at once, triggering the trap.

Stone constructs erupted from mechanical vents and attacked. Bloodied and exhausted from the battle above and a full day of hiking, the group fought grimly. Tark channelled his Ki — and triggered a wild-magic surge that reversed gravity within thirty feet. He, Buumi, and two constructs began falling toward the ceiling.

Feanon stepped into the reversed zone, somehow fixing himself in space, and snatched Buumi with a telekinetic grip — arresting the paladin's fall and arcing him gracefully through the air toward the remaining enemies. The floating constructs hit the ceiling and exploded into shards. Gravity normalized, and the survivors were smashed to dust.

Too battered to continue, they rested. Their spoiled rations forced them to rely on Gundren's Horn of Plenty stashed in the [[Bag of Holding]] — risky, so they skipped breakfast entirely.

During the rest, they worked out the mirror puzzle properly. Liara explained that Preservation and Ruin were analogous to the Positive and Negative energy planes. They identified the correct symbols, aligned the mirrors, and the dais carried them down.

The next trial was a climbing wall of inscribed tiles flanked by spiked protrusions. *"Avoid the pitfalls of Ruin,"* the voice intoned. Tark ascended, the group calling out which tiles were safe. The tiles shifted toward the spikes as he climbed. At one point Buumi called the wrong symbol and Tark was ejected by a flash of light, falling ten feet before catching himself. He made it to the top on the second attempt and pressed the sourcestone device on the altar above.

## The Visions

The deepest chamber opened onto a misty expanse — a mirror-like waterplane extending into infinity. Each monolith on this level contained a large mirror, each glowing with a colour corresponding to the question each party member had answered: *"What do you seek at this font of power?"*

Each was drawn to their mirror, reached out, and was transported to the [[Ethereal Plane]].

**Tark** found himself face to face with a younger [[Zhengyi]] — the embodiment of Power and Control. Furious, he attacked immediately. The being laughed and fought back, explaining that this was exactly what Tark should be doing: getting stronger. A servant of [[Orcus]] had come to this world over a thousand years ago, and Tark had to stop it. In the vision, Tark saw the great black pyramid and a glowing black skull above it, a white eye burning in its forehead.

**Feanon** met [[Theodemir]], his old teacher. He saw through the guise immediately and asked the being's true name: [[Narhan]], god of Knowledge. Feanon began questioning eagerly — but quickly realized that to Narhan, all knowledge held equal value. Mid-conversation about the world's destruction, the god asked Feanon to guess its physical size and began reciting the nutritional content of grilled squid. What Feanon did glean was devastating: Zalaam, the god of death for this world, was an imposter. To continue his search, he should seek the Isle of the Elves in the northwest.

**[[Snorbul]]** met his Uncle [[Gruttag]] — the gnome responsible for saving their village. The vision confirmed that [[The Calamity]] had brought cataclysmic change a thousand years ago. To get home to [[Faerun]], Snorbul should seek the [[Feywild]] of this universe and a portal within. To gain the strength for that journey, he should visit the Temple of Veketh.

**Buumi** met his mother, young as if from her adventuring days. She was [[Orrah]], goddess of harmony, balance, and time. She told Buumi he was already on the path to protecting his friends. To restore harmony to this world, two of Feanon's ritual reagents could be found at the Black Sea, where this world and the [[Shadowfell]] met.
