## The Wyrmsmith

The group headed through the great set of brass doors, escorted by the Stoneguard and Flameguard.

They entered a massive cavern that curved gradually, hiding most of the path from view beyond a couple of hundred feet.

As the heat intensified and sulfur hung heavy on the air, the group walked for a good ten minutes through the carved cavern.

Eventually they rounded the last bend and saw before them the largest pile of gold and treasure any of them had ever witnessed, atop which lay the fat Red Dragon [[themberchaud|Themberchaud]].

Lava dribbled from volcanic vents and small glowing lizards shifted and moved around in the intense heat — Salamanders.

The dragon spoke at their approach, chuckling as it joked to its minion about eating the party.

The jokes stopped quickly as he appraised the group, and his tone turned to one of curiosity.

Legran an Anvilthew recounted the events that had led the group there to the Dragon.

Thinking quickly, [[patches|Patches]] began to suggest that she did not think the Dragon was powerful enough to destroy the Mummy heart, and [[cirrus-al-kayan|Cirrus]] played off this by insisting that he clearly was.

With a shout, Thember demanded the amber egg be placed before him so he could demonstrate his power.

A Stoneguard rushed forward, placed it before the dragon, and then rushed away as the Dragon waddled up to it.

The torrent of flame that followed started as a messy deluge, but Thember controlled the gout and focused it down to an almost coherent beam of blue flame.

As the breath continued for several seconds, the stone turned molten around the amber as the unholy artifact shattered and exploded — a whirlwind of dark sand engulfed the area as a skeletal face screamed in the twisting dust.

It dissipated quickly, leaving Thember blinking in surprise.

Turning to Legran, Thember declared that whatever debt the group owed, he would take on himself.

The Duergar were shocked but did not protest.

Legran and the Stoneguard were dismissed, and Legran re-welcomed the group back to the smithing complex for further business now that the Mummy had been cleared up.

Thember turned to his handlers and servants — the Flameguard — and dismissed them too, wanting time to speak with the group in private. He called out several invisible Duergar to leave as well.

Now alone with the group, Thember shifted down to a form more comfortable for a discussion, taking on the shape of a massive Duergar.

Eight feet tall, obese, and with a long black beard ending in glowing embers, the red-and-gold-robed form of the dragon walked over to a setup of ornate furniture decorated and filigreed in a style that was decidedly not Duergar.

A massive spread of food and drink covered the table, and the group was welcomed to partake.

The discussion began with the group recounting their tale to the Dragon, [[tark|Tark]] taking the lead.

At one point, [[tark|Tark]] took the axe the Mummy Lord had stolen from the group and tossed it to [[snorbul|Snorbul]], replacing the makeshift weapon he had carried before.

[[snorbul|Snorbul]] was astounded by the powerful and ancient weapon.

Thember growled with hunger at the sight of it but restrained himself.

Convinced of the group's capabilities, Thember offered them a deal to resolve any lingering debt and provide easy movement around the city — the Dragon had the Flameguard present the group with golden badges marking them as personal servants of the Dragon.

In exchange, the group was to find out what had become of the previous Wyrmsmiths, the dragons before [[themberchaud|Themberchaud]]. He was beginning to worry that the Duergar intended to kill him and wanted the group, as outsiders with no loyalties in the city, to do some digging.

Additionally, if the Flameguard had tasks for the group, he asked them to carry those out and report back to him.

As they talked, one detail demanded a question — Thember mentioned the "Fiery Bitch" currently residing in Overlake Hold. An Archdevil that had arrived at the city a few weeks earlier and had been pushing to augment the city guard with Devils.

The group agreed. As they prepared to leave the audience, Thember asked one last thing of [[fey|Fey'Anon]] — he wished to learn wizardry from the elf. Fey agreed.

Leaving the Dragon's chambers, they were met by Gartokkar of the Flameguard. The Duergar was incensed that a group of foreigners had been given golden badges.

He practically demanded the group do something for him and his organization — hunt down the Derro [[droki|Droki]] and bring him in for questioning. He did not elaborate.

The group then took a rickshaw, trying to make good time through the city to pick up [[snorbul|Snorbul]] some clothes before heading to their dinner at the Shattered Spire.

## Traversing the City

Heading through the nicer side of the city, the rickshaw, pulled by Derro, passed the hold of the Deep King.

Passing the ornate black granite fortress trimmed in gold, the group noted it bristling with guards — seen and unseen.

Out front of the palace, a large plaza extended almost to the great chasm of Laduguer's Furrow, and at the center of the plaza stood a massive thirty-foot-tall humanoid covered in a white silk tarp.

The newest Adamantine Golem of the city — awaiting activation.

The Derro driver told the group that the Lairds of the city were squabbling over who would get the honor of sacrificing a set of twins (very rare) to fuel the construct with their souls.

Noting that the addled Derro seemed to know things, [[cirrus-al-kayan|Cirrus]] asked whether there had been any deaths in the Derro section of the city involving missing teeth in an area of iron bars and dirty scrap-filled caverns.

The Derro said there was death there in droves, and that he must be talking about the West Cleft — the old prison, now one of the Derro ghettos.

The Whorlstone Caverns were mentioned as a possible place [[droki|Droki]] could be found — the great cave complex filled with Faerzress beneath the city.

[[cirrus-al-kayan|Cirrus]] shifted his form to look like the Derro he had seen in his vision from long ago, the Warlock he had been hunting. He was not recognized.

Cirrus knew the Warlocks of [[ceregorach|Ceregorach]] were around, but he felt two pulses in the back of his mind — not one.

The group asked about [[droki|Droki]] and gathered a little more information, though vague. The Derro apparently moved back and forth between the West Cleft and the Blade Bazaar.

They also learned that he was shifty, exhibiting strange powers even by Derro standards.

Before long the group made it back to the Darklake District and stopped at a fancy Duergar-run clothing store, picking up a dress military uniform for [[snorbul|Snorbul]] — all black silk and silver buttons.

As [[snorbul|Snorbul]] was being outfitted, [[patches|Patches]] felt uneasy and looked around. She saw nothing, but felt watched, and asked [[tark|Tark]] and [[cirrus-al-kayan|Cirrus]] to keep an eye out.

One other thing the group noted in the market — a group of three Bearded Devils, apparently on patrol.

The rickshaws continued through the city, heading along the docks toward the Shattered Spire.

As they passed the abandoned area from earlier in the day — Hemeth's ship now gone — they spotted a lone Duergar at the end of a small pier.

Suddenly two Duergar became visible, knifing the Duergar on the pier with blades of glowing force energy, and a struggle began.

Seeing the conflict, the group rushed to intervene, [[tark|Tark]] shadowstepping into the fray.

The group struck the attackers with brutal force and managed to drive them off quickly as they went invisible, saving the life of the attacked Dwarf.

In payment, the Duergar — Werz an Saltbaron — gave the group a pouch of four large gemstones. [[patches|Patches]] took them and felt a slight buzz from the stones.

Werz was furious and suspected his trading partner had sent the killers. He asked the group to bring him the head of the Svirfneblin Kazzook from [[blingdenstone|Blingdenstone]].

[[tark|Tark]] was aghast and protested. As the group took their leave, [[patches|Patches]] winked at Werz, indicating they might actually do the job.

The group headed back to the waiting rickshaws. As they climbed in, [[patches|Patches]] spotted movement on a nearby rooftop — a Drow scout ducking into cover.

She told the group, and the rickshaw trundles the last stretch to the massive feast hall, fighting pit, and tavern that was the Shattered Spire.

## The Shattered Spire

They left the rickshaws and began crossing the massive wooden rope bridge connecting the Shattered Spire to the city.

The bridge was massive — easily large enough for the Stone Giants of the city to cross.

The area bustled with patrons coming and going.

Oddly for a tavern, there was no music — only the constant thrum of conversation.

Entering the massive establishment, the group saw that the first two floors were scaled up massively, clearly large enough for the Giants to spend time there. Above that, the next three floors returned to a regular scale.

The whole thing appeared to be serviced by a complex system of dumbwaiters, dispatching good food and drink with efficiency and speed.

Across the main floor of the common area, a massive set of wooden double doors swung open as patrons departed, and the group could hear the raucous cheers of the fighting pits.

The group headed to the nearest bar and slapped down ninety gold for everyone, with the food and drink kept flowing for the night.

Morgran the barkeep in this section grinned and took the money, assuring them a good evening.

The group took a quick detour to the fighting pits, witnessing an Orc and Goblin team fighting an Umber Hulk as the crowd cheered them on.

As they moved to head up to the third floor to find Chadle and Hemeth, a Drow approached them.

The scout from outside, her job done, had decided to approach the group and negotiate, worried she would be hunted down after [[patches|Patches]] spotted her.

They learned that Ilvara Mizzrym — the high priestess of Velkynvelve — had paid the Drow scout, Xalith, to report the party's location. She had done so, and Ilvara now knew they were in the city.

Xalith, as a Drow commoner, was now looking for her next job and offered her services to the group, though she would not go directly against a Priestess of Lolth.

The group decided to come back and talk to her later, leaving Xalith by the fighting pits.

The group took their leave and headed upstairs to find their other contacts.

They quickly found Chadle and Hemeth and sat with them as a large feast was brought out over the course of the evening.

Chadle and Hemeth noted the Flameguard badges the group now wore, shocked to see that the group had risen in stature so quickly. The group still paid Chadle for the papers he had acquired.

They talked with Chadle, clearly a member of the Grey Ghosts, the thieves guild in town.

Chadle was awfully chatty, and the group quickly realized this was information meant to reach [[themberchaud|Themberchaud]] — the Flameguard had a Dragon egg intended to replace Themberchaud, but it had been stolen recently. Previous Wyrmsmiths had been killed by the city, specifically by the Adamantine Golems — each dragon helping to complete one before being used to kill the dragon. They built their own murder weapons.

Chadle took his leave, smiling and expressing hope that the group would come to him and his friends with further business.

Hemeth remained and mentioned he had asked around about the possibility of stealing a Golem in the city.

He had a lead that Clan Steelshadow was finishing an order for an unknown benefactor — two Golems, currently inactive and awaiting delivery. He was still trying to find out where.

Hemeth left as the hours drew on.

[[patches|Patches]] began to grow impatient. She had asked almost an hour earlier whether her contact was present in the Shattered Spire. She finally received a note reading "prep your friends please."

Moments later, a grinning Duergar — almost clean-shaven and wearing distinctly non-Duergar-styled clothes — approached the group.

They recognized him but could not quite place the face, until [[patches|Patches]] greeted [[veygar|Veygar]] — her one-time kidnapper from beneath the Troll Hills of Baldur's Gate.

[[veygar|Veygar]] was their contact down here for Lady Faye. It appeared that after the botched kidnapping job, the two had gone into business together.

[[veygar|Veygar]] sat with them and described what had been found — ancient ruins depicting another fragment of the [[iron-tome|Last Word]].

He had lost contact with his crew out in the [[underdark|Underdark]], and with all that was currently happening with the demons, he refused to go check on them himself.

He lamented his lack of a crew and the group offered up Hemeth as a new crew member. [[veygar|Veygar]] was intrigued and decided to take Hemeth under his wing.

The group asked him about the stolen Dragon egg, disturbances in the West Cleft (confirming strange goings-on there), and asked for any news about Faen Tlabbar trading operations in the area.

[[veygar|Veygar]] raised a large mug of lager to the group — to letting bygones be bygones and to their current and future business dealings.
