## Izzy's Stuff

The group crossed the quiet cul-de-sac from [[tazmikella|Taz]]'s shop to the more impressive establishment opposite — Izzy's Stuff. They passed a few shoppers in the lane and stepped aside for a customer leaving just as they entered. A tinkling bell announced them.

Behind the counter stood a beautiful Drow man with cascading curls of silver hair, reading a book. He looked up over spectacles with warm blue eyes, and his smile showed perfect teeth that shone bright against plum-coloured skin. A delicate wine glass sat half-full beside him. He welcomed them to Izzy's — his name was [[dima|Dima]], and he minded the store while Izzy was away on business.

The group browsed a plethora of simple magical goods and chatted with the Drow. He was a warlock, he explained without preamble, and Izzy was likely his patron. A magical anklet and chain adorned his leg — the delicate rings fuzzing to insubstantiality a few inches from the limb. The group exchanged concerned looks, wondering whether Dima was a slave or a willing servant. [[gundren-rockseeker|Gundren]] nearly swore to end Izzy on the spot before Dima placated them — he had entered into the arrangement of his own volition.

Gundren grumbled about filthy slavers, but they resumed haggling.

[[aliyah-faen-tlabbar|Patches]] noted a cat that had followed them in from the street, watching from the doorway with the same green eyes as Taz. She suspected immediately that the old lady had followed them across the cul-de-sac.

## The Trade

[[tarkathee-daru|Tark]] pulled out the schematics for his [[windrunner-armor|Windrunner Armor]] and asked whether Izzy stocked the last ingredient he needed — [[elder-brain-ichor|Elder Brain Ichor]].

Dima disappeared into the back room and returned a minute later carrying a large purple jar, stoppered with some kind of exotic cork and placed it on his side of the counter. Tark's eyes went wide. It was the final component — the one he had been searching for across years of travel. Dima remarked that this was the only specimen of the ichor he had ever seen, let alone heard of. Elder Brains did not normally part with their ichor.

The haggling was steep but they settled on a trade. Tark set aside a number of rare goods and drew out the [[saurian-deathlord-greatsword|Saurian Deathlord's blazing greatsword]] — the titanium sourcework weapon of the last great general of a long-dead interplanetary empire, unique and otherworldly. Dima fawned over the blade and agreed to the terms: the Deathlord's sword, the Signet of [[house-faen-tlabbar|House Faen Tlabbar]], and a copy of the armor schematics in exchange for the ichor, three minor magical items, and a platinum-and-diamond pendant that Patches needed enchanted by [[feanon-serinde|Fëanon]].

Patches felt a little stiffed by the steep price. She sidled over to the cat, bent down, and whispered that she would appreciate getting her signet ring back.

The cat chirped and ran off into the back room.

It did not return. But as Patches shifted her foot, she felt something against her toe and looked down. The Signet Ring of House Faen Tlabbar lay on the floor beside her boot.

Content with their trade — and [[tazmikella|Taz]]'s quiet intervention — the group left the mysterious cul-de-sac behind.

## The Gnomish Diviner

Tark began working with Gundren on scheduling the armor's creation. Patches asked Fëanon about enchanting the new necklace for non-detection and planeshifting.

The conversation turned to [[gaddock|Gaddock]]. Tark was at odds with the others — he had sworn to Gaddock that he would slay the vampire and put him to rest, but the group wanted to scout first or recover the [[ashbringer|Sunsword]]. If they staked out [[house-muskov|House Muskov]] and Tark spotted Gaddock, he would attack to kill. The others tried to talk him out of it — unsuccessfully — until Fëanon pointed out that even with the Sunsword, they could not kill Gaddock permanently without staking him in his coffin. They did not know where his coffin was. To truly end Gaddock, they would have to find the vampire's resting place first.

Tark fell silent at that. The logic was irrefutable.

Fëanon consulted the [[iron-tome|Iron Tome]] and realised he lacked the divination magic that would help locate Gaddock or his coffin. The group asked a nearby guard for directions and before long found a small gnomish divination shop in one of the inner rings — a stone cottage that looked entirely out of place, surrounded by a tiny moss garden.

Fëanon entered to find the interior packed to the brim with books and scrolls. A small gnomish man greeted him by name as he walked through the door. Of course he knew — it was a divination shop.

A particular book caught the wizard's eye. At the front desk sat a glass case with no apparent way in, and inside, resting on a velvet pillow, was a book that looked remarkably like the Iron Tome on his hip. Penned across the cover in gold ink it read: *"Fëanon's Fantastic Spells."*

Fëanon inquired immediately. The gnome spun a tale — a time-travelling spellbook, written by Fëanon's future self. A hefty sum of gold would secure it.

The rest of the group clocked the scam instantly. Patches distracted the gnome by pulling books off shelves while Fëanon enchanted the little man, catching him off guard. The truth came quickly: the book was a fake. It automatically labelled itself as belonging to the first spellcaster who walked through the door, mimicking the appearance of their spellbook. The gnome then talked them into buying what was, in essence, a glorified mirror.

Fëanon collected his actual purchases — a selection of new divination spells — and they left the gnome unharmed and his pride somewhat dented.

## The Road to Bloodstone Pass

The group decided to ride out in pursuit of the Sunsword.

Everyone took to their mounts and headed northwest from the city gate along the main road toward [[bloodstone-pass|Bloodstone Pass]]. They rode for hours, the sun setting behind them, the stars emerging overhead as the landscape shifted from urban sprawl to open farmland.

They pulled off the main road at a well-used campsite, the lights of [[helioglabulus|Helios]] barely visible on the horizon. Fëanon cast the [[magnificent-mansion|Magnificent Mansion]] and most of the group retired inside for a pampered night's rest. Patches, [[cassian|Cassian]], and Tark remained outside by the fire, swapping adventure stories before taking watch.

Sometime past midnight, Cassian came alert. He sensed two or three large flying creatures passing far overhead — so far out of sight that only his extraordinary expertise registered their stealthy passing. He alerted Tark, but the things did not return, and the rest of the night passed without incident.

## The Village of the Bearcat

The group continued along the main road come morning, stopping at a small village for directions. At the centre of the settlement stood a massive sacred tree, and beneath its spreading boughs slumbered a creature that smelled, inexplicably, of buttered popcorn.

The Binturong was enormous — a great furry bearcat, some manner of patron of the land to whom the villagers brought regular offerings. Whether supernatural or simply ancient, the creature was a gentle giant. The group paid their respects, offering food, and the bearcat chuffed and purred its appreciation.

The villagers confirmed that the Aimu estate lay on the right-hand fork in the road, off the start of the Bloodstone Pass, beneath the shadow of [[ironfang-keep|Ironfang Keep]] in the foothills. They also warned the group: the patrolling army had reported raiders in the area.

The group thanked them and continued on. Before long they came to a ruined bridge. Tark led them across a fallen tree. Gundren, never one to mess about, simply took to the skies on [[arkto|Arkto]] and opted to scout ahead.

In the distance, he spotted a huge plume of smoke. Something big had recently started burning.

## The Raiders

Gundren flew higher to clear the rolling hills, the snowy Galena Mountains forming a backdrop behind him. Below, a small hamlet nestled in the hillside — a handful of buildings, several on fire, surrounded by a whirling mass of a hundred horse archers.

He dove to alert the others, then banked back up to engage from range. The group rushed to catch up.

Gundren opened with the Thundercannon from the air, shells exploding into the horsemen as they looted the town. The raiders rallied and returned fire, sending dozens of arrows skyward. Gundren took numerous shots and raised Arkto out of range as the rest of the group moved in.

Cassian rushed forward on Uni, spotted the enemy commander, and hit him repeatedly — knocking the man from his mount and forcing him into cover. Gundren hurled down a Dust of Dryness, conjuring a small tidal wave that swept even more archers from their horses and drowned several mounts.

Tark blitzed toward the conflict, leaping conspicuously across the hillside. Patches rushed forward, invisible. Fëanon flew low, hugging the terrain.

The raider commander took aim with his own bow — and Cassian shot him dead before the arrow left the string.

Between Gundren's bombardment and Cassian's marksmanship, they pinned down the bulk of the horsemen and killed many outright. A contingent split from the main group and charged toward the party. Tark met them head-on, catching dozens of arrows and deflecting or dodging as best he could. Gundren saw his friend turning into a pincushion and hurled down a Wall of Fire just as Fëanon called down an Ice Storm on the horde. Dozens of riders were knocked from their mounts. Many did not rise.

Tark rushed into the downed men and engaged them in close quarters. More horsemen bypassed the wall of fire and charged at Fëanon, loosing arrows at the spellcaster — and rode directly into Patches' tripwire. Horses went sprawling, bucking their riders. Patches hurled a tar bomb, then a firebomb, leaving the riders and their mounts restrained and burning. She shot the only survivor in the head with her flintlock.

The enemy charge was halted completely. With their commander slain, the remaining raiders fell into disarray. Fëanon decided to rout them fully and Wished a hallucinatory terrain across the landscape — the rolling hills transforming into an oppressive mushroom jungle of broken terrain and deep mud. The raiders cried out in terror and confusion and broke in full retreat.

The group hunted the fleeing horsemen through the illusory jungle, eliminating pockets of resistance until only a handful of survivors remained and threw down their weapons.

## Patches' Justice

Fëanon dismissed the illusion. The group gathered the prisoners outside the ransacked hamlet.

Patches picked out the highest-ranking survivor and told him that he and his tribe were never to return.

He agreed.

She slit his throat.

The rest of the group looked at one another, aghast. These were surrendered men. Prisoners.

Patches pulled her flintlock and pointed it at the next one. The man drew a knife and lunged, gouging her arm. She killed him in a quick riposte.

The remaining prisoners broke and ran. The Drow shot them in the back as they fled.

[[horatio-von-bumidere|Buumi]] put a hand on her arm to still her. She lashed out — not at him, but at the sentiment. These raiders had already murdered innocents. By letting them go, the group would simply be releasing them to kill again. She had seen it too many times, especially from her own kin.

They chose to live by the sword, she said. So they would die by it.

The hamlet burned behind her. No one had a rebuttal.
