## Captured

Fey'Anon's blindfold was ripped away and he was face to face with the Drow Wizard.

He had a good look around the dimly lit room and understood it to be a guard tower.

Two warriors stood looking out of arrow slits, a Quaggoth Servant stood with a platter of refreshments over by a trap door to a lower level.

The Drow Wizard looked over Fey, one of the priestesses standing at the door leading out to a spider-web bridge.

The priestess was the other from the battle. Now without her helmet, Fey recognized her as being younger than the other.

The priestess watched as the Wizard turned to a nearby table and looked over a large array of torture tools.

He selected a small pair of clippers and turned to begin questioning Fey'Anon about the hoard of magical items the group had with them.

The wizard wanted assistance in identifying the trove.

Fey tried to respond but realized the collar and manacles on him were silencing him from speaking.

The Drow reached up and touched the collar, allowing the wizard to speak twenty words.

Fey began ranting and the Drow sighed, leaned over and clipped Fey's left pinkie finger at the last knuckle.

The other Drow in the room let out chuckles and the priestess smiled.

The Wizard, Shoor Vandree, turned to the Priestess and said he could take care of it from there.

She turned and left, heading toward the Shrine of Lolth.

The wizard went on to offer, in common, Fey leniency and a good posting as a slave in exchange for his help with the spell books he had in his possession.

Outside the guard tower and near the prison, the group watched as one of the Drow warriors played with a Deep Gnome chained to the wall, cutting at the poor wretch.

The Gnome tried to defend himself with a steel rod, and did alright for himself, but was looking quite bloodied after the Drow's flurry.

Two corpses could be seen splayed on the wall nearby, a female dwarf and a Quaggoth, both eviscerated and infested with Spiderlings as a warning to those held within the slave pen.

Looking around in the prison, Patches spotted the younger priestess leave the tower and noted her being a lower rank than [[ilvara-mizzrym|Ilvara Mizzrym]].

She saw another couple of Quaggoth servants cleaning and patrolling with refreshments.

Feeling anxious, Patches sat down in a quiet corner to meditate and pray to Elistraee, summoning her bead of mercury to help her focus.

As the hours went by, Patches began to feel a building tension.

She understood the message: something was coming, an opportunity, and Elistraee blessed her to take advantage of it.

JmJm looked over the other prisoners and spotted the Myconid sprout, Toadstool.

Smiling to himself, he walked over, buck naked.

The little myconid was standing with [[shuushar|Shuushar]] and the large Kuo-Toa spotted the Bard approach. The large fishy eyes studied the bard.

JmJm and [[shuushar|Shuushar]] talked for a moment and then JmJm picked up the little myconid, which puffed out his rapport spores to talk to JmJm.

They communicated and the little myconid liked what it heard. It agreed to be the Bard's new squire, in exchange for the group taking him home — to the great Mushroom grove.

JmJm put the little guy down, satisfied at his new acquisition.

[[gundren-rockseeker|Gundren]] meanwhile began to investigate the web-like bars of the prison.

Seeing no entrance or exit, the Dwarf headed over to Patches and nudged her, asking if she had seen a cage like this before.

Patches stirred from her meditation and informed the Dwarf that she had: it was a standard slave cage with an antimagic field within.

The door appeared when a noble Drow used an insignia to command the bars to open. The insignia could be a ring, necklace or something similar.

Patches remembered the houses she noted in command here: Mizzryn, the fourth house, and Vandree, the seventh of [[menzoberranzan|Menzoberranzan]].

She then called the group over to her for some instruction.

As the group gathered around, the other prisoners grew close to listen as best they could without being invasive.

The Derro however, with the stark white hair and red robes, was right in the huddle with the rest of the group, and in undercommon, began to jabber about getting out.

Patches was not having any of it and sicced Mr. Pickles on the Derro.

Both small creatures pulled tiny knives, one made of flint and one of a sharpened claw, and began to go at it, knifing one another and circling.

JmJm began to yell "PRISON RIOT!" but was quickly silenced by Patches as she decked him with a punch.

JmJm looked sullen and went quiet — and suddenly yelled "RIOOOOT!!!"

Patches told the others that the story to stick to was a variant of the truth: they were down in the [[underdark|Underdark]] to escort their priestly friend [[cirrus-al-kayan|Cirrus]] to [[gracksuluth|Gracksuluth]] for religious reasons.

As Patches told the others the story and JmJm yelled out to start a riot, the Drow outside battling the Svirfneblin both stopped and watched the craziness beginning inside the slave pen: two small folk having a knife fight and a naked half-elf screaming to start a larger fight. The Drow shot his hand crossbow into the pen.

Toadstool felt the riot beginning and rushed over to one of the Gnome twins, bumping into him — harshly.

The Orc began to bellow and rage at the bars until Sarith, the Drow, leapt on his back and began attacking him.

[[shuushar|Shuushar]] did nothing but stand and watch.

[[gundren-rockseeker|Gundren]] tried to calm everyone down for a moment, surprising everyone.

Doc B decided to use the moment as a distraction, finding the largest hole in the bars and going over to see if he could squeeze out.

As he tested by poking his head out the bars, the fired crossbow dart skipped off Doc's skull, cutting him.

The Drow shook his head at the Cat-Gnome and then unchained [[snorbul|Snorbul]], the Svirfneblin he had been fighting, and shoved him toward the cage.

Seeing his chance, [[snorbul|Snorbul]] swung at the Drow with his metal bar.

The Drow deftly parried and riposted in a flurry, cutting and pummeling the Gnome into submission.

He picked up the body and threw it toward the bars of the Slave Pens.

Far across the [[underdark|Underdark]], the rescue group was wrapping up their long rest, allowing Megno to memorize his spells.

Buumi handed [[cirrus-al-kayan|Cirrus]] a written letter from Li Tan detailing the goings on in the Barony.

The letter said that mages from [[silverymoon|Silverymoon]] had been assigned to speed the construction of the keep, all the buildings had laid foundations and construction was going well.

It also mentioned that the War seemed to have stalled as the Balduran Navy managed to win their engagement and the Dale-Lands had thrown in with the North, giving the aggressive Amnians and Thayans pause.

No one thought the pause would last, but it was giving the north time to collect themselves.

The final part of the letter said that the Teleport circle being made at the Barony would be operational in 1 week.

The rescue party decided to start heading out, Buumi mentioning that he could only see about 15ft without light.

[[cirrus-al-kayan|Cirrus]] offered to hold his hand — he accepted.

Megno held up a small opal and began to cast Locate the Path.

A small beam of light showed him the direction and the group began to follow, Tark taking point but keeping darkness wrapped around them with Pass without Trace.

[[cirrus-al-kayan|Cirrus]] began to relay Patches' rules of the [[underdark|Underdark]] to Megno and Buumi and got it mostly right.

They headed out and quickly made it back to [[the-crossroads|the Crossroads]]. Looking into the cavern, they saw the carnage of the fight — dead Orcs and Goblins scattered.

Three large, grub-like creatures could be seen eating the corpses: Carrion Crawlers.

Remembering that their mucus was a powerful paralytic, they decided to try and kill one and harvest its mucus glands.

They managed to get close before the Crawlers got spooked and started to flee the cavern, but the group sprang their ambush.

They made quick work of the Crawler and Tark looked over the ruined corpse.

With his small knife, he managed to extract one of the glands and tie off the ends, securing it for later use.

Following Megno's guidance, they resumed their journey.

## Doc's Escape Attempt

The unconscious form of [[snorbul|Snorbul]] flew through the air at the slave pen and the Drow warrior clutched at his chest.

The bars spiralled outward, creating an opening as the body fell in.

Doc B leapt out of the hole as it closed and then leapt atop the surprised Drow, grabbing down the front of his armor.

The Drow came across with a flurry of attacks, hitting Doc badly, and the little Druid fell to the floor — clutching the Drow's insignia.

Thinking quickly, Doc B called out to the Feywilds and found a creature to answer his call. He brought forth a giant Scorpion.

The huge arachnid rushed forward, catching the Drow off guard.

The Drow parried both pincers but took the stinger in the shoulder, poison flooding his system.

Patches and [[gundren-rockseeker|Gundren]] started pelting the warrior with rocks to distract and give Doc an opening.

Seeing his opportunity, Doc turned and threw the insignia into the cage.

Inside the guard tower, the Drow Wizard heard the commotion and exited, leaving Fey behind.

The wizard walked across the bridge and cast a spell.

Just as the insignia was about to pass through the bars, Patches' hand outstretched to catch it, it bounced off of a Wall of Force.

The insignia landed on the outside of the slave pen and Doc B pounced on it and then leapt off the cliff, falling the three hundred feet to the water pool beneath.

As he went, Doc tried to slow his descent, doing a decent job until the last 100ft and tumbling the rest of the way.

Doc hit the water and plunged into the depths, alive, but hurt.

A voice called into his head: "Flesh for the Faceless Lord!"

High above, the wizard walked up to the cage smirking.

The escape attempt ended, the wizard mocked the group.

JmJm sauntered up to the bars and engaged the wizard.

After a few moments, the Wizard decided that Fey'Anon was not being forthcoming enough and decided to try the Bard instead.

JmJm jumped at the chance to leave the slave pens and try to escape.

The wizard commanded the Bard to walk forward and just as JmJm was about to hit the wall, the door opened and he stepped out.

As he exited the slave pens, magical restraints and the silencing collar appeared on him. The bard was shackled.

Following the Drow, JmJm headed into the guard tower and saw Fey chained up and bleeding.

As he passed, he tapped out a little signal to his friend, inspiring him to free himself.

The Wizard left the guard tower, taking the bridge over to the other main stalactite tower and into the Shrine of Lolth.

Inside, JmJm saw the younger priestess attending the shrine — a place of stone bleachers and pillows arrayed around a large carved statue of a half Drow, half spider.

The Wizard approached a trap door and headed down; JmJm followed.

Beneath the shrine was a lavish set of quarters, showing a plush bed on a raised dais, a writing desk, a large trunk and the entire place draped in lavish silks.

Still they continued, heading down another trap door to the chamber below, a more modest bed chamber belonging to the Wizard.

The place was packed with the group's gear laid out for inspection.

JmJm saw his sword and inched closer to it, but the Wizard kept him at arm's length from all of the gear.

Asking what each thing was, the Wizard began to go over the gear and JmJm obliged, beginning to tell him slowly and vaguely by recounting many of their adventures.

Hours went by and JmJm had the wizard eating out of the palm of his hand.

## Peade

Far below [[velkynvelve|Velkynvelve]], Doc B rushed from the water, desperate to outdistance the Grey Ooze swimming his way.

Bedraggled, the gnome hopped onto the shore and took a quick look around.

Two startled orc children looked over the cat and then turned and ran to the nearby village.

Not wanting to stick around, Doc rushed north into the tunnels. As soon as he found a secluded spot, the little gnome cast Wind-Walk and began to rush along the tunnels, searching for a way to help his friends.

Out in the [[underdark|Underdark]], the rescue team was making good progress.

Any time they saw signs of trouble, they avoided it and then re-checked their spell, staying on target.

The hours dragged on without issue, until Tark caught a whiff of a familiar smell — Carrion Crawlers.

Investigating, the group came into a large tunnel and spotted the creature ambling along a nearby wall. It had a saddle on its back.

Tark shadowstepped into the shadow and the creature freaked out, bolting but not attacking with its whisker-like tendrils.

Seeing leg straps on the saddle for inverted travel, the Monk quickly strapped himself in and pulled back on the reins.

[[cirrus-al-kayan|Cirrus]] saw the creature bolting and popped it with a single eldritch blast, hoping to knock it out of its panic.

As the blast hit it and Tark tried to calm it, it was enough to get it to calm down and the Monk got it under control.

Heading back along the ceiling, Tark admired the creature's ability to traverse any surface. He called it "Peade."

Arriving back at the rescue party, they began to go through the saddle bags.

They found a fancy bottle of syrupy red liquor, a map of the area and a letter.

The map was somewhat rudimentary but detailed enough that the group could see how to circle around [[velkynvelve|Velkynvelve]] and come at it from the south, as they were currently approaching from the north.

The map also showed a highlighted area in the north west, north of [[gracksuluth|Gracksuluth]].

[[cirrus-al-kayan|Cirrus]] cast Comprehend Languages on himself and began to read the letter.

It said: "Blurg, The Society of Brilliance is being assembled. We must save the [[underdark|Underdark]] from this growing malaise. Meet at Gravenhollow — Skriss."

Intrigued, the group stowed everything for later use and continued south, toward their missing friends.

Now with one mount, [[cirrus-al-kayan|Cirrus]] summoned a Phantom Steed, a beast of stars and moonlight, and the group rode on, surrounded by stealth magic.

An hour or two went by and a breeze rushed past them.

A moment later, Doc B said hi from the saddle behind Buumi on [[cirrus-al-kayan|Cirrus]]' phantom steed.

Coming to a halt, the rescue group greeted their friend, noting his lack of equipment.

Doc B recounted his tale, holding up the Drow insignia and telling the group everything he had learned of where the others were held.

Now with another group member, and a great deal of information, the group began to circumvent [[velkynvelve|Velkynvelve]] and head in from the south.

## Back in the Slave Pens

[[snorbul|Snorbul]] woke in the slave pen and sat up, sore.

He walked over to [[gundren-rockseeker|Gundren]] and introduced himself as [[snorbul|Snorbul]].

The Dwarf understood enough and said his name was [[gundren-rockseeker|Gundren]].

Patches walked over and [[snorbul|Snorbul]] saw the Drow, scowled and turned away.

The deep gnome twins, who had been pretty standoffish, began talking to [[snorbul|Snorbul]] and he got the down low from them about what they thought of the surface dwellers.

Mr. Pickles and Buppido were still fighting — they were exhausted and both on their last legs.

Patches went over and broke up the fight and Buppido praised their tenacity with breathlessness.

Mr Pickles and Buppido went off to the corner to discuss knifing techniques.

Sarith, the imprisoned Drow, called out to one of the guards and Patches overheard them talk.

Sarith said he would tell his captors where he stashed treasure in exchange for leniency.

The heavily scarred guard went off to tell one of the priestesses.

[[gundren-rockseeker|Gundren]] headed over and beat Sarith again, demanding to know what the Drow prisoner had asked for.

Sarith whimpered and told [[gundren-rockseeker|Gundren]] that he had promised some trinkets he had stashed in the tunnels in exchange for freedom.

[[snorbul|Snorbul]] approached, noting [[gundren-rockseeker|Gundren]] beating the Drow. He liked this.

Patches introduced herself to the Deep Gnome and [[snorbul|Snorbul]] seemed to accept her this time. He asked if she was with the other Drow and Patches cleared it up that she was not with these other Drow.

[[snorbul|Snorbul]] began to tell them what he had noticed over his week of imprisonment: 16 Drow, 6 of which were elites, and then another dozen Quaggoth slaves.

He said they could not fight them in a pitched battle.

Patches told him to get ready for a breakout — their friends were coming to get them.

[[shuushar|Shuushar]] ambled over and said he would get everyone ready to move.

They began to chat with [[ront|Ront]], telling him of the Barony and their Orc friends there.

[[ront|Ront]] said he had come down here for a reason and Gruumsh had a task for him: to find an object of power in the [[underdark|Underdark]]. He would not leave until it was done.

He told the group he had left his clan to come down here, fleeing the war to do a task for Gruumsh.

Patches said she too was a deserter and [[ront|Ront]] spat at her feet.

Patches responded that being a deserter was simply a path to move forwards and get better.

His Orc mind churning it over, [[ront|Ront]] went quiet.

Some time went by and eventually, the heavily scarred guard came back and let Sarith out of the cage.

He told him that [[ilvara-mizzrym|Ilvara Mizzrym]] had purchased his slave contract. He now served her.

Upset at not being truly freed but seeing it as a positive, Sarith shuddered and headed off to begin his tasks working for [[ilvara-mizzrym|Ilvara]].

## JmJm's Gambit

JmJm finished up one of his tales and Shoor Vandree nodded his head.

The Drow Wizard mentioned that JmJm would be a fantastic acquisition for his house and offered to buy him in exchange for continued help.

JmJm acted bashful, thanking the Wizard for the compliments.

The Wizard continued on, saying they had been waiting for reinforcements from [[menzoberranzan|Menzoberranzan]], but the patrol was late.

Shoor Vandree continued on, saying that with his pull, JmJm would be performing for a Matron mother — the Bard just needed to tell him which of the treasures he should try and hide away for himself.

Thinking that it was now or never, and with the collar temporarily deactivated for his reply, JmJm cast Dominate Person on Shoor.

The Wizard staggered, trying to fight past the wave of debilitating energies — but failed.

JmJm's new friend looked up at him and smiled.

He removed the collar and the restraints and JmJm went about equipping himself with his gear, getting assistance with his armor from Shoor.

Then JmJm took all the remaining gear and deposited it into the two bags of holding.

Shoor headed up to [[ilvara-mizzrym|Ilvara]]'s chambers — she was not there — so JmJm followed.

Then they did the same with the Shrine to Lolth, Shoor heading up first.

Through their telepathic bond, JmJm knew that as Shoor headed up the ladder, he was greeted and addressed by two Drow: the Priestesses, and a third Drow, Sarith, was in the room.

In the nearby tower, Fey thought it was about time to make his move.

He began to struggle and make a scene, mouthing words at the one remaining guard — the other now standing outside watching the prisoners.

Eventually, the remaining guard came over to see what the wizard was about. He did not touch the collar.

As the Drow got within 5ft of him, they locked eyes and the Drow was incapacitated by Fey's magical gaze.

Having worked subtly for hours to loosen his shackles, Fey finally managed to slip them, using the blood on his raw wrists.

The Quaggoth Servant began to growl and hoot, shifting uncomfortably, but Fey was quick — he grabbed the Drow's hand, touching the collar around his neck.

As he regained the ability to speak, the Drow shook his head, coming out of his stupor, just in time for Fey to grab one of his swords from his belt and slice upwards, taking a finger.

Uttering the command word, Fey teleported back to [[silverymoon|Silverymoon]].

He arrived in the teleport circle of the city, bloody and livid, screaming at the attending mage for a Spellbook before the silencing collar took effect again.

## The Ascent

Far below [[velkynvelve|Velkynvelve]], the rescue group had arrived and begun their ascent.

Avoiding the Orcs and heading up through the carpet of massive spiderwebs, the group moved through them as nimbly as they could so as not to alert the Arachnids.

They touched a few strands of webbing and noticed some of the massive spiders stir, attempting to see some prey.

A truly massive spider shifted from its position, slowly walking in the general direction of the rescue party, but they outdistanced it quickly and ascended past the webbing.

Continuing upwards, they made it to the ceiling among the stalactites.

Doc B started to hear a droning buzzing sound coming from the North.

Off at the prisoner cage, Patches began hearing the sound too.

Before long, the guards began to shift uncomfortably and look around, just as the alarm went up from within the guard tower that Fey had escaped.

The guards blew the alarm whistle, a shrill scream that pierced through the roar of the waterfall, just as a flight of horrid creatures darted from the northern passageway toward the Drow base.

[[snorbul|Snorbul]] and Patches looked over the creatures, lit in purple from the Faerie Fire lights of the Drow complex, and recognized them as Chasme Demons, followed closely by a flight of Vrock Demons.

The base began to scramble their defenses.
