## The Wardens

The debate between [[Cirrus Al-Kayan|Cirrus]] and the Speaker intensified until a voice cut through. The Wardens — [[Iyum]]'s city guard — moved in to disperse the crowd, led by a scarred female Tabaxi named Ime. She ordered [[Sebek]] to cease his preaching as it disturbed the peace. About half the crowd left with the [[Cult of Zalaam|cultists]].

Ime introduced herself as one of the [[Usurpers of Rudjek]] and asked the group for statements. Most cooperated, but [[Tarkathee Daru|Tark]] and [[Gundren Rockseeker|Gundren]] had already slipped into the departing crowd, hot on the Speaker's heels. Gundren opened a [[Bag of Holding]] and [[Gwen]] emerged — the crowd recoiled in terror at the machina. Gundren dashed toward Sebek.

Then a blade sank into Gundren's back.

Tark spotted the assassin instantly and gave chase. He and Gwen converged on the dark-hooded figure, pinning the cultist. [[Horatio Von Bumidere|Buumi]] rushed over with healing magic while Ime and her Wardens arrived to arrest the man.

The group asked to interrogate the assassin first. In an abandoned building, they revealed to Ime that the assassin — and Sebek himself — were undead. Ime was shocked. The cultist confirmed that many of the Black Skull's members were undead, that they planned to seize the city, and that their headquarters lay somewhere in the Harbour District.

Ime ended the interrogation with a flurry of invisible punches that killed the cultist. A wave of energy radiated from her, revealing two astral arms. Tark was impressed by the Tabaxi's ki control — unlike anything he had seen, yet it resonated with his own training. He estimated her a decent opponent — no match for his group, but worthy of respect.

Ime told them that [[Liara]] had warned about the cult months ago and had gone to the Red Hills to find proof. She had returned only recently with [[Djoser]] and some extraordinary stories. She directed the group to the Brickyard in the Smith District, where Djoser was organising the spelljammer expedition.

## The City of Iyum

The heroes moved west through [[Iyum]]. The ancient road through the city's heart was flanked by truly colossal ruins, now divided into smaller residences and businesses. Each side of the street had a distinct look — one mostly new stone construction, the other wood and mud brick. They passed through an industrial souk where Buumi purchased carapace armour to finally cover his sunburns, and [[Jack Mayer-Johnson Mathews|JmJm]] found a tailor and a pigment merchant, dyeing his new titanium armour in greens and blues to match his turban.

Beyond the titanic city wall lay the industrial quarter — not quite a slum, but dirty, reeking of smoke and dust. In a clearing near a quarry, they found Djoser under a pavilion surrounded by palettes of brick, conferring with a man in noble attire over a map. The [[Hammer of Niraz]] glittered across the Grand Warden's back. Djoser looked up at the approaching crowd of outlanders — including the machina lion — frowned slightly, shook his head, and welcomed them amicably.

He introduced his companion as Nazim of [[Ras Alkan]], who departed to prepare for the expedition. Djoser led the group to a cantina where they told their tale over drinks. Several hours and a drinking contest later, a somewhat inebriated Djoser was convinced. He agreed to let [[Feanon Serinde|Feanon]] cast Arcane Recall on the hammer.

## The Mob

[[Aliyah Faen Tlabbar|Patches]] heard it first. A rhythmic chanting, growing louder. The ground trembled. A fetid stench of unwashed bodies reached them.

*"ALHAMDU ZALAAAAAM!"* — from a thousand throats.

A mob had formed in the streets. Peasants brandishing torches, makeshift weapons, and stones surged toward the cantina. On a nearby rooftop, backlit by the small moon, stood a crooked figure — Sebek, the Speaker of the Black Skull.

*"I told you this was not over,"* he called. *"The citizens of Iyum do not want you here anymore. Leave this city, or die by the hands of Zalaam's worshippers. You cannot kill all of us."*

Gundren threw down [[Arkto]] and mounted — but Sebek cackled and cast a Dispel, unravelling the magic. Gundren spat, shouldered his rifle, and Gwen stepped up beside him.

Cirrus and Feanon determined the crowd was mind-controlled by the Corruption — a spell of Vecna's that covered the land. This mob was only the beginning. As Vecna's will waxed, the sickness would spread and enslave more.

JmJm, Feanon, and Cirrus each cast sleep and enchantment spells into the crowd, dropping hundreds unconscious and unharmed — only for the horde behind to trample over them. Buumi sensed undead mixed among the living. Tark shadow-stepped to the rooftop and attacked Sebek, who was protected by Mirror Image. An assassin struck from the crowd and buried a knife in Tark's back. The monk staggered, bleeding badly.
